Loaded Moroccan Harissa Falafel Naan Plate

Crispy deep-fried harissa falafel balls served over golden cilantro couscous with jammy apricot-orange preserved lemon sauce, green olives, preserved lemon cilantro sauce, sweet-spicy cumin cashews, and warm naan for scooping and folding.

Flavor Profile

This plate is built around contrast: hot crispy falafel, jammy apricot-orange preserved lemon sauce, briny green olives, golden couscous, fresh cilantro sauce, sweet-spicy cashews, and warm naan.

Target bite: warm naan + golden couscous + crispy harissa falafel + apricot-orange preserved lemon sauce + cilantro sauce + sweet-spicy cashew crunch.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Harissa Falafel Balls

Dried chickpeas, herbs, preserved lemon, warm spices, cayenne, and paprika become the crispy star of the plate.

Apricot-Orange Sauce

Jammy onion, garlic, ginger, apricot, preserved lemon, green olives, orange marmalade, and warm spices create the sauce heart.

Cilantro Sauce

Fresh, tangy, green, spicy sauce keeps the plate bright.

Golden Couscous

A smaller supporting base that catches sauce without taking over the plate.

Spiced Cashews

Sweet-spicy cumin cashews add crunch, salt, sweetness, and warmth.

Warm Naan

Serve on the side so each bite can be scooped, folded, or dragged through sauce.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Moroccan Harissa Falafel Balls

1 lb dried chickpeas, soaked 18–24 hours; 1 small onion; 4 scallions; 5 garlic cloves; 1 small carrot; 1/2 packed cup parsley; 1 1/2 packed cups cilantro; 2 Tbsp mint; 2 tsp preserved lemon rind; 2 1/2 tsp cumin; 1 1/2 tsp coriander; 1 tsp paprika; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp cardamom; tiny pinch cinnamon or allspice; 1/2 tsp cayenne; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 2 tsp kosher salt; 1 Tbsp baking powder; 2 Tbsp chickpea flour to start, up to 4 Tbsp total as needed; AP flour only as backup; neutral oil for frying.

Falafel Binder Rule

Use chickpea flour first. Start with 2 Tbsp chickpea flour. After chilling, test shape and test fry one ball. If the mixture is loose, wet, or breaks in the fryer, add 1 Tbsp more chickpea flour at a time, up to 4 Tbsp total. Use all-purpose flour only if chickpea flour is unavailable. Add cold water only if the mixture is too dry or crumbly to hold.

Jammy Apricot-Orange Preserved Lemon Sauce

1 very large or 2 medium yellow onions, thinly sliced; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 1 Tbsp butter; pinch salt; 3 minced garlic cloves; 1 Tbsp grated ginger; 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste; 1 cup broth; 1/2 cup chopped dried apricots; 1 preserved lemon quarter, rind minced; 1/3 cup halved green olives; 1 Tbsp orange marmalade; 1 tsp honey; 1/2 tsp soy sauce or Worcestershire; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika; 1/4 tsp turmeric; 1/8 tsp cinnamon; 1/4 tsp cayenne; 1 tsp preserved lemon brine or lemon juice.

Preserved Lemon Cilantro Sauce

1 packed cup cilantro; 2 Tbsp mint; 1 small garlic clove; 1 tsp preserved lemon rind; 1 Tbsp lemon juice or brine; 3 Tbsp olive oil; 1 Tbsp water; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/8 tsp coriander; pinch cayenne; 1/4 tsp salt; optional 1/2 tsp honey or orange marmalade.

Golden Cilantro Couscous

1 cup couscous; 1 cup broth; 1 Tbsp butter; 1/4 tsp turmeric; tiny pinch cumin; 1–2 tsp preserved lemon rind; 2–3 Tbsp chopped cilantro; salt to taste.

Sweet-Spicy Cumin Cashews

1/3 cup chopped cashews; 1 tsp butter or olive oil; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/8 tsp coriander; pinch cayenne; 1/2 tsp brown sugar; 1/4 tsp kosher salt.

Service

4–6 warm naan; extra cilantro and mint; lemon wedges; extra preserved lemon rind; optional pickled onions.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Soaked Chickpeas

1 lb dried chickpeas, soaked 18–24 hours, drained, rinsed, and dried.

Bowl 2 — Falafel Aromatics & Herbs

1 small onion; 4 scallions; 5 garlic cloves; 1 carrot; 1/2 cup parsley; 1 1/2 cups cilantro; 2 Tbsp mint; 2 tsp preserved lemon rind.

Bowl 3 — Falafel Dry Seasoning & Binder

2 1/2 tsp cumin; 1 1/2 tsp coriander; 1 tsp paprika; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp cardamom; tiny pinch cinnamon/allspice; 1/2 tsp cayenne; 1/2 tsp pepper; 2 tsp salt; 1 Tbsp baking powder; 2 Tbsp chickpea flour to start, plus up to 2 Tbsp more if needed. AP flour is backup only.

Bowl 4 — Sauce Onion

1 very large or 2 medium thin-sliced onions with pinch salt.

Bowl 5 — Sauce Aromatics

3 minced garlic cloves and 1 Tbsp grated ginger.

Bowl 6 — Sauce Spice Bloom

1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika; 1/4 tsp turmeric; 1/8 tsp cinnamon; 1/4 tsp cayenne.

Bowl 7 — Apricot Broth

1/2 cup chopped apricots plus 1/2 cup hot broth.

Bowl 8 — Sauce Finish

Preserved lemon rind; 1/3 cup olives; 1 Tbsp marmalade; 1 tsp honey; 1/2 tsp soy or Worcestershire; 1 tsp lemon brine.

Bowl 9 — Cilantro Sauce

Cilantro, mint, garlic, preserved lemon, lemon/brine, olive oil, water, cumin, coriander, cayenne, salt, honey if needed.

Bowl 10 — Couscous

1 cup couscous; 1 cup broth; 1 Tbsp butter; turmeric; cumin; preserved lemon; cilantro.

Bowl 11 — Cashews

Cashews, butter/oil, cumin, coriander, cayenne, brown sugar, salt.

Bowl 12 — Service

Warm naan, herbs, lemon wedges, optional pickled onions.

Do Not Forget

Do not cook the chickpeas or use canned chickpeas for this version.

Dry chickpeas well, grind twice, chill the mixture, and fry one test falafel before the full batch.

Use chickpea flour first for binder; AP flour is backup only. Start with 2 Tbsp chickpea flour, then add 1 Tbsp more at a time only if the chilled mixture is loose or breaks during the test fry.

Add cold water only if the mixture is too dry or crumbly to hold.

Keep oil at 350°F.

Shape as balls, not patties: about 1 heaping tablespoon each.

The apricot-orange sauce should be glossy and spoonable, not watery.

The cilantro sauce should be punchy and fresh.

Warm naan right before service.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Soak chickpeas. Place 1 lb dried chickpeas in a large bowl. Cover with cold water by 3–4 inches and add 1/2 tsp baking soda if using. Refrigerate 18–24 hours. Do not cook.
  2. Drain and dry. Drain, rinse, and spread chickpeas on towels. Dry 30–60 minutes.
  3. Grind falafel. Grind chickpeas with onion, scallions, garlic, carrot, parsley, cilantro, mint, and preserved lemon rind. Grind twice for a cohesive, coarse mixture.
  4. Season falafel. Mix in cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cardamom, cinnamon/allspice, cayenne, black pepper, salt, baking powder, and 2 Tbsp chickpea flour. Use AP flour only if chickpea flour is unavailable. Add more chickpea flour only if the mixture is wet or loose. Add cold water only if too crumbly to hold.
  5. Chill. Cover falafel mixture and chill 45–60 minutes.
  6. Start sauce. Pour 1/2 cup hot broth over chopped apricots. Cook onions with olive oil, butter, and salt until collapsed, golden, and jammy, 15–20 minutes.
  7. Build sauce base. Add garlic and ginger; cook 45–60 seconds. Add sauce spices; bloom 30 seconds. Add tomato paste; cook 60–90 seconds until darker.
  8. Simmer sauce. Add softened apricots with broth, remaining 1/2 cup broth, preserved lemon rind, marmalade, honey, and soy or Worcestershire. Simmer 10–15 minutes until glossy. Add olives near the end. Balance with preserved lemon brine or lemon juice.
  9. Blend cilantro sauce. Blend cilantro, mint, garlic, preserved lemon, lemon juice/brine, olive oil, water, cumin, coriander, cayenne, and salt. Taste. Add honey or marmalade only if too sharp.
  10. Toast cashews. Toast cashews with butter/oil, cumin, coriander, cayenne, brown sugar, and salt for 2–4 minutes. Remove before scorching.
  11. Shape falafel balls. Roll into balls about 1 heaping tablespoon each.
  12. Heat oil. Heat deep-frying oil to 350°F.
  13. Test fry. Fry one falafel. If it falls apart, add 1 Tbsp chickpea flour and chill briefly. Repeat only if needed, up to 4 Tbsp total chickpea flour. If it tastes flat, add salt or cayenne.
  14. Fry batch. Fry falafel in small batches at 350°F until deep golden and crisp, about 3–5 minutes. Drain and salt lightly while hot.
  15. Make couscous. Bring broth with butter, turmeric, cumin, and salt to a boil. Pour over couscous, cover 5 minutes, fluff, then fold in preserved lemon and cilantro.
  16. Warm naan. Warm naan until soft and flexible.
  17. Plate. Add a smaller mound of golden couscous. Spoon jammy apricot-orange sauce around and over it. Add hot falafel balls. Drizzle with preserved lemon cilantro sauce. Scatter sweet-spicy cashews, herbs, and preserved lemon. Serve warm naan on the side for scooping and folding.

Chef's Note

This plate should feel like a Moroccan-inspired street-food platter: crispy, saucy, bright, sweet-spicy, herb-heavy, and interactive. The falafel is the star; the couscous is the sauce catcher; the naan turns every bite into a scoopable or foldable moment.
