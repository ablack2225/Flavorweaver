# Old Fashioned Salmon Patties

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Open canned salmon and remove the large bone/vertebrae, as Sue always did. Keep the salmon liquid with the fish. |
| 2 | In a large mixing bowl, combine salmon and egg. Mix well. |
| 3 | Stir in chopped jalapeño, or serrano for a spicier flare. |
| 4 | Season with salt and pepper to taste. |
| 5 | Gradually mix in crushed saltine crackers until mixture is firm enough to shape into patties. |
| 6 | Form mixture into 4 evenly sized patties. |
| 7 | Heat vegetable oil in an electric skillet over medium heat. |
| 8 | Once oil is hot, carefully place patties in the skillet. |
| 9 | Fry patties for about 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy. |
| 10 | Reduce heat to low and continue cooking for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until patties are cooked through. |

## Notes

This was Sue Wallesch's handwritten recipe.

Sue made these salmon patties in an electric skillet.

Amy noted that Sue always removed the fish bone/vertebrae from the canned salmon.

Sue said this recipe must have jalapeño, so jalapeño is included as part of the active recipe. Serrano may be used for a spicier flare.

Timing and servings come from the clarified recipe. Sue's electric skillet, bone-removal, and jalapeño notes are preserved from family practice.
