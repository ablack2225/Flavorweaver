# Chicken Shawarma Naan Wraps with Garlic Sauce, Lemon Tahini & Crispy Potatoes

> Deeply spiced chicken thighs cooked hot, sliced thin, and re-crisped into shawarma-style edges, wrapped in warm homemade naan with bold garlic sauce, lemon tahini, shawarma-dusted crispy potatoes, pickled acid, tomato, cucumber, onion, flat-leaf parsley, and optional fresh mint.

## Flavor Target

The finished wrap should taste juicy, deeply spiced, garlicky, lemon-bright, lightly smoky, tangy from pickles, creamy from garlic sauce, nutty from a thin tahini stripe, and fresh from tomato, cucumber, onion, flat-leaf parsley, and optional mint.

The chicken should have browned edges and a sliced shawarma texture. The naan should stay warm and flexible. The potatoes should stay crisp enough to give a clear bite inside the wrap. The sauces should be bold without making the wrap soggy.

## Before Cooking / Advance Prep

- **Marinade timing** — Marinate the chicken **12 to 18 hours before cooking**. Do not extend the lemon-garlic marinade beyond 24 hours.
- **Dry spice blend** — The shawarma spice blend can be mixed 1 to 2 days ahead and kept covered.
- **Sauces** — Garlic sauce and lemon tahini can be made up to 1 day ahead and refrigerated.
- **Naan component** — If making [Fluffy Skillet Naan](../../indian/breads/fluffy-skillet-naan.md), the dough can be refrigerated after the first rise, then brought to room temperature before shaping and cooking.
- **Potato prep** — Potatoes can be cut and held in cold water the day of cooking, then dried very well before cooking.

## Do Not Forget

- Marinate the chicken **12 to 18 hours before cooking**.
- Keep the lemon juice controlled at **1 Tbsp** for the overnight chicken marinade.
- Use liquid smoke sparingly if using it; the target is a quiet smoky note, not barbecue flavor.
- Use chicken thighs for juiciness and browned shawarma-style edges.
- Cook chicken pieces whole, then rest, slice thin, and re-crisp.
- Garlic sauce is the primary sauce. Lemon tahini is a stripe or side dip.
- Drain pickles or pickled onions very well.
- Potatoes should be crisp before they go into the wrap.
- Warm naan before filling. Cold naan will make the wrap feel flat.
- Use flat-leaf parsley as the main fresh herb.
- Use fresh mint only as a small optional cooling note.
- Do not overload fresh vegetables; controlled freshness keeps the wrap tight.
- Griddle seam-side down after wrapping if the naan needs help setting.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Mix the dry shawarma spice blend.** In a small bowl, combine **1 1/4 tsp kosher salt**, **2 1/2 tsp ground cumin**, **1 1/2 tsp ground coriander**, **1 1/2 tsp plain paprika**, **3/4 tsp turmeric**, **3/4 tsp allspice**, **1/2 tsp ground cardamom**, **1/4 tsp cinnamon**, **1/2 tsp ground ginger**, a **tiny pinch nutmeg**, **3/4 tsp black pepper**, and **1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne**. |
| 2 | **Make the chicken marinade.** Whisk **3 Tbsp oil**, **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, **1 tsp lemon zest**, **5 grated garlic cloves**, **2 Tbsp Greek yogurt or sour cream**, optional **1/8 tsp liquid smoke**, **1 1/2 tsp honey or brown sugar**, and the full spice blend. |
| 3 | **Marinate the chicken.** Add **1 1/2 lb chicken thighs**, turn until coated, keep pieces whole, cover, and refrigerate **12 to 18 hours**. |
| 4 | **Make the garlic sauce.** Stir **1/2 cup mayonnaise**, **1/4 cup Greek yogurt or sour cream**, **3 grated garlic cloves**, **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, **2 tsp olive oil**, **1/4 tsp salt**, and a tiny pinch sugar or honey. Thin with **1 to 2 tsp cold water** only if needed. |
| 5 | **Make the lemon tahini.** Whisk **1/3 cup tahini**, **2 Tbsp lemon juice**, **1 small grated garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp salt**, and **1/4 tsp cumin**. Add **4 to 6 Tbsp ice-cold water**, **1 Tbsp at a time**, until pourable. |
| 6 | **Prepare the naan.** Make or warm **4 naan**. For the house version, use [Fluffy Skillet Naan](../../indian/breads/fluffy-skillet-naan.md). Keep naan covered and warm. |
| 7 | **Prepare shawarma-dusted crispy potatoes.** Cut **2 to 3 medium potatoes** into 1/4-inch fries or small wedges, or use frozen fries. Cook until crisp. While hot, season with **3/4 tsp salt**, **1/2 tsp plain paprika**, **1/2 tsp garlic powder**, **1/4 tsp cumin**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, optional cayenne, and a little lemon zest or lemon juice. |
| 8 | **Prep the fresh and pickled fill.** Slice **1 large tomato**, **1/2 to 1 cucumber**, and **1/4 to 1/2 onion**. Chop **a small handful flat-leaf parsley** and optional **1 to 2 tsp fresh mint**. Drain **1/2 to 3/4 cup pickles or pickled onions** very well. |
| 9 | **Cook the chicken hot.** Remove chicken from the refrigerator **20 to 30 minutes** before cooking. Cook in a hot skillet, griddle, broiler, grill pan, or electric skillet until browned and cooked through, about **4 to 6 minutes per side** depending on thickness. |
| 10 | **Rest and slice the chicken.** Rest cooked chicken **5 to 10 minutes**. Slice very thin across the grain into shawarma-style strips. Save the juices. |
| 11 | **Re-crisp the sliced chicken.** Return sliced chicken and saved juices to a hot skillet for **1 to 2 minutes**, just until edges pick up color. |
| 12 | **Warm the naan for assembly.** Warm naan until soft and flexible. Do not fill cold naan. |
| 13 | **Build each wrap with measured components.** For each warm naan, spread about **1 1/2 Tbsp garlic sauce**, add **1/4 of the hot sliced chicken**, about **1/4 cup crispy potatoes**, **1 to 2 Tbsp drained pickles or pickled onions**, tomato, cucumber, onion, flat-leaf parsley, optional mint, another **1 to 2 tsp garlic sauce**, and a thin **1 to 2 tsp stripe lemon tahini**. |
| 14 | **Set the wrap and serve.** Fold or roll the naan. Optional: griddle seam-side down for **30 to 60 seconds**. Serve immediately with extra garlic sauce, lemon tahini, pickles, and lemon wedges. |

## Building the Final Wrap

| Layer | Component | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Warm Naan | Use 1 warm naan per wrap, preferably Fluffy Skillet Naan. |
| 2 | Garlic Sauce Foundation | Spread about 1 1/2 Tbsp garlic sauce over each naan. |
| 3 | Hot Shawarma Chicken | Add 1/4 of the thin-sliced re-crisped chicken. |
| 4 | Crispy Potato Tuck-In | Add about 1/4 cup shawarma-dusted crispy potatoes per wrap. |
| 5 | Pickled Acid | Add 1 to 2 Tbsp drained pickles or pickled onions. |
| 6 | Fresh Crunch | Add tomato, cucumber, onion, flat-leaf parsley, and optional mint in controlled amounts. |
| 7 | Sauce Finish | Add 1 to 2 tsp garlic sauce and a thin 1 to 2 tsp lemon tahini stripe. |
| 8 | Wrap and Griddle | Fold tightly and griddle seam-side down if desired. Serve immediately. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished wrap should taste juicy, deeply spiced, garlicky, lemon-bright, lightly smoky, tangy from pickles, creamy from garlic sauce, nutty from a thin tahini stripe, and fresh from vegetables, flat-leaf parsley, and optional mint. The chicken should have browned edges and a sliced shawarma texture; the naan should stay warm and flexible.

## Chef's Notes

Plain paprika works well here. If using liquid smoke for a smoky note, add it to the chicken marinade in a tiny amount only. Do not add liquid smoke to the potatoes or sauces.

Garlic sauce is the main chicken-shawarma sauce. Lemon tahini adds nutty depth as a thin finishing stripe or side dip.

This wrap is built from required layers: hot sliced shawarma chicken, warm naan, garlic sauce, pickled acid, crisp potato texture, fresh vegetables, flat-leaf parsley, and controlled tahini. Fresh mint may be added lightly for cool brightness. Each layer should be present, but none should make the wrap wet or loose.
