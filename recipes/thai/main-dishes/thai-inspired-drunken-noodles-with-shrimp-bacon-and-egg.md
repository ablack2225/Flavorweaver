# Thai-Inspired Drunken Noodles with Shrimp, Bacon & Egg

> Wide rice noodles tossed hot and fast with shrimp, bacon, soft egg curds, chunky garlic, Thai chiles, shallot, red bell pepper, vine tomato, Thai basil, and a glossy oyster-fish-soy sauce.

## Proper Defining Cuts

- **Garlic + Thai chiles:** smash the garlic, rough chop into visible pieces, slice or rough chop the Thai chiles, then chop them together a few passes. Keep chunky, not pasted.
- **Shallot:** thin 1/8-inch half-moons.
- **Onion:** 1/4-inch strips.
- **Red bell pepper:** 1/4-inch wide by 2-inch long strips.
- **Vine tomato:** thin wedges or chunky bite-size pieces; remove loose seed gel if very juicy.
- **Bacon:** 1/4-inch pieces.
- **Shrimp:** pat dry; leave whole unless jumbo.
- **Thai basil:** leaves picked from stems; whole unless very large.

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

| Bowl | Contents | Amounts |
|---|---|---|
| Bowl 1 - Noodles | Soaked, drained wide rice noodles + tiny drizzle neutral oil if needed | 8 oz dry noodles, soaked and drained |
| Bowl 2 - Sauce | Oyster sauce, fish sauce, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, honey/brown sugar, water, optional pepper | 2 Tbsp oyster; 1 Tbsp fish; 1 Tbsp soy; 1 tsp dark soy; 1 1/2 Tbsp honey/brown sugar; 1 Tbsp water |
| Bowl 3 - Shrimp Marinade | Shrimp, fish sauce, garlic, tiny pinch honey/sugar, neutral oil | 8 oz-1 lb shrimp; 1 tsp fish sauce; 1 small clove garlic; tiny pinch sweetener; 1 tsp oil |
| Bowl 4 - Bacon | Bacon cut into 1/4-inch pieces | 2 strips |
| Bowl 5 - Eggs | Lightly beaten eggs, optional 1 tsp water | 2 large eggs |
| Bowl 6 - Chunky Aromatics | Smashed rough-chopped garlic, sliced/chopped Thai chiles, thin-sliced shallot | 8 garlic cloves; 2-5 Thai chiles; 1 large shallot |
| Bowl 7 - Stir-Fry Vegetables | Sliced onion and red bell pepper | 1/2 onion; 1 red bell pepper |
| Bowl 8 - Finish | Vine tomato, Thai basil, reserved cooked bacon | 1 medium tomato; 1 1/2-2 packed cups basil; cooked bacon |

## Noodle Prep

1. Place dry wide rice noodles in a large bowl.
2. Cover with hot tap water or very warm water.
3. Start checking at 20 minutes.
4. Stop soaking when noodles are flexible and bendy but still slightly firm in the center.
5. Drain very well and rest 10-15 minutes before cooking.
6. Toss with a tiny drizzle of neutral oil only if they are sticking badly.

Do not boil the noodles fully soft. They need room to finish in the sauce.

## Cooking Timeline

1. **Render bacon.** Add bacon pieces to a wok or large skillet over medium to medium-high heat. Cook until fat renders and edges are lightly crisp. Remove bacon pieces to Bowl 8. Leave about 1 Tbsp bacon fat in the pan; spoon off extra if needed.
2. **Heat the pan.** Raise heat to high. Add 1 Tbsp neutral oil if the pan looks dry.
3. **Bloom chunky aromatics.** Add Bowl 6: garlic, Thai chiles, and shallot. Stir-fry 30-60 seconds until fragrant. Do not burn the garlic.
4. **Add eggs.** Push aromatics slightly aside. Add beaten eggs and let them set for a few seconds, then fold into large soft curds.
5. **Add shrimp.** Add marinated shrimp. Toss with eggs and aromatics until shrimp just starts turning pink.
6. **Add onion and red bell pepper.** Add Bowl 7 and stir-fry 45-75 seconds.
7. **Add noodles and sauce.** Add Bowl 1 noodles and Bowl 2 sauce. Fold and toss gently for 2-4 minutes until noodles are glossy, darker, and tender.
8. **Adjust dry noodles only if needed.** If noodles are still too firm or tight, add water 1 Tbsp at a time and keep tossing.
9. **Finish off heat.** Turn heat low or off. Add Bowl 8: cooked bacon, vine tomato, and Thai basil. Fold gently for 30-60 seconds, just until basil wilts and tomato releases a little juice into the sauce.
10. **Serve immediately.**

## Do Not Forget

- Use the full 8 cloves garlic.
- Keep garlic and Thai chiles chunky, not pasted.
- Do not boil dry noodles fully soft.
- Drain noodles very well before cooking.
- Keep egg curds large and soft.
- Add water only 1 Tbsp at a time if dry noodles need help finishing.
- Tomato and Thai basil go in at the end.
