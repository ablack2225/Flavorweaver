# Hushpuppies

> A preserved family hushpuppy recipe made with cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, buttermilk, onion, and mayonnaise, fried until golden brown.

## Measured Ingredients

### Hushpuppies

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 cup | Cornmeal |  |
| 1 1/2 cups | Flour |  |
| 1/2 cup | Sugar |  |
| 3 tsp | Baking powder |  |
| 1/2 tsp | Salt |  |
| 2 Tbsp | Mayonnaise | Add when mixture is moist |
| 1 cup | Buttermilk | Mix with water; may not need all |
| 1 cup | Water | Mix with buttermilk; add gradually |
| 1 | Onion | Chopped very fine |
| As needed | Cooking oil | For deep frying |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Mix the dry ingredients together. |
| 2 | Add the finely chopped onion. |
| 3 | Mix the water and buttermilk together. |
| 4 | Gradually add the water-buttermilk mixture to the dry ingredients. |
| 5 | When the mixture is moist, add the mayonnaise. |
| 6 | Continue adding the milk mixture a little at a time until you have a stiff dough that will hold together to form hushpuppies. You may not need all of the liquid. |
| 7 | Heat a deep fryer about half full of cooking oil over medium-high heat. Do not let the oil get too hot. |
| 8 | Drop spoonfuls of dough into the hot oil. |
| 9 | Fry until golden brown, flipping when necessary, and cook through the middle. |
| 10 | Drain on paper towels and serve warm. |

## Notes

Original family note: this recipe came many years ago from a restaurant in Longview called The Trotline. The family thought they were the best hushpuppies they had ever eaten. A sweet waitress shared the recipe and said it was her recipe.

Original note also says the recipe can easily be halved for a smaller amount.
