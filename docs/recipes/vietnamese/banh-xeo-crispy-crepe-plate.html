Banh Xeo Crispy Crepe Plate

Crispy Vietnamese-inspired rice crepes filled with shrimp, thin pork, scallion, and bean sprouts, served with lettuce, herbs, cucumber, pickled vegetables, and bright nước chấm.

Meal TypeFeatured Meal

Kitchen Execution Summary

Crispy Batter

Rice flour, cornstarch, turmeric, coconut milk, cold bubbly water, and scallion.

Shrimp + Pork

Quick-seared shrimp and thin pork for the classic savory filling.

Bean Sprouts

Added late so they stay crisp and fresh.

Lettuce + Herb Plate

Fresh wrap plate that keeps the crepes bright instead of heavy.

Nước Chấm

Sweet-sour-salty fish sauce dip; required.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Crispy Banh Xeo Batter

1 cup rice flour, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp sugar, 3/4 cup coconut milk, 3/4 cup cold sparkling water or cold water, 2 sliced scallions, and 1–2 Tbsp neutral oil per crepe as needed.

Shrimp + Pork Filling

8 oz shrimp, 8 oz thin-sliced pork loin or pork butt, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp fish sauce, optional 1 tsp neutral oil, 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts, and optional 1/2 small thin-sliced onion.

Lettuce Plate + Nước Chấm

Lettuce plate: 8–10 lettuce leaves, 1 cup herbs, 1/2 cucumber, optional 1/2 cup pickled veg, lime wedges. Nước chấm: 3 Tbsp warm water, 2 Tbsp sugar, 2 Tbsp fish sauce, 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 garlic clove, chile to taste, optional 1 Tbsp carrot.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Batter Dry

1 cup rice flour, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp sugar.

Bowl 2 — Batter Liquids

3/4 cup coconut milk, 3/4 cup cold sparkling water or cold water.

Bowl 3 — Scallions

2 thin-sliced scallions.

Bowl 4 — Pork + Shrimp

8 oz shrimp, 8 oz thin pork, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp fish sauce, optional 1 tsp oil.

Bowl 5 — Crepe Fill

1 1/2 cups bean sprouts, optional 1/2 small thin-sliced onion.

Bowl 6 — Lettuce + Herb Plate

8–10 lettuce leaves, 1 cup herbs, 1/2 cucumber, optional 1/2 cup pickled veg, lime wedges.

Bowl 7 — Nước Chấm

3 Tbsp warm water, 2 Tbsp sugar, 2 Tbsp fish sauce, 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 garlic clove, chile to taste, optional 1 Tbsp carrot.

Bowl 8 — Optional Peanut-Chile Drizzle

2 Tbsp peanut butter, 1 Tbsp nước chấm, 1–2 tsp warm water, 1 tsp chili-garlic paste, 1/2 tsp lime juice.

Do Not Forget

Batter should be thin like heavy cream, not pancake batter.

Rest batter at least 30 minutes; longer is better.

Stir batter before every crepe because rice flour settles.

Use enough oil and a hot pan for crisp edges.

Do not overload the crepe; too much filling makes it steam instead of crisp.

Serve immediately with lettuce, herbs, and nước chấm.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Make batter. Whisk 1 cup rice flour, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp sugar. Whisk in 3/4 cup coconut milk and 3/4 cup cold sparkling water or cold water until smooth.
  2. Rest batter. Cover and rest at least 30 minutes, or refrigerate up to 24 hours. After resting, stir in 2 thin-sliced scallions. Stir well before each crepe.
  3. Make nước chấm. Stir 3 Tbsp warm water with 2 Tbsp sugar until dissolved. Add 2 Tbsp fish sauce, 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 minced garlic clove, chile to taste, and optional 1 Tbsp shredded carrot.
  4. Make optional drizzle. Stir 2 Tbsp peanut butter, 1 Tbsp nước chấm, 1–2 tsp warm water, 1 tsp chili-garlic paste, and 1/2 tsp lime juice until spoonable.
  5. Prep fresh plate. Arrange 8–10 lettuce leaves, 1 cup herbs, 1/2 cucumber, optional 1/2 cup pickled veg, and lime wedges. Keep cold.
  6. Season filling. Toss 8 oz shrimp, 8 oz thin pork, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp fish sauce. Rest 10–15 minutes.
  7. Pre-cook filling lightly. Cook pork and shrimp over medium-high heat with optional 1 tsp oil just until pork loses raw color and shrimp is barely opaque, about 2–3 minutes. Remove to a plate.
  8. Heat crepe pan. Wipe skillet clean. Heat over medium-high until hot. Add 1–2 Tbsp neutral oil and swirl. Batter should sizzle immediately.
  9. Pour one crepe. Stir batter well. Add about 1/2 cup batter and immediately swirl thin, about 9–10 inches wide.
  10. Fill one side. Scatter one-quarter of pork/shrimp filling over one half. Add one-quarter of 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts and optional onion over that same half.
  11. Crisp. Cook uncovered 4–6 minutes, adjusting heat as needed, until edges are crisp, bottom is golden, and center is set. Add a small drizzle of oil around the edge if needed.
  12. Fold. Fold empty half over filling. Slide onto a rack or plate. Do not stack directly.
  13. Repeat. Repeat with remaining batter, filling, and sprouts for 4 crepes total, stirring batter before each one and adding fresh oil as needed.
  14. Serve immediately. Tear hot crepe into lettuce with herbs, cucumber, pickled veg, and nước chấm. Use peanut-chile drizzle lightly if desired.

Electric Skillet Notes

Preheat around 325°F, then adjust between 300–325°F based on browning. If the batter browns before crisping, lower slightly. If it steams and stays soft, raise slightly and use a little more oil.

Final Taste Target

The plate should be hot-cold, crisp-fresh, and sauce-bright: brittle golden crepe edges, tender shrimp and pork, crunchy bean sprouts, cold lettuce, herbs, cucumber, and punchy nước chấm.
