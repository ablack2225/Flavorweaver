## Cross-Component Prep Map

- **Garlic total:** prep **4 cloves** for Doro Wat, plus what is needed for [Misir Wat](../sides/misir-wat.md).
- **Ginger total:** prep **1 tsp** for Doro Wat, plus what is needed for Misir Wat.
- **Onion:** finely dice **1 large onion** for Doro Wat, plus the onion needed for Misir Wat.
- **Eggs:** cook, chill, peel, and lightly score or prick **4 large eggs** so they are ready to stain in the Doro sauce.
- **Naan:** prepare or rewarm naan so it is ready for final plating.

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

### Doro Wat-Style Chicken & Eggs

| Bowl | Contents | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bowl 1 — Berbere Blend | Measured House Berbere-Style Spice Blend | Reserve 1 Tbsp for marinade and 1 Tbsp for sauce. |
| Bowl 2 — Chicken Marinade | 2 whole chicken thighs; 1 Tbsp berbere blend; 1 Tbsp oil; 1 Tbsp lemon juice; 2 minced garlic cloves; 1/2 tsp salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper | Marinate overnight. |
| Bowl 3 — Doro Sauce Base | 1 large finely diced onion; 1 to 2 Tbsp unsalted butter or oil; 1 Tbsp reserved berbere blend; 2 minced garlic cloves; 1 tsp ginger; 1 tsp tomato paste | Onion cooks first; spices bloom after onion softens. |
| Bowl 4 — Doro Simmer | 1 to 1 1/2 cups chicken broth; optional 1 bay leaf; 4 peeled hard-boiled eggs, lightly scored or pricked | Eggs go into sauce near the end. |

## Do Not Forget

- This Hall of Fame version uses **Misir Wat / red lentils moving forward**.
- Do **not** default this platter back to Split Pea Alicha.
- Do **not** include Timatim Salad in the platter; Amy and Don did not like it.
- Do **not** include Atakilt Wat as part of the default Hall of Fame platter.
- Keep [Atakilt Wat](../sides/atakilt-wat.md) linked as an optional standalone side only.
- Prepare naan separately and have it ready before final plating.
- Keep the chicken thighs whole through marinating, searing, simmering, and plating.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Day before — make the spice blend.** Measure the House Berbere-Style Spice Blend. Reserve **1 Tbsp** for the chicken marinade and **1 Tbsp** for the Doro sauce base. |
| 2 | **Day before — marinate the chicken.** Coat **2 whole chicken thighs** with **1 Tbsp berbere blend**, **1 Tbsp oil**, **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, **2 minced garlic cloves**, **1/2 tsp salt**, and **1/4 tsp black pepper**. Cover and refrigerate overnight. |
| 3 | **Bread planning.** Prepare [Fluffy Skillet Naan](../../indian/breads/fluffy-skillet-naan.md) from its own recipe so it is ready before final plating. |
| 4 | **Start Misir Wat.** Prepare [Misir Wat](../sides/misir-wat.md) from its standalone recipe. Hold warm. |
| 5 | **Hard-boil eggs.** Cook **4 large eggs**, chill in an ice bath, peel, and lightly score or prick so sauce can cling. |
| 6 | **Start Doro sauce.** Cook **1 large finely diced onion** until softened, reduced, and beginning to turn golden, about **10 to 15 minutes**. Add tiny splashes of water if needed. |
| 7 | **Bloom Doro sauce spices.** Add **1 to 2 Tbsp unsalted butter or oil** and **1 Tbsp reserved berbere blend**. Bloom **30 to 60 seconds**, stirring constantly. Add **2 minced garlic cloves**, **1 tsp ginger**, and **1 tsp tomato paste**; cook **1 to 2 minutes**. |
| 8 | **Sear and simmer chicken.** Add the **2 whole marinated chicken thighs** and sear lightly **2 to 3 minutes per side**. Add **1 cup chicken broth** and optional **1 bay leaf**. Simmer covered on low **25 to 35 minutes**, adding up to **1/2 cup more broth** as needed. |
| 9 | **Sauce the eggs.** During the last **10 to 15 minutes** of the Doro sauce, nestle the **4 peeled eggs** into the sauce. Spoon sauce over them and turn gently so they stain evenly. |
| 10 | **Thicken Doro sauce.** Uncover and simmer **5 to 10 minutes** until the sauce clings to the whole thighs and eggs. Adjust with salt, black pepper, lemon, or tiny sugar only if needed. |
| 11 | **Rewarm prepared naan.** Warm naan for the warm sauced components. |

## Building the Final Plate

| Layer | Build Detail |
|---|---|
| 1. Naan | Place prepared naan beside the warm sauced components. Use it to scoop the chicken, eggs, Doro sauce, and Misir Wat. |
| 2. Chicken Anchor | Place **1 whole Doro Wat-style chicken thigh** on each plate. Spoon sauce over the top so the whole thigh stays glossy. |
| 3. Eggs | Add **2 sauced eggs per plate**, nestled near the chicken with extra sauce spooned over them. |
| 4. Misir Wat | Add a generous spoon of warm red-lentil Misir Wat beside the chicken and eggs. |
| 5. Final Sauce Check | Spoon extra Doro sauce over the chicken and eggs. Keep the plate rich, saucy, and scoopable. |

## Final Taste Target

The plate should move between spicy rich Doro sauce, tender whole-thigh chicken, sauced eggs, earthy red-lentil Misir Wat, and warm naan.

The winning version is the **Doro Wat + Misir Wat pairing**. Misir Wat brings enough depth and earthiness to stand beside the Doro sauce without muting it.
