# Applesauce Fruitcake

> A preserved fruitcake recipe from Sue Wallesch, made with applesauce, dates, raisins, walnuts, maraschino cherries, warm spices, and a pecan-and-cherry topping.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Preheat oven to 325°F. |
| 2 | Grease a 10-inch tube pan. Line the bottom with parchment paper and grease again. |
| 3 | In a large bowl, cream sugar and shortening until light and fluffy. |
| 4 | Add eggs and blend well. |
| 5 | Combine 1/2 cup flour with walnuts, raisins, dates, and 1/2 cup chopped maraschino cherries. Stir until fruit and nuts are coated; set aside. |
| 6 | Add remaining 2 3/4 cups flour, baking soda, cinnamon, allspice, cloves, and salt to the egg mixture. Blend on low speed until moistened, then beat 2 minutes on medium speed. |
| 7 | Fold fruit and nut mixture and applesauce into batter. |
| 8 | Pour batter into prepared 10-inch tube pan. |
| 9 | Arrange cherry halves and pecan halves on top. |
| 10 | Bake at 325°F for 1 hour 15 minutes to 1 hour 45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in center comes out clean. |
| 11 | Cool upright in pan for 5 minutes. |
| 12 | Turn upright onto a wire rack. Remove pan and parchment paper. |
| 13 | Cool completely before serving. |
