# Thai-Inspired Drunken Noodles with Shrimp, Bacon & Egg

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

- **Confirm noodles** - Use dry wide rice noodles for this test. Fresh wide rice noodles are the future upgrade path.
- **Confirm basil** - Thai basil is the available house version. Holy basil is the future ideal.
- **Sauce can be mixed ahead** - Mix up to 1 day ahead and refrigerate. Stir before cooking.
- **Do not marinate shrimp early** - Toss shrimp with marinade only about 10 minutes before cooking.
- **No zucchini in this test** - It was intentionally removed to protect noodle texture.

## Service Countdown

- **45-50 minutes before serving** - Start soaking the dry wide rice noodles.
- **35 minutes before serving** - Cut garlic, Thai chiles, shallot, onion, red bell pepper, tomato, bacon, and basil.
- **25 minutes before serving** - Mix sauce and beat eggs.
- **15 minutes before serving** - Toss shrimp with marinade.
- **At service** - Render bacon, stir-fry aromatics, eggs, shrimp, vegetables, noodles, and sauce. Finish with tomato, Thai basil, and bacon.

## Proper Defining Cuts

| Ingredient | Cut | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Garlic | Smash, then rough chop into visible pieces | Keeps garlic bold and present. |
| Thai chiles | Slice or rough chop with garlic | Distributes heat while staying chunky. |
| Shallot | 1/8-inch half-moons | Softens quickly. |
| Onion | 1/4-inch strips | Holds shape while softening. |
| Red bell pepper | 1/4-inch wide by 2-inch strips | Folds into noodles better than dice. |
| Vine tomato | Thin wedges or chunky pieces | Releases enough juice to carry basil aroma. |
| Bacon | 1/4-inch pieces | Distributes as a savory accent. |
| Thai basil | Whole leaves unless very large | Keeps the finish fragrant and visible. |

## Do Not Forget

- Use the full **8 large cloves garlic** in the aromatic bowl.
- Keep garlic and Thai chiles **chunky, not pasted**.
- Do not boil the dry noodles fully soft.
- Drain noodles very well and rest them before stir-frying.
- Render bacon first, but leave only about **1 Tbsp bacon fat** in the pan.
- Keep egg curds large and soft.
- Add water only **1 Tbsp at a time** if dry noodles need help finishing.
- Tomato and Thai basil go in at the end.
- Serve immediately.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Soak the noodles.** Place **8 oz dry wide rice noodles** in a large bowl and cover with hot tap water or very warm water. Start checking at **20 minutes**. Stop when noodles are flexible and bendy but still slightly firm in the center. Drain very well and rest **10-15 minutes**. |
| 2 | **Mix the sauce.** Stir together **2 Tbsp oyster sauce**, **1 Tbsp fish sauce**, **1 Tbsp soy sauce**, **1 tsp dark soy sauce**, **1 1/2 Tbsp honey or brown sugar**, and **1 Tbsp water**. |
| 3 | **Marinate the shrimp briefly.** Toss **8 oz to 1 lb shrimp** with **1 tsp fish sauce**, **1 small finely chopped or grated garlic clove**, a **tiny pinch honey or sugar**, and **1 tsp neutral oil**. Rest **10 minutes only**. |
| 4 | **Render the bacon.** Cook **2 strips bacon, cut into 1/4-inch pieces**, over medium to medium-high heat until fat renders and edges are lightly crisp. Remove bacon pieces to Bowl 8. Leave about **1 Tbsp bacon fat** in the pan. |
| 5 | **Heat the pan.** Raise heat to high. Add **1 Tbsp neutral oil** if the pan looks dry. |
| 6 | **Bloom the chunky aromatics.** Add **8 rough-chopped garlic cloves**, **2-5 Thai chiles**, and **1 thin-sliced shallot**. Stir-fry **30-60 seconds**, just until fragrant. Do not burn the garlic. |
| 7 | **Add the eggs.** Push aromatics aside. Add **2 lightly beaten eggs**, let set briefly, then fold into large soft curds. |
| 8 | **Add the shrimp.** Add marinated shrimp and toss until just starting to turn pink. |
| 9 | **Add onion and red bell pepper.** Add **1/2 sliced onion** and **1 sliced red bell pepper**. Stir-fry **45-75 seconds**. |
| 10 | **Add noodles and sauce.** Add soaked noodles and sauce. Fold and toss gently for **2-4 minutes**, until noodles are glossy, darker, and tender. |
| 11 | **Adjust only if needed.** If noodles are still too firm, add water **1 Tbsp at a time** and keep folding. |
| 12 | **Finish off heat.** Turn heat low or off. Add reserved bacon, vine tomato, and **1 1/2 to 2 packed cups Thai basil**. Fold gently for **30-60 seconds**. |
| 13 | **Serve immediately.** Transfer to bowls or a wide platter while hot and glossy. |

## Building the Final Noodle Bowl

| Layer | Build Detail |
|---|---|
| 1. Glossy Noodle Base | Divide hot noodles between bowls while loose and glossy. |
| 2. Protein Visibility | Keep shrimp, bacon pieces, and egg curds visible. |
| 3. Pepper and Tomato Color | Let red bell pepper and tomato show for color and freshness. |
| 4. Basil Finish | Keep wilted Thai basil visible as part of the identity. |
| 5. Immediate Service | Serve right away so the noodles stay tender-chewy and basil-fragrant. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished noodles should be glossy, spicy, garlicky, savory-sweet, and basil-heavy. The shrimp should be tender, the bacon should support without taking over, the egg should stay in soft curds, and the red bell pepper should keep a little crispness.

## Chef's Notes

This version intentionally keeps the garlic and Thai chiles chunky instead of turning them into a smooth paste. The garlic should remain visible and bold.

Red bell pepper is the preferred pantry vegetable for this test because it brings color and sweetness without the water risk of zucchini.

The tomato belongs at the end with Thai basil. It should release a little juice into the hot noodles, not cook down into a sauce.
