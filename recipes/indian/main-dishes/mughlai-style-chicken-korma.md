# Mughlai-Style Chicken Korma

> Tender chicken thighs in a generous browned onion-cashew gravy with yogurt marinade, whole warm spices, chicken broth, and a small cream finish. This version leans Mughlai-style in technique and flavor architecture while staying practical for the Amy & Don Kitchen.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Yogurt-Marinated Chicken | Boneless thighs are marinated with yogurt, ginger-garlic, turmeric, coriander, mild chile, garam masala, salt, and oil for tenderness and flavor penetration. |
| Browned Onion-Cashew Gravy | Slow-browned onions and toasted cashews are blended smooth to create the silky body of the korma. |
| Whole Spice Bloom | Cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and bay leaf are bloomed in fat for fragrant Mughlai-style depth. |
| Extra Sauce Build | Chicken broth is used gradually so the final gravy is generous enough for rice, naan, and sides without becoming watery. |
| Cream Finish | A small amount of heavy cream rounds the sauce at the end without turning the dish into a cream curry. |

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

- Marinate the chicken at least **4 hours**, preferably overnight.
- Slice the onions before cooking day if desired.
- Cashews can be measured ahead and kept with the sauce prep.
- This recipe is written for an **electric skillet** because the controlled heat helps protect the yogurt and cream from splitting.
- If making this as part of a larger Indian-inspired plate, start rice and any sides early so the korma can be served immediately after its final rest.

## Cross-Component Prep Map

- **Onions:** the onion is used only for the browned onion-cashew paste. Cook it deeply enough to give sweetness and color to the gravy.
- **Cashews:** toast briefly with the browned onions, then blend smooth. Do not let cashews burn.
- **Chicken broth:** appears in the paste and the final gravy. Keep at least **1 cup** nearby, divided as needed.
- **Garam masala:** appears lightly in the marinade and again as a finish. Do not overload it early or the sauce can taste muddy.
- **Cream:** only added at the end on low heat or off heat.
- **Whole spices:** bloom them for aroma, then remove before serving if you can find them.

## Electric Skillet Temperature Map

| Stage | Temperature | Target |
|---|---:|---|
| Brown onions | 300 to 325°F | Deep golden sweetness without scorching. |
| Toast cashews | 300°F | Light nutty aroma. |
| Sear chicken | 325 to 350°F | Light browning without burning marinade. |
| Bloom spices | 275 to 300°F | Fragrant, not bitter. |
| Simmer sauce | 250 to 275°F | Gentle bubbling only. |
| Add cream | Warm / 200 to 225°F or off heat | Silky finish without splitting. |
| Rest | Off heat, covered | Sauce settles and thickens slightly. |

## Do Not Forget

- This recipe is written with **yogurt** as the proper korma marinade base.
- The sauce is intentionally generous because Amy and Don usually serve saucy dishes with rice, naan, or sides.
- Brown the onions slowly. Pale onions make a flat korma; burnt onions make a bitter korma.
- Blend the onion-cashew paste very smooth. Texture is part of the restaurant-level finish.
- Do not hard-boil the sauce after the marinated chicken goes in.
- Add heavy cream at the end on low heat or off heat.
- Add sugar or honey only if the sauce needs rounding; do not make the korma sweet.
- Rest the finished korma 5 to 10 minutes before serving.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Marinate the chicken.** In a bowl, combine **1/3 cup plain full-fat yogurt**, **1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste**, **3/4 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/2 tsp ground coriander**, **1/2 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile powder**, **1/4 tsp garam masala**, and **1 tsp neutral oil**. Coat **4 boneless chicken thighs** completely. Cover and refrigerate at least **4 hours**, preferably overnight. |
| 2 | **Start the browned onion-cashew paste.** Set the electric skillet to **300 to 325°F**. Add **1 1/2 Tbsp neutral oil or butter/oil mix** and **1 large or 1 1/2 medium thin-sliced onions** with a small pinch of salt. Cook, stirring often, until the onions are deeply golden, about **15 to 25 minutes**. |
| 3 | **Toast the cashews.** Lower heat around **300°F**. Add **1/3 cup cashews** to the browned onions and cook **1 to 2 minutes**, just until lightly toasted and fragrant. Do not burn. |
| 4 | **Blend the paste.** Transfer the onion-cashew mixture to a blender cup. Add **3 Tbsp chicken broth or hot water** and blend smooth, adding up to **2 Tbsp more liquid** as needed. The paste should be thick, silky, and pourable enough to scrape back into the skillet. |
| 5 | **Sear the marinated chicken.** Set the electric skillet to **325 to 350°F**. Add a little oil if the skillet is dry. Add the marinated chicken and sear **2 to 3 minutes per side**, just until lightly browned. The chicken does not need to cook through yet. Remove to a plate. |
| 6 | **Clean only if needed.** If any marinade scorched black, wipe that spot out. Keep golden fond if it smells savory. |
| 7 | **Bloom the whole spices.** Lower skillet to **275 to 300°F**. Add **1 Tbsp neutral oil or butter/oil mix**. Add **2 cracked green cardamom pods**, **1 small cinnamon stick**, **2 cloves**, and **1 bay leaf**. Bloom **30 to 60 seconds**, until fragrant. |
| 8 | **Bloom the powdered spices.** Add **1 tsp ground coriander**, **1/2 tsp ground cumin**, **1/4 tsp turmeric**, **1/4 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile powder**, and optional **pinch cayenne**. Stir constantly **20 to 30 seconds**. If the pan looks dry, splash in **1 Tbsp broth** so the spices do not scorch. |
| 9 | **Fry the onion-cashew paste.** Add the blended paste and cook **2 to 4 minutes**, stirring often, until it smells nutty and rounded. |
| 10 | **Build the gravy.** Add **3/4 cup chicken broth** slowly, stirring until smooth. For extra sauce, add more broth gradually, up to **1 cup total**, keeping the gravy thick and spoonable rather than watery. |
| 11 | **Simmer the chicken.** Return the seared chicken and any juices to the skillet. Lower to **250 to 275°F**. Cover and simmer gently **12 to 18 minutes**, turning once, until the chicken is tender and cooked through. Gentle bubbles only. |
| 12 | **Reduce if needed.** If the sauce is thinner than desired, uncover and simmer gently **3 to 5 minutes** before adding cream. |
| 13 | **Finish the korma.** Turn skillet to warm, **200 to 225°F**, or turn heat off. Stir in **2 Tbsp heavy cream** and **1/4 tsp garam masala**. Taste and adjust with salt. Add **1/4 tsp sugar or honey** only if the sauce tastes sharp or too tangy. Add a tiny squeeze of lemon or lime only if the sauce tastes flat. |
| 14 | **Rest.** Turn off heat, cover, and rest **5 to 10 minutes** before serving. Remove the cinnamon stick, bay leaf, cloves, and cardamom pods if you can find them. |
| 15 | **Serve.** Spoon generously over basmati rice or serve with naan and sides. The sauce should be generous enough to carry rice or bread without feeling thin. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished korma should be silky, warmly spiced, savory-sweet from browned onion, rounded with cashew, gently tangy from the yogurt marinade, and lightly enriched with cream.

The chicken should be tender and juicy. The sauce should be generous, smooth, and spoonable, with enough body to cling to rice or naan without becoming heavy.

## Chef's Notes

This korma gets its restaurant-level feel from technique, not expensive finishing ingredients. The important moves are slow-browning the onions, lightly toasting the cashews, blending the paste very smooth, blooming whole spices in fat, controlling heat in the electric skillet, and finishing with a small amount of cream.

The sauce is intentionally more generous than many standard korma recipes because Amy and Don usually serve saucy dishes with rice, naan, or sides.

This recipe does not use saffron, rose water, kewra, silver leaf, or other expensive luxury finishes. It leans Mughlai-style through its browned onion-cashew gravy, yogurt marinade, warm whole spices, gentle richness, and restrained heat.

## Sour Cream Substitution Note

For the Amy and Don live-cook version, replace the **1/3 cup plain yogurt** in the marinade with **1/3 cup sour cream**.

Because sour cream is richer and can taste slightly sharper in this context:

- Sear the chicken gently and avoid scorching the marinade.
- Do not hard-boil the sauce after the chicken is added.
- Add **1/4 tsp sugar or honey** near the end only if the sauce tastes too sharp or tangy.
- Keep the **2 Tbsp heavy cream finish** for silkiness unless the sauce already tastes too rich.
