# Thai-Inspired Bang Bang Shrimp Bowls

> Creamy-sweet, spicy, crispy shrimp bowls with coconut-lime basmati rice, garlic-oyster broccoli, toasted coconut, crispy fried onions, green onion, mint, and lime.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Element | Notes |
|---|---|
| Sauce | The Bang Bang sauce is creamy-sweet first, spicy second, with garlic underneath. A full 1/4 cup sweet chili sauce keeps it from tasting like plain spicy mayonnaise. |
| Shrimp | Shrimp soak for 10 minutes, drain well, coat in seasoned cornstarch, fry in small batches, and get sauced immediately before serving. |
| Rice | Basmati rice cooks with coconut milk, water, salt, and sugar, then gets lime zest, lime juice, toasted coconut, and green onion after cooking. |
| Broccoli | Prepare one batch of linked Garlic-Oyster Broccoli. It may be dried ahead and finished close to service. |
| Crunch | Packaged crispy fried onions are added only at final assembly. |
| Fresh Finish | Green onion, a small amount of mint, and lime wedges brighten the bowl. |

## Make First / Can Hold / Make Last

| Timing Group | Components | Holding Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Make First | Chili-garlic paste, sweet chili sauce, toasted coconut | Cool sweet chili sauce completely. Hold toasted coconut on a plate at room temperature. |
| Can Hold | Coconut-lime rice, garlic-oyster broccoli after moisture is driven off, Bang Bang sauce | Hold rice warm. Hold dried broccoli uncovered, then finish with aromatics and sauce near service. Keep Bang Bang sauce chilled. |
| Make Last | Crispy shrimp, final broccoli finish, bowl assembly | Fry and sauce shrimp immediately before serving. Add crispy onions only after bowls are built. |

## Measured Ingredients by Component

### Quick Chili-Garlic Paste

- 2 to 4 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
- 4 cloves garlic
- 2 Tbsp rice vinegar or white vinegar
- 1 Tbsp water, plus more only if needed
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper or 1/4 tsp cayenne

### Homemade Sweet Chili Sauce

- 1/2 cup water
- 1/4 cup rice vinegar or white vinegar
- 1/3 cup sugar
- 1 to 2 Tbsp homemade chili-garlic paste
- 1 garlic clove, grated or very finely minced
- 1/2 tsp ginger paste
- 1/4 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp cornstarch
- 1 Tbsp cold water

### Quick Dairy Soak

#### Evaporated Milk Version

- 1/2 cup evaporated milk
- 1/2 cup water
- 1 Tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice

#### Heavy Cream Version

- 1/3 cup heavy cream
- 2/3 cup water
- 1 Tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice

### Bang Bang Sauce

- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 1/4 cup homemade sweet chili sauce
- 1 to 2 tsp homemade chili-garlic paste
- 1/2 tsp lime juice
- Tiny pinch kosher salt

### Crispy Shrimp

- 1 lb shrimp, peeled, deveined, thawed, and patted dry
- 1 cup quick dairy soak
- 1 cup cornstarch
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- Neutral oil, for frying

### Coconut-Lime Rice

- 1 cup basmati rice, rinsed until water runs mostly clear
- 3/4 cup coconut milk
- 3/4 cup water
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp sugar
- Zest of 1 lime
- 1 to 2 tsp lime juice
- 1/4 to 1/3 cup unsweetened coconut flakes, toasted
- 1 green onion, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp neutral oil, optional

#### Thick Coconut Milk Ratio

- 1 cup basmati rice
- 1/2 cup coconut milk
- 1 cup water
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp sugar

### Linked Vegetable Component

- 1 full batch [Garlic-Oyster Broccoli with Ginger, Lime & White Pepper](../../asian/sides/garlic-oyster-broccoli-with-ginger-lime-and-white-pepper.md)

### Final Bowl Finish

- 4 to 6 Tbsp crispy fried onions total, about 2 to 3 Tbsp per generous bowl
- 1 to 2 green onions, thinly sliced
- 1 to 2 Tbsp fresh mint leaves, lightly torn or thinly sliced
- 1 lime, cut into wedges
- Remaining toasted coconut
- Extra Bang Bang sauce, served on the side

## Do Not Forget

- Cool sweet chili sauce completely before mixing it with mayonnaise.
- Use 1/4 cup sweet chili sauce in the Bang Bang sauce.
- Soak shrimp exactly 10 minutes.
- Let excess dairy soak drip off before coating.
- Do not add water to the cornstarch coating.
- Fry shrimp in small batches at 350°F to 375°F.
- Drain shrimp on a rack.
- Sauce shrimp immediately before serving.
- Do not drown shrimp in sauce.
- Toast coconut separately and remove it from the hot skillet immediately.
- Add toasted coconut only after rice cooks.
- Add crispy fried onions only during final assembly.
- Use mint lightly.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Make the chili-garlic paste.** Soak **2 to 4 guajillo chiles** in very hot water for **10 to 15 minutes**. Drain and blend with **4 garlic cloves**, **2 Tbsp vinegar**, **1 Tbsp water**, **1 tsp sugar**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper or 1/4 tsp cayenne**. Add water **1 tsp at a time** only if needed.

2. **Make the sweet chili sauce.** Combine **1/2 cup water**, **1/4 cup vinegar**, **1/3 cup sugar**, **1 to 2 Tbsp chili-garlic paste**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp ginger**, and **1/4 tsp kosher salt** in a saucepan. Simmer **3 to 5 minutes**. Stir together **1 tsp cornstarch** and **1 Tbsp cold water**, add the slurry, and simmer **30 to 60 seconds**. Cool completely.

3. **Start the rice.** Add **1 cup rinsed basmati rice**, **3/4 cup coconut milk**, **3/4 cup water**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1 tsp sugar** to the rice cooker. When the coconut milk is very thick, use **1/2 cup coconut milk** and **1 cup water**. Cook according to the rice cooker.

4. **Toast the coconut.** Heat a dry electric skillet to about **300°F**. Add **1/4 to 1/3 cup unsweetened coconut flakes** in a thin layer. Stir constantly for **2 to 5 minutes**, until pale golden and fragrant. Transfer immediately to a plate.

5. **Finish the rice.** Fluff the cooked rice and fold in **zest of 1 lime**, **1 to 2 tsp lime juice**, most of the toasted coconut, **1 sliced green onion**, and optional **1 tsp neutral oil**. Reserve some coconut for the bowl tops. Hold warm.

6. **Make the Bang Bang sauce.** Whisk **1/2 cup mayonnaise**, **1/4 cup cooled sweet chili sauce**, **1 to 2 tsp chili-garlic paste**, **1/2 tsp lime juice**, and a tiny pinch of kosher salt. Chill until service.

7. **Prepare the broccoli.** Make **1 full batch Garlic-Oyster Broccoli** using the linked recipe. The broccoli may be cooked dry until its moisture is gone and held uncovered, then finished with aromatics and sauce near service.

8. **Make the quick dairy soak.** Mix **1/2 cup evaporated milk**, **1/2 cup water**, and **1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice**. Rest **5 to 10 minutes**. For the heavy cream version, mix **1/3 cup heavy cream**, **2/3 cup water**, and **1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice**.

9. **Soak the shrimp.** Place **1 lb patted-dry shrimp** in **1 cup quick dairy soak** for exactly **10 minutes**.

10. **Coat the shrimp.** Mix **1 cup cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/4 tsp black pepper**. Lift shrimp from the soak, let excess drip away, coat thoroughly, and shake off excess cornstarch.

11. **Fry the shrimp.** Heat neutral oil to **350°F to 375°F**. Fry shrimp in small batches for **1 to 2 minutes**, until crisp and cooked through. Drain on a rack.

12. **Sauce the shrimp.** Toss the hot shrimp with enough Bang Bang sauce to coat lightly. Keep extra sauce on the side.

13. **Build the bowls.** Divide coconut-lime rice among **2 generous or 3 lighter bowls**. Add Garlic-Oyster Broccoli to one side. Pile sauced shrimp as the main feature. Sprinkle with reserved toasted coconut and **2 to 3 Tbsp crispy fried onions per generous bowl**. Finish with sliced green onion, a small amount of mint, and lime wedges. Serve immediately.

## Final Taste Target

The bowl should be creamy, crispy, sweet-spicy, garlicky, coconut-fragrant, lime-bright, savory from the broccoli, crunchy from the fried onions, and freshened with green onion and mint.

The rice should remain soft and fragrant. The shrimp should retain crispness beneath a light coating of sauce. The broccoli should be glossy and savory rather than watery. The crispy onions should stay crisp until eating.
