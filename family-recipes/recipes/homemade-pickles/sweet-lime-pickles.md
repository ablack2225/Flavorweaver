# Sweet Lime Pickles

> A preserved family sweet lime pickle recipe made from sliced cucumbers soaked in lime water, then finished with sugar, white vinegar, celery seed, cloves, mixed pickling spice, and salt.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Wash cucumbers and slice into 1/4-inch slices. |
| 2 | Mix 2 gallons water with household lime in a large stock pot. |
| 3 | Soak cucumbers in the lime water for 24 hours. |
| 4 | Drain cucumbers and rinse thoroughly. |
| 5 | Rinse the stock pot. Return cucumbers to the pot and cover with cold water. |
| 6 | Soak in cold water for 3 hours. |
| 7 | Drain cucumbers and return them to the stock pot. |
| 8 | Mix sugar, white vinegar, 1 cup water, celery seeds, whole cloves, mixed pickling spice, and salt. |
| 9 | Pour syrup mixture over the cucumbers. |
| 10 | Let stand overnight. |
| 11 | Cook 45-60 minutes. |
| 12 | Pack into hot jars and seal. |

## Notes

Original family note: these pickles are very sweet and crunchy.

Original family note: Sue used these in potato salad and tuna fish salad. Both tasted much better with homemade pickles.

Important timing note from the original recipe: study the recipe before beginning so the steps are timed correctly. The cucumbers must be removed from the lime water after 24 hours or it will mess them up. The rest of the steps are not as critical as this one.

For modern shelf-stable canning, use current tested canning guidance. This file preserves the family recipe as written, including the original hot-jar and seal method.
