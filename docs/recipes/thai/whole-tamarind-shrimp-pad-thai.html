Whole-Tamarind Shrimp Pad Thai

Sauce-forward Thai-inspired shrimp Pad Thai with whole tamarind pulp, shrimp-shell concentrate, double shallot, egg ribbons, cooked-then-finished medium rice stick noodles, warmed unsalted peanuts, bean sprouts, lime, and herbs.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Whole-Tamarind Sauce

Fresh tamarind pulp, fish sauce, brown sugar, chile, water, and shrimp-shell concentrate form the sour-salty-sweet backbone.

Shrimp-Shell Concentrate

Shrimp peels and tails are roasted briefly with oil, simmered with water, pressed, and strained for clean seafood depth.

Cooked Noodle Method

Medium rice stick noodles are cooked per package directions, rinsed, lightly oiled, and finished briefly in sauce at the end.

Double Shallot

Thin-sliced golden shallot topping plus minced shallot-garlic aromatic base.

Finish

Egg ribbons, warmed crushed peanuts, fresh bean sprouts, green onion, lime wedges, and table chili flakes.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Whole Tamarind Pulp

2 oz whole tamarind plus 1/2 cup hot water. Soak, mash, strain, and keep thick enough to lightly coat a spoon.

Shrimp-Shell Concentrate

Shells and tails from 1 lb shrimp, 1 tsp neutral oil, and 3 Tbsp water.

Pad Thai Sauce

1/4 cup thick tamarind pulp, 3 Tbsp fish sauce, 3 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 Tbsp shrimp-shell concentrate, 1 Tbsp water, 1/2 to 1 tsp red pepper flakes or chili-garlic paste. Optional: 1 tsp rice vinegar only if tamarind is weak, or 1 tsp honey only if sauce tastes sharp.

Quick-Seasoned Shrimp

1 lb peeled shrimp, 1 tsp fish sauce, 1 tsp neutral oil, 1/2 tsp brown sugar, 1 small grated garlic clove, pinch black pepper, optional tiny pinch red pepper flakes. Season 10–15 minutes, 20 minutes max.

Noodles and Finish

7 to 8 oz medium rice stick noodles, 2 large shallots, 3 cloves garlic, 2 eggs, 1 to 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts, 2 to 3 green onions, optional 2 Tbsp cilantro, 1/3 cup dry-roasted unsalted peanuts, lime wedges, extra chili flakes, and neutral oil.

Do Not Forget

Cook noodles per package directions, then rinse, drain, lightly oil, and add only at the end.

Do not use the soak-only method for this recipe.

Use 80% of the sauce first, then hold back 20% for correction after tasting a noodle.

Pull shrimp at 80–90% cooked; they finish in the final toss.

Warm dry-roasted unsalted peanuts 2–3 minutes before crushing.

Fresh lime wedges are required at service.

For hot-running electric skillets, keep most cooking around 275°F to 300°F.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Tamarind Bowl

2 oz whole tamarind plus 1/2 cup hot water. Soak, mash, strain.

Shrimp Bowl

1 lb shrimp, 1 tsp fish sauce, 1 tsp oil, 1/2 tsp brown sugar, 1 small garlic clove, pepper, optional chile pinch.

Shell Concentrate Bowl

Shells/tails, 1 tsp oil, 3 Tbsp water.

Sauce Bowl

1/4 cup tamarind, 3 Tbsp fish sauce, 3 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 Tbsp shell concentrate, 1 Tbsp water, chile.

Double Shallot Bowls

1 thin-sliced shallot for topping; 1 minced shallot plus 3 minced garlic cloves for aromatic base.

Finish Bowl

Cooked noodles, eggs, bean sprouts, green onion, optional cilantro, warmed peanuts, lime wedges, and chili flakes.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Make thick tamarind pulp. Break 2 oz whole tamarind into pieces and cover with 1/2 cup hot water. Soak 15–20 minutes, mash thoroughly, then strain. If watery, simmer briefly to concentrate.
  2. Peel and quick-season shrimp. Peel 1 lb shrimp, saving shells/tails. Toss shrimp with 1 tsp fish sauce, 1 tsp neutral oil, 1/2 tsp brown sugar, 1 small grated garlic clove, pepper, and optional chile pinch. Rest 10–15 minutes.
  3. Make shrimp-shell concentrate. Cook shells/tails with 1 tsp neutral oil for 2–3 minutes. Add 3 Tbsp water, simmer 2 minutes, pressing shells, then strain.
  4. Mix sauce. Stir together 1/4 cup tamarind pulp, 3 Tbsp fish sauce, 3 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 Tbsp shrimp-shell concentrate, 1 Tbsp water, and 1/2 to 1 tsp red pepper flakes or chili-garlic paste. Rest 10–15 minutes.
  5. Cook noodles separately. Cook 7 to 8 oz medium rice stick noodles according to package directions, aiming for the low end. Drain, rinse briefly with cool water, drain well, and toss with a few drops neutral oil.
  6. Warm and crush peanuts. Warm 1/3 cup dry-roasted unsalted peanuts in a dry skillet or air fryer for 2–3 minutes until fragrant. Crush. Reserve about 2 Tbsp for the toss and the rest for topping.
  7. Prep fresh finish. Rinse and dry bean sprouts. Slice green onions, chop optional cilantro, and cut lime wedges.
  8. Make golden shallot topping. Cook 1 large thin-sliced shallot at about 275–300°F until golden and lightly crisp. Remove to paper towels and season lightly.
  9. Sear shrimp and remove. At about 300°F, sear shrimp just until 80–90% cooked. Remove to a plate.
  10. Build aromatic base. Cook 1 large minced shallot at 275–300°F until softened and lightly golden. Add 3 minced garlic cloves and cook briefly until fragrant, not browned.
  11. Make egg ribbons. Push aromatics aside. Add 2 lightly beaten eggs with a tiny pinch salt. Let set into soft curds, then fold through.
  12. Wake the sauce. Add about 80% of the sauce and let bubble 30–60 seconds until glossy, not syrupy.
  13. Finish noodles. Add cooked noodles and toss quickly 1–2 minutes max. Taste one noodle. Add remaining sauce only if needed.
  14. Final fold. Fold shrimp back in with 1/2 cup bean sprouts, 2 Tbsp crushed peanuts, and most green onion. Toss 10–15 seconds.
  15. Plate immediately. Top with fresh bean sprouts, golden shallots, remaining peanuts, green onion, optional cilantro, lime wedges, and extra chili flakes.

Final Taste Target

The finished Pad Thai should be glossy but not wet, sour-fruity from tamarind, salty-savory from fish sauce and shrimp-shell concentrate, rounded with brown sugar, aromatic from double shallot and garlic, rich with egg ribbons, juicy with shrimp, fresh with bean sprouts and lime, and crunchy from warmed peanuts and golden shallots. The noodles should be tender but resilient, never mushy or underdone.
