# Beef Fajita Quesadillas

> A preserved family quesadilla recipe made with marinated sirloin-tip steak, sautéed onion and green bell pepper, sour cream, grated cheese, and flour tortillas browned on a griddle.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Trim sirloin steak to remove any excess fat. |
| 2 | Pound steak with a meat mallet until very thin. |
| 3 | Place steak in a dish and season with garlic powder, black pepper, optional Adolph's meat tenderizer, and a generous amount of Worcestershire sauce. |
| 4 | Cover, refrigerate, and marinate for at least 1 hour before cooking. |
| 5 | Place meat on a gas grill and cook about 6-8 minutes, or until done, turning once halfway through cooking. Do not overcook. |
| 6 | Bring steak in and slice into long, slender strips. |
| 7 | Sauté green pepper and onion in a small amount of oil or cooking oil until slightly tenderized. |
| 8 | Add meat to the onion and pepper mixture. |
| 9 | Coat flour tortillas with sour cream. |
| 10 | Place meat mixture down the middle of each tortilla and top with grated cheddar cheese. |
| 11 | Fold tortillas in half. |
| 12 | Place on a preheated medium-hot griddle and brown on both sides. |

## Notes

Original family note: sirloin steak was preferred over traditional skirt steak because sirloin is a very tender cut of meat, while skirt steak can be very chewy.

Ingredient amounts for sour cream, grated cheese, Worcestershire sauce, seasonings, and cooking oil are not specified in the original recipe and are preserved as flexible amounts.

Servings, prep time, cook time, and total time are estimated. The marinade time and grill time are from the recipe text.
