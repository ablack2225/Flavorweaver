# Garlic-Oyster Broccoli with Ginger, Lime & White Pepper

> Glossy, savory broccoli stir-fried with garlic, ginger, oyster sauce, soy, rice wine, white pepper, and a tiny lime finish. This side is built to stay glossy instead of watery by cooking frozen broccoli first until its moisture evaporates.

|---|---|

| Sauce | Mix before cooking so it can be added quickly. |

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

| Bowl | Contents | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bowl 1 — Broccoli | 3 cups frozen broccoli florets | Add directly to hot dry skillet first. |
| Bowl 2 — Aromatics | 3 minced garlic cloves; 1 tsp ginger; optional 1 small sliced shallot; optional 1/4 to 1/2 sliced or minced jalapeño | Garlic and ginger cook fast; keep ready. |
| Bowl 3 — Sauce | 1 Tbsp oyster sauce; 1 tsp soy sauce; 1 tsp rice wine, Chinese rice wine, or dry sherry; 1 tsp water; optional 1/2 tsp fish sauce; 1/2 tsp sugar; 1/8 tsp white pepper | Stir smooth before cooking. Do not add lime yet. |
| Bowl 4 — Cooking Fat | 1 Tbsp neutral oil | Add after broccoli moisture cooks off and broccoli is removed. |
| Bowl 5 — Finish | 1/2 tsp lime juice; optional green onion; optional toasted sesame seeds; optional crispy fried shallot rings | Lime goes in off heat. Shallot rings go on last. |

## Do Not Forget

- Do not sauce watery broccoli.
- Cook the frozen moisture off first.
- Use high heat.
- Sauce at the end.
- Lime goes in off heat.
- The finished side should be glossy, not soupy.
- Do not cook low and slow; that makes frozen broccoli watery.
- If the skillet gets wet, keep cooking before adding aromatics.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Mix the sauce.** In a small bowl, stir together **1 Tbsp oyster sauce**, **1 tsp soy sauce**, **1 tsp rice wine, Chinese rice wine, or dry sherry**, **1 tsp water**, optional **1/2 tsp fish sauce**, **1/2 tsp sugar**, and **1/8 tsp white pepper**. Set aside. Do not add lime juice yet. |
| 2 | **Drive off broccoli moisture.** Heat a skillet or electric skillet over medium-high heat. For an electric skillet, use **375°F**. Add **3 cups frozen broccoli florets** directly to the hot dry skillet. Cook until the ice melts and excess water steams off. Let broccoli sit against the hot surface briefly so some edges lightly char. Remove broccoli to a plate. |
| 3 | **Stir-fry the aromatics.** Add **1 Tbsp neutral oil** to the hot skillet. Add optional shallot and jalapeño if using. Cook **30 to 60 seconds**. Add **3 cloves minced garlic** and **1 tsp ginger**. Stir **15 to 30 seconds**, just until fragrant. Do not let garlic burn. |
| 4 | **Return broccoli.** Add broccoli back to the skillet. Toss with garlic, ginger, shallot, jalapeño, and oil. Let broccoli hit the hot surface again for **30 to 60 seconds**. |
| 5 | **Sauce quickly.** Pour the sauce around the edge of the hot skillet if possible. Toss quickly until broccoli is glossy and coated. Cook only **30 to 60 seconds** after adding the sauce. The sauce should cling lightly to the broccoli and should not pool in the pan. |
| 6 | **Finish.** Turn off heat. Add **1/2 tsp lime juice** and toss once. Finish with optional green onion, toasted sesame seeds, or crispy fried shallot rings. Serve immediately. |

## Troubleshooting and Flavor Adjustments

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Skillet gets wet | Keep cooking before adding aromatics or sauce. |
| Sauce thickens too fast | Add **1 tsp water** and toss quickly. |
| Dish tastes flat | Add a few drops soy sauce or fish sauce. |
| Dish tastes heavy | Add one more tiny squeeze of lime. |
| Broccoli is watery | Next time, cook frozen moisture off longer before saucing. |

## Final Taste Target

The broccoli should be savory, garlicky, lightly charred, ginger-warm, and glossy.

The oyster sauce should add depth without making the side too sweet. White pepper should give a quiet lift. Lime should brighten the finish without making the dish sour.

This side should anchor creamy Bang Bang shrimp and coconut-lime rice with something green, savory, and sharp.

## Chef's Notes

Frozen broccoli works best when treated like a moisture-management ingredient. Do not add sauce until the broccoli stops steaming heavily.

This recipe is built as a quick high-heat side for saucy, creamy, or fried mains. It should taste glossy and savory, not wet or steamed.
