Fresh Vegetable Spring Rolls

Fresh rice paper spring rolls filled with crisp vegetables, cilantro, and cooled angel hair pasta, served with Peanut Dipping Sauce and Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham.

Prep35-45 min
Cook10 min

Kitchen Execution Summary

Roll Style

Softened rice paper wrappers filled with crisp vegetables, fresh cilantro, and cooled angel hair pasta.

Texture Target

Cool, fresh, crisp, flexible, and lightly chewy. The rolls should feel full but not overstuffed.

Dipping Sauces

Serve with Peanut Dipping Sauce and Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham.

Best Use

Works as a fresh appetizer, side, or cooling contrast beside a richer bowl meal.

Required Components

Rice Paper Wrappers

Use 8 to 10 wrappers, with a few extras nearby in case any tear.

Fresh Vegetable Filling

Napa cabbage, cucumber, carrot, and cilantro provide the crisp fresh base.

Noodle Layer

Cooked, rinsed, cooled angel hair pasta adds soft chew and helps the rolls feel complete.

Two Dipping Sauces

Peanut Dipping Sauce brings richness; Ginger Nuoc Cham brings brightness.

Full Roll Ingredient Roll-Up

This roll-up covers the spring rolls and serving sauces. Prepare sauces separately before rolling so the finished rolls can be served right away.

Cross-Component Bulk Prep Map

Vegetables to prep: shred 2 cups napa cabbage, julienne 1 cup cucumber, and julienne 1 cup carrot. Keep cucumber patted dry so the rolls do not get watery.

Herbs: gather 1/2 cup cilantro leaves. Remove stems if preferred.

Noodles: cook 4 oz dry angel hair pasta, then drain, rinse or cool, and keep loose for easy portioning.

Wrappers: set out 8 to 10 rice paper wrappers plus a few extras in case any tear.

Rolling station: set up a shallow dish of warm water, a damp towel or board, the prepared fillings, and both dipping sauces before softening the first wrapper.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Crisp Vegetables

2 cups shredded napa cabbage, 1 cup julienned cucumber, and 1 cup julienned carrot. Pat cucumber dry if very wet.

Bowl 2 — Herbs

1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves, stems removed if preferred.

Bowl 3 — Noodles

4 oz dry angel hair pasta, cooked, drained, rinsed or cooled, and loosened for portioning.

Bowl 4 — Wrappers & Water

8 to 10 rice paper wrappers, extra wrappers nearby, and a shallow dish of warm water.

Bowl 5 — Sauces

Prepared Peanut Dipping Sauce and Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham for serving.

Do Not Forget

Do not overfill the wrappers. Smaller, tighter rolls are easier to eat and less likely to split.

Soften one wrapper at a time only until pliable. If it gets too soft in the water, it will tear more easily.

Keep finished rolls lightly covered so they do not dry out before serving.

Have both sauces ready before rolling so the rolls can be eaten while fresh.

Full Rolling Timeline and Instructions

  1. Cook the noodles. Cook 4 oz dry angel hair pasta until tender. Drain, rinse or cool, and set aside loose enough to portion into rolls.
  2. Prep the vegetables. Shred 2 cups napa cabbage, julienne 1 cup cucumber, julienne 1 cup carrot, and gather 1/2 cup cilantro leaves.
  3. Set up the rolling station. Arrange 8 to 10 rice paper wrappers, a shallow dish of warm water, a damp towel or board, the prepared vegetables, cooled pasta, and both sauces.
  4. Soften one wrapper. Dip one rice paper wrapper in warm water according to package directions, just until pliable.
  5. Layer the filling. In the lower third of the wrapper, layer a small amount of shredded napa cabbage, julienned cucumber, julienned carrot, cilantro, and cooled angel hair pasta. Do not overfill.
  6. Roll tightly. Fold the sides inward, then roll from the bottom up to form a compact spring roll.
  7. Repeat. Continue with the remaining wrappers and filling. Keep finished rolls lightly covered so they do not dry out.
  8. Serve. Serve with Peanut Dipping Sauce and Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham.

Building the Final Plate

1. Arrange the Rolls

Place the fresh spring rolls on a platter in a single layer or loose stack so the wrappers do not crush.

2. Add Sauce Bowls

Serve Peanut Dipping Sauce and Ginger Nuoc Cham in separate small bowls so each bite can go rich, bright, or both.

3. Keep Covered Briefly

If not serving immediately, cover the rolls lightly with a damp towel. Do not let them sit uncovered.

Final Taste Target

The roll should be cool, crisp, fresh, lightly chewy, and easy to dip. The vegetables should stay bright and crunchy, the noodles should soften the bite, and the sauces should carry the flavor.

Chef's Notes

Fresh vegetable spring rolls are flexible by nature. The amounts are written as a practical starting point, but the best roll is the one that feels balanced in your hand: crisp vegetables, a little noodle softness, fresh herbs, and enough sauce to make each bite bright.

Do not overfill the wrappers. Smaller, tighter rolls are easier to eat and less likely to split.
