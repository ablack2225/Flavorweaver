## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Before cooking day or early cooking day, marinate the brisket.** Combine **2 to 2 1/2 lb brisket chunks** with **1 1/2 Tbsp fish sauce**, **1 Tbsp soy sauce**, **1 Tbsp neutral oil**, **1 Tbsp brown sugar**, **1 Tbsp tomato paste**, **1 Tbsp ginger**, **3 to 4 minced garlic cloves**, **1 Tbsp lemongrass paste**, **1 tsp Chinese five-spice**, **1 tsp paprika**, and **1/2 tsp black pepper**. Marinate at least **2 hours**, or overnight for deeper flavor.

2. **Before cooking day or at least 2 hours before service, make the pickled vegetables.** Combine **1 thin-sliced zucchini**, **1 thin-sliced yellow squash**, **1 carrot cut into ribbons or julienne**, and **2 sliced green onions** with a brine of **1/4 cup rice vinegar**, **2 Tbsp sugar**, **1 tsp kosher salt**, **1 Tbsp water**, and optional **1/4 tsp red pepper flakes**. Chill at least **2-4 hours** or overnight. Do not add sesame, lime, cilantro, or serrano yet.

3. **About 4 hours before serving, start the stew.** Remove the marinated brisket from the refrigerator. Do not rinse. If the marinade is thick, scrape only heavy excess from the meat and reserve that marinade for the braise.

4. **Sear the brisket.** Heat **1 Tbsp neutral oil** in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the brisket in batches until deeply browned. Transfer browned beef to a bowl.

5. **Cook the onion separately.** Lower heat to medium. Add **1 medium sliced or diced onion** by itself and cook **5-7 minutes**, scraping browned bits, until softened and lightly golden.

6. **Bloom the fast aromatics.** Add **2 minced garlic cloves**, **1 Tbsp ginger**, and **1 Tbsp lemongrass paste** to the softened onion. Cook **30-60 seconds**, just until fragrant. Do not let the garlic burn.

7. **Toast the tomato paste and spices.** Add **2 Tbsp tomato paste** and cook **1-2 minutes** until slightly darkened. Stir in **1/2 tsp paprika**, **1/2 tsp Chinese five-spice if using**, **2 bay leaves**, and **1 star anise pod**.

8. **Start the braise.** Return the beef and juices to the pot. Add **4 cups beef broth**, the reserved marinade, **1 Tbsp fish sauce**, **1 Tbsp hoisin**, optional **1-2 tsp brown sugar**, optional **1-2 tsp sriracha**, and enough of the **1-2 cups water** to cover the beef comfortably. Bring to a simmer, then reduce to low.

9. **Simmer the stew gently.** Cook partially covered for **2 1/2 to 3 1/2 hours**, adding water as needed so the broth stays stew-like.

10. **About 2 hours 45 minutes before serving, start the rolls.** Bloom **2 tsp active dry yeast** with **1 cup water between 105°F and 108°F** and **1 tsp sugar** for **5-10 minutes**, until foamy. If it does not foam, restart before continuing.

11. **Mix and rise the roll dough.** Add **2 cups all-purpose flour**, **1 cup bread flour**, **1 1/2 tsp fine salt**, **1 Tbsp neutral oil**, and **1 tsp lime juice**. Add up to **1/4 cup additional warm water**, 1 Tbsp at a time, if dry. Knead **5-7 minutes**, then rise **60-90 minutes** until doubled.

12. **About 1 hour 15 minutes before serving, shape the rolls.** Divide dough into **4 pieces**, shape into short baguette-style rolls, and let rise **35-45 minutes** until puffy.

13. **During the final 35-45 minutes of stew cooking, add carrots.** Add **3 large carrots cut into thick diagonal pieces** and simmer until tender but not collapsing.

14. **About 35 minutes before serving, preheat the oven.** Heat to **450°F** with an empty metal steam pan on the lower rack.

15. **Bake the rolls close to service.** Slash the rolls, place them in the oven, pour **1 cup hot water** into the steam pan, and bake **18-22 minutes**, until deeply golden and crisp. Cool **10-15 minutes** before tearing.

16. **About 15 minutes before service, finish the pickles for this plate.** Drain the Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables lightly. Finish with fresh lime juice, cilantro, and optional thin-sliced serrano. Do not add sesame oil or sesame seeds for this Bò Kho meal.

17. **Finish the stew.** Remove the **2 bay leaves** and **1 star anise pod**. Taste and adjust with fish sauce or kosher salt if flat, lime juice or rice vinegar if heavy, a small pinch of sugar if sharp, sriracha if more warmth is wanted, and black pepper for lift. Rest **10 minutes**.

18. **Serve the complete plate.** Serve hot stew in bowls with fresh dipping rolls, pickled vegetables beside the stew, and a garnish plate of lime wedges, cilantro, sliced green onion, thin-sliced serrano, black pepper, and sriracha at the table.

## Hold / Warm / Do Not Hold Notes

| Item | Hold Rule |
|---|---|
| Stew | Can hold gently warm once tender; do not boil hard. |
| Rolls | Best baked close to service; can cool briefly 10-15 minutes before tearing. |
| Pickled Vegetables | Keep chilled until service; drain and finish right before plating. |
| Garnish Plate | Cut close to service. Herbs and serrano should feel fresh. |

## Final Plating Checklist

- Hot stew in bowls with visible brisket and carrots.
- Rolls served warm, whole or torn open, for dipping.
- Pickled vegetables served beside the stew, not submerged in it.
- Garnish plate on the table with lime, cilantro, green onion, thin-sliced serrano, black pepper, and sriracha.
- Taste the broth one last time before service: rich, savory, aromatic, lightly sweet, bright enough, not five-spice heavy.

## Troubleshooting / Rescue Notes

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Meal tastes heavy | Add lime to stew at finish, serve more pickles, and use fresh herbs. |
| Meal lacks salt/depth | Add fish sauce in small increments, then retaste. |
| Meal lacks freshness | Increase lime wedges, cilantro, green onion, serrano, and pickle presence. |
| Bread is behind schedule | Keep stew warm gently and bake rolls fresh before serving. |
| Pickles feel too Korean for the plate | Skip sesame oil/seeds and finish with lime, cilantro, and serrano. |
