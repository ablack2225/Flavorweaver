# Pie Crust for Fried Pies

> A preserved family fried pie recipe made with a milk-based Crisco dough, filled with apple filling, sealed with a fork, and fried until golden brown.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Mix flour and salt together. |
| 2 | Cut in shortening. |
| 3 | Gradually add milk until dough comes off the sides of the bowl and holds together. |
| 4 | Make dough into small balls and roll each ball into a thin circle. |
| 5 | Fill with your favorite filling. The family favorite on this page is apple. |
| 6 | For apple filling, combine thinly sliced apples, sugar, nutmeg, cinnamon, and margarine in a large pot. |
| 7 | Add a small amount of water, just enough to keep apples from sticking to the pan. |
| 8 | Bring to a boil and simmer until apples begin to soften slightly. |
| 9 | Mix flour with a small amount of water to form a smooth mixture. |
| 10 | Gradually add flour mixture to the apples to thicken the filling. |
| 11 | Fill each dough circle with filling. |
| 12 | Seal edges by putting a small amount of water around the edges, then pressing together with the tines of a fork dipped in flour. |
| 13 | Heat 1-2 inches of oil over medium heat in a large skillet. |
| 14 | Add pies and fry until golden brown, flipping about halfway through cooking time. |
| 15 | Drain on paper towels and serve hot. |
