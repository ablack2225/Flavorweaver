# Moroccan-Inspired Chicken Tagine with Apricot-Orange Glaze

> Bold Moroccan-inspired chicken tagine built for the Amy & Don table: deeply marinated chicken thighs, jammy onion sauce, preserved lemon, green olives, apricots, orange marmalade, clean soy/Worcestershire umami, cayenne warmth, golden cilantro couscous, and sweet-spicy cumin cashews.

## Flavor Profile

This version is not a polite lemon chicken stew. It is built around Flavorweaver's full flavor arc: preserved-lemon tang, apricot-orange sweetness, olive salt, jammy onion savoriness, cayenne heat, tomato-spice depth, clean soy/Worcestershire umami, butter gloss, cilantro brightness, and sweet-spicy cashew crunch.

Target bite:

> marinated spiced chicken + glossy jammy onion-apricot sauce + preserved lemon tang + green olive salt + orange marmalade chile glaze + golden cilantro couscous + sweet-spicy cumin cashews.

## Do Not Forget

- **Marinade is mandatory** for the Flavorweaver version. Direct seasoning alone is not enough.
- Use mostly **preserved lemon rind**, not a heavy amount of pulp.
- Cook onions **15–20 minutes** until collapsed, glossy, golden, and sweet. This is the sauce body.
- Use orange marmalade as a house citrus bridge, not just sugar.
- Use soy/Worcestershire in tiny amounts for background umami. It should not taste like soy sauce.
- Add olives late so they stay bright.
- Reduce sauce uncovered until glossy before plating.
- Use cilantro-forward finishing, not parsley-only garnish.
- Keep cashews out of the braise; they are a crunchy finish.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Marinate the chicken.** Combine **4 chicken thighs** with all marinade ingredients: **1 Tbsp olive oil**, **2 grated garlic cloves**, **1 Tbsp grated ginger**, **1 tsp minced preserved lemon rind**, **1 tsp lemon juice or preserved lemon brine**, **1 tsp soy sauce**, **1/2 tsp Worcestershire**, **1 tsp kosher salt**, **1 tsp cumin**, **1 tsp coriander**, **3/4 tsp paprika**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, **1/4 tsp cayenne**, and a tiny pinch cinnamon. Cover and marinate **2 hours to overnight**.
2. **Temper chicken.** Pull chicken from the fridge **20–30 minutes** before cooking.
3. **Soften apricots.** Pour **1/2 cup hot chicken broth** over **1/2 cup chopped dried apricots**. Let sit while the onions cook.
4. **Prep preserved lemon.** Scrape pulp from **1 preserved lemon quarter**. Mince the rind. Reserve a tiny pinch for garnish; use the rest in the sauce.
5. **Brown chicken.** Heat the electric skillet around **300°F** with a thin layer of oil. Scrape excess marinade from chicken only if it is very wet. Brown chicken on both sides until golden but not cooked through. Remove to a plate.
6. **Build jammy onions.** Add **1 Tbsp olive oil**, **1 Tbsp butter**, **1 very large or 2 medium thin-sliced onions**, and a pinch salt to the skillet. Cook **15–20 minutes**, lowering toward **275°F** if needed, until onions are collapsed, glossy, golden, and sweet. Splash in a spoonful of broth if fond threatens to burn.
7. **Add aromatics.** Stir in **3 minced garlic cloves** and **1 Tbsp grated ginger**. Cook **45–60 seconds**.
8. **Bloom spices and tomato paste.** Add the spice bloom: **1/2 tsp cumin**, **1/2 tsp coriander**, **1/2 tsp paprika**, **1/4 tsp turmeric**, **1/8 tsp cinnamon**, **1/4 tsp cayenne**, and a pinch black pepper. Stir **30 seconds**. Add **1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste** and cook **60–90 seconds** until darker and fragrant.
9. **Build sauce.** Add the softened apricots with their **1/2 cup broth**, the remaining **1/2 cup chicken broth**, minced preserved lemon rind, and **1 tsp orange marmalade**. Scrape the skillet thoroughly.
10. **Braise.** Return chicken and accumulated juices to the skillet. Cover and simmer gently until chicken is tender and cooked through, about **25–35 minutes** depending on thigh size.
11. **Add olives late.** Add **1/3 cup halved green olives** during the final **10–15 minutes** of cooking.
12. **Make couscous.** Bring **1 cup chicken broth** with **1 Tbsp butter**, **1/4 tsp turmeric**, tiny pinch cumin, and salt to a boil. Pour over **1 cup couscous**, cover **5 minutes**, then fluff. Fold in **1–2 tsp minced preserved lemon rind** and **2–3 Tbsp chopped cilantro**.
13. **Toast cashews.** In a small skillet, warm **1 tsp butter or olive oil**. Add **1/3 cup cashews**, **1/4 tsp cumin**, **1/8 tsp coriander**, pinch cayenne, **1/2 tsp brown sugar**, and **1/4 tsp salt**. Toast **2–4 minutes** until fragrant. Remove before scorching.
14. **Make finishing glaze.** In a small bowl or saucepan, combine **1 Tbsp orange marmalade**, **1 tsp honey**, **1 tsp tomato paste**, **1 tsp preserved lemon brine or lemon juice**, **1/2 tsp soy sauce**, **1/4 tsp cumin**, **1/8–1/4 tsp cayenne**, and **1 Tbsp butter**. Warm just enough to melt together.
15. **Reduce and glaze.** Uncover tagine. If sauce is watery, simmer uncovered **3–8 minutes** until spoonable and glossy. Stir in the finishing glaze. Taste.
16. **Final correction.** If flat, add a few drops soy or a pinch salt. If too sweet, add preserved lemon brine or lemon juice. If too sharp, add 1/2 tsp honey or marmalade. If boring, add cayenne and a tiny splash Worcestershire.
17. **Plate.** Spoon golden cilantro couscous onto plates. Add chicken, generous glossy sauce, olives and apricots. Finish with spiced cashews, cilantro, and the reserved pinch of preserved lemon rind.

## Sauce Balance Check

| Need | Correct With |
|---|---|
| More sour/tangy | Preserved lemon brine, lemon juice, tiny vinegar splash. |
| More sweet | Orange marmalade or honey, 1/2 tsp at a time. |
| More salty | Kosher salt, olive brine, or a few drops soy. |
| More savory | Reduce sauce longer, add tiny Worcestershire, or add a small knob butter. |
| More spicy | Cayenne or red pepper flakes. |
| More umami | Tiny soy, tiny Worcestershire, or darker tomato paste bloom next time. |
