Thai-Inspired Drunken Noodles with Shrimp, Bacon & Egg

Wide rice noodles tossed hot and fast with shrimp, bacon, soft egg curds, chunky garlic, Thai chiles, shallot, red bell pepper, vine tomato, Thai basil, and a glossy oyster-fish-soy sauce.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Dry Wide Rice Noodles

Soak until bendy but still slightly firm so they finish in the wok.

Shrimp + Bacon + Egg

Shrimp brings sweetness, bacon gives rendered pork-style depth, and eggs create soft curds.

Chunky Garlic-Chile-Shallot

Big visible garlic, Thai chiles, and shallot bloom in hot fat. Do not turn them into paste.

Red Pepper, Tomato + Basil

Crisp red pepper, juicy vine tomato, and heavy Thai basil finish the noodles.

Measured Ingredients

Noodles

8 oz dry wide rice noodles, soaked in hot tap water or very warm water until bendy but still slightly firm; tiny drizzle neutral oil only if needed after draining.

Proteins

8 oz to 1 lb shrimp, peeled, deveined, and patted dry; 2 strips bacon, cut crosswise into 1/4-inch pieces; 2 large eggs, lightly beaten.

Shrimp marinade: 1 tsp fish sauce, 1 small garlic clove finely chopped or grated, tiny pinch honey or sugar, and 1 tsp neutral oil. Marinate 10 minutes only.

Chunky Garlic-Chile-Shallot Aromatics

8 large cloves garlic, smashed and rough chopped; 2-5 Thai chiles, sliced or rough chopped with the garlic; 1 large shallot, sliced into thin 1/8-inch half-moons.

Keep the garlic and chiles chunky and visible. This is not a paste.

Vegetables + Finish

1/2 medium yellow or white onion, sliced into 1/4-inch strips; 1 medium red bell pepper, sliced into 1/4-inch by 2-inch strips; 1 medium or 2 small vine tomatoes, cored and cut into wedges or chunky pieces; 1 1/2 to 2 packed cups Thai basil leaves.

Glossy Drunken Noodle Sauce

2 Tbsp oyster sauce; 1 Tbsp fish sauce; 1 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 tsp dark soy sauce; 1 1/2 Tbsp honey or brown sugar; 1 Tbsp water; optional tiny pinch black pepper.

Proper Defining Cuts

Garlic + Thai chiles: smash garlic, rough chop into visible pieces, slice or rough chop chiles, then chop together a few passes. Keep chunky, not pasted.

Shallot: thin 1/8-inch half-moons. Onion: 1/4-inch strips. Red bell pepper: 1/4-inch wide by 2-inch long strips.

Tomato: thin wedges or chunky bite-size pieces. Remove loose seed gel if very juicy. Bacon: 1/4-inch pieces. Thai basil: leaves picked from stems.

Noodle Prep

  1. Place dry wide rice noodles in a large bowl and cover with hot tap water or very warm water.
  2. Start checking at 20 minutes.
  3. Stop soaking when noodles are flexible and bendy but still slightly firm in the center.
  4. Drain very well and rest 10-15 minutes before cooking.
  5. Toss with a tiny drizzle of neutral oil only if they are sticking badly.

Do not boil the noodles fully soft. They need room to finish in the sauce.

Cooking Timeline

  1. Render bacon. Cook bacon pieces over medium to medium-high heat until fat renders and edges are lightly crisp. Remove bacon to Bowl 8. Leave about 1 Tbsp bacon fat in the pan.
  2. Heat the pan. Raise heat to high. Add 1 Tbsp neutral oil if the pan looks dry.
  3. Bloom aromatics. Add Bowl 6 and stir-fry 30-60 seconds until fragrant. Do not burn the garlic.
  4. Add eggs. Push aromatics slightly aside. Add beaten eggs, let set briefly, then fold into large soft curds.
  5. Add shrimp. Add marinated shrimp and toss until it just starts turning pink.
  6. Add onion and red bell pepper. Add Bowl 7 and stir-fry 45-75 seconds.
  7. Add noodles and sauce. Add Bowl 1 and Bowl 2. Fold and toss gently for 2-4 minutes until glossy, darker, and tender.
  8. Adjust only if needed. If noodles are still too firm or tight, add water 1 Tbsp at a time and keep tossing.
  9. Finish off heat. Turn heat low or off. Add Bowl 8 and fold 30-60 seconds until basil wilts and tomato releases a little juice.
  10. Serve immediately.

Do Not Forget

Use the full 8 cloves garlic. Keep garlic and Thai chiles chunky, not pasted. Do not boil dry noodles fully soft. Drain noodles very well. Keep egg curds large and soft. Add water only 1 Tbsp at a time if needed. Tomato and Thai basil go in at the end.
