# Home-Ground Brisket · 80/20 Planning Standard

## Reference Status

**Status:** Calculated household standard  
**Use:** Flavorweaver recipe macro calculations and Vitalweaver portion planning  
**Added:** 2026-06-17  

---

## Household Standard

Amy and Don commonly buy whole Choice beef brisket and grind it at home.

Don trims off the large hard fat portions and renders those separately into frying oil / tallow.

For recipe nutrition planning, the ground brisket portion should be calculated as:

```text
80% lean / 20% fat
```

This is intentionally conservative so calories and fat are not undercounted.

---

## Tracking Rule

| Item | Tracking Treatment |
|---|---|
| Ground brisket used in recipe | Track as 80/20 ground beef equivalent unless a different ratio is known. |
| Rendered brisket fat / tallow | Track separately only if added back to a recipe as cooking fat. |
| Fat drained after browning | Subtract or note separately only if the fat is actually drained and not used in the recipe. |
| Fat retained in pan / sauce | Count as retained in the recipe. |

---

## Recipe Calculation Notes

For recipes such as Bold Bulgogi Bowls:

```text
1 lb home-ground brisket = calculate as 1 lb 80/20 ground beef equivalent
```

Unless Amy says otherwise, assume:

- beef fat is retained in the pan / sauce
- rendered tallow is not included unless added separately
- 80/20 is the planning value

---

## Verification Notes

This is not a package-label verification because home-ground brisket varies by trim and cut.

It is a stable household calculation standard based on Don's normal trim target and Amy's instruction to use 80/20 for safe tracking.
