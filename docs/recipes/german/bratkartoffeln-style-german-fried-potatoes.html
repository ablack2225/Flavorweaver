Bratkartoffeln-Style German Fried Potatoes

Fresh baking potatoes are par-cooked, steam-dried, and crisped in bacon fat for a smoky, golden German-inspired fried potato side with onion, marjoram, crushed caraway, black pepper, and a fresh green onion finish.

Total1 hr 15 min
Serves3 to 4
DifficultyModerate
StatusRecreation Candidate
German-Inspiredbratkartoffelnfresh potatoesbacon onion potatoesschnitzel sideelectric skillet

Kitchen Execution Summary

Fresh Baking Potatoes

Par-cooked and steam-dried so they fry with crisp golden edges and fluffy centers.

Bacon

Provides smoky fat, salty depth, and crisp pieces for the final toss.

Onion

Cooked separately so it becomes sweet and savory without burning while the potatoes crisp.

Green Onion Finish

Mandatory fresh finish for lift, color, and balance against the rich plate.

Flavor Target

These potatoes should be deeply golden, crisp-edged, fluffy inside, smoky from bacon, savory-sweet from onion, peppery enough to stand beside schnitzel, and lightly herbal from marjoram. The caraway should be present but subtle.

The Amy & Don target is not bland potato comfort. This version should taste intentionally seasoned, bacon-rich, crisp, savory, and lively enough to hold its own beside blackberry-finished Rotkohl.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

Par-cook and dry the potatoes early if possible. The cooler and drier the potato surface, the better the crust.

Peeled potatoes give a cleaner, more elevated plate. Skins can stay on for a more rustic version.

Chop bacon and onion ahead if needed. Lightly crush the caraway seed before cooking.

Slice the green onions shortly before finishing so they taste fresh.

Measured Ingredients

Main Potatoes

2 to 2 1/4 lb baking potatoes / russets, peeled or scrubbed and cut into 1/2-inch cubes; kosher salt for the cooking water; 4 slices bacon, chopped; 1 medium onion, diced or thin sliced; 1 to 2 Tbsp reserved bacon drippings; optional 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp butter, added late.

Seasoning

1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/4 tsp plain paprika; 1/8 tsp caraway seed, lightly crushed; 1/4 tsp dried marjoram; tiny pinch dried thyme.

Mandatory Finish

2 to 3 Tbsp green onions, thin sliced; optional tiny pat butter only if the potatoes need gloss.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Potatoes: par-cook only until barely tender. If they fully soften, they break apart before crisping.

Drying: drain well and spread uncovered so steam escapes. Wet potatoes steam instead of crisp.

Bacon and onion: cook bacon first, then onion, then remove both before crisping the potatoes.

Crisping: do not stir constantly. Contact time creates crust.

Seasoning: add herbs and spices near the end so they stay fragrant instead of scorching.

Balance: do not add vinegar or mustard here. The Rotkohl already carries the sweet-sour role.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Fresh Potatoes

2 to 2 1/4 lb baking potatoes/russets, cut into 1/2-inch cubes. Par-cook in salted water, drain, and steam-dry.

Bowl 2 — Bacon

4 slices bacon, chopped. Cook first and reserve.

Bowl 3 — Onion

1 medium onion, diced or thin sliced. Cook after bacon, then remove.

Bowl 4 — Frying Fat

1 to 2 Tbsp reserved bacon drippings; optional 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp butter for late finish.

Bowl 5 — Seasoning

1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp plain paprika, 1/8 tsp crushed caraway, 1/4 tsp marjoram, tiny pinch thyme.

Bowl 6 — Mandatory Finish

2 to 3 Tbsp thin-sliced green onions; optional tiny pat butter.

Do Not Forget

Start potatoes early enough to par-cook and dry them.

Drain and steam-dry hard before frying.

Use the electric skillet surface to your advantage: spread the potatoes out.

Remove onion before crisping potatoes.

Do not stir constantly; browning needs contact time.

Add seasoning near the end. Add butter late, not at the beginning.

Green onion is mandatory for this version.

Keep potatoes away from cabbage juices at service.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Cut potatoes. Peel or scrub 2 to 2 1/4 lb baking potatoes/russets. Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
  2. Par-cook potatoes. Add potatoes to cold salted water. Bring to a simmer and cook 5 to 7 minutes, until barely tender with slight resistance in the center. Do not cook until falling apart.
  3. Drain and dry. Drain thoroughly. Spread potatoes on a tray or plate and let steam-dry uncovered for 20 to 30 minutes. Chill briefly if time allows.
  4. Preheat skillet. Set electric skillet to 350°F and let it heat fully.
  5. Cook bacon. Add 4 slices chopped bacon to the dry skillet. Cook until crisp. Remove bacon to a plate. Leave about 2 Tbsp drippings in the skillet; spoon off and reserve any extra.
  6. Cook onion. Add 1 medium diced or thin-sliced onion to the bacon drippings. Cook 5 to 7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and lightly golden. Remove onion and hold with the bacon.
  7. Crisp potatoes. Turn skillet to 375°F. Add reserved bacon drippings and 1 Tbsp neutral oil only if needed. Add the dried potatoes in as even a layer as possible. Let sit untouched 4 to 5 minutes before turning. Continue cooking 20 to 25 minutes total, turning only every few minutes, until browned and crisp-edged.
  8. Season. Once potatoes are mostly golden, sprinkle over 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp plain paprika, 1/8 tsp lightly crushed caraway seed, 1/4 tsp dried marjoram, and a tiny pinch dried thyme. Toss well and cook 1 to 2 minutes.
  9. Butter finish. Add 1 Tbsp butter and toss just until glossy and fragrant.
  10. Return bacon and onion. Add the cooked bacon and onion back to the skillet. Toss and cook 2 to 3 minutes, until hot and deeply savory.
  11. Finish. Add 2 to 3 Tbsp thin-sliced green onions and toss briefly. Taste and adjust salt or pepper.
  12. Serve. Plate as the savory starch beside pork schnitzel and Rotkohl. Keep potatoes away from cabbage juices so they stay crisp.

Plate Role

These potatoes anchor the Pork Loin Schnitzel with Rotkohl plate. Schnitzel brings crisp fried pork, Rotkohl brings glossy blackberry sweet-sour cabbage, these potatoes bring smoky-salty savory crunch, and lemon brings brightness.
