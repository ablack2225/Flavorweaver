Restaurant-Style Fried Rice

Simple restaurant-style fried rice with day-old rice, eggs, onion, peas and carrots, green onions, soy sauce, butter, neutral oil, and sesame oil.

Measured Ingredients

Fried Rice

4 to 5 cups day-old rice; 2 eggs; 1/2 onion, diced; 1 cup peas and carrots; 3 green onions, sliced; 3 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 Tbsp butter; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 tsp sesame oil.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Rice

4 to 5 cups day-old rice. Break up clumps before cooking.

Bowl 2 — Eggs

2 eggs, lightly beaten. Cook first and reserve.

Bowl 3 — Vegetables

1/2 diced onion; 1 cup peas and carrots. Saute before adding rice.

Bowl 4 — Green Onions

3 sliced green onions. Add near the end.

Bowl 5 — Seasoning

3 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 Tbsp butter; 1 tsp sesame oil. Soy sauce seasons during cooking; sesame oil finishes.

Do Not Forget

Day-old rice gives the best texture.

Cook eggs first, then reserve so they do not overcook.

Let the rice toast lightly after adding it to the pan.

Finish with sesame oil and green onions.

No nutrition panel is needed for this recipe card.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Cook the eggs first. Heat a little oil or butter in a hot pan. Add 2 beaten eggs, scramble just until set, then remove and reserve.
  2. Saute vegetables. Add 1 Tbsp neutral oil and 1/2 diced onion to the pan. Cook until softened. Add 1 cup peas and carrots and cook until warmed through.
  3. Add rice. Add 4 to 5 cups day-old rice. Break up clumps and spread rice across the pan so it can toast lightly.
  4. Season. Add 3 Tbsp soy sauce and 1 Tbsp butter. Stir-fry until the rice is hot, seasoned, and lightly glossy.
  5. Return eggs. Add the reserved eggs back to the pan and fold through the rice.
  6. Finish. Add 1 tsp sesame oil and 3 sliced green onions. Toss briefly and serve hot.

Final Taste Target

The finished fried rice should be savory, lightly buttery, hot, separated rather than mushy, and lightly toasted with tender vegetables and soft egg throughout.
