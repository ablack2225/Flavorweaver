# Lebanese-Inspired Brisket Beef Bowls

> Buttery toasted vermicelli lemon-herb basmati rice layered with juicy Lebanese-spiced ground brisket patties, bold garlic-lemon tahini yogurt sauce, cucumber-tomato parsley salad, pickle-brine onion and cucumber, and scratch lemon-garlic pita chips.

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

- If possible, shape the brisket patties and chill them **20 to 30 minutes** before cooking.
- The scratch pita dough can rest while the salad, sauce, and rice prep are staged.
- Sumac is ideal for the final tangy finish. If sumac is unavailable, use a **very light dusting of Tajín** only on the pickled onion-cucumber topping.
- Amy's electric skillet runs hot. Start the brisket patties at **300°F** and use **325°F only if browning is too slow**.

## Do Not Forget

- Prep bowls must include measurements so the cook does not have to return to the ingredient list mid-cook.
- Timeline and instructions must repeat measurements when ingredients are added.
- Do not add lemon juice to the rice before cooking. Add **1 to 2 tsp lemon juice** and **1/2 tsp lemon zest** after the rice is cooked.
- Squeeze the grated onion very dry before adding **1/4 cup squeezed grated onion** to the beef.
- Chill shaped patties **20 to 30 minutes** before cooking if time allows.
- Cook 75/25 brisket patties at **300°F** in Amy's electric skillet. Use **325°F only if browning is too slow**.
- Rest/drain cooked patties briefly so the bowl tastes juicy, not greasy.
- Use sumac if available. If not, use only **1/16 tsp Tajín** on the pickled onion-cucumber topping, not in the beef, rice, sauce, or salad.
- Scratch pita chips are the preferred crunch element for this bowl.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Make the pita dough.** In a bowl, mix **1 cup AP flour**, **1/2 tsp instant yeast**, **1/2 tsp sugar**, and **1/2 tsp kosher salt**. Add **1 Tbsp olive oil** and **1/3 cup warm water**. Mix and knead **4 to 6 minutes**, adding **1 to 2 Tbsp extra water only if needed**. Cover and rest **45 to 60 minutes**. |
| 2 | **Shape the brisket patties.** Combine **1 lb 75/25 ground brisket**, **1/4 cup grated onion squeezed dry**, **1 1/2 tsp grated garlic**, **1/3 cup chopped parsley**, **1 1/4 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp black pepper**, **1 tsp allspice**, **3/4 tsp cumin**, **1/2 tsp coriander**, **1/2 tsp paprika**, **1/8 tsp cinnamon**, and optional **pinch cayenne**. Mix just until tacky. Use **1 Tbsp breadcrumbs/crushed pita only if loose**. Shape into about **8 small oval patties**, roughly **2 oz each**, **3 inches long**, and **1/2 inch thick**. Chill **20 to 30 minutes**. |
| 3 | **Make the garlic-lemon tahini yogurt sauce.** Whisk **1/2 cup Greek yogurt or sour cream**, **2 Tbsp tahini**, **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, **1/2 tsp lemon zest**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1 tsp olive oil**, **1/4 tsp kosher salt**, and a **pinch black pepper**. Thin with **1/4 to 1 tsp water only if needed**. Add a tiny drizzle of honey only if the sauce tastes too sharp. Refrigerate until service. |
| 4 | **Start the salad drain.** Toss **1 cup diced cucumber** and **1 cup diced tomato** with **1/8 tsp kosher salt**. Rest **5 to 10 minutes**, then drain off excess liquid. Finish with **2 Tbsp chopped parsley**, optional **1 Tbsp finely diced onion**, **1 tsp lemon juice**, **1 tsp olive oil**, a **pinch black pepper**, and optional **1 Tbsp chopped mint**. |
| 5 | **Finish the pickled topping.** Drain **1/2 cup pickle-brine red onion and cucumber** very well. Toss with **1 tsp lemon juice**, **1/4 tsp lemon zest**, **1 Tbsp chopped parsley**, and a few drops of olive oil. Add a small pinch of sumac if available. If sumac is unavailable, use **1/16 tsp Tajín**, taste, and increase only if needed. |
| 6 | **Cook the toasted vermicelli rice.** Rinse **3/4 cup basmati rice**. In the rice pot or skillet, melt **1 Tbsp butter** with **1 tsp olive oil**. Toast **1/4 cup broken vermicelli** until golden. Add the rinsed **3/4 cup basmati rice** and stir **1 minute**. Add **1 1/2 cups chicken broth or water plus bouillon** and **1/4 tsp kosher salt**. Cook until tender. After cooking, fluff and fold in **1/2 tsp lemon zest**, **1 to 2 tsp lemon juice**, **2 Tbsp chopped parsley**, and optional **1 Tbsp chopped mint**. |
| 7 | **Cook scratch pita.** Divide rested dough into **3 to 4 balls** and roll about **1/8 inch thick**. Cook on a dry skillet or electric skillet at **275°F**, increasing to **300°F only if needed**, about **1 to 2 minutes per side**, until cooked through and lightly spotted. |
| 8 | **Turn pita into chips.** Heat oven to **350°F**. Cut or tear cooked pita into chips. Toss with **1 Tbsp olive oil**, **1/4 tsp garlic powder**, **1/4 tsp paprika**, optional **tiny pinch cumin**, and a **pinch kosher salt**. Bake **8 to 12 minutes**, stirring once, until crisp. Finish warm chips with a tiny pinch of lemon zest if using. |
| 9 | **Sear the brisket patties.** Heat Amy's electric skillet to **300°F**. Add only a thin film of neutral oil if needed. Cook patties **3 to 4 minutes per side**, or until browned and cooked through. If browning is too slow, raise to **325°F**. Transfer to a rack or paper towel and rest **3 to 5 minutes**. |
| 10 | **Build the bowls.** Divide the toasted vermicelli lemon-herb rice between **2 bowls**. Add **4 brisket patties per bowl**. Spoon or drizzle garlic-lemon tahini yogurt sauce over the beef and rice. Add cucumber-tomato parsley salad, then a small bright pile of pickled onion-cucumber topping. Finish with scratch pita chips, extra parsley, lemon wedge, and a final tiny olive oil drizzle if desired. |

## Building the Final Bowl

| Layer | Build Detail |
|---|---|
| 1. Warm Rice Foundation | Divide all toasted vermicelli lemon-herb basmati rice between **2 bowls**. |
| 2. Brisket Patty Anchor | Add about **4 small brisket patties per bowl**, letting them sit partly over the rice. |
| 3. Sauce Layer | Drizzle with garlic-lemon tahini yogurt sauce; keep extra sauce available at the table. |
| 4. Fresh Salad | Add cucumber-tomato parsley salad beside the beef, not buried underneath it. |
| 5. Pickled Brightness | Add a small pile of pickle-brine onion-cucumber topping. Keep it bright and drained. |
| 6. Crunch Finish | Add scratch lemon-garlic pita chips last so they stay crisp. |
| 7. Final Finish | Finish with parsley, lemon, and sumac if available; if not, the pickled topping may carry a tiny Tajín dusting. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished bowl should taste juicy, buttery, garlicky, lemon-bright, creamy, herbaceous, and deeply savory. The toasted vermicelli rice should feel nutty and fragrant, the brisket patties should be browned and juicy without greasing out the bowl, the sauce should be bold enough to bind everything, the salad should keep the bowl fresh, the pickled topping should snap, and the scratch pita chips should give a clean dry crunch.

## Chef's Notes

- This is intentionally not titled around kafta/kofta, even though the beef patties borrow from that warm-spiced Lebanese grill direction.
- Mini patties are preferred over loose beef because they give better browning, plating, and main-component identity.
- The scratch pita chips are not a garnish afterthought. They are the dry crunch that keeps the bowl from becoming soft-on-soft.
- If tahini is unavailable, keep the sauce creamy-garlicky-lemony with yogurt or sour cream, plus **1 Tbsp mayo** for body if needed.
