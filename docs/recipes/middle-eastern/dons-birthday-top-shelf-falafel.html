Don's Birthday Top-Shelf Falafel

Crisp, deeply golden falafel with a fluffy herb-green interior, strong chickpea flavor, warm cumin-coriander depth, gentle garlic, subtle cardamom, bright herbs, lemon tahini sauce, and quick green zhug-style sauce.

Golden Rule

Do not use canned chickpeas.

Falafel needs dried chickpeas that have been soaked overnight but not cooked. Cooked or canned chickpeas make the mixture too wet and soft, which can cause the falafel to fall apart or turn dense.

Measured Ingredients

Chickpea Soak

1 lb dried chickpeas; cold water to cover by at least 3 inches; optional 1/2 tsp baking soda.

Falafel Grind

All soaked, drained, dried chickpeas; 1 small roughly chopped onion; 4 scallions, white and light green parts; 5 garlic cloves; 1 small peeled carrot; 1 packed cup parsley; 1 packed cup cilantro; optional 2 Tbsp fresh mint.

Falafel Seasoning

2 tsp cumin; 1 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp cardamom; 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 2 tsp kosher salt; 1 Tbsp baking powder; 2 to 3 Tbsp chickpea flour or all-purpose flour; 1 to 3 Tbsp cold water only if needed.

Lemon Tahini Sauce

1/2 cup tahini; 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice; 1 grated small garlic clove; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/4 to 1/2 cup ice-cold water.

Quick Green Zhug-Style Sauce

1 cup cilantro; 1/2 cup parsley; 1 serrano or jalapeño; 1 garlic clove; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/4 tsp coriander; pinch cardamom; pinch salt; 1 Tbsp lemon juice; 2 to 4 Tbsp oil.

Do Not Forget

Do not use canned chickpeas.

Chickpeas should be soaked, drained, rinsed, and dried, but not cooked.

Meat grinder is preferred; pass mixture through twice on a fine plate.

Food processor backup should be pulsed, not pureed.

Finished mixture should look like damp couscous or very finely chopped nuts, not hummus paste.

Chill mixture at least 1 hour before shaping and frying.

Test-fry one falafel before frying the whole batch.

Fry fresh right before dinner and keep oil between 350°F and 365°F.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Soak chickpeas. Place 1 lb dried chickpeas in a large bowl. Cover with cold water by at least 3 inches. Add optional 1/2 tsp baking soda. Soak 18 to 24 hours.
  2. Drain and dry. Drain chickpeas very well, rinse, then spread on towels or a baking sheet and dry thoroughly. The chickpeas should be hydrated but not cooked.
  3. Grind with the preferred meat-grinder method. Run the soaked chickpeas, onion, scallions, garlic, carrot, parsley, cilantro, and mint through the meat grinder on a fine plate. For best texture, pass the mixture through twice.
  4. Use food processor backup only if needed. Work in batches and pulse until finely minced. Do not puree. The mixture should look like damp couscous or very fine chopped nuts.
  5. Season the mixture. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, cardamom, cayenne, black pepper, kosher salt, baking powder, and flour. Mix by hand until evenly combined.
  6. Check texture. Squeeze a small handful. It should hold together when pressed but should not feel like hummus paste. If it crumbles badly, add cold water 1 Tbsp at a time only as needed.
  7. Chill mixture. Cover and refrigerate at least 1 hour.
  8. Make lemon tahini sauce. Whisk tahini, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and cumin. Slowly whisk in ice-cold water until smooth, pale, creamy, and pourable.
  9. Make quick green zhug-style sauce. Blend or finely chop cilantro, parsley, chile, garlic, cumin, coriander, cardamom, salt, lemon juice, and oil into a loose green sauce.
  10. Shape falafel. Use wet hands or a small scoop. Shape into small balls or slightly flattened patties, about 1 heaping tablespoon each. Press firmly so they hold together.
  11. Test-fry one falafel. Heat oil to 350°F. Fry one test falafel before committing to the full batch.
  12. Adjust after test fry if needed. If it falls apart, press more firmly, chill longer, add 1 Tbsp more flour, or grind a small portion finer. If flavor is flat, add salt, cumin, or coriander. If dense, shape smaller and confirm oil temperature.
  13. Deep-fry in small batches. Fry at 350°F for 3 to 4 minutes, until deeply golden and crisp. Do not overcrowd. Keep oil between 350°F and 365°F.
  14. Drain and salt. Lift with a spider or slotted spoon. Drain on a rack or paper towels. Salt lightly immediately.
  15. Serve hot. Serve fresh from the fryer with lemon tahini sauce, zhug-style green sauce, warm pita or flatbread, pickles, salad or soft cooked vegetables, herbs, and lemon wedges.

Test Fry Troubleshooting

Falls Apart

Press shaped falafel more firmly, chill longer, add 1 Tbsp more flour, or grind/pulse a small portion finer and mix it back in.

Tastes Flat

Add a little more salt, cumin, or coriander.

Too Dense

Shape smaller, avoid packing too hard, and make sure oil is hot enough.

Too Wet

Dry chickpeas better next time, add a little more flour, and chill longer.

Make-Ahead and Leftover Notes

The mixture can be ground and seasoned earlier in the day, then chilled until dinner.

Do not fry too early for the birthday dinner. Falafel is best immediately after frying.

Leftover cooked falafel can be reheated in the air fryer at 350°F until hot and re-crisped.

Final Taste Target

The falafel should be crisp and deeply golden outside, fluffy and herb-green inside, strongly chickpea-forward, warmly spiced with cumin and coriander, gently garlicky, subtly cardamom-warm, bright from herbs, and finished with clean salt.

The lemon tahini should be creamy, pale, pourable, nutty, lemony, and balanced. The zhug-style sauce should be fresh, green, spicy, and bright.
