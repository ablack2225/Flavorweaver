# Butterflake Biscuits

> A preserved family buttermilk yeast biscuit recipe with dough that can be refrigerated and used over several days.

## Measured Ingredients

### Butterflake Biscuits

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 5 cups | Flour | Plus a little extra if needed |
| 1 tsp | Salt |  |
| 1 tsp | Baking soda |  |
| 1 Tbsp | Baking powder |  |
| 1 Tbsp | Sugar |  |
| 1 cup | Margarine | Cut into dry ingredients |
| 2 cups | Buttermilk |  |
| 1 package | Dry yeast |  |
| 2 Tbsp | Very warm water | For yeast |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Mix the yeast with the very warm water and set aside. |
| 2 | Mix all the dry ingredients together. |
| 3 | Cut in the margarine until the mixture feels like coarse meal. |
| 4 | Add yeast to buttermilk, then gradually mix into the flour mixture to make a stiff dough. |
| 5 | Add a little extra flour if necessary to make the dough the right consistency. |
| 6 | Pour the dough onto a floured surface and knead until smooth. |
| 7 | Make out as many biscuits as desired and refrigerate remaining dough to use later. |
| 8 | Let biscuits rise for 30-40 minutes. |
| 9 | Bake at 425°F for 10-15 minutes, until golden brown on top. |
