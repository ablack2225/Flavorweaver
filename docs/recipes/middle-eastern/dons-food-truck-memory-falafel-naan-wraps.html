Don's Food-Truck Memory Falafel Naan Wraps

Hot herb falafel in warm homemade naan with tomato, onion, tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce, fiery red sauce, and optional lemon tahini. Don called the finished wraps spot on.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Falafel

Soaked dried chickpeas are dried, ground twice with herbs and aromatics, seasoned, chilled, shaped, and deep-fried.

White Sauce

Thick, creamy, garlicky, herby, lemony, and cooling.

Red Sauce

Spicy, tangy, vinegary, garlicky, and pourable enough to stripe.

Wrap

Warm naan, tomato, onion, hot falafel, white sauce, red sauce, and optional tahini.

Measured Ingredients

Herb Falafel

1 lb dried chickpeas; cold water to cover by 3 to 4 inches; optional 1/2 tsp baking soda; 1 small onion; 4 scallions; 5 garlic cloves; 1 small carrot; 1 packed cup parsley; 1 packed cup cilantro; 2 Tbsp mint; 2 tsp cumin; 1 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp cardamom; 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 2 tsp kosher salt; 1 Tbsp baking powder; 2 to 3 Tbsp flour; up to 3 Tbsp cold water; neutral oil.

Tzatziki-ish Ranch White Sauce

1/2 cup mayonnaise; 1/2 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt; 1 Tbsp lemon juice; 1 grated garlic clove; 1 tsp dried dill or 1 Tbsp fresh dill; 1/2 tsp onion powder; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper; up to 2 Tbsp milk or water; optional 1 Tbsp squeezed-dry cucumber.

Fiery Red Sauce

4 guajillo chiles; 3 garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp white vinegar; 1 Tbsp lemon juice; 2 Tbsp water; 1 Tbsp vinegar-style hot sauce; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika; 1/2 tsp chipotle powder; 1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper; 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp sugar; 1 Tbsp oil.

Lemon Tahini

1/2 cup tahini; 3 Tbsp lemon juice; 1 grated garlic clove; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/4 cup ice-cold water; optional 1/4 to 1/2 tsp honey and 1 Tbsp olive oil.

Wrap Build

4 to 6 naan; 24 to 30 falafel; 1 to 2 tomatoes; 1 small onion; 1 cup white sauce; 3/4 to 1 cup red sauce; optional 3/4 to 1 cup tahini.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Herb Falafel

Bowl 1 — Chickpeas

1 lb dried chickpeas, soaked 18 to 24 hours, drained, rinsed, and dried.

Bowl 2 — Aromatics & Herbs

1 small onion; 4 scallions; 5 garlic cloves; 1 small carrot; 1 cup parsley; 1 cup cilantro; 2 Tbsp mint.

Bowl 3 — Seasoning

2 tsp cumin; 1 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp cardamom; 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne; 1/2 tsp pepper; 2 tsp salt; 1 Tbsp baking powder; 2 to 3 Tbsp flour.

Bowl 4 — Adjustment

1 to 3 Tbsp cold water, 1 Tbsp at a time only if needed.

White Sauce

Bowl 1 — Base

1/2 cup mayonnaise; 1/2 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt.

Bowl 2 — Flavor

1 Tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove; 1 tsp dried dill or 1 Tbsp fresh dill; 1/2 tsp onion powder; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/2 tsp salt; 1/4 tsp pepper.

Bowl 3 — Texture

Up to 2 Tbsp milk or water; optional 1 Tbsp cucumber.

Fiery Red Sauce

Bowl 1 — Chiles

4 guajillo chiles; very hot water.

Bowl 2 — Sauce Base

3 garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp vinegar; 1 Tbsp lemon juice; 2 Tbsp water; 1 Tbsp hot sauce; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika; 1/2 tsp chipotle powder; 1/2 tsp salt; 1/2 tsp sugar; 1 Tbsp oil.

Bowl 3 — Heat

1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper; 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne; extra water 1 tsp at a time.

Lemon Tahini

Bowl 1 — Base

1/2 cup tahini; 3 Tbsp lemon juice; 1 garlic clove; 1/2 tsp salt; 1/4 tsp cumin.

Bowl 2 — Water

1/4 cup ice-cold water, plus more as needed.

Bowl 3 — Rounding

1/4 to 1/2 tsp honey; 1 Tbsp olive oil, only if needed.

Wrap Build

Bowl 1 — Naan

4 to 6 naan.

Bowl 2 — Fresh Fill

1 to 2 tomatoes; 1 small onion.

Bowl 3 — Falafel

24 to 30 hot falafel.

Bowl 4 — Sauces

1 cup white sauce; 3/4 to 1 cup red sauce; optional 3/4 to 1 cup tahini.

Do Not Forget

Do not cook or use canned chickpeas. Dry them well. Grind twice. Chill before shaping. Fry one falafel before the batch. Keep oil at 350°F. Warm naan immediately before building. Use red sauce as a stripe sauce. White sauce and red sauce are required; tahini is optional.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Soak. Cover 1 lb chickpeas with cold water by 3 to 4 inches. Add optional 1/2 tsp baking soda. Refrigerate 18 to 24 hours.
  2. Dry. Drain, rinse, and dry thoroughly. Rest uncovered 30 to 60 minutes.
  3. Grind. Grind chickpeas with 1 onion, 4 scallions, 5 garlic cloves, 1 carrot, 1 cup parsley, 1 cup cilantro, and 2 Tbsp mint twice.
  4. Season. Mix in 2 tsp cumin, 1 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/4 tsp cardamom, 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne, 1/2 tsp pepper, 2 tsp salt, 1 Tbsp baking powder, and 2 to 3 Tbsp flour. Add up to 3 Tbsp cold water only if needed.
  5. Chill. Refrigerate 45 to 60 minutes.
  6. White sauce. Whisk 1/2 cup mayonnaise and 1/2 cup sour cream or yogurt. Add 1 Tbsp lemon juice, 1 garlic clove, dill, powders, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp pepper. Thin gradually if needed.
  7. Red sauce. Soak 4 guajillo chiles 10 to 15 minutes. Blend with all measured red-sauce ingredients until smooth and pourable.
  8. Tahini. Whisk 1/2 cup tahini, 3 Tbsp lemon juice, 1 garlic clove, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp cumin. Add 1/4 cup ice water gradually.
  9. Shape. Form 24 to 30 falafel, about 1 heaping tablespoon each.
  10. Fry. Fry one falafel at 350°F. Then fry the batch in small groups for 3 to 4 minutes.
  11. Stage. Warm 4 to 6 naan. Set out tomatoes, onion, and sauces.
  12. Build. Spread 2 to 3 Tbsp white sauce on each naan. Add 2 to 3 tomato slices, 2 to 3 Tbsp onion, and 4 to 6 falafel. Stripe with 1 to 2 tsp red sauce, add 1 Tbsp white sauce, fold, and serve.

Final Taste Target

Hot crispy falafel, soft warm naan, cool creamy white sauce, fiery red sauce, juicy tomato, sharp onion, and optional nutty lemon tahini. The red sauce brings heat and tang; the white sauce cools the bite.
