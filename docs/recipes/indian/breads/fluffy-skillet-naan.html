Fluffy Skillet Naan

Amy & Don's fluffy skillet naan: soft, buttery, scoopable flatbread for curries, saucy plates, and any meal that needs warm bread at the table.

Prep20m active + 2h rise
Cook10m
Total2h 30m
Yield6 naan
House stapleIndian breadSkillet naan

Ingredient List

Dough

Yeast, warm water, sugar, room-temperature milk, room-temperature egg, salt, flour, and ghee or butter.

Finish

Melted ghee or butter, optional garlic, optional nigella seeds, and optional cilantro.

Optional Cheese Naan

Shredded melting cheese for a stuffed variation.

Measured Ingredients

Naan Dough

1 tspInstant or rapid-rise yeast
1/2 cupWarm water105-108°F; measure with a kitchen thermometer so the water is warm enough to activate yeast but not hot enough to damage it.
1 TbspWhite sugarHelps bloom yeast.
2 TbspMilkRoom temperature; full-fat preferred, low-fat acceptable.
1 1/2 TbspWhisked eggRoom temperature; equals about 1/2 large egg. Double the amount when making the full/double recipe, which equals 1 whole egg.
1/2 tspSalt
1 3/4 cupsBread flour or all-purpose flourBread flour gives softer/fluffier result; all-purpose works.
2 Tbsp / 30gGhee or unsalted butterMelted.

Finish

2 Tbsp / 30gGhee or butterMelted, for brushing.
OptionalGarlic, nigella seeds, cilantroFor finishing.

Instructions

  1. Measure the warm water with a kitchen thermometer. The target temperature is 105-108°F. Combine yeast, warm water, and sugar. Cover and let stand until foamy, about 10 minutes. Do not use overly hot water, because it can damage or kill the yeast and prevent the dough from rising.
  2. Whisk room-temperature milk and room-temperature egg together. Measure 1 1/2 Tbsp of the whisked egg for this batch. If making the full/double recipe, double the egg amount to 1 whole egg.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine flour and salt.
  4. Add the wet ingredients, yeast mixture, and melted ghee or butter to the flour. Mix until a dough forms.
  5. Knead until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. It should be soft but workable. Add flour only as needed.
  6. Cover and let rise in a warm place until doubled.
  7. Divide into 6 portions, rest briefly, then roll each piece into a thin round or oval.
  8. Heat a cast iron skillet or heavy skillet over high heat until very hot.
  9. Cook one naan at a time until bubbles form and the underside has golden spots. Flip and cook the second side briefly.
  10. Brush hot naan with melted ghee or butter. Add garlic butter, nigella seeds, or cilantro if desired.

Chef's Notes

This recipe belongs under Indian breads. It can still be linked from the Ethiopian-inspired Doro Wat platter as a practical scoop-bread substitute.

For best texture, use room-temperature milk and egg, keep the dough soft, measure the warm water so the yeast activates reliably, and cook the naan in a very hot skillet so it bubbles and chars quickly without drying out.
