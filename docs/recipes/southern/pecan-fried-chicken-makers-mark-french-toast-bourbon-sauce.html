Pecan Fried Chicken with Maker's Mark French Toast Bourbon Sauce

Lightly pecan-breaded fried chicken finished with a warm maple-brown-sugar Maker's Mark bourbon glaze, toasted chopped pecans, Southern cinnamon fried apples, honey-butter sweet potatoes, and savory garlic broccoli.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Pecan Fried Chicken

Thin chicken cutlets are marinated, dredged in a flour-forward pecan breading, and fried crisp without creating a heavy nut crust.

Maker's Mark Sauce

Butter, maple, brown sugar, cinnamon, salt, and wheated bourbon create a warm syrup-style sauce that tastes like French toast and caramelized butter.

Southern Cinnamon Fried Apples

Honeycrisp and Granny Smith apples cook into a glossy cinnamon-sweet side with tender slices that still hold their shape.

Balance Sides

Honey-butter sweet potatoes echo the warm sauce, while savory garlic broccoli keeps the plate balanced.

Flavor Target

This plate should feel like a Southern brunch comfort plate with food-truck special energy.

The chicken should be crisp, savory, lightly pecan-flavored, and flour-forward. The breading should not become a thick pecan crust.

The sauce should taste like French toast syrup met caramelized butter: maple, brown sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, a little salt, and warm Maker's Mark bourbon depth. The sauce should feel like syrup, not gravy.

The sides should balance the plate: soft glossy cinnamon apples, sweet potatoes with honey butter, and savory lemon-garlic broccoli.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

Marinate the chicken at least 30 minutes; overnight is better if timing allows.

Grind the pecans for the breading finely so they do not burn in the fryer.

Toast the chopped pecans for garnish ahead and keep them separate.

Sweet potatoes can be baked ahead and reheated.

Set up a rack over a sheet pan for resting breaded chicken and draining fried chicken.

Full Ingredient Roll-Up

Chicken Marinade

Chicken breasts, buttermilk or soured milk, egg, kosher salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika.

Light Pecan Breading

All-purpose flour, finely ground pecans, cornstarch, kosher salt, black pepper, plain paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, optional cayenne.

Bourbon Sauce

Unsalted butter, maple syrup, brown sugar, Maker's Mark bourbon, cinnamon, kosher salt, vanilla, optional cream or evaporated milk.

Full Plate Sides

Toasted pecans, Honeycrisp and Granny Smith apples, sweet potatoes, honey butter, broccoli, garlic, lemon.

Measured Ingredients

Chicken Marinade

2 large chicken breasts, butterflied into 4 thinner cutlets; 1 cup buttermilk or 1 cup milk plus 1 Tbsp vinegar/lemon juice; 1 egg; 1 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/2 tsp onion powder; 1/4 tsp smoked paprika.

Light Pecan Breading and Frying

1 cup all-purpose flour; 1/3 cup finely ground pecans; 2 Tbsp cornstarch; 1 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1/2 tsp plain/sweet paprika; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/4 tsp onion powder; optional pinch cayenne; neutral oil for frying; kosher salt for finishing.

Maker's Mark French Toast Bourbon Sauce

3 Tbsp unsalted butter; 1/3 cup maple syrup; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 2 to 3 Tbsp Maker's Mark bourbon; 1/4 tsp cinnamon; pinch kosher salt; 1 tsp vanilla; optional 1 to 2 Tbsp cream or evaporated milk.

Toasted Pecan Finish

1/3 to 1/2 cup chopped pecans, toasted and held separate until plating.

Southern Cinnamon Fried Apples

2 Honeycrisp apples; 2 Granny Smith apples; 3 Tbsp butter; 1/3 cup brown sugar; 1/2 tsp cinnamon; pinch kosher salt; 1 tsp lemon juice; 1/4 cup apple juice, cider, or water; optional slurry of 1 tsp cornstarch plus 1 Tbsp water.

Honey-Butter Sweet Potatoes

4 medium sweet potatoes; 4 Tbsp softened butter; 1 1/2 Tbsp honey; pinch cinnamon; pinch kosher salt; optional tiny splash vanilla.

Savory Garlic Broccoli

1 large head broccoli, cut into florets, or frozen broccoli; 1 to 2 Tbsp butter or olive oil; 2 cloves garlic, minced, or 1/2 tsp garlic powder; kosher salt to taste; black pepper to taste; small squeeze lemon juice.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Pecans: finely ground pecans go into the chicken breading; chopped pecans are toasted separately for garnish.

Butter: appears in the bourbon sauce, fried apples, honey butter, and broccoli. Measure each component separately.

Cinnamon: appears in the bourbon sauce, fried apples, and honey butter. Keep the broccoli free of sweet spices.

Salt: every sweet component needs a small amount of salt so the plate tastes balanced instead of sugary.

Bourbon sauce timing: make the sauce close to service so it stays warm and glossy.

Chicken crispness: sauce the chicken at plating, not early.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Chicken and Fry Bowls

Bowl 1 — Chicken Cutlets

2 large chicken breasts, butterflied into 4 thinner cutlets. Pound evenly to about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.

Bowl 2 — Chicken Marinade

1 cup buttermilk, 1 egg, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp onion powder, 1/4 tsp smoked paprika.

Bowl 3 — Light Pecan Breading

1 cup all-purpose flour, 1/3 cup finely ground pecans, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp plain paprika, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp onion powder, optional pinch cayenne.

Bowl 4 — Fry Finish

Kosher salt, to taste. Lightly salt fried chicken while hot.

Sauce and Garnish Bowls

Bowl 5 — Bourbon Sauce Base

3 Tbsp unsalted butter, 1/3 cup maple syrup, 2 Tbsp brown sugar, 2 to 3 Tbsp Maker's Mark bourbon, 1/4 tsp cinnamon, pinch kosher salt.

Bowl 6 — Sauce Finish

1 tsp vanilla; optional 1 to 2 Tbsp cream or evaporated milk. Add vanilla at the end.

Bowl 7 — Toasted Pecan Finish

1/3 to 1/2 cup chopped pecans, toasted separately and held for plating.

Side Bowls

Bowl 8 — Fried Apple Pan Bowl

2 Honeycrisp apples, sliced; 2 Granny Smith apples, sliced; 3 Tbsp butter; 1/3 cup brown sugar; 1/2 tsp cinnamon; pinch kosher salt; 1 tsp lemon juice; 1/4 cup apple juice, cider, or water.

Bowl 9 — Optional Apple Gloss Slurry

1 tsp cornstarch plus 1 Tbsp water. Add only if the apple syrup needs a thicker glossy finish.

Bowl 10 — Honey Butter

4 Tbsp softened butter, 1 1/2 Tbsp honey, pinch cinnamon, pinch kosher salt, optional tiny splash vanilla.

Bowl 11 — Garlic Broccoli Finish

1 to 2 Tbsp butter or olive oil, 2 minced garlic cloves or 1/2 tsp garlic powder, kosher salt to taste, black pepper to taste, small squeeze lemon juice.

Do Not Forget

Do not make a thick pecan crust. The target is a light, flour-forward coating with pecan flavor.

Use finely ground pecans in the breading and chopped toasted pecans only as the final garnish.

Use plain paprika in the breading; the small amount of smoked paprika belongs only in the marinade.

Rest breaded chicken 10 to 15 minutes before frying so the coating sets.

Fry around 350°F. Pecans darken quickly, so do not let the oil run too hot.

Add vanilla to the bourbon sauce at the end, not during the full simmer.

Sauce chicken at plating so the crust stays crisp.

Keep broccoli savory and lemony because it is the balance point of the plate.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Prep the chicken. Butterfly 2 large chicken breasts into 4 thinner cutlets. Pound evenly to about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.
  2. Make the marinade. Whisk 1 cup buttermilk, 1 egg, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp onion powder, and 1/4 tsp smoked paprika. Add chicken and marinate at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight.
  3. Prepare the breading. In a shallow dish, combine 1 cup all-purpose flour, 1/3 cup finely ground pecans, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp plain paprika, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp onion powder, and optional pinch cayenne.
  4. Bread the chicken. Remove chicken from marinade and let excess drip off. Dredge in pecan-flour breading, pressing lightly so the coating sticks. Place on a rack and rest 10 to 15 minutes while oil heats.
  5. Toast garnish pecans. Toast 1/3 to 1/2 cup chopped pecans in a dry skillet over medium-low heat until fragrant. Reserve for plating.
  6. Start sweet potatoes. Bake, roast, or microwave 4 medium sweet potatoes until tender. Hold warm.
  7. Make honey butter. Mash 4 Tbsp softened butter, 1 1/2 Tbsp honey, pinch cinnamon, pinch kosher salt, and optional tiny splash vanilla. Hold at room temperature.
  8. Cook the fried apples. In a skillet, combine 2 sliced Honeycrisp apples, 2 sliced Granny Smith apples, 3 Tbsp butter, 1/3 cup brown sugar, 1/2 tsp cinnamon, pinch kosher salt, 1 tsp lemon juice, and 1/4 cup apple juice, cider, or water. Cook covered over medium-low heat until apples soften but still hold their shape. Uncover and simmer until glossy. If needed, stir in slurry made from 1 tsp cornstarch and 1 Tbsp water.
  9. Fry the chicken. Heat oil to 350°F. Fry chicken in batches until golden, crisp, and cooked through. Target internal temperature is 165°F. Drain on a rack and lightly salt while hot.
  10. Cook the broccoli. Steam, sauté, roast, or air-fry 1 large head broccoli until tender-crisp. Toss with 1 to 2 Tbsp butter or olive oil, 2 minced garlic cloves or 1/2 tsp garlic powder, kosher salt, black pepper, and a small squeeze of lemon juice.
  11. Build the bourbon sauce. In a small saucepan, melt 3 Tbsp unsalted butter over medium-low heat. Add 1/3 cup maple syrup, 2 Tbsp brown sugar, 2 to 3 Tbsp Maker's Mark bourbon, 1/4 tsp cinnamon, and pinch kosher salt. Simmer gently 3 to 5 minutes, stirring often, until glossy and slightly thickened. Do not boil hard.
  12. Finish the sauce. Turn heat low or off. Stir in 1 tsp vanilla. Add 1 to 2 Tbsp cream or evaporated milk only if the sauce tastes too intense or needs softening.
  13. Plate. Place fried chicken on plates. Spoon warm bourbon sauce over the top. Sprinkle generously with toasted chopped pecans. Add fried apples, sweet potato with honey butter, and savory garlic broccoli. Serve immediately.

Bourbon Sauce Adjustment Map

Sauce IssueAdjustment
Too sweetAdd a tiny pinch of kosher salt.
Too sharp or boozyAdd a tiny splash of maple syrup or 1 Tbsp cream/evaporated milk.
Too flatAdd a little more cinnamon or a tiny pinch of salt.
Too thickLoosen with a tiny splash of water, cream, or maple syrup.
Too thinSimmer gently 1 to 2 minutes longer.

Final Texture and Plating Targets

The fried apples should be soft, glossy, cinnamon-sweet, and syrupy, with tender slices that still hold their shape. They should not become applesauce.

Place the chicken slightly off-center so the sauce and pecans are visible. Spoon bourbon sauce over the chicken, not underneath it. Sprinkle toasted pecans over the sauced chicken at the very end.

Place fried apples beside the chicken so the apple syrup can mingle slightly with the bourbon sauce. Split sweet potato and top with honey butter. Place broccoli on the opposite side for color and savory balance.

Live-Test Notes to Capture

Did the pecan breading stay light, or did it get too dark?

Was 1/3 cup finely ground pecans enough pecan flavor in the breading?

Did the sauce land better with 2 Tbsp or 3 Tbsp Maker's Mark?

Was the sauce better clear and glossy, or did it need cream/evaporated milk?

Were Honeycrisp and Granny Smith the right apple blend?

Did the sweet potato make the plate too sweet, or did the savory broccoli balance it?
