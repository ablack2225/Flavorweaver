# Harvard Beets

> A preserved family side dish made with sliced beets in a sweet-and-sour beet juice and vinegar sauce thickened with cornstarch.

## Measured Ingredients

### Harvard Beets

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 (16 oz) can | Sliced beets | Drain juice into measuring cup |
| 1 Tbsp | Cornstarch |  |
| 3 Tbsp | Sugar |  |
| 2/3 cup | Drained beet juice | Add water as needed to make 2/3 cup total liquid |
| Small amount | Water | Add to beet juice if needed |
| 1/4 cup | Vinegar |  |
| Dash | Black pepper |  |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Drain juice from beets into a measuring cup. |
| 2 | Add enough water to beet juice to make 2/3 cup total liquid. |
| 3 | In a small saucepan, stir together cornstarch, sugar, and black pepper. |
| 4 | Gradually stir in beet juice mixture and vinegar. |
| 5 | Cook, stirring constantly, until mixture thickens and boils. |
| 6 | Boil and stir for 1 minute. |
| 7 | Stir in beets and heat through. |
