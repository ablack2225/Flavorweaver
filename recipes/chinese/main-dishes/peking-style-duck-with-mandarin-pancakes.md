# 48-Hour Peking-Style Duck with Mandarin Pancakes

> A home-kitchen, restaurant-aimed Peking-style duck project built around 48 hours of uncovered drying, boiling-water skin tightening, thin lacquer layers, careful roasting, soft Mandarin-style pancakes, hoisin-plum sauce, cucumber, and scallions. The goal is glossy mahogany skin, deeply rendered fat, juicy duck meat, and the full pancake-wrapped service Don asked for.

## Advance Prep / 48-Hour Drying Plan

### Day 1 — Duck Prep, Scald, and First Glaze

1. Remove giblets and neck from the duck. Pat the duck very dry inside and out.
2. Trim only the large loose fat flaps at the cavity opening. Save those pieces for rendering. Do not trim away breast or leg skin.
3. Gently loosen the skin over the breast and thighs with your fingers or the handle of a wooden spoon. Do not tear the skin.
4. Prick the skin all over with a toothpick or skewer, especially the breast, thighs, and back. Pierce only the skin and fat layer, not deep into the meat.
5. Mix the cavity seasoning: **1 1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1 tsp five-spice**, and **1/2 tsp pepper**. Rub inside the cavity.
6. Add ginger, garlic, scallions, and optional star anise or orange peel to the cavity.
7. Place the duck on a rack in the sink or over a sheet pan. Slowly pour boiling water over the entire skin surface. The skin should tighten.
8. Pat the duck very dry.
9. Warm the glaze ingredients just enough to loosen the honey. Brush the duck with a very thin layer of glaze.
10. Set the duck breast-side up on a rack over a sheet pan. Refrigerate uncovered.

### Day 2 — Continue Drying

1. Keep the duck uncovered on the rack.
2. After about 24 hours of drying, brush on another very thin glaze layer if the skin feels dry or tacky.
3. If the skin still feels damp, do not glaze again yet. Airflow matters more than extra glaze.
4. Around 36 to 40 hours, brush one final whisper-thin layer only if the skin is dry enough to absorb it.
5. By roasting day, the skin should feel tight, dry, and almost parchment-like.

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

### Duck Roasting Day Prep

- **Duck:** fully dried duck, uncovered.
- **Glaze:** remaining lacquer glaze, warmed gently.
- **Roasting pan:** rack over pan with about 1 cup water in the bottom to reduce smoking.
- **Fat bowl:** heat-safe bowl or cup for spooning off rendered duck fat.

### Pancake Station

- **Dough bowl:** flour, salt, boiling water, and cool water as needed.
- **Rolling station:** flour-dusted board, rolling pin, oil for brushing paired pancakes.
- **Holding setup:** clean towel or foil packet to keep cooked pancakes soft.

### Service Station

- **Sauce:** hoisin-plum sauce warmed and loosened.
- **Garnishes:** cucumber matchsticks and scallion slivers.
- **Duck platter:** carved crispy skin and meat.
- **Pancakes:** warm and covered.

## Instructions

### Roast the Duck

1. **Temper the duck.** Remove the duck from the refrigerator **45 to 60 minutes** before roasting. Keep it uncovered. Do not wet the skin.
2. **Preheat and set pan.** Preheat oven to **425°F**. Place duck breast-side up on a rack over a roasting pan. Add about **1 cup water** to the pan bottom.
3. **Set the skin.** Roast at **425°F for 20 minutes**.
4. **Render gently.** Lower oven to **325°F** and roast **60 to 75 minutes**, depending on duck size. Spoon off excess rendered fat from the pan every 25 to 30 minutes.
5. **Optional thin glaze.** If the skin looks dry and not wet, brush on one very thin layer of glaze during the render stage. Do not make the surface sticky-heavy.
6. **Final crisp.** Raise oven to **450°F** and roast **8 to 12 minutes**, watching constantly. Pull before the sugars burn.
7. **Rest.** Rest **15 to 20 minutes** before carving. Do not tent tightly with foil or the skin will steam.
8. **Carve for pancake service.** Slice crispy skin and meat into pieces that fit inside pancakes. Keep skin-facing pieces visible on the platter.

### Make the Mandarin-Style Pancakes

1. **Make hot-water dough.** Add **2 cups flour** and **1/4 tsp salt** to a bowl. Stir in **3/4 cup boiling water** with chopsticks, a fork, or a wooden spoon.
2. **Adjust texture.** Add cool water **1 Tbsp at a time** only until a soft dough forms. Do not make it wet.
3. **Knead.** Knead **5 to 8 minutes** until smooth.
4. **Rest.** Cover and rest **30 to 60 minutes**.
5. **Divide.** Roll dough into a log and divide into **20 small pieces** for smaller restaurant-style pancakes, or **16 pieces** for slightly larger pancakes.
6. **Roll in pairs.** Flatten two dough balls. Brush one lightly with oil, place the second on top, and roll the pair into a very thin circle, about **5 to 6 inches wide**.
7. **Cook dry.** Heat a dry skillet over medium heat. Cook each paired pancake **45 to 60 seconds per side**, just until pale golden spots appear. Do not crisp.
8. **Peel.** While warm, carefully peel the pair apart into two thin pancakes.
9. **Hold soft.** Stack under a towel or in foil. Rewarm by steaming briefly or microwaving covered with a damp towel for 15 to 25 seconds.

### Make the Sauce

1. Combine **1/3 cup hoisin**, **1 Tbsp honey or brown sugar**, **1 Tbsp plum/apricot jam or marmalade**, **1 tsp soy sauce**, **1 tsp rice vinegar**, **1/2 tsp sesame oil**, and a pinch of five-spice.
2. Warm gently until glossy.
3. Add a splash of warm water only if needed for a spreadable sauce.

### Serve

1. Set out warm pancakes, sauce, cucumber matchsticks, scallion slivers, and carved duck.
2. Build each bite with **pancake + sauce + crispy duck skin/meat + cucumber + scallion**.
3. Serve immediately while the duck skin is crisp and the pancakes are soft.

## Troubleshooting / Rescue Notes

| Issue | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Skin is rubbery | Duck was not dry enough or rested under tight foil | Next time dry longer, keep uncovered, and avoid tight tenting |
| Skin browns too fast | Glaze too thick or oven too hot | Lower heat and use thinner glaze layers |
| Pan smokes heavily | Too much fat collecting in the pan | Spoon off rendered fat during roasting; keep water in pan bottom |
| Pancakes are stiff | Overcooked or left uncovered | Keep covered; re-steam or microwave with damp towel |
| Pancakes stick in pairs | Not enough oil between dough rounds | Brush a very thin but complete oil layer |

## Chef's Notes

This is not a shortcut roast duck. The 48-hour dry is the recipe's spine. The scald tightens the skin, the uncovered rest dries the surface, and the thin glaze layers build shine without trapping moisture. The pancakes make the meal feel complete, and they are worth treating as a required component rather than an optional side.

## Final Verdict

This is a Flavorweaver project recipe with a high ceiling. It should be tested with a full 48-hour dry before being judged. If the skin crisps properly and the pancakes stay soft, this has clear Don-request / special-occasion potential.
