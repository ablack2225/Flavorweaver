# 🧈 Fats, Oils & Cooking Liquids Inventory

> This file tracks fats, oils, cooking liquids, acids, alcohols, smoke/umami liquids, broths, and liquid flavor builders commonly kept in the Amy & Don Kitchen.
>
> Chef Echo should use this as a planning reference when building sauces, marinades, soups, stews, braises, dressings, breads, sautés, and pan sauces.

---

# Pantry Status

Amy keeps a broad inventory of fats, oils, cooking liquids, acids, and liquid flavor builders.

Chef Echo may assume a well-stocked cooking-liquid pantry unless Amy says otherwise.

---

# Core Fats

Usually kept:

- Unsalted butter
- Olive oil
- Neutral oil
- Sesame oil
- Bacon fat, when available
- Beef tallow, when available

## Butter Standard

Unsalted butter is the house default.

Use unsalted butter for better salt control.

Butter is acceptable for Amy.

Do not substitute salted butter unless Amy specifically requests it.

---

# Cooking Wines & Alcohol-Based Cooking Liquids

Usually kept:

- White cooking wine
- Red cooking wine
- Sherry

Useful for:

- Deglazing
- Pan sauces
- Soups
- Stews
- Braises
- French onion soup
- Beef dishes
- Chicken dishes
- Onion-based sauces

## Cooking Wine Note

Cooking wines can be salty.

When using cooking wine, reduce added salt until the dish is tasted near the end.

Sherry is especially useful for:

- French onion soup
- Mushroom sauces
- Beef stews
- Pan sauces
- Onion-heavy dishes

---

# Vinegars & Acids

Usually kept:

- Apple cider vinegar
- White vinegar
- Rice vinegar
- Distilled vinegar
- Pickle brine
- Lemon juice
- Lime juice

Do not assume available unless confirmed:

- Red wine vinegar
- White wine vinegar
- Sherry vinegar

## Acid Planning Note

Use acids to balance richness, fat, sweetness, and heavy sauces.

Default substitutions:

- Apple cider vinegar for warm fruity acidity
- Rice vinegar for softer acidity
- White vinegar for sharper acidity
- Lemon or lime juice for fresh brightness
- Pickle brine when the dish can handle salty-sour flavor

# Umami & Smoke Liquids

Usually kept:

- Worcestershire sauce
- Liquid smoke
- Soy sauce
- Dark soy sauce
- Fish sauce
- Red Boat fish sauce
- Coconut aminos
- Oyster sauce
- Hoisin sauce

## Umami Planning Note

Use these intentionally.

Worcestershire is useful for:

- Beef dishes
- Gravies
- Hamburger steak
- French onion soup
- Stews
- Marinades
- Steakhouse-style sauces

Liquid smoke is powerful.

Use sparingly, usually by drops or small measured amounts.

Red Boat fish sauce is strong and should be used carefully.

# Sauce-Building Rule

Chef Echo should assume Amy has a strong liquid flavor pantry.

When building sauces, marinades, soups, stews, or braises, consider:

- Fat for blooming spices
- Acid for balance
- Broth for body
- Wine or sherry for depth
- Worcestershire for beefy umami
- Fish sauce or soy for savory depth
- Liquid smoke only when smoky flavor is intentional
- Fruit juice or syrup for sweet-savory balance

---

# Salt Control Rule

Many liquid ingredients contain salt.

Use caution with:

- Cooking wine
- Soy sauce
- Fish sauce
- Oyster sauce
- Hoisin
- Bouillon
- Better Than Bouillon
- Worcestershire
- Pickle brine
- Cajun-style seasoning blends

Default approach:

- Use unsalted butter.
- Salt separately.
- Taste before final seasoning.
