# Peanut Brittle

> A preserved family peanut brittle cooked to candy temperature with raw shelled peanuts, light corn syrup, baking soda, vanilla, and margarine.

## Measured Ingredients

### Peanut Brittle

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 2 cups | Sugar |  |
| 1 cup | Light Karo syrup | Light corn syrup |
| 1/2 cup | Water |  |
| 1/8 tsp | Salt | Add after removing from heat |
| 2 cups | Raw shelled peanuts |  |
| 2 tsp | Baking soda |  |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla |  |
| 1 Tbsp | Margarine |  |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | In a large saucepan, combine sugar, light corn syrup, and water. |
| 2 | Cook over medium-high heat, covered, for about 3 minutes. |
| 3 | Uncover and use a candy thermometer to cook the mixture until it reaches 234°F. |
| 4 | Add peanuts and stir frequently. |
| 5 | Continue cooking until the mixture reaches 300°F. |
| 6 | Remove from heat and add salt, baking soda, vanilla, and margarine. |
| 7 | Blend well. |
| 8 | Pour mixture onto lightly buttered cookie sheets to cool. |
| 9 | When brittle is cooled, break into large pieces. |
| 10 | Store in an airtight container. |
