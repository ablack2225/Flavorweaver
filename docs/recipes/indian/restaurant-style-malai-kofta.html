Restaurant-Style Malai Kofta

Crisp paneer-potato koftas served in a silky onion-tomato-cashew malai gravy finished with butter, cream, kasuri methi, and fresh coriander. This version is organized for actual kitchen flow: gravy first, kofta mix while the gravy simmers, test-fry before committing, and assemble only when ready to serve.

Measured Ingredients

Gravy Saute Base

1 1/2 Tbsp neutral oil or ghee; 2 to 3 green cardamoms; 1 small bay leaf; 1/2 inch cinnamon; 2 cloves; 3/4 tsp cumin seeds or shahi jeera; 1 1/2 cups sliced onion; 1 inch ginger or 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste; 5 to 6 large garlic cloves; 2 1/4 cups ripe red tomatoes; 18 raw cashews; 3/4 tsp sea salt.

Gravy Spice Bloom

3/4 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder; 3/4 to 1 tsp garam masala; 1 tsp coriander powder.

Gravy Blend and Finish

1 cup cold water; up to 1/2 cup water for final simmer; 2 Tbsp unsalted butter; 1 Tbsp jaggery powder or sugar; 1 1/2 tsp kasuri methi; 1/4 cup heavy cream; optional 1 Tbsp coriander leaves.

Paneer Kofta Base

1 medium boiled potato, or 1/2 cup finely grated boiled potato, cooled completely; 1 1/2 cups finely grated paneer, patted dry if needed; 2 1/2 Tbsp cornstarch; 1/2 tsp sea salt; 1/2 to 3/4 tsp garam masala or 1/4 tsp cardamom powder.

Kofta Flavor Mix

1 Tbsp chopped cashews; 1 Tbsp chopped raisins; 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste; 1 to 2 deseeded green chilies or 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 Tbsp coriander leaves.

Fry and Assemble

2 cups oil for deep frying; warm malai gravy; cream and coriander leaves for garnish.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Gravy Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Whole Spice Bloom

2 to 3 green cardamoms; 1 small bay leaf; 1/2 inch cinnamon; 2 cloves; 3/4 tsp cumin seeds or shahi jeera. Discard bay leaf before blending.

Bowl 2 — Onion Base

1 1/2 cups sliced onion. Saute until light golden.

Bowl 3 — Ginger Garlic

1 inch sliced ginger and 5 to 6 garlic cloves, or 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste.

Bowl 4 — Tomato Cashew Base

2 1/4 cups tomatoes; 18 raw cashews; 3/4 tsp sea salt. Cook until tomatoes are soft and mushy.

Bowl 5 — Gravy Spices

3/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder; 3/4 to 1 tsp garam masala; 1 tsp coriander powder.

Bowl 6 — Blend Water

1 cup cold water. Helps cool and blend the gravy base smooth.

Bowl 7 — Gravy Finish

2 Tbsp butter; 1 Tbsp jaggery or sugar; 1 1/2 tsp kasuri methi; 1/4 cup cream; coriander leaves.

Kofta Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Dry Paneer and Potato

1 medium boiled potato, cooled and finely grated, or 1/2 cup finely grated boiled potato; 1 1/2 cups finely grated paneer. Cool potato completely and pat paneer dry if moist.

Bowl 2 — Kofta Seasoning

2 1/2 Tbsp cornstarch; 1/2 tsp sea salt; 1/2 to 3/4 tsp garam masala or 1/4 tsp cardamom powder.

Bowl 3 — Kofta Flavor Add-Ins

1 Tbsp chopped cashews; 1 Tbsp chopped raisins; 1 tsp ginger; 1 to 2 green chilies or 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 Tbsp coriander leaves.

Bowl 4 — Test Fry Station

2 cups oil and one small test kofta. Test before frying the whole batch.

Bowl 5 — Finished Kofta Hold

Cooling rack or steel colander. Keep koftas crisp and lifted after frying.

Do Not Forget

Make the gravy base first so it can simmer and settle while the koftas are shaped.

Discard the bay leaf before blending the gravy.

Blend the gravy very smooth, then strain it back into the pan for restaurant-style texture.

Use 1 medium boiled potato, or measure 1/2 cup finely grated boiled potato after cooling.

Use 1 1/2 cups finely grated paneer and pat it dry if it feels moist.

Cool potato completely before mixing koftas.

Test-fry one kofta before frying the full batch.

Do not stir or disturb koftas for the first 2 minutes of frying.

Add koftas to the gravy only at serving time so they stay intact.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Start the gravy base. Heat 1 1/2 Tbsp oil or ghee. Fry cardamoms, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and cumin seeds for about 1 minute.
  2. Saute onion. Add 1 1/2 cups sliced onion and saute 6 to 8 minutes, until light golden.
  3. Add ginger and garlic. Stir in ginger and garlic, or 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste. Saute 1 to 2 minutes.
  4. Cook tomato-cashew base. Add 2 1/4 cups tomatoes, 18 raw cashews, and 3/4 tsp sea salt. Cook until mushy, about 8 minutes.
  5. Bloom the ground spices. Stir in 3/4 tsp Kashmiri chili powder, 3/4 to 1 tsp garam masala, and 1 tsp coriander powder. Saute about 3 minutes.
  6. Cool and blend. Add 1 cup cold water to the blender. Discard bay leaf, transfer the gravy base, and blend until very smooth.
  7. Strain and simmer. Melt 2 Tbsp butter in the pan. Strain blended gravy into the pan. Add up to 1/2 cup water only as needed. Simmer about 10 minutes.
  8. Finish the gravy. Add crushed 1 1/2 tsp kasuri methi, 1 Tbsp jaggery or sugar, and 1/4 cup cream. Cover and keep warm.
  9. Prepare potato and paneer. Boil 1 medium potato until fork tender, cool completely, and finely grate. You want about 1/2 cup finely grated boiled potato. Pat 1 1/2 cups finely grated paneer dry if it feels moist.
  10. Mix kofta dough. Combine potato, paneer, 2 1/2 Tbsp cornstarch, salt, garam masala, cashews, raisins, ginger, chilies or black pepper, and coriander. Mix 2 to 3 minutes into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
  11. Shape koftas. Divide into 12 to 13 portions. Roll smooth balls. Dust lightly with flour or cornstarch only if sticky.
  12. Test-fry one kofta. Heat 2 cups oil. Fry one small test kofta. If it breaks, add a little more flour or cornstarch and test again.
  13. Fry the koftas. Slide koftas gently into hot oil. Do not disturb for the first 2 minutes. Fry over medium flame until crisp and golden.
  14. Assemble only when serving. Spoon warm gravy into the serving dish and place koftas gently into the gravy. Garnish with cream and coriander leaves.

Building the Final Plate

1. Warm Gravy Base

Spoon warm, silky malai gravy into the serving dish first. The gravy should be thick enough to hold the koftas but loose enough to pool.

2. Kofta Placement

Place fried koftas gently into the warm gravy right before serving. Do not boil koftas in the gravy.

3. Cream Finish

Drizzle a little cream over the top for the restaurant-style finish.

4. Herb Finish

Add chopped coriander leaves if desired.

5. Bread or Rice

Serve with Fluffy Skillet Naan, plain basmati rice, jeera rice, tandoori roti, or kulcha.

Final Taste Target

The finished malai kofta should be rich, creamy, gently sweet, warmly spiced, and silky smooth.

The koftas should be crisp outside, soft and paneer-rich inside, and tender enough to cut with a spoon without falling apart in the gravy.
