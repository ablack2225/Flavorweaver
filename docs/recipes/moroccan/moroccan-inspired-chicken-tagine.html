Moroccan-Inspired Chicken Tagine

Warm-spiced Moroccan-inspired chicken tagine with jammy onion, preserved lemon, green olives, dried apricots, golden cashew-herb couscous, toasted cumin cashews, and a fresh herb finish.

Meal TypeMain Dish
Total1 hr 50 min
Serves4 portions

Kitchen Execution Summary

Spiced Chicken Thighs

Direct seasoning keeps the chicken flavorful, not just the sauce.

Jammy Onion Base

Slow-cooked onions create sweetness, body, and sauce depth.

Preserved Lemon

Minced preserved lemon rind gives salty citrus depth and Moroccan-style brightness.

Apricot + Olive Balance

Dried apricots bring sweet-tart roundness while green olives add controlled briny contrast.

Golden Couscous + Cashews

Brothy couscous catches sauce; toasted cumin cashews finish with savory crunch.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Spiced Chicken Rub

4 chicken thighs, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, tiny pinch cinnamon, 1 tsp olive oil.

Tagine Sauce

1 very large or 2 medium onions, 4 garlic cloves, 1 Tbsp fresh ginger, 1 Tbsp tomato paste, 1 cup chicken broth, 1/2 cup dried apricots, 1/3 cup green olives, 1 preserved lemon quarter, optional 1 tsp honey or orange marmalade, optional 1 tsp butter.

Couscous and Cashews

1 cup couscous, 1 cup chicken broth, 1 Tbsp butter or olive oil, 1/4 tsp turmeric, tiny pinch cumin, 2 Tbsp parsley, 1 Tbsp cilantro, 1/3 cup cashews.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Chicken Rub

4 chicken thighs, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, tiny pinch cinnamon, 1 tsp olive oil.

Bowl 2 — Jammy Onion

1 very large or 2 medium yellow onions, thinly sliced, plus tiny pinch salt for the skillet.

Bowl 3 — Aromatics

4 garlic cloves, minced and 1 Tbsp fresh ginger, grated.

Bowl 4 — Spice Bloom

1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, 1/8 tsp cinnamon, pinch to 1/4 tsp cayenne or red pepper flakes.

Bowl 5 — Tomato Paste

1 Tbsp tomato paste.

Bowl 6 — Apricots + Broth

1/2 cup dried apricots, sliced plus 1/2 cup hot chicken broth.

Bowl 7 — Sauce Finish

Remaining 1/2 cup chicken broth, 1 preserved lemon quarter rind, minced, with a small pinch reserved for garnish.

Bowl 8 — Late Olives

1/3 cup green olives, halved.

Bowl 9 — Couscous

1 cup couscous, 1 cup chicken broth, 1 Tbsp butter or olive oil, 1/4 tsp turmeric, tiny pinch cumin, 2 Tbsp parsley, 1 Tbsp cilantro.

Bowl 10 — Cashews

1/3 cup cashews, roughly chopped, tiny pinch salt, tiny pinch cumin.

Bowl 11 — Final Correction

Optional 1 tsp preserved lemon or olive brine, optional 1 tsp honey or orange marmalade, optional 1 tsp butter, fresh lemon juice to taste.

Do Not Forget

Use mostly preserved lemon rind, not a large amount of pulp.

Cook onions 10–12 minutes until collapsed, glossy, lightly golden, and sweet-smelling.

Add olives only in the final 10–15 minutes of braising.

Use honey or orange marmalade only as a final correction, not a main sweetener.

Keep cashews out of the braise; toast and add at plating.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Rub the chicken. Combine 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, tiny pinch cinnamon, and 1 tsp olive oil. Rub over 4 chicken thighs. Rest 20–30 minutes.
  2. Soften apricots. Warm 1/2 cup chicken broth and pour it over 1/2 cup sliced dried apricots. Let sit 10 minutes. Save both apricots and broth.
  3. Prep preserved lemon. Scrape pulp from 1 preserved lemon quarter. Mince the rind. Reserve a small pinch for garnish and use the rest in sauce.
  4. Brown chicken. Heat a large electric skillet around 300°F with a thin layer of oil. Brown the 4 rubbed chicken thighs on both sides until golden but not cooked through. Remove to a plate.
  5. Build jammy onion. Add 1 very large or 2 medium thin-sliced onions with a tiny pinch salt. Cook 10–12 minutes, lowering toward 275°F if needed.
  6. Add aromatics. Stir in 4 minced garlic cloves and 1 Tbsp fresh grated ginger. Cook 45–60 seconds.
  7. Bloom spices and tomato paste. Add 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, 1/8 tsp cinnamon, and pinch to 1/4 tsp cayenne or red pepper flakes. Cook 30 seconds. Add 1 Tbsp tomato paste and cook 60–90 seconds.
  8. Build sauce. Add the softened 1/2 cup apricots with their 1/2 cup soaking broth, plus the remaining 1/2 cup chicken broth. Scrape the skillet and stir in minced rind from 1 preserved lemon quarter.
  9. Braise chicken. Return chicken and juices. Cover and simmer gently until cooked through and tender, about 25–35 minutes.
  10. Add olives late. Add 1/3 cup halved green olives during the final 10–15 minutes.
  11. Reduce sauce. Uncover and simmer 3–5 minutes if sauce is too thin.
  12. Make couscous. Bring 1 cup chicken broth with 1 Tbsp butter or olive oil, 1/4 tsp turmeric, and tiny pinch cumin to a boil. Pour over 1 cup couscous, cover 5 minutes, then fluff. Fold in 2 Tbsp parsley, 1 Tbsp cilantro, and up to 2 Tbsp toasted cashews.
  13. Toast cashews. Toast 1/3 cup roughly chopped cashews in a dry pan 2–4 minutes. Season with tiny pinch salt and tiny pinch cumin.
  14. Correct sauce. Add 1 tsp brine if it needs briny pop; add 1 tsp honey or orange marmalade if it needs roundness; add fresh lemon juice if it needs lift; add a splash of broth if too intense. Stir in 1 tsp butter off heat for gloss if desired.
  15. Plate. Spoon couscous into bowls. Top with chicken and sauce. Finish with toasted cashews, 2 Tbsp parsley, 1 Tbsp cilantro, optional tiny mint, and reserved preserved lemon rind.

Final Taste Target

The finished plate should be savory first, warm-spiced second, then lemon-bright, apricot-rounded, and olive-briny. The chicken should be juicy and seasoned all the way through. The onions should disappear into the sauce body. The couscous should be plush and sauce-catching. The cashews should bring buttery crunch without softening in the braise.
