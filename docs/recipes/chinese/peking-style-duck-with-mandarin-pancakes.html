48-Hour Peking-Style Duck with Mandarin Pancakes

A home-kitchen, restaurant-aimed Peking-style duck project built around 48 hours of uncovered drying, boiling-water skin tightening, thin lacquer layers, careful roasting, soft Mandarin-style pancakes, hoisin-plum sauce, cucumber, and scallions.

Kitchen Execution Summary

48-Hour Dry

The uncovered refrigerator dry is the spine of the recipe. The skin should feel tight, dry, and almost parchment-like before roasting.

Scalded + Lacquered Skin

Boiling water tightens the skin; thin glaze layers build mahogany color without turning sticky or wet.

Mandarin Pancakes

Soft hot-water pancakes are required for Don's requested service. They should stay flexible, not crisp.

Restaurant-Style Bite

Warm pancake, sauce, crispy duck skin and meat, cucumber, and scallion create the full sweet-savory-fatty-fresh balance.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

Day 1: Trim only the loose cavity fat, loosen the skin, prick the skin, season the cavity, scald with boiling water, dry thoroughly, brush with a thin glaze layer, and refrigerate uncovered on a rack.

Day 2: Continue drying uncovered. Brush another whisper-thin glaze layer only if the skin already feels dry and tacky.

Day 3: Roast the duck, make the pancakes, warm the sauce, cut the cucumber and scallions, carve, and serve immediately.

Critical rule: Wet skin will not crisp properly. Do not cover the duck during the drying window.

Service Countdown

60 minutes before roasting: pull the duck from the refrigerator and leave uncovered.

Roasting window: start at 425°F for 20 minutes, lower to 325°F to render, then finish at 450°F for 8 to 12 minutes while watching closely.

While duck roasts: make and rest the pancake dough; prep cucumber, scallions, and sauce.

While duck rests: cook and peel the paired pancakes, keep them covered, and carve the duck just before serving.

Full Meal Ingredient Roll-Up

Duck

Whole duck, kosher salt, five-spice, pepper, ginger, garlic, scallions, optional star anise, and optional orange peel.

Lacquer Glaze

Honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, Shaoxing wine or water, hoisin, five-spice, and optional maltose.

Pancakes

All-purpose flour, boiling water, cool water, salt, and oil for rolling paired pancakes.

Sauce and Garnish

Hoisin, honey or brown sugar, fruit jam, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, cucumber, and scallions.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Duck and Cavity Seasoning

1 whole duck, 5 to 6 lb; 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1 tsp Chinese five-spice; 1/2 tsp white or black pepper; 3 slices ginger; 2 smashed garlic cloves; 2 scallions; optional 1 star anise; optional orange peel strip.

Lacquer Glaze

3 Tbsp honey, or 2 Tbsp honey plus 1 Tbsp maltose; 2 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 Tbsp rice vinegar; 1 Tbsp Shaoxing wine, dry sherry, or water; 1 tsp hoisin; 1/2 tsp Chinese five-spice.

Mandarin-Style Pancakes

2 cups all-purpose flour; 3/4 cup boiling water; up to 1/4 cup cool water, as needed; 1/4 tsp fine salt; 2 Tbsp neutral oil or sesame oil for rolling paired pancakes.

Hoisin-Plum Duck Sauce

1/3 cup hoisin; 1 Tbsp honey or brown sugar; 1 Tbsp plum jam, apricot jam, or orange marmalade; 1 tsp soy sauce; 1 tsp rice vinegar; 1/2 tsp sesame oil; pinch of five-spice; optional warm water to loosen.

Garnishes

1 cucumber, cut into thin matchsticks; 4 to 6 scallions, cut into long slivers; optional radish matchsticks; optional cilantro.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Duck fat: trim large loose fat from the cavity and save it for rendering. Spoon off rendered fat during roasting and strain it for later cooking.

Glaze discipline: every glaze layer should be thin. Thick glaze makes the skin sticky and increases burn risk.

Pancake timing: the dough can be made during roasting and cooked during the duck's resting window.

Service flow: carve duck last, after sauce, pancakes, cucumber, and scallions are ready.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Duck Bowl

Fully dried duck, remaining thin glaze, roasting rack, pan with water, and heat-safe fat bowl.

Pancake Station

Hot-water dough, floured rolling board, rolling pin, oil, dry skillet, and towel or foil for holding.

Sauce Bowl

Hoisin, sweetener, fruit jam, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, five-spice, and warm water if needed.

Garnish Tray

Cucumber matchsticks, scallion slivers, optional radish, optional cilantro, and serving platter.

Do Not Forget

Dry the duck uncovered for the full 48 hours if possible.

Prick the skin only through the skin and fat, not deep into the meat.

Keep glaze layers thin enough to dry.

Do not tent the rested duck tightly with foil.

Cook pancakes pale and soft, not browned and crisp.

Watch constantly during the final 450°F crisp because honey can burn fast.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Prep the duck on Day 1. Remove giblets, dry thoroughly, trim large loose cavity fat, loosen skin over breast and thighs, prick skin all over, season the cavity, add aromatics, scald with boiling water, pat dry, brush with a thin glaze layer, and refrigerate uncovered on a rack.
  2. Continue drying on Day 2. Keep uncovered. Brush another whisper-thin glaze layer only when the skin feels dry or tacky. If it feels damp, wait and prioritize airflow.
  3. Temper before roasting. On Day 3, remove duck from the refrigerator 45 to 60 minutes before roasting. Keep uncovered and dry.
  4. Start hot. Preheat oven to 425°F. Place duck breast-side up on a rack over a roasting pan with about 1 cup water in the pan bottom. Roast 20 minutes.
  5. Render gently. Lower oven to 325°F and roast 60 to 75 minutes. Spoon off rendered fat every 25 to 30 minutes.
  6. Final crisp. Raise oven to 450°F for 8 to 12 minutes, watching closely. Pull when the skin is glossy deep mahogany, before sugars burn.
  7. Rest properly. Rest 15 to 20 minutes. Do not tent tightly.
  8. Make pancake dough. Stir 2 cups flour and 1/4 tsp salt with 3/4 cup boiling water. Add cool water 1 Tbsp at a time only as needed. Knead 5 to 8 minutes and rest 30 to 60 minutes.
  9. Roll and cook pancakes. Divide into 16 to 20 pieces. Roll in oiled pairs into thin 5 to 6 inch rounds. Cook in a dry skillet 45 to 60 seconds per side, peel while warm, and hold covered.
  10. Make sauce. Warm hoisin, sweetener, fruit jam, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and five-spice until glossy. Loosen with warm water if needed.
  11. Carve and serve. Slice crispy skin and meat into pancake-sized pieces. Serve with warm pancakes, sauce, cucumber, and scallions.

Troubleshooting / Rescue Notes

Rubbery skin: drying was likely incomplete, or the duck steamed during rest. Next time dry longer and avoid tight foil.

Burnt spots: glaze was too thick or final heat ran too long. Keep lacquer layers thin and watch the last stage closely.

Stiff pancakes: they cooked too long or sat uncovered. Hold under towel or rewarm with damp heat.

Heavy smoke: spoon off rendered fat during roasting and keep some water in the pan bottom.

Pairs Well With

Required: Mandarin-style pancakes, hoisin-plum duck sauce, cucumber, and scallions.

Optional sides: jasmine rice, stir-fried greens, garlic-oyster broccoli, light cucumber salad, or hot tea.

Chef's Notes

This is a project recipe, not a shortcut roast duck. The drying window, scald, and thin lacquer layers are what move the result toward restaurant-style instead of ordinary roast duck.

Save the rendered duck fat. Strain and refrigerate it for potatoes, fried rice, eggs, or future Chinese-inspired skillet work.

Final Verdict

This is a high-ceiling Don request and should be judged only after a full 48-hour dry. If the skin crisps properly and the pancakes stay soft, this has special-occasion canon potential.
