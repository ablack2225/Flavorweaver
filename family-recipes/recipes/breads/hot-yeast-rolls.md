# Hot Yeast Rolls

> A preserved family yeast roll recipe made with milk, water, margarine, flour, dry yeast, sugar, salt, and eggs, with a rapid-rise shortcut option.

| 1 stick | Margarine |  |
| 5 cups | Flour | Plus a small amount more if dough is too sticky |

| 1/2 cup | Sugar |  |
| 1 tsp | Salt |  |
| 2 | Eggs |  |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Heat water, milk, and margarine in a small saucepan until very warm. |
| 2 | Mix all dry ingredients together. |
| 3 | Pour the liquid over the dry ingredients and mix well. |
| 4 | If the dough is too sticky, add a small amount of flour to make it easier to work with. |
| 5 | Turn dough out onto a floured surface and knead lightly until smooth. |
| 6 | Place in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise 35-45 minutes. |
| 7 | Shape dough into rolls. |
| 8 | Let rolls rise again 35-45 minutes. |
| 9 | Bake in a moderate oven at 375°F until brown, about 15-20 minutes. |

## Notes

Original family hint: the first rising time can be cut out by using rapid-rise yeast. Let the dough rest for about 10 minutes, shape into rolls, cover, and let rise 35-45 minutes.
