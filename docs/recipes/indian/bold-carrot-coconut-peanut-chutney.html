Bold Carrot Coconut Peanut Chutney

A spicy-sweet, nutty carrot-coconut chutney built to give aloo pakora bold purpose beside creamy korma and palak paneer.

Meal TypeCondiment
Total15 min
YieldAbout 3/4 cup
StatusLive Test
Indian-Inspiredcarrot chutneycoconut chutneypeanut chutneypakora sauce

Kitchen Execution Summary

Toasted Base

Sweetened coconut flakes, cashews, and sesame seeds are gently toasted for aroma and body.

Carrot Identity

Shredded carrot keeps the chutney bright, sweet, and distinct from a standard peanut sauce.

Bold Body

Peanut butter gives the chutney a bold, clingy dipping texture for fried pakora.

Spicy Acid Balance

Cumin, garam masala, chili, salt, and lime or lemon cut richness and keep the sauce savory.

Ingredient Roll-Up

Toasted Base

2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes, 1 Tbsp cashews, 1 tsp sesame seeds.

Blender Bowl

1/2 cup shredded carrot, toasted base, 1 Tbsp peanut butter, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/4 tsp crushed cumin seed, 1/8 tsp garam masala, 1/8 tsp cayenne/chili powder, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 2 1/2 to 3 tsp lime/lemon juice, and water.

Optional Boldness Boost

1/4 tsp soy sauce, extra lime/lemon, extra chili, or extra salt as needed.

Measured Ingredients

Toasted Coconut-Nut Base

2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes; 1 Tbsp cashews; 1 tsp sesame seeds.

Blender Bowl

1/2 cup shredded carrot; all toasted coconut-nut base; 1 Tbsp peanut butter; 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste; 1/4 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed; 1/8 tsp garam masala; 1/8 tsp cayenne or chili powder; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; 2 1/2 to 3 tsp lime or lemon juice; 2 Tbsp water to start; 1 to 2 Tbsp additional water as needed.

Optional Adjustments

1/4 tsp soy sauce, optional; extra lime/lemon to taste; extra cayenne/chili powder to taste; extra kosher salt to taste.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Toasted Coconut-Nut Base

2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes, 1 Tbsp cashews, 1 tsp sesame seeds.

Bowl 2 — Blender Bowl

1/2 cup shredded carrot, all toasted coconut-nut base, 1 Tbsp peanut butter, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/4 tsp crushed cumin seed, 1/8 tsp garam masala, 1/8 tsp cayenne/chili powder, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 2 1/2 to 3 tsp lime/lemon juice, 2 Tbsp water.

Bowl 3 — Adjustment Bowl

1 to 2 Tbsp additional water, 1/4 tsp soy sauce optional, extra lime/lemon, extra cayenne/chili powder, extra kosher salt.

Do Not Forget

Toast gently; sweetened coconut burns fast.

Start with only 2 Tbsp water so the chutney does not get thin.

This chutney should be bold, spicy, tangy, and purposeful.

If it tastes sweet, add lime/lemon and salt.

If it tastes flat, add cumin, chili, salt, or optional soy sauce.

Texture should be thick enough to cling to pakora but loose enough to dip.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Toast the base. In a dry skillet over medium-low heat, add 2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes, 1 Tbsp cashews, and 1 tsp sesame seeds. Stir constantly until fragrant and lightly golden. Do not walk away.
  2. Build the blender bowl. Add the toasted base to a blender or Ninja cup with 1/2 cup shredded carrot, 1 Tbsp peanut butter, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/4 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed, 1/8 tsp garam masala, 1/8 tsp cayenne or chili powder, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 2 1/2 to 3 tsp lime/lemon juice, and 2 Tbsp water.
  3. Blend. Blend until mostly smooth. Stop and scrape as needed. Add water 1 tsp to 1 Tbsp at a time until thick and dippable.
  4. Taste hard. It should be sweet-savory, nutty, tangy, and spicy. Adjust with lime/lemon, salt, chili, cumin, or water.
  5. Optional boldness boost. If it tastes too polite or not savory enough, blend in 1/4 tsp soy sauce.
  6. Serve. Serve in a small bowl beside hot aloo pakora.

Taste Adjustment Map

Too Sweet

Add more lime/lemon and a tiny pinch salt.

Too Flat

Add more cumin, chili, acid, or optional soy sauce.

Too Thick

Add water 1 tsp at a time.

Too Thin

Add a few cashews or 1/2 tsp peanut butter.

Too Peanut-Heavy

Add more carrot and lime/lemon.

Not Spicy Enough

Add cayenne or chili powder.

Final Taste Target

The finished chutney should hit sweet first, nutty second, then tangy-spicy-savory right behind it. It should stand out against creamy korma and palak paneer and make crispy aloo pakora feel intentional.
