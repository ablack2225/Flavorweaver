# Chicken and Dumplings

> A preserved family favorite chicken and dumplings recipe made with a whole chicken, homemade rolled dumplings, rich broth, and a creamy finish.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Cut chicken into pieces and place in a large pot. |
| 2 | Cover chicken with water and season with salt. |
| 3 | Bring to a boil. |
| 4 | Cover and cook for about 1 1/2-2 hours, until chicken is tender. |
| 5 | Remove chicken from broth. Take off skin, debone chicken, and chop meat into small pieces. |
| 6 | In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and baking powder together. |
| 7 | Cut margarine into the dry ingredients. |
| 8 | Add enough milk to make a stiff dumpling dough. |
| 9 | Roll dough out on a lightly floured surface. This may be easier in about 3 sections. |
| 10 | Cut dough into about 2x3-inch strips. |
| 11 | Bring the chicken broth back to a boil. |
| 12 | Add dumpling strips a few at a time, until all are added. |
| 13 | Season with a few sprinkles of black pepper. |
| 14 | Turn down to a low simmer, cover, and cook for about 5 minutes. |
| 15 | Add a little milk to make the broth creamy. |
| 16 | Add chopped chicken back to the pot. |
| 17 | Add an extra can of chicken broth if more broth/juice is needed. |

## Notes

This is remembered as a family favorite.

Shortcut dumpling option: when homemade dumplings are not practical, use flaky layered butter canned biscuits. Separate the layers, tear them into 2-4 piece sections depending on preferred dumpling size, and drop them into the simmering broth as you would homemade dumplings.

Servings, prep time, and total time are estimated. Chicken cooking time and dumpling simmer time are from the recipe text. Milk and extra broth are preserved in the flexible style of the original recipe.
