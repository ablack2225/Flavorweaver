# Southern Pralines

> A preserved family praline recipe made with granulated sugar, brown sugar, half-and-half cream, margarine, salt, and pecan halves.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Lightly butter a sheet of aluminum foil. |
| 2 | Combine granulated sugar, brown sugar, cream, and salt in a large saucepan. |
| 3 | Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until mixture reaches 228°F on a candy thermometer. |
| 4 | Stir in margarine and pecans. |
| 5 | Continue cooking, stirring constantly, until mixture reaches 236°F. |
| 6 | Remove from heat and cool 5 minutes. |
| 7 | Beat mixture with a wooden spoon until slightly thickened and candy just coats the nuts, but does not lose its gloss. |
| 8 | Drop candy by large spoonfuls onto the buttered foil. |
| 9 | Allow to cool. |

## Notes

The candy thermometer stages are preserved from the corrected recipe: cook to 228°F, add margarine and pecans, then continue to 236°F.
