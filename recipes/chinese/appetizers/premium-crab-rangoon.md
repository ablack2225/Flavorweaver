## Do Not Forget

- Drain crab meat well so the filling is not watery.
- Chill the filling before filling wrappers.
- Do not overfill wontons or they may burst while frying.
- Seal edges firmly with water.
- Fry at **350°F** until golden.
- The sweet and sour sauce belongs with this recipe card.
- No nutrition panel is needed for this recipe card.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Mix the filling.** Combine **8 oz softened cream cheese**, **4 to 6 oz drained crab meat**, **2 minced green onions**, **1 tsp Worcestershire sauce**, **1/2 tsp soy sauce**, **1/2 tsp garlic powder**, **1/4 tsp onion powder**, a **pinch of black pepper**, and **2 tsp powdered sugar**. |
| 2 | **Chill the filling.** Cover and chill until firm enough to scoop cleanly. |
| 3 | **Make the sweet and sour sauce.** Simmer **1/4 cup tomato paste**, **1/3 cup pineapple juice**, **2 Tbsp rice vinegar**, **2 Tbsp brown sugar**, **1 Tbsp honey**, **1 Tbsp orange juice**, **1 tsp soy sauce**, and a **pinch of ginger** until smooth. |
| 4 | **Thicken the sauce.** Stir together **1 tsp cornstarch** and **1 Tbsp cold water**. Add slurry to the simmering sauce and cook until glossy. Keep warm or cool for dipping. |
| 5 | **Fill wontons.** Place a small spoonful of filling in the center of each wonton wrapper. Wet edges with water and seal firmly. |
| 6 | **Fry.** Fry wontons at **350°F** until golden and crisp. Drain on a rack or paper towels. |
| 7 | **Serve.** Serve hot with homemade sweet and sour sauce. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished crab rangoon should be crisp, creamy, lightly sweet, savory, and rich, with enough crab and green onion flavor to balance the cream cheese.

The sweet and sour sauce should be glossy, tangy, fruity, and lightly sweet.
