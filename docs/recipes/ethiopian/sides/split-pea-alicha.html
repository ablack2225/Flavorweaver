Split Pea Alicha

A mild golden split pea stew designed as the practical, repeatable side for the Ethiopian-inspired Doro Wat platter.

Prep15m
Cook40-60m
Total1h 15m
Serves2-4 side servings
Yellow split peasMild sideDoro Wat platter

Ingredient List

Split Pea Base

Yellow split peas with water or broth.

Aromatic Base

Onion, butter or oil, garlic, and ginger.

Spices

Turmeric, cumin, coriander, black pepper, and optional cayenne or berbere.

Measured Ingredients

Split Pea Alicha

1 cupYellow split peasRinsed well; green split peas are backup only.
3-3 1/2 cupsWater or brothPlus more as needed.
1 smallOnionFinely diced.
1-2 TbspUnsalted butter or oilButter is acceptable.
2 clovesGarlicMinced.
1 tspGingerFresh grated or paste.
1/2 tsp eachTurmeric, cumin, coriander
1/4 tspBlack pepper
To tasteSaltAdd near the end.

Instructions

  1. Rinse split peas until the water runs mostly clear.
  2. Cook onion in butter or oil until softened, reduced, and lightly golden.
  3. Add garlic, ginger, turmeric, cumin, coriander, black pepper, and optional gentle heat. Cook briefly until fragrant.
  4. Add split peas and 3 cups water or broth. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  5. Simmer uncovered or partially covered for 40-60 minutes, adding liquid as needed, until peas are very tender.
  6. Mash some peas to create a creamy body, then simmer until thick and spoonable.
  7. Season with salt. Add a small squeeze of lemon only if the stew tastes heavy or flat.

Chef's Notes

Yellow split peas are the preferred version. They cook into a creamy golden stew and provide a mild counterpoint to the spicy Doro Wat-style chicken.

Green split peas are backup only. They work with the same method, but the flavor is earthier and the color is darker.
