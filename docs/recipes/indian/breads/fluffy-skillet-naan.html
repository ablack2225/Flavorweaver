Fluffy Skillet Naan

Amy & Don's fluffy skillet naan: soft, buttery, bubbly, chewy, and scoopable for curries, saucy plates, and any meal that needs warm bread at the table.

Prep20m active + 2h rise
Cook10m
Total2h 30m
Yield6 naan
House stapleIndian breadSkillet naanMake aheadCheese naan

Kitchen Execution Summary

Recipe Role

Amy & Don house naan: soft, bubbly, buttery skillet bread for curries, saucy plates, and warm table service.

Dough Style

No-knead soft dough with yeast, warm water, sugar, room-temperature milk, room-temperature egg, flour, salt, and melted ghee or butter.

Heat Method

Very hot well-seasoned cast iron skillet for fast bubbles, color, and light char without drying the naan out.

Finish

Brush hot naan with melted ghee or butter. Optional garlic butter, nigella seeds, cilantro, or cheese-stuffed variation.

Before Cooking / Rise Plan

Water temperature matters: measure warm water at 105-108°F so the yeast activates reliably without being damaged.

Use room-temperature dairy and egg: room-temperature milk and egg help keep the dough warm enough for a good rise.

Plan for two proofs: Proof 1 takes 1 to 1 1/2 hours, then shaped dough balls proof about 15 minutes before rolling.

Make-ahead option: after Proof 1, refrigerate the covered dough overnight, then bring it out at least a couple of hours before cooking and continue with Proof 2.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Yeast Bloom

1 tsp yeast, 1/2 cup warm water at 105-108°F, 1 Tbsp white sugar.

Bowl 2 — Egg and Milk

Room-temperature milk and room-temperature whisked egg; measure 1 1/2 Tbsp whisked egg for this batch.

Bowl 3 — Dry Ingredients

1 3/4 cups bread flour or all-purpose flour and 1/2 tsp salt, sifted or whisked together.

Bowl 4 — Fat

2 Tbsp / 30g melted ghee or unsalted butter for the dough.

Bowl 5 — Finish

Melted ghee or butter, optional garlic butter, optional nigella seeds, optional cilantro.

Bowl 6 — Cheese Variation

About 1/4 cup shredded melting cheese per naan, only if making stuffed cheese naan.

Do Not Forget

Measure water temperature; target 105-108°F for reliable yeast activation.

Use room-temperature milk and egg so the dough does not cool down too much.

Keep the dough soft and use only enough flour to make it workable.

No kneading is required; mix until the dough comes together into a soft ball.

Use a very hot well-seasoned cast iron skillet for bubbles and color.

Avoid nonstick pans because the high heat can damage the coating.

Brush naan while hot so the butter or ghee melts into the surface.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Bloom yeast: Measure the warm water with a kitchen thermometer. The target temperature is 105-108°F. Combine yeast, warm water, and sugar in a small bowl. Cover and let stand until foamy, about 10 minutes. Do not use overly hot water, because it can damage or kill the yeast and prevent the dough from rising.
  2. Egg and milk: Whisk room-temperature milk and room-temperature egg together. Measure 1 1/2 Tbsp of the whisked egg for this batch. If making the full/double recipe, double the egg amount to 1 whole egg.
  3. Flour: Sift or whisk flour and salt into a separate bowl.
  4. Add wet ingredients: Make a well in the flour. Add the yeast mixture, the egg-milk mixture, and the melted ghee or butter.
  5. Mix dough: Mix with a spatula until the flour is mostly incorporated, then switch to your hands and bring it together into a soft dough ball. No kneading is required.
  6. Proof 1: Cover the bowl and leave in a warm place for 1 to 1 1/2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  7. Cut into 6 pieces: Place the dough on a lightly floured surface. Cut into 6 equal pieces, then shape each piece into a ball by tucking the surface under so the top is smooth.
  8. Proof 2: Place dough balls on a lightly floured tray or plate. Sprinkle lightly with flour, cover loosely with a lightweight towel, and place in a warm spot for about 15 minutes, or until increased in size by about 50%.
  9. Roll out: Place one dough ball on a lightly floured surface and flatten with your hand. Roll into a 3-4mm / about 1/8-inch thick round, about 16cm / 6.5 inches wide.
  10. Heat skillet: Heat a well-seasoned cast iron skillet over high heat until very hot. Lightly oil by rubbing about 1/2 tsp oil over the surface with a paper towel. Avoid nonstick pans because the high heat can damage the coating.
  11. Cook naan: Place one naan in the skillet and cook for 1 to 1 1/2 minutes, until the underside is deep golden or lightly charred and the surface becomes bubbly.
  12. Cook second side: Flip and cook the other side about 1 minute, until golden. Adjust the heat down slightly if the naan is browning too fast before the bubbles form.
  13. Repeat: Remove cooked naan and continue with the remaining dough, oiling the pan lightly only as needed.
  14. Finish: Brush freshly cooked naan with melted ghee or butter. For garlic butter, warm melted butter or ghee with crushed garlic briefly, let it stand a few minutes, then brush over hot naan. Sprinkle with nigella seeds and cilantro if desired. Serve hot.

Optional Cheese Naan

  1. Roll out one naan dough ball as above. Brush lightly with melted butter or garlic butter.
  2. Place about 1/4 cup shredded melting cheese in the middle.
  3. Bundle the dough up around the cheese like a money bag, twist to seal, then turn smooth side up.
  4. Roll gently into a 6-7mm / about 1/4-inch thick round. Do not roll too thin or the cheese may break through.
  5. Cook in a hot skillet that is slightly less aggressive than the plain naan skillet heat. Cook about 1 1/2 minutes on the first side until golden and puffed, then flip and cook the second side about 45 seconds.

Make-Ahead Naan

After Proof 1, place the covered bowl of dough in the refrigerator overnight without punching the dough down. The next day, remove it from the refrigerator at least a couple of hours before cooking so the chill comes out and the dough can rise for Proof 2. Then cut, shape, proof, roll, and cook as written.

Rise Helper

If the kitchen is cool, place the covered dough in a warm enclosed space. A briefly warmed, turned-off oven or another warm draft-free space can help the dough rise.

Final Taste Target

The finished naan should be soft, buttery, bubbly, chewy, lightly charred in spots, and scoopable. It should feel fluffy enough to tear easily but strong enough to carry saucy curries and table plates.

Chef's Notes

For best texture, use room-temperature milk and egg, keep the dough soft, measure the warm water so the yeast activates reliably, and cook the naan in a very hot cast iron skillet so it bubbles and chars quickly without drying out.

Bread flour makes the softest, fluffiest naan, but all-purpose flour works well too.
