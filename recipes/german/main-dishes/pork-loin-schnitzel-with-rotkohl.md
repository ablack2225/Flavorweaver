# Pork Loin Schnitzel with Rotkohl

> Thin pork-loin schnitzel fried feather-crisp and served with bold blackberry-finished Rotkohl, fresh-potato Bratkartoffeln, lemon, and an optional tiny cucumber-dill palate reset for contrast.

## Flavor Target

The schnitzel should be thin, savory, tender inside, and feather-crisp outside. The crust should puff and shatter lightly; it should never feel compacted, greasy, or pork-chop heavy.

The Rotkohl should be glossy, dark, fruit-deep, sweet-sour, buttery, gently spiced, and bold enough for the Amy & Don palate. It should taste like cabbage, apple, vinegar, brown sugar, blackberry, salt, and warm spice in layered balance.

The full plate should feel rich but not dull: crisp pork, bright lemon, smoky potatoes, sweet-sour blackberry cabbage, and a fresh green finish.

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

- Slice the pork loin into cutlets ahead if desired.
- Rotkohl can be made a day ahead; it often tastes even better after resting.
- Start the Bratkartoffeln early enough to par-cook and steam-dry the fresh potatoes.
- Set up a rack over a sheet pan for resting breaded schnitzel and draining fried schnitzel.
- If using cucumber-dill salad as a palate reset, keep it tiny and cold; it is an accent, not another heavy side.

## Cross-Component Prep Map

- **Pork loin:** slice across the loin into cutlets before pounding. Thin, even cutlets are the key to tender schnitzel.
- **Thickness:** pound to **1/8 to 3/16 inch**, not a full 1/4 inch.
- **Breadcrumbs:** half plain breadcrumbs and half crushed panko gives classic coverage with a lighter crunch.
- **Breading pressure:** coat lightly. Do not smash or pack the crumbs into the pork.
- **Cabbage:** thin slicing creates silky Rotkohl; thick slicing makes the braise take longer.
- **Apple:** Granny Smith gives tart balance against brown sugar, vinegar, and blackberry jelly.
- **Blackberry finish:** add near the end so the fruit note stays dark and glossy instead of disappearing.
- **Bratkartoffeln:** fresh potatoes need par-cooking and drying time; start early.
- **Timing:** Rotkohl can wait gently; schnitzel should be fried close to service.

## Do Not Forget

- Start the Bratkartoffeln early if using fresh potatoes.
- Use boneless pork loin for economy, but slice and pound it thin so it eats like schnitzel, not a pork chop.
- Pound pork to **1/8 to 3/16 inch thick**.
- Use **half plain breadcrumbs and half crushed panko** for this test.
- Do not pack the breading hard.
- Let breaded schnitzel rest **10 minutes** before frying.
- Fry quickly around **350°F** so the crust browns before the pork dries out.
- Move the pan gently and spoon hot oil over the top for a lighter crust.
- Rotkohl needs enough time to soften fully. Rushed cabbage tastes raw and sharp.
- Finish Rotkohl with blackberry jelly, Dijon, butter, and final acid only after the cabbage is tender.
- Serve schnitzel immediately after frying; Rotkohl and potatoes can wait warm.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Start the required Bratkartoffeln.** Follow the fresh-potato Bratkartoffeln recipe: cut, par-cook, drain, and steam-dry the baking potatoes before crisping. |
| 2 | **Start the Rotkohl.** Core and thin slice **1 medium head purple/red cabbage**. Thin slice **1 medium yellow onion**. Peel and grate or finely dice **1 Granny Smith apple**. |
| 3 | **Sauté onion.** In a Dutch oven or deep skillet, melt **3 Tbsp butter, bacon fat, or a mix** over medium heat. Add the onion and cook **4 to 5 minutes**, until softened. |
| 4 | **Add cabbage and apple.** Add the sliced cabbage, apple, **1 tsp kosher salt**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, **1 bay leaf**, optional tiny pinch cloves, and optional tiny pinch cinnamon. Stir well. |
| 5 | **Add sweet-sour liquid.** Add **1/3 cup apple cider vinegar**, **2 Tbsp brown sugar**, and **1/2 cup apple juice, apple cider, chicken broth, or water**. Stir. Reserve the extra **1 Tbsp brown sugar** for adjustment. |
| 6 | **Braise Rotkohl.** Cover and cook on low to medium-low for **45 to 75 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until tender and glossy. Uncover for the last **10 to 15 minutes** if too liquidy. |
| 7 | **Slice pork loin.** Slice **1 1/4 to 1 1/2 lb boneless pork loin** into **4 cutlets**, about 5 to 6 oz each. Pound each cutlet to **1/8 to 3/16 inch thick**. |
| 8 | **Season pork.** Mix **1 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp black pepper**, **1/2 tsp garlic powder**, **1/2 tsp plain paprika**, and **1/4 tsp onion powder**. Season both sides of the cutlets. |
| 9 | **Set breading station.** Dish 1: **1/2 cup flour**. Dish 2: **2 eggs** beaten with **1 Tbsp milk or water**. Dish 3: **3/4 cup plain breadcrumbs + 3/4 cup crushed panko** with a pinch salt and pinch pepper. |
| 10 | **Bread schnitzel.** Dredge pork in flour, dip in egg wash, then coat in breadcrumbs. Press lightly, but do not pack the coating hard. Rest on a rack for **10 minutes**. |
| 11 | **Finish and balance Rotkohl.** Remove bay leaf. Stir in **1 Tbsp blackberry jelly**, **1 tsp Dijon or stone-ground mustard**, and **1 tsp butter**. Taste. Add up to **1/2 Tbsp more blackberry jelly** for deeper fruit, a tiny splash vinegar for brightness, brown sugar if too sharp, or salt if flat. Simmer uncovered a few minutes until glossy. |
| 12 | **Fry schnitzel.** Heat neutral oil to about **350°F**. Fry schnitzel in generous shallow oil, gently moving the pan and spooning hot oil over the top, about **2 minutes per side** depending on thickness, until golden and cooked through. Add **1 Tbsp butter** near the end if desired for flavor. |
| 13 | **Drain and salt.** Drain schnitzel on a rack and lightly salt while hot. |
| 14 | **Plate.** Serve schnitzel immediately with warm blackberry Rotkohl, Bratkartoffeln, and lemon wedges. Keep potatoes away from cabbage juices. Add optional tiny cucumber-dill palate reset only if desired. |

## Rotkohl Balance Map

| Issue | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Too sharp | Add brown sugar, 1 tsp at a time, or a little more blackberry jelly. |
| Too sweet | Add apple cider vinegar, 1 tsp at a time. |
| Flat | Add salt first, then reassess. |
| Too watery | Simmer uncovered longer. |
| Too heavy | Add a tiny splash vinegar and black pepper. |
| Missing depth | Add Dijon or stone-ground mustard, 1/2 tsp at a time. |
| Missing dark fruit | Add up to 1/2 Tbsp more blackberry jelly. |

## Final Taste Target

The final plate should land as crisp, rich, bright, smoky, sweet-sour, and dark-fruited. The schnitzel should be hot and shatter-crisp. The Rotkohl should be glossy and bold. The potatoes should be crisp-edged and smoky. Lemon should make the fried pork wake up immediately.

## Pork Loin Notes

A whole boneless pork loin is the cost-effective choice. For schnitzel, slice only what you need into cutlets and freeze the rest in meal-sized portions. Cutlets should be pounded thin so they fry quickly and stay tender.

## Plating Plan

Place schnitzel slightly off-center with lemon wedges nearby. Spoon blackberry Rotkohl into a tight glossy mound beside it. Plate Bratkartoffeln separately from the cabbage juices so the potatoes stay crisp. Finish potatoes with green onion. Do not sauce the schnitzel; lemon and Rotkohl provide the contrast.

## Testing Notes to Capture

- Did pork loin stay tender at 1/8 to 3/16 inch after frying?
- Was the half plain breadcrumb / half crushed panko texture ideal?
- Did the blackberry jelly improve Rotkohl depth without making it jammy?
- Was 1 tsp Dijon enough structure without tasting mustardy?
- Did fresh-potato Bratkartoffeln outperform the frozen shortcut?
- Did Don respond to the complete plate as comfort food elevated into something memorable?
