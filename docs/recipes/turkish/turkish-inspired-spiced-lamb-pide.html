Turkish-Inspired Spiced Lamb Pide

Homemade yogurt pide boats filled with garlic-paprika spiced lamb, mozzarella, feta, garlic-parsley butter, sumac onion salad, and cooling garlic yogurt.

Meal TypeMain Dish
TotalAbout 3 hr
Serves4 pide boats
StatusFlavorweaver Test Recipe

Kitchen Execution Summary

Soft-Chewy Yogurt Dough

Homemade bread flour/AP dough with yogurt and olive oil for tenderness.

Turkish-Spiced Lamb

Garlic-forward, paprika-rich lamb filling with cumin, allspice, cinnamon, and lemon.

Mozzarella + Feta

Mozzarella melts; feta gives salty-tangy lift.

Garlic-Parsley Butter

Brushed on hot crust after baking for the restaurant-style finish.

Sumac Onion + Garlic Yogurt

Required fresh and cooling contrast for lamb richness.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Soft-Chewy Yogurt Pide Dough

1 cup warm water, 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast, 1 tsp sugar, 2 cups bread flour, 1 cup AP flour, 1 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 2 Tbsp olive oil, 2 Tbsp plain yogurt or Greek yogurt.

Turkish-Spiced Lamb Filling

1 lb ground lamb 80/20, 4 garlic cloves, 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste, 2 tsp paprika, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp kosher salt, 3/4 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes, 1/4 tsp allspice, tiny pinch cinnamon, 1 tsp lemon juice or vinegar, optional 1 Tbsp parsley.

Cheese, Butter, Salad, Yogurt

1 1/2 cups mozzarella, 1/3 to 1/2 cup feta. Butter: 3 Tbsp butter, 1 garlic clove, 1 Tbsp parsley. Salad: 1/2 onion, 1 tsp sumac, 1 Tbsp lemon juice, 1 Tbsp parsley, 1 tsp olive oil. Yogurt: 1/2 cup yogurt, 1 small garlic clove, 1 tsp lemon juice, 1 tsp olive oil, salt.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Yeast Bloom

1 cup warm water, 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast, 1 tsp sugar.

Bowl 2 — Dough Dry Mix

2 cups bread flour, 1 cup AP flour, 1 1/4 tsp kosher salt.

Bowl 3 — Dough Enrichment

2 Tbsp olive oil, 2 Tbsp plain yogurt or Greek yogurt.

Bowl 4 — Lamb Seasoning

4 garlic cloves, 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste, 2 tsp paprika, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp kosher salt, 3/4 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes, 1/4 tsp allspice, tiny pinch cinnamon, 1 tsp lemon juice or vinegar, optional 1 Tbsp parsley. Mix into 1 lb ground lamb.

Bowl 5 — Cheese

1 1/2 cups mozzarella, 1/3 to 1/2 cup feta.

Bowl 6 — Garlic-Parsley Butter

3 Tbsp melted butter, 1 grated garlic clove, 1 Tbsp parsley, pinch salt if needed.

Bowl 7 — Sumac Onion Salad

1/2 thin-sliced onion, 1 tsp sumac, 1 Tbsp lemon juice, 1 Tbsp parsley, 1 tsp olive oil, pinch salt.

Bowl 8 — Garlic Yogurt

1/2 cup yogurt, 1 small grated garlic clove, 1 tsp lemon juice, 1 tsp olive oil, pinch salt.

Do Not Forget

Rest the lamb filling at least 1 hour, ideally overnight if timing allows.

Partially pre-cook the lamb filling before topping the dough so the pide does not get greasy or soggy.

Keep feta as an accent; mozzarella is the main melt.

Sumac onion salad and garlic yogurt are required for the 9.5+ version.

Brush the crust with garlic-parsley butter immediately after baking.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Bloom yeast. Combine 1 cup warm water, 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast, and 1 tsp sugar. Rest 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix dough. Combine 2 cups bread flour, 1 cup AP flour, and 1 1/4 tsp kosher salt. Add yeast mixture plus 2 Tbsp olive oil and 2 Tbsp yogurt. Mix until shaggy.
  3. Knead dough. Knead 8–10 minutes by hand or 5–6 minutes in a mixer until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
  4. First rise. Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and rise 60–90 minutes until doubled.
  5. Season lamb. Mix 1 lb ground lamb with 4 grated garlic cloves, 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste, 2 tsp paprika, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp kosher salt, 3/4 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes, 1/4 tsp allspice, tiny pinch cinnamon, 1 tsp lemon juice or vinegar, and optional 1 Tbsp parsley. Mix until tacky. Rest refrigerated at least 1 hour.
  6. Divide dough. Punch down dough and divide into 4 equal balls. Cover and rest 10–15 minutes.
  7. Partially cook lamb. Cook seasoned lamb in a skillet until mostly cooked but still juicy, about 5–7 minutes. Break into small crumbles. Drain excess fat if needed and cool slightly.
  8. Make garlic yogurt. Stir together 1/2 cup yogurt, 1 grated small garlic clove, 1 tsp lemon juice, 1 tsp olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Chill.
  9. Make sumac onion salad. Toss 1/2 thin-sliced onion, 1 tsp sumac, 1 Tbsp lemon juice, 1 Tbsp parsley, 1 tsp olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Rest 15–20 minutes.
  10. Preheat oven. Heat oven to 475°F. Preheat a sheet pan, pizza steel, or stone if using.
  11. Shape pide. Roll each dough ball into a long oval, about 10–12 inches long. Add a light layer from the 1 1/2 cups mozzarella, leaving a border.
  12. Fill pide. Divide cooked lamb across the 4 ovals. Sprinkle remaining mozzarella and 1/3 to 1/2 cup feta lightly over the filling.
  13. Form boats. Fold long edges inward and pinch both ends tightly to create pide boats.
  14. Bake. Bake at 475°F for 10–14 minutes, until crust is deeply golden, cheese is melted, and filling is sizzling.
  15. Butter crust. Combine 3 Tbsp melted butter, 1 grated garlic clove, 1 Tbsp parsley, and a pinch salt if needed. Brush over hot crusts immediately.
  16. Plate. Top each pide with a small amount of sumac onion salad or serve it alongside. Drizzle or dollop with garlic yogurt. Finish with extra parsley and a light pinch of sumac if desired.

Final Taste Target

The pide should have a soft-chewy interior, crisp golden edges, juicy spiced lamb, melty mozzarella, salty feta pops, buttery garlic crust, sharp sumac onion, and cooling yogurt. It should feel Turkish-inspired and chef-level, not like pizza with lamb.
