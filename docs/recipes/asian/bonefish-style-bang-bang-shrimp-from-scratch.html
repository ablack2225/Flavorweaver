Bonefish-Style Bang Bang Shrimp — From Scratch Version

Light crispy shrimp tossed in a creamy, sweet, spicy, garlicky Bang Bang sauce. This scratch-built version includes homemade chili-garlic paste, homemade sweet chili sauce, a quick buttermilk substitute, Bang Bang sauce, crispy cornstarch-fried shrimp, and optional purple cabbage-carrot slaw.

Measured Ingredients

Quick Chili-Garlic Paste

2 to 4 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded; optional 1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper or cayenne; 4 garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp rice vinegar or white vinegar; 1 Tbsp water, plus more only as needed; 1 tsp sugar; 1/2 tsp kosher salt.

Homemade Sweet Chili Sauce

1/2 cup water; 1/4 cup rice vinegar or white vinegar; 1/3 cup sugar; 1 to 2 Tbsp homemade chili-garlic paste; optional 1 grated garlic clove; optional 1/2 tsp ginger paste; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; slurry of 1 tsp cornstarch plus 1 Tbsp cold water.

Quick Buttermilk Substitute

Preferred evaporated milk version: 1/2 cup evaporated milk, 1/2 cup water, and 1 Tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice. Heavy cream backup: 1/3 cup heavy cream, 2/3 cup water, and 1 Tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice.

Bang Bang Sauce

1/2 cup mayonnaise; 2 to 3 Tbsp cooled homemade sweet chili sauce; 1 to 2 tsp homemade chili-garlic paste; tiny pinch kosher salt; optional tiny squeeze lime.

Crispy Bang Bang Shrimp

1 lb peeled and deveined shrimp; 1 cup buttermilk substitute; 1 cup cornstarch, plus more if needed; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper; neutral oil; Bang Bang sauce; sliced green onions.

Optional Purple Cabbage-Carrot Slaw

2 cups thin-sliced purple cabbage; 1 cup shredded or julienned carrots; optional sliced green onion; optional cilantro. Dressing: 1 1/2 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 Tbsp lime juice, 1 tsp sugar or honey, 1 tsp soy sauce or fish sauce, 1 tsp neutral oil, tiny pinch kosher salt.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Chili-Garlic Paste Bowls

Bowl 1 — Chiles

2 to 4 stemmed, seeded guajillo chiles. Soak in very hot water 10 to 15 minutes, then drain.

Bowl 2 — Paste Aromatics

4 garlic cloves; optional 1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper or cayenne.

Bowl 3 — Paste Liquid

2 Tbsp vinegar; 1 Tbsp water; 1 tsp sugar; 1/2 tsp kosher salt. Add water 1 tsp at a time only if needed.

Sweet Chili Sauce Bowls

Bowl 1 — Sauce Base

1/2 cup water; 1/4 cup vinegar; 1/3 cup sugar; 1 to 2 Tbsp chili-garlic paste; optional garlic; optional ginger; 1/4 tsp salt.

Bowl 2 — Slurry

1 tsp cornstarch; 1 Tbsp cold water. Stir into simmering sauce.

Bowl 3 — Cooling Cup

Finished sweet chili sauce. Cool before mixing into mayonnaise.

Buttermilk and Bang Bang Sauce Bowls

Bowl 1 — Buttermilk Substitute

Preferred: 1/2 cup evaporated milk, 1/2 cup water, 1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice. Rest 5 to 10 minutes.

Bowl 2 — Backup Buttermilk

1/3 cup heavy cream, 2/3 cup water, 1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice.

Bowl 3 — Bang Bang Sauce

1/2 cup mayo; 2 to 3 Tbsp cooled sweet chili sauce; 1 to 2 tsp chili-garlic paste; tiny pinch salt; optional lime. Keep chilled.

Shrimp Fry Bowls

Bowl 1 — Shrimp

1 lb peeled, deveined shrimp, patted dry.

Bowl 2 — Shrimp Soak

Shrimp plus 1 cup buttermilk substitute. Soak 10 minutes only.

Bowl 3 — Cornstarch Coating

1 cup cornstarch; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper.

Bowl 4 — Fry Oil

Neutral oil heated to 350°F to 375°F. Fry in small batches.

Bowl 5 — Sauce Toss

Hot fried shrimp plus Bang Bang sauce. Toss gently and lightly.

Bowl 6 — Garnish

Sliced green onions.

Optional Slaw Bowls

Bowl 1 — Vegetables

2 cups purple cabbage; 1 cup carrots; optional green onion; optional cilantro.

Bowl 2 — Dressing

Rice vinegar, lime juice, sugar or honey, soy sauce or fish sauce, neutral oil, tiny pinch salt.

Bowl 3 — Rest

Dressed slaw. Massage 30 seconds and rest 15 to 20 minutes.

Do Not Forget

Cool the homemade sweet chili sauce before mixing it into mayonnaise.

Soak shrimp only 10 minutes.

Use cornstarch for the lightest crispy coating.

Shake excess cornstarch off before frying.

Fry shrimp in small batches.

Shrimp cook fast: usually 1 to 2 minutes.

Toss shrimp with Bang Bang sauce right before serving.

Do not drown the shrimp in sauce.

Serve immediately for best texture.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Soak guajillo chiles. Place 2 to 4 stemmed and seeded dried guajillo chiles in a bowl. Cover with very hot water and soak 10 to 15 minutes, until softened. Drain.
  2. Blend chili-garlic paste. Add softened chiles, garlic, vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and optional crushed red pepper or cayenne to a Ninja Bullet, blender, or food processor. Blend into a thick paste. Add water 1 tsp at a time only if needed.
  3. Make sweet chili sauce. Simmer water, vinegar, sugar, chili-garlic paste, optional garlic, optional ginger, and salt for 3 to 5 minutes, stirring until sugar dissolves.
  4. Thicken sweet chili sauce. Mix cornstarch with cold water. Stir slurry into simmering sauce and cook 30 to 60 seconds, until glossy and lightly thickened. Cool before using.
  5. Make buttermilk substitute. Preferred: mix evaporated milk, water, and vinegar or lemon juice. Let sit 5 to 10 minutes. Use the heavy cream backup only if needed.
  6. Make Bang Bang sauce. Whisk mayo, cooled sweet chili sauce, chili-garlic paste, salt, and optional lime. Taste and adjust. Keep chilled.
  7. Make optional slaw. Toss vegetables. Whisk dressing, pour over vegetables, massage lightly 30 seconds, and rest 15 to 20 minutes.
  8. Soak shrimp. Pat shrimp dry. Place in buttermilk substitute and soak 10 minutes only.
  9. Prepare coating. Mix cornstarch, kosher salt, and black pepper.
  10. Coat shrimp. Remove shrimp from buttermilk and let excess drip off. Toss in cornstarch mixture until coated. Shake off excess.
  11. Fry shrimp. Heat oil to 350°F to 375°F. Fry in small batches for 1 to 2 minutes, just until crisp, lightly golden, and cooked through.
  12. Sauce shrimp. Place hot fried shrimp in a bowl. Add enough Bang Bang sauce to coat and toss gently. Do not drown the shrimp.
  13. Serve immediately. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve over rice, beside slaw, tucked into lettuce or cabbage cups, or as an appetizer with extra sauce.

Sauce Adjustments

Too Sweet

Add a tiny squeeze of lime or a touch more chili-garlic paste.

Too Spicy

Add more mayonnaise or a little more sweet chili sauce.

Too Thick

Add a few drops of water or lime juice.

Too Mild

Add more chili-garlic paste.

Too Flat

Add a tiny pinch of salt or lime.

Frying Troubleshooting

Coating Is Gummy

Let excess buttermilk drip off longer and shake off excess cornstarch.

Shrimp Are Greasy

Oil is too cool or the batch is crowded.

Shrimp Are Tough

They cooked too long; fry just 1 to 2 minutes.

Sauce Makes Shrimp Soggy

Toss with less sauce and serve immediately.

Final Taste Target

The shrimp should be light, crisp, tender, and just-coated in creamy, sweet, spicy, garlicky Bang Bang sauce.

The homemade chili-garlic paste should be thick, red, garlicky, and gently earthy from guajillo chiles. The sweet chili sauce should be glossy, sweet, tangy, garlicky, and lightly spicy. The slaw should be bright, crisp, lightly softened, and acidic enough to balance the creamy shrimp sauce.
