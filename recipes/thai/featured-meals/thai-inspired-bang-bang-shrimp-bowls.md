# Thai-Inspired Bang Bang Shrimp Bowls

> Sweet, spicy, creamy, crispy shrimp bowls layered with coconut-lime rice, purple cabbage-carrot mint slaw, toasted coconut, crispy fried shallot rings, green onion, mint, cashews, and lime.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Element | Notes |
|---|---|
| Meal Role | Complete bowl. |
| Full Bowl | Homemade chili-garlic paste, homemade sweet chili sauce, creamy Bang Bang sauce, crispy fried shrimp, coconut-lime rice, purple cabbage-carrot mint slaw, and crispy fried shallot rings. |
| Shrimp | Shrimp are soaked briefly in quick dairy soak, coated in seasoned cornstarch, fried until light and crisp, then tossed with creamy sweet-spicy sauce right before serving. |
| Rice | Basmati rice is cooked with coconut milk, water, salt, and a tiny touch of sugar, then finished with lime zest, lime juice, toasted coconut flakes, and green onion. |
| Slaw | Purple cabbage and carrots are softened with lime, rice vinegar, fish sauce, ginger, garlic, chile, mint, green onion, and toasted cashews. It should be bright enough to cut the creamy shrimp. |
| Crunch Finish | Crispy fried shallot rings act like tiny onion rings and give the bowl its restaurant-style finish. |
| Critical Balance | Creamy shrimp, bright slaw, soft coconut-lime rice, nutty coconut and cashews, crisp shallot rings, fresh herbs, and lime. |

## Before Cooking / Advance Prep

- **Shrimp** — Make sure shrimp are thawed, peeled, deveined, and patted dry.
- **Cabbage and carrots** — Slice cabbage very thin and julienne or shred carrots.
- **Shallots** — Slice shallots into rings and separate into rings and petals.
- **Equipment** — Set out rice cooker, deep fryer or frying pot, sheet tray or rack, and mixing bowls.
- **Toasted finishes** — Toast coconut and cashews before final assembly if possible.
- **Sauce timing** — Do not sauce the shrimp early.
- **Crunch timing** — Do not add crispy shallots to the bowl until the very end.

## Service Countdown

| Time Before Serving | Task |
|---:|---|
| 90 minutes | Make chili-garlic paste and sweet chili sauce; cool sweet chili sauce. |
| 75 minutes | Start coconut-lime rice in rice cooker. |
| 60 minutes | Make slaw dressing; slice cabbage and carrots. |
| 45 minutes | Toss slaw and let it soften; make Bang Bang sauce. |
| 35 minutes | Slice and coat shallot rings. |
| 25 minutes | Fry shallot rings; hold crisp. |
| 15 minutes | Soak shrimp briefly. |
| 10 minutes | Coat and fry shrimp. |
| At service | Toss shrimp with sauce, build bowls, finish with crispy shallots, toasted coconut, cashews, green onion, mint, and lime. |

## Do Not Forget

- Cool the homemade sweet chili sauce before mixing it into mayonnaise.
- Make enough quick dairy soak for both the shrimp and shallots.
- Soak shrimp only **10 minutes**.
- Do not add water to the shrimp coating.
- Fry shrimp in small batches.
- Toss shrimp with sauce right before serving.
- Add toasted coconut after rice cooks.
- Add mint, cashews, and crispy shallots at the end.
- Use tongs for slaw if it releases extra dressing.
- Keep extra Bang Bang sauce on the side instead of drowning the bowl.
- This bowl needs contrast: creamy shrimp, bright slaw, soft rice, and crispy shallots.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Make the quick chili-garlic paste.** Place **2 to 4 stemmed and seeded guajillo chiles** in a bowl. Cover with very hot water and soak **10 to 15 minutes**, until softened. Drain. Blend softened chiles with **4 garlic cloves**, **2 Tbsp vinegar**, **1 Tbsp water**, **1 tsp sugar**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and crushed red pepper or cayenne into a thick red paste. Add more water **1 tsp at a time** only if needed to blend.

2. **Make the homemade sweet chili sauce.** In a small saucepan, combine **1/2 cup water**, **1/4 cup vinegar**, **1/3 cup sugar**, **1 to 2 Tbsp chili-garlic paste**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp ginger paste**, and **1/4 tsp kosher salt**. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook **3 to 5 minutes**, stirring until the sugar dissolves.

3. **Gloss the sweet chili sauce.** Mix **1 tsp cornstarch** with **1 Tbsp cold water**. Stir the slurry into the simmering sauce. Simmer **30 to 60 seconds**, until glossy and lightly thickened. Remove from heat and cool completely.

4. **Make the quick dairy soak.** Mix either the evaporated milk version or the heavy cream version and let sit **5 to 10 minutes**. Use it for both shrimp soak and shallot rings.

5. **Start the coconut-lime rice.** Rinse **1 cup basmati rice** until the water runs mostly clear. Add rinsed rice to the rice cooker with coconut milk, water, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1 tsp sugar**. Cook according to rice cooker directions. When finished, fluff gently. Fold in **lime zest**, **1 to 2 tsp lime juice**, **toasted coconut flakes**, **1 sliced green onion**, and a small drizzle of neutral oil or clean shallot oil. Hold warm.

6. **Toast the coconut flakes.** Place **1/4 to 1/3 cup unsweetened coconut flakes** in a dry skillet over medium-low heat. Stir constantly until lightly golden and fragrant, about **2 to 4 minutes**. Remove immediately to a plate.

7. **Toast the cashews.** Toast **1/3 cup cashews** in a dry skillet over medium-low heat until fragrant and lightly golden. Cool, chop, and reserve for slaw and final garnish.

8. **Make the slaw dressing.** Whisk **2 Tbsp lime juice**, **1 Tbsp rice vinegar**, **1 Tbsp fish sauce**, **1 tsp soy sauce**, **1 1/2 tsp honey or sugar**, **1 tsp ginger paste**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1 tsp chili-garlic paste**, **1 tsp neutral oil**, **1/2 tsp sesame oil**, and a pinch of black pepper. It should be bright, salty, lightly sweet, garlicky, and a little spicy.

9. **Build the slaw.** Add **3 cups thin-sliced purple cabbage** and **1 1/2 cups shredded or julienned carrots** to a large bowl. Pour dressing over the vegetables. Massage gently **30 to 60 seconds**. Let sit **15 to 20 minutes**. Right before serving, add **2 sliced green onions**, **1 to 2 Tbsp chopped mint**, **1/3 cup toasted chopped cashews**, and lime zest.

10. **Make the Bang Bang sauce.** Whisk **1/2 cup mayonnaise**, **2 to 3 Tbsp cooled sweet chili sauce**, **1 to 2 tsp chili-garlic paste**, **1/2 tsp lime juice**, and a tiny pinch of kosher salt. Adjust with more sweet chili sauce for sweetness and gloss, more chili-garlic paste for heat, more mayonnaise for a milder creamy sauce, or a tiny lime squeeze for brightness. Keep chilled until shrimp are fried.

11. **Make the crispy shallot rings.** Slice **3 to 4 large shallots** crosswise into **1/8 to 1/4 inch rings** and separate into rings and petals. Dip shallot rings into **1/2 cup quick dairy soak**, let excess drip off, then toss in the dry mix of **1/2 cup flour**, **1/4 cup cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp salt**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, **1/4 tsp garlic powder**, **1/4 tsp paprika**, **1/4 tsp baking powder**, and cayenne. For extra crunch, repeat the coating sequence once. Fry at **350°F** in small batches for **1 to 2 minutes**, until golden and crisp. Drain, salt lightly, and hold uncovered.

12. **Soak the shrimp.** Pat **1 lb shrimp** dry. Place shrimp in **1 cup quick dairy soak**. Soak **10 minutes only**.

13. **Coat the shrimp.** Mix **1 cup cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/4 tsp black pepper**. Remove shrimp from the dairy soak and let excess drip off. Toss shrimp in the cornstarch mixture until fully coated. Shake off excess and place coated shrimp on a rack or plate while oil heats.

14. **Fry the shrimp.** Heat oil to **350°F to 375°F**. Fry shrimp in small batches for **1 to 2 minutes**, until crisp, lightly golden, and cooked through. Do not overcrowd. Drain on a rack.

15. **Sauce the shrimp.** Place hot fried shrimp in a bowl. Add enough Bang Bang sauce to coat. Toss gently. Keep extra sauce for drizzling or serving on the side.

16. **Build the final bowl.** Add coconut-lime rice to each bowl. Add slaw beside the rice or slightly over one edge, using tongs so excess dressing stays behind. Pile sauced crispy shrimp as the main anchor. Top with crispy fried shallot rings, toasted coconut flakes, chopped toasted cashews, green onion, mint, and a lime wedge. Serve immediately with extra sauce on the side.

## Building the Final Bowl

| Layer | Component | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Coconut-Lime Rice Foundation | Add rice to each bowl and spread slightly so it can catch sauce without becoming buried. |
| 2 | Slaw Layer | Add purple cabbage-carrot mint slaw beside the rice or slightly over one edge. Keep the color visible. |
| 3 | Shrimp Anchor | Pile sauced crispy shrimp as the main anchor of the bowl. Let some sauce touch the rice, but do not cover everything. |
| 4 | Crunch Finish | Top with crispy fried shallot rings, toasted coconut flakes, and chopped toasted cashews. |
| 5 | Fresh Finish | Finish with green onion, mint, and lime wedge. Serve extra Bang Bang sauce on the side. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished bowl should be creamy, crispy, sweet, spicy, bright, savory, nutty, and fresh.

The shrimp should be crisp under a light coating of sauce. The rice should be soft, fragrant, coconut-lime bright, and lightly nutty from toasted coconut. The slaw should be sharp enough to cut the creamy shrimp but not harsh. The shallot rings should give a sweet-savory onion-ring crunch. The toasted coconut and cashews should add nutty depth without making the bowl heavy.

The whole bowl should feel layered, intentional, and restaurant-worthy.

## Chef's Notes

The homemade sweet chili sauce should be cooled before mixing into mayonnaise. Warm sauce can loosen the Bang Bang sauce.

The shrimp coating works best when the shrimp are damp from the short dairy soak but not dripping wet. Cornstarch gives the lightest crisp coating for this style of shrimp.

The slaw should rest before serving, but the mint, cashews, and crispy shallots should go in at the end.

Toasted coconut belongs as a finish for the rice, not inside the rice cooker.

The crispy shallot rings are a major bowl upgrade. Treat them as a final crunch element, not a cooked vegetable.
