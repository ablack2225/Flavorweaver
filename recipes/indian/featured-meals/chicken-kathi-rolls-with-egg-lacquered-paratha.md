# Chicken Kathi Rolls with Egg-Lacquered Paratha

> Scratch flaky roll paratha griddled with a thin egg lacquer, wrapped around charred yogurt-spiced chicken thighs, lime-dressed onion crunch, cilantro-mint green chutney, and a final hit of lime and chaat-style tang.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Element | Notes |
|---|---|
| Meal Role | Complete hot wrap / project meal |
| Main Dish | Yogurt-spiced chicken thighs seared until juicy, deeply browned, and lightly charred at the edges. |
| Bread | Scratch flaky roll paratha; thin, layered, flexible, lightly crisp, and egg-lacquered before filling. |
| Fresh Crunch | Onion-lime-chile crunch with cilantro and chaat-style finish. |
| Sauce / Condiment | Bright cilantro-mint-green chile chutney; punchy enough to carry through bread, egg, and chicken. |
| Table Finish | Lime wedges, extra chutney, sliced chile, cilantro, and optional chaat masala. |
| Critical Balance | Hot flaky bread + egg + charred spiced chicken must be cut by lime, onion, herbs, and chutney so the roll tastes bold but not heavy. |

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

- **Chicken marinade** — Marinate at least **2 hours**, preferably overnight, for deeper seasoning and better browning.
- **Paratha dough** — Can be mixed earlier the same day and rested. For best texture, do not rush the dough rests.
- **Green chutney** — Can be made up to **1 day ahead**, but re-brighten with lime before serving.
- **Onion crunch** — Best mixed **15–30 minutes before service** so it stays crisp but loses harshness.

## Service Countdown

- **Morning or night before** — Marinate chicken.
- **2 hours before serving** — Mix paratha dough and rest.
- **75 minutes before serving** — Make chutney; prep onions, herbs, and limes.
- **60 minutes before serving** — Laminate and coil parathas; rest again.
- **35 minutes before serving** — Cook chicken and hold warm loosely covered.
- **20 minutes before serving** — Griddle parathas, egg-lacquer each one, fill, roll, and serve hot.

## Do Not Forget

- This is a **scratch paratha recipe**, not naan, tortilla, or shortcut wrap.
- Rest the dough twice: once after mixing and once after coiling. Skipping rest makes paratha tough and hard to roll.
- Roll paratha thin enough to wrap but not so thin that it tears.
- Cook chicken in batches so it browns instead of steaming.
- The egg layer should be thin and bonded to the paratha, not a scrambled egg filling.
- Chutney should be bright, salty, spicy, and lime-forward.
- Onion crunch should stay crisp; do not overdress it hours ahead.
- Serve rolls hot. The magic is hot flaky bread + egg + charred chicken + cold bright chutney/onion.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Marinate the chicken.** In Bowl C2, whisk **1/3 cup yogurt**, **1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste**, **1 Tbsp lime juice**, **1 Tbsp oil**, **1 1/2 tsp garam masala**, **1 tsp cumin**, **1 tsp coriander**, **1 tsp paprika/Kashmiri chile**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/4–1/2 tsp cayenne**, **1 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/2 tsp black pepper**. Toss with **1 1/2 lb thin-sliced chicken thighs**. Cover and marinate **2 hours to overnight**.

2. **Mix paratha dough.** In Bowl P1, whisk **2 cups AP flour**, **3/4 tsp kosher salt**, and **1 tsp sugar**. Add Bowl P2: **2 Tbsp oil/ghee** and **3/4 cup warm water**. Mix until shaggy, then knead **6–8 minutes** until smooth and elastic. If dry, add **1 Tbsp warm water at a time**. Dough should be soft and slightly tacky, not stiff.

3. **Rest dough.** Cover dough and rest **45–60 minutes**. This rest is required for thin rolling and tenderness.

4. **Make chutney.** Blend Bowl S1: **1 cup cilantro**, **1/2 cup mint**, **1 serrano**, **1 garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp ginger**, **2 Tbsp lime juice**, **1 tsp sugar/honey**, **1/2 tsp salt**, and **2 Tbsp cold water**. Add more water only as needed. Taste and adjust salt/lime. It should taste slightly intense because it seasons the whole roll.

5. **Make onion-lime crunch.** Toss Bowl S2: **1 thin-sliced onion**, **1 Tbsp lime juice**, **1/4 tsp salt**, **1/4 tsp sugar**, **1/4 tsp paprika/chile**, **2 Tbsp cilantro**, optional **1/2 tsp chaat masala**, and optional serrano. Let sit **15–30 minutes**.

6. **Laminate parathas.** Divide rested dough into **4 balls**. Roll one ball into a thin circle or oval. Brush lightly with Bowl P3 layering fat. Pleat like a fan or roll into a rope, then coil into a spiral. Repeat with remaining dough. Cover coils and rest **20–30 minutes**.

7. **Cook chicken.** Heat electric skillet to about **300°F** with a thin film of oil. Add chicken in batches so pieces sear instead of steam. Cook **3–5 minutes per batch**, turning as needed, until browned, lightly charred at edges, and cooked through. Finish with Bowl C3: **1 Tbsp butter/ghee** and **1–2 tsp lime juice**. Hold warm loosely covered.

8. **Roll and griddle parathas.** Roll each rested coil into an **8–9 inch** paratha. Heat skillet around **300°F**. Cook each paratha with a little oil/ghee until lightly golden, flexible, and layered, about **1–2 minutes per side**. Do not cook until cracker-crisp; it must still wrap.

9. **Egg-lacquer each paratha.** Beat Bowl S3: **4 eggs** with a pinch of salt. For each roll, add a few drops oil/ghee to the skillet. Pour about **1 beaten egg** in a thin circle slightly smaller than the paratha. Immediately press one cooked paratha onto the wet egg. Cook until the egg bonds and sets, about **45–75 seconds**. Flip briefly to re-warm the outside.

10. **Fill each roll.** Place egg-side-up paratha on a board. Spread with chutney, add a line of hot chicken, add onion-lime crunch, cilantro, optional chaat masala, and a squeeze of lime. Do not overfill.

11. **Roll tight.** Fold the bottom slightly over the filling, tuck sides if needed, and roll firmly. For a street-roll finish, return seam-side down to the skillet for **30–45 seconds** to seal and lightly crisp.

12. **Serve immediately.** Serve hot with extra chutney, lime wedges, and optional chaat masala at the table.

## Electric Skillet Notes

- Amy's electric skillet runs hot; start chicken and paratha around **300°F**.
- If yogurt marinade darkens too fast, drop to **275°F** and keep turning.
- If paratha stays pale and oily, raise slightly toward **325°F**.
- Egg lacquer should set gently, not brown hard. Use **275–300°F** for the egg step if needed.

## Final Taste Target

The finished roll should be hot, flaky, juicy, tangy, and herb-bright: crisp-edged layered paratha, thin savory egg bonded to the bread, deeply spiced charred chicken, limey onion crunch, and vivid cilantro-mint chutney. It should eat like elite street food — bold, handheld, hot, fresh, and impossible to mistake for a generic chicken wrap.

## Chef's Notes

- The paratha is the ceiling-setter. If the bread is thick, dry, or bready, the roll drops below the Flavorweaver target.
- Chicken thighs are preferred because they stay juicy through high-heat skillet searing.
- The chutney and onion crunch are not garnish; they are required balance against bread, egg, and spiced chicken.
- Chaat masala is optional but useful for the street-food tang profile.
