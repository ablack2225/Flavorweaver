# Misir Wat

> Ethiopian-style spiced red lentils simmered with onion, garlic, ginger, tomato paste, and House Berbere-Style Spice Blend until thick, soft, spoonable, and deeply seasoned.

## Cross-Component Bulk Prep Map

- **Lentils:** rinse **1 cup red lentils** well and set aside.
- **Onion:** finely dice **1 small onion**.
- **Garlic:** mince **2 garlic cloves**.
- **Ginger:** grate or measure **1 tsp fresh ginger or ginger paste**.
- **Liquid:** measure **2 1/2 cups water or broth** first, with up to **1/2 cup more** available if the lentils thicken before softening.

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

| Bowl 3 — Spice & Tomato Base | 1 to 1 1/2 Tbsp House Berbere-Style Spice Blend; 1 Tbsp tomato paste | Bloom briefly with aromatics. |

## Do Not Forget

- Start with **2 1/2 cups** water or broth and add more only as needed.
- Stir more often near the end as the lentils thicken so they do not scorch.
- Add salt near the end after the lentils soften and the stew thickens.
- Use lemon only if the finished lentils taste heavy or flat.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Rinse the lentils.** Rinse **1 cup red lentils** well and set aside. |
| 2 | **Cook the onion.** In a pot, cook **1 small finely diced onion** in **1 Tbsp unsalted butter or oil** over medium heat until soft and reduced. |
| 3 | **Bloom the aromatics and spice.** Add **2 minced garlic cloves**, **1 tsp fresh grated ginger or ginger paste**, **1 to 1 1/2 Tbsp House Berbere-Style Spice Blend**, and **1 Tbsp tomato paste**. Cook **1 to 2 minutes**, stirring often, until fragrant and deepened. Do not let the spices burn. |
| 4 | **Add lentils and liquid.** Add the rinsed red lentils and **2 1/2 cups water or broth**. Stir well, scraping the bottom of the pot. |
| 5 | **Simmer.** Simmer uncovered for **20 to 25 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until the lentils soften, break down, and become thick. |
| 6 | **Adjust liquid.** Add up to **1/2 cup more water or broth** as needed if the lentils thicken before they are fully soft. |
| 7 | **Season.** Add salt near the end, once the lentils are soft and thick. |
| 8 | **Finish.** Add a small squeeze of lemon only if the lentils taste heavy. |

## Final Taste Target

Misir Wat should be thick, soft, spoonable, deeply seasoned, gently spicy, and rich without being watery. The red lentils should mostly break down into a cohesive stew.

## Chef's Notes

Misir Wat is the more traditional red-lentil direction for the Ethiopian platter. The working platter may use Split Pea Alicha when red lentils are unavailable, but Misir Wat is the component to use when building the more traditional-style version.

Misir Wat cooks faster than Split Pea Alicha. It can be started while Doro Wat-style chicken simmers and held warm briefly, but stir and loosen with water or broth if it thickens too much.
