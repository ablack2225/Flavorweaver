Bratkartoffeln-Style German Fried Potatoes

A shortcut electric-skillet version of German fried potatoes using cubed hashbrown potatoes, bacon, onion, caraway, marjoram, thyme, butter, and mandatory green onion for a crisp, smoky, savory schnitzel side.

Total45 min
Serves3 to 4
DifficultyEasy / Moderate
StatusRecreation Candidate
German-Inspiredbratkartoffelnfried potatoesbacon onion potatoesschnitzel sideelectric skillet

Kitchen Execution Summary

Cubed Hashbrown Potatoes

Shortcut base that crisps well in the electric skillet and keeps the side practical.

Bacon and Onion

Bacon gives smoky depth while onion adds sweetness and classic fried-potato flavor.

German Herb Profile

Caraway, marjoram, and thyme create a German-leaning flavor without overpowering the schnitzel plate.

Green Onion Finish

Mandatory fresh finish for color, lift, and balance.

Flavor Target

These potatoes should be crisp and golden on the outside, soft inside, smoky from bacon, savory-sweet from onion, buttery at the finish, lightly herbal from marjoram and thyme, and gently German-feeling from caraway.

Measured Ingredients

Main Potatoes

4 cups frozen cubed hashbrown potatoes; 4 slices bacon, chopped; 1 medium onion, diced or thin sliced; 1 Tbsp butter.

Seasoning

1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/4 tsp plain paprika; 1/8 tsp caraway seed, lightly crushed; 1/4 tsp dried marjoram; tiny pinch dried thyme.

Mandatory Finish

2 Tbsp green onions, thin sliced; optional tiny pat butter only if the potatoes need gloss.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Bacon

4 slices bacon, chopped. Cook first and reserve.

Bowl 2 — Onion

1 medium onion, diced or thin sliced. Cook after bacon.

Bowl 3 — Potato Base

4 cups frozen cubed hashbrown potatoes and 1 Tbsp butter.

Bowl 4 — Seasoning

1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp plain paprika, 1/8 tsp lightly crushed caraway seed, 1/4 tsp dried marjoram, tiny pinch dried thyme.

Bowl 5 — Mandatory Finish

2 Tbsp thin-sliced green onions; optional tiny pat butter.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Preheat skillet. Set electric skillet to 350°F and let it heat fully.
  2. Cook bacon. Add 4 slices chopped bacon to the dry skillet. Cook until crisp. Remove bacon to a plate. Leave about 2 Tbsp rendered bacon oil in the skillet; spoon off and reserve any extra.
  3. Cook onion. Add 1 medium diced or thin-sliced onion. Cook 5 to 7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and lightly golden. Remove onion and hold with the bacon.
  4. Raise heat and add potatoes. Turn skillet to 375°F. Add 1 Tbsp butter. Add 4 cups frozen cubed hashbrown potatoes in as even a layer as possible.
  5. Build crust. Let potatoes sit untouched 4 to 5 minutes before turning. Continue cooking 15 to 20 minutes total, turning every few minutes, until browned and crisp.
  6. Season. Once potatoes are mostly golden, sprinkle over 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp plain paprika, 1/8 tsp lightly crushed caraway seed, 1/4 tsp dried marjoram, and a tiny pinch dried thyme. Toss well and cook 1 to 2 minutes.
  7. Return bacon and onion. Add the cooked bacon and onion back to the skillet. Toss and cook 2 to 3 minutes, until hot and savory.
  8. Finish. Add 2 Tbsp thin-sliced green onions and toss briefly. Add a tiny pat of butter only if the potatoes need gloss. Taste and adjust salt or pepper.
  9. Serve. Plate as the savory starch beside pork schnitzel and Rotkohl. Keep potatoes away from cabbage juices so they stay crisp.

Do Not Forget

Spread the potatoes out in the electric skillet.

Remove onion before crisping potatoes.

Do not stir constantly; browning needs contact time.

Add seasoning near the end.

Green onion is mandatory for this version.

Do not add a mustard-vinegar finish here; Rotkohl already brings tang.
