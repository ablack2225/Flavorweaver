Chinese Orange Chicken

Crispy double-fried chicken thighs tossed in an extra-generous glossy orange sauce with orange juice, marmalade, garlic, ginger, honey, brown sugar, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and fresh orange zest.

Measured Ingredients

Chicken

3 boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces.

Marinade

3 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 Tbsp sesame oil; 1 1/2 tsp grated ginger; 4 cloves garlic, minced; 1/2 tsp black pepper.

Velveting

1 egg white; 3 Tbsp cornstarch.

Coating

1/2 cup flour; 1/2 cup cornstarch; 1/2 tsp baking powder; 1/2 tsp salt.

Extra Orange Sauce

3/4 cup premium orange juice; 3 Tbsp orange marmalade; 3 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 1/2 Tbsp honey; 3 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 1/2 Tbsp rice vinegar; 3 cloves garlic, grated; 1 1/2 tsp grated ginger; zest of 1 orange, generous; 1 1/2 tsp sesame oil.

Slurry and Finish

1 1/2 Tbsp cornstarch; 3 Tbsp cold water; green onion, sesame seeds, and fresh orange zest as desired.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Chicken Bowls

Bowl 1 — Chicken

3 boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces. Keep pieces even so they fry evenly.

Bowl 2 — Marinade

3 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 Tbsp sesame oil; 1 1/2 tsp grated ginger; 4 minced garlic cloves; 1/2 tsp black pepper. Marinate chicken 6 to 24 hours.

Bowl 3 — Velveting

1 egg white; 3 Tbsp cornstarch. Add after marination and before coating.

Bowl 4 — Dry Coating

1/2 cup flour; 1/2 cup cornstarch; 1/2 tsp baking powder; 1/2 tsp salt. Whisk well before coating chicken.

Sauce Bowls

Bowl 1 — Extra Orange Sauce

3/4 cup orange juice; 3 Tbsp orange marmalade; 3 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 1/2 Tbsp honey; 3 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 1/2 Tbsp rice vinegar; 3 grated garlic cloves; 1 1/2 tsp grated ginger; generous orange zest; 1 1/2 tsp sesame oil.

Bowl 2 — Slurry

1 1/2 Tbsp cornstarch; 3 Tbsp cold water. Stir smooth before adding to sauce.

Bowl 3 — Garnish

Green onion, sesame seeds, and fresh orange zest. Hold for service.

Do Not Forget

Marinate chicken 6 to 24 hours.

Cut chicken into even bite-size pieces so it fries evenly.

Add egg white and cornstarch after marination for the velveting step.

Double-fry the chicken: first fry at 350°F, rest 5 minutes, then second fry at 375°F.

Toss chicken with sauce while both are hot.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken. Cut 3 boneless skinless chicken thighs into bite-size pieces. Combine with 3 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 Tbsp sesame oil, 1 1/2 tsp grated ginger, 4 minced garlic cloves, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Marinate 6 to 24 hours.
  2. Velvet the chicken. Add 1 egg white and 3 Tbsp cornstarch to the marinated chicken. Toss until coated.
  3. Mix the dry coating. Whisk together 1/2 cup flour, 1/2 cup cornstarch, 1/2 tsp baking powder, and 1/2 tsp salt.
  4. Coat the chicken. Dredge the velveted chicken pieces in the dry coating. Shake off excess.
  5. First fry. Fry chicken at 350°F for 3 to 4 minutes, until lightly golden and cooked through. Work in batches so the oil temperature stays steady.
  6. Rest. Let fried chicken rest 5 minutes on a rack.
  7. Second fry. Fry again at 375°F for 1 to 2 minutes, until deeply crisp. Drain on a rack.
  8. Make the extra orange sauce. Simmer 3/4 cup orange juice, 3 Tbsp orange marmalade, 3 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 1/2 Tbsp honey, 3 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 1/2 Tbsp rice vinegar, 3 grated garlic cloves, 1 1/2 tsp grated ginger, generous orange zest, and 1 1/2 tsp sesame oil until cohesive.
  9. Thicken the sauce. Stir together 1 1/2 Tbsp cornstarch and 3 Tbsp cold water. Add slurry to the simmering sauce and cook until glossy.
  10. Toss and finish. Toss hot crispy chicken with glossy orange sauce. Garnish with green onion, sesame seeds, and fresh orange zest. Serve immediately.

Building the Final Plate

1. Rice or Side Base

Serve with Restaurant-Style Fried Rice or steamed rice.

2. Orange Chicken Anchor

Pile the glossy crispy orange chicken as the main dish.

3. Garnish Finish

Finish with green onion, sesame seeds, and fresh orange zest.

4. Optional Appetizer

Serve with Premium Crab Rangoon if making the full dinner.

Final Taste Target

The finished chicken should be crisp, glossy, citrus-forward, lightly sweet, savory, garlicky, gingery, and bright from fresh orange zest.

The sauce should be generous enough to coat the chicken and lightly pool without making the coating soggy.
