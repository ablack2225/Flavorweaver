Lemon Cream Cheese Pound Cake

A rich, velvety, deeply lemony cream cheese pound cake built from fresh lemons, lemon sugar, lemon-infused dairy, warm lemon syrup, and a cream cheese lemon glaze. Designed for a moist, dense-but-tender crumb with no lemon extract required.

TypeDessert / Bundt Cake
TotalAbout 3 hr including cooling
Yield1 large cake / 12 to 16 slices

Kitchen Execution Summary

Fresh Lemon Architecture

The lemon flavor is layered through zest rubbed into sugar, lemon peel steeped into dairy, controlled lemon juice in the batter, warm lemon syrup, and lemon cream cheese glaze.

Moist Cream Cheese Crumb

Butter, cream cheese, sour cream, egg yolk, shortening, and a little neutral oil create a rich pound cake crumb that stays moist after cooling.

AP Flour Shortcut

All-purpose flour is blended with cornstarch to mimic the tenderness of cake flour while keeping the recipe aligned with Amy's current pantry.

Critical Bake Cue

Start checking around 70 minutes. The center should be about 200 to 205°F, with a skewer coming out clean or with a few moist crumbs.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

Room-temperature ingredients — Bring butter, cream cheese, eggs, sour cream, and the strained lemon-infused milk or cream to room temperature before mixing.

Pan prep — Use a 12 to 15 cup Bundt pan or tube pan. Grease every crease thoroughly with shortening or softened butter, then flour the pan and tap out excess.

Lemon prep — Zest 5 lemons for the lemon sugar. Use only yellow peel; avoid the bitter white pith.

No extract needed — Do not add lemon extract. The fresh lemon layering is the flavor strategy.

Service Countdown

45 minutes before mixing: pull refrigerated ingredients so they can come toward room temperature.

30 minutes before mixing: rub lemon zest into sugar and let it rest; steep lemon peel strips in warm milk or cream, then strain and cool.

Before batter mixing: preheat oven to 325°F, prepare the Bundt pan, and sift the AP flour/cornstarch dry mix.

After baking: cool in pan 15 to 20 minutes, syrup while warm, invert, syrup the outside, then cool completely before glazing.

At service: slice after the glaze sets. The cake is excellent same day and often even better the next day.

Full Cake Ingredient Roll-Up

Cake Flour Shortcut Dry Mix

All-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, baking soda, and fine salt.

Granulated sugar rubbed with zest from 5 lemons, plus lemon peel strips steeped into whole milk or heavy cream.

Unsalted butter, cream cheese, shortening, neutral oil, eggs, extra yolk, sour cream, lemon-infused milk or cream, fresh lemon juice, and vanilla.

Measured Ingredients by Component

These component sections are reference only. The cooking flow is handled by the stage-based prep bowls and full timeline below.

Cake Flour Shortcut Dry Mix

300 g all-purpose flour, or 3 cups minus 6 Tbsp; 45 g cornstarch, or 6 Tbsp; 2 tsp baking powder; 1/4 tsp baking soda; 3/4 tsp fine salt.

Lemon Sugar

2 3/4 cups granulated sugar; zest from 5 lemons, yellow peel only.

Lemon-Infused Dairy

1/2 cup whole milk or heavy cream; wide yellow peel strips from 1 to 2 lemons. Warm the dairy until steaming, steep the peel 15 to 20 minutes, strain, and cool.

Cream Cheese Fat Base

1 cup / 2 sticks unsalted butter, softened; 6 oz cream cheese, softened; 2 Tbsp shortening; 2 Tbsp neutral oil.

Eggs and Main Liquids

5 large eggs, room temperature; 1 extra egg yolk; 1/2 cup sour cream; 1/2 cup strained lemon-infused milk or cream; 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice; 2 tsp vanilla extract.

Lemon Syrup

1/2 cup fresh lemon juice; 1/2 cup granulated sugar; tiny pinch of salt.

Lemon Cream Cheese Glaze

2 oz very soft cream cheese; 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar; 2 to 3 Tbsp fresh lemon juice; 1 to 2 Tbsp milk or heavy cream; 1/2 tsp vanilla extract; tiny pinch of salt; optional lemon zest.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Lemons: zest 5 lemons for the sugar, use strips of yellow peel from 1 to 2 lemons for the dairy infusion, reserve 1/4 cup juice for the batter, 1/2 cup juice for the syrup, and 2 to 3 Tbsp juice for the glaze.

Dairy: cream cheese appears in both the batter and glaze. Use 6 oz softened cream cheese in the batter and 2 oz very soft cream cheese in the glaze.

Flour blend: use 300 g AP flour plus 45 g cornstarch for the cake-flour-style shortcut. Sift if possible.

Lemon intensity: do not increase batter lemon juice beyond 1/4 cup. Push extra lemon through zest, syrup, and glaze instead.

Timing: syrup the cake warm; glaze the cake only after it is fully cool.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Lemon and Dry Prep

Bowl 1 — Lemon Sugar

2 3/4 cups granulated sugar rubbed with zest from 5 lemons; rest 20 to 30 minutes if time allows.

Bowl 2 — Lemon-Infused Dairy

1/2 cup whole milk or heavy cream steeped with yellow peel strips from 1 to 2 lemons, then strained and cooled.

Bowl 3 — Dry Mix

300 g AP flour; 45 g cornstarch; 2 tsp baking powder; 1/4 tsp baking soda; 3/4 tsp fine salt.

Batter and Finish Prep

Bowl 1 — Creaming Base

1 cup softened unsalted butter; 6 oz softened cream cheese; 2 Tbsp shortening; 2 Tbsp neutral oil.

Bowl 2 — Eggs

5 large room-temperature eggs plus 1 extra egg yolk. Add gradually during mixing.

Bowl 3 — Liquid Mixture

1/2 cup sour cream; 1/2 cup cooled lemon-infused milk or cream; 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice; 2 tsp vanilla extract.

Bowl 4 — Syrup and Glaze

Syrup: 1/2 cup lemon juice, 1/2 cup sugar, pinch salt. Glaze: 2 oz cream cheese, 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar, lemon juice, milk or cream, vanilla, salt.

Do Not Forget

Use room-temperature dairy and eggs so the batter emulsifies instead of splitting.

Grease and flour the Bundt pan aggressively, especially if it has deep decorative ridges.

Do not overmix once the flour blend goes in. Finish with a spatula.

Start checking doneness around 70 minutes, but expect 75 to 90 minutes depending on pan and oven.

Syrup while warm, glaze when completely cool.

For more lemon, increase zest, syrup, or glaze intensity — not batter lemon juice.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Build the lemon sugar. Add 2 3/4 cups granulated sugar to a bowl with the zest from 5 lemons. Rub the zest into the sugar until the sugar looks slightly damp and smells intensely lemony. Let it rest 20 to 30 minutes if time allows.
  2. Infuse the dairy. Warm 1/2 cup whole milk or heavy cream until steaming but not boiling. Add wide yellow peel strips from 1 to 2 lemons, cover, and steep 15 to 20 minutes. Strain and cool to room temperature.
  3. Prepare the oven and pan. Preheat the oven to 325°F. Grease a 12 to 15 cup Bundt pan or tube pan thoroughly, then dust with flour and tap out excess.
  4. Make the dry mix. Whisk together 300 g AP flour, 45 g cornstarch, 2 tsp baking powder, 1/4 tsp baking soda, and 3/4 tsp fine salt. Sift if possible.
  5. Cream the fat base. Beat 1 cup softened unsalted butter, 6 oz softened cream cheese, 2 Tbsp shortening, and 2 Tbsp neutral oil until smooth. Add the lemon sugar and beat 4 to 5 minutes, scraping the bowl as needed, until lighter and fluffy.
  6. Add the eggs gradually. Add 5 large room-temperature eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Add the extra egg yolk last. Scrape the bowl well and keep the mixture emulsified.
  7. Combine the liquid mixture. Stir together 1/2 cup sour cream, 1/2 cup cooled lemon-infused milk or cream, 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice, and 2 tsp vanilla extract.
  8. Alternate dry and wet additions. On low speed, add one third of the dry mix, half of the liquid mixture, another third of dry mix, the remaining liquid mixture, then the remaining dry mix. Mix only until combined and finish with a spatula.
  9. Fill and bake. Spoon batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake at 325°F for 75 to 90 minutes. The cake is done when a skewer comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, the top springs back lightly, the cake pulls slightly from the pan edges, and the center is about 200 to 205°F.
  10. Make the lemon syrup. Warm 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice, 1/2 cup granulated sugar, and a tiny pinch of salt just until the sugar dissolves.
  11. Syrup the warm cake. Cool in pan 15 to 20 minutes. Poke the exposed bottom lightly with a skewer and brush on part of the warm syrup. Invert carefully, then brush the outside with more syrup while still warm.
  12. Cool and glaze. Let the cake cool completely. Beat 2 oz very soft cream cheese smooth, then add 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar, 2 to 3 Tbsp fresh lemon juice, 1/2 tsp vanilla, and a tiny pinch of salt. Thin with 1 to 2 Tbsp milk or cream only as needed. Spoon over the cooled cake and finish with optional zest.
  13. Serve. Slice after the glaze sets. The crumb should be rich, moist, dense-but-tender, creamy at the edges, and brightly lemony from crumb to finish.

Final Taste Target

The finished cake should taste like an old-fashioned luxury lemon pound cake crossed with cream cheese pound cake: buttery, creamy, moist, dense but not heavy, vividly lemony, lightly tangy, and polished enough to serve plain without needing berries, cream, or sauce.

Chef's Notes

The lemon extract is intentionally omitted. Fresh zest, lemon-infused dairy, lemon syrup, and lemon glaze provide a cleaner, brighter lemon profile.

Cream cheese and sour cream should create tang and moisture, not cheesecake heaviness. The cake should still slice like pound cake and hold its structure.

The AP flour and cornstarch shortcut is part of this house version because cake flour is not currently available in Amy's kitchen.

Troubleshooting

IssueFix
Cake tastes less lemony than expectedAdd extra fresh zest to the glaze or brush a little more syrup over the sliced side before serving. Do not increase batter lemon juice next time; increase zest, syrup, or glaze instead.
Cake is dryIt likely baked too long or had too much flour. Use grams for the AP/cornstarch shortcut and check internal temperature near 200°F.
Cake is gummy in the centerIt was underbaked, overmixed after flour, or sliced before the crumb set. Bake until the center reaches about 200 to 205°F and cool fully.
Cake stuck to panUse shortening or softened butter plus flour in every Bundt crease. Cool only 15 to 20 minutes before inverting.
