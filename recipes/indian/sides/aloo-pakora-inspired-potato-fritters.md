# Aloo Pakora-Inspired Potato Fritters

> Pantry-modified potato fritters built from the aloo pakora method: thin potato slices, spiced coating batter, and hot frying oil. This version uses AP flour, cornstarch, dried thyme, and crushed cumin seed until besan, rice flour, ajwain, amchur, and kala namak are stocked.

## Do Not Forget

- Slice potatoes about **1/8 inch thick**.
- Soak potatoes **10 to 15 minutes**, then dry very well.
- Batter should be medium-thick and cling in a thin layer.
- If the coating tastes bready, thin the batter slightly.
- If the coating slides off, add **1 tsp AP flour plus 1 tsp cornstarch**.
- Add **1 tsp hot frying oil** to the batter after the oil heats.
- Fry in batches and do not overcrowd.
- Serve hot.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Slice and soak potatoes.** Slice **2 medium potatoes** into **1/8-inch rounds**. Soak in cold water **10 to 15 minutes**. Drain and dry very well. |
| 2 | **Mix dry batter.** In a bowl, whisk together **1/2 cup AP flour**, **3 Tbsp cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp ground coriander**, **1/4 tsp garam masala**, **1/4 tsp paprika or chili powder**, **1/8 tsp turmeric**, **1/8 tsp dried thyme**, **1/8 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed**, and optional **pinch cayenne**. |
| 3 | **Add wet ingredients.** Add **1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste** and **1/3 cup water**. Whisk smooth. Add more water **1 tsp at a time** until the batter coats potato slices in a thin layer. |
| 4 | **Heat oil.** Heat neutral oil to about **350°F**. If not using a thermometer, drop in a tiny bit of batter; it should rise steadily and bubble without turning dark immediately. |
| 5 | **Add hot oil to batter.** Stir **1 tsp hot frying oil** into the batter. |
| 6 | **Fry potatoes.** Dip potato slices into batter one at a time. Let excess drip off briefly. Fry in batches until golden and crisp, about **3 to 5 minutes per batch**, flipping as needed. |
| 7 | **Drain and finish.** Drain on a rack or paper towels. While hot, sprinkle lightly with salt, a tiny pinch garam masala, and a tiny pinch cumin. Add a tiny squeeze lemon or lime right before serving if desired. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished fritters should be golden and crisp outside, tender inside, savory and gently spiced, with enough crunch to contrast creamy curries.

## Chef's Notes

This version exists because the proper ingredients were not yet stocked. It is designed to keep dinner moving while preserving the role of aloo pakora on the plate: crispy potato, spiced batter, fried texture, and curry contrast.

The AP flour and cornstarch combination is a temporary bridge. It can make a good crispy potato fritter, but it will not have the nutty flavor, color, or traditional texture of besan-based pakora.

## Future Traditional Upgrade Notes

When stocked, rebuild this recipe with:

- Besan / gram flour as the primary batter base
- Rice flour for crispness
- Ajwain / carom seed instead of thyme-cumin substitute
- Amchur powder or house chaat masala for finish
- Kala namak / black salt if making house chaat masala

Future test should compare:

1. Pantry AP-cornstarch version
2. Besan + rice flour version
3. Besan + rice flour + hot oil batter finish
4. Final chaat masala / amchur finish
