Smokehouse Pulled Pork Hawaiian-Style Sliders

Oven-seared pork butt, pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus, sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch, and from-scratch Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls built for a food-truck-level pulled pork slider plate.

Flavor Target

Smoky, savory, pull-apart pork with sweet barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar lift, soft pineapple-butter rolls, and sweet-spicy pickle brightness.

This should not taste like generic slow-cooker pork in bottled sauce. The difference comes from overnight seasoning, a hard sear, oven braising, reduced pork jus, and a broiled bark finish.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Overnight Smokehouse Pork

Pork butt is salted, slathered, rubbed, and chilled 8 to 18 hours so the seasoning penetrates beyond the surface.

Oven Braise

A hard sear builds crust, then pineapple juice, vinegar, broth, onion, garlic, mustard, brown sugar, and Worcestershire build a tangy BBQ jus.

BBQ Jus Glaze

Reduced pork cooking juices become the base for a glossy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze.

Broiled Bark Finish

Lightly sauced pulled pork is broiled so the edges turn sticky, smoky, and caramelized.

Hawaiian-Style Rolls

Pineapple juice, milk, eggs, butter, brown sugar, and dry potato flakes make plush sweet rolls sturdy enough for sliders.

Pickle-Onion Crunch

Sweet-spicy pickles, purple onion, mustard, honey, brine, pepper, and cayenne cut the richness.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

8 to 18 hours before cooking: salt the pork, apply wet slather, apply dry rub, cover, and refrigerate.

Cook day morning: pull pork from the fridge 30 to 45 minutes before searing.

Early cook day: start Hawaiian-style roll dough so rolls can rise while pork braises.

While pork braises: make pickle-onion crunch and chill.

After pork braises: strain and skim juices, build BBQ jus glaze, shred pork, glaze lightly, and broil for barky edges.

Service Countdown

Night before: salt, slather, rub, and refrigerate pork.

7 to 8 hours before serving: start roll dough, pull pork from fridge, preheat oven, and prepare braise base.

6 to 7 hours before serving: sear pork, build braise, cover, and oven-braise at 300°F.

3 to 4 hours before serving: shape rolls and let rise.

1 hour before serving: strain pork juices, skim fat, simmer BBQ jus glaze, pull pork, and heat rolls if needed.

30 minutes before serving: broil pulled pork for barky edges; slice and toast rolls.

At service: build sliders with glaze, pork, pickle-onion crunch, optional cheese, and honey-buttered tops.

Measured Ingredients by Component

These component sections are reference only. The cooking flow is handled by the stage-based prep bowls and full timeline below.

Overnight Pork Seasoning

4 to 5 lb pork butt or pork shoulder, bone-in or boneless; 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt, about 1/2 tsp per pound.

Wet Slather

2 Tbsp yellow mustard; 1 Tbsp hot sauce; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar or sweet-spicy pickle brine; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp brown sugar.

Dry Rub

1 Tbsp smoked paprika; 1 Tbsp paprika; 1 Tbsp chili powder; 2 tsp garlic powder; 2 tsp onion powder; 2 tsp mustard powder; 1 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 tsp ground coriander; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp chipotle powder or cayenne; optional 1/4 tsp ground ginger; optional tiny pinch cinnamon. Do not add extra salt to the dry rub if the pork has already been salted overnight.

Oven Braising Base

1 large sliced onion; 6 smashed garlic cloves; 1/2 cup pineapple juice; 1/2 cup chicken broth, apple juice, or water; 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp yellow/stone-ground mustard; optional tiny dash liquid smoke.

Pineapple-Vinegar BBQ Jus Glaze

1 cup reduced pork cooking juices; 1/2 cup tomato sauce or ketchup; 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 Tbsp honey or molasses; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp yellow mustard or stone-ground mustard; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1/2 tsp chili powder; 1/4 tsp black pepper; optional pinch cayenne; optional 1 Tbsp bourbon or whiskey.

Hawaiian-Style Sweet Slider Rolls

1/4 cup warm water at 100°F to 110°F; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1 Tbsp sugar; 1/2 cup warm pineapple juice; 1/2 cup warm milk; 2 large eggs; 1 egg yolk; 1/3 cup brown sugar; 4 Tbsp unsalted butter; 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 2 Tbsp plain instant potato flakes; 3 3/4 cups bread flour or AP flour; optional 1 tsp vanilla; egg wash from 1 egg white or 1 whole egg plus 1 Tbsp water or milk; butter top from 2 Tbsp melted butter, optional 1 tsp honey, and tiny pinch salt.

Use plain instant potato flakes dry. If using fresh pineapple juice, heat it to 200°F, then cool to lukewarm before adding it to the dough.

Sweet-Spicy Pickle-Onion Crunch

1/2 cup finely chopped sweet-spicy pickles; 1/4 cup finely minced purple onion; 1 Tbsp pickle brine; 1 tsp yellow mustard or stone-ground mustard; 1 tsp honey or brown sugar; pinch black pepper; optional pinch cayenne.

Slider Assembly

12 Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls; warm pulled pork with broiled barky edges; pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus glaze; sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch; optional extra pickles; optional thin purple onion; optional pepper jack, cheddar, or provolone; honey butter for brushing tops.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Pineapple juice: appears in the braise and roll dough. Warm the dough portion; keep the braise portion measured separately.

Brown sugar: appears in the slather, braise, glaze, rolls, and optional crunch. Stage by component so the roll sugar does not get confused with BBQ sugar.

Worcestershire: appears in the slather, braise, and glaze for savory depth in small amounts.

Mustard: appears in the slather, braise, glaze, and pickle crunch. Use mustard powder or prepared mustard as directed.

Pork cooking juices: strain, skim, and reduce before building the BBQ jus glaze.

Pickle brine: can appear in the slather and crunch. Sweet-spicy pickle brine is especially good here.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Night-Before Pork Bowls

Bowl 1 — Pork Salt

4 to 5 lb pork butt or shoulder; 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.

Bowl 2 — Wet Slather

2 Tbsp yellow mustard; 1 Tbsp hot sauce; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp vinegar or pickle brine; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp brown sugar.

Bowl 3 — Dry Rub

1 Tbsp smoked paprika; 1 Tbsp paprika; 1 Tbsp chili powder; 2 tsp garlic powder; 2 tsp onion powder; 2 tsp mustard powder; 1 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 tsp coriander; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp chipotle/cayenne; optional ginger and cinnamon.

Cook-Day Pork Bowls

Bowl 4 — Braise Bed

1 large sliced onion; 6 smashed garlic cloves.

Bowl 5 — Braise Liquid

1/2 cup pineapple juice; 1/2 cup broth/apple juice/water; 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp prepared mustard; optional tiny liquid smoke.

Bowl 6 — BBQ Jus Glaze

1 cup reduced pork juices; 1/2 cup tomato sauce/ketchup; 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 Tbsp honey/molasses; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp mustard; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1/2 tsp chili powder; 1/4 tsp black pepper; optional cayenne and bourbon.

Roll Bowls

Bowl 7 — Yeast Bloom

1/4 cup warm water; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1 Tbsp sugar.

Bowl 8 — Wet Dough Additions

1/2 cup warm pineapple juice; 1/2 cup warm milk; 2 eggs; 1 egg yolk; 4 Tbsp butter; optional 1 tsp vanilla.

Bowl 9 — Dry Dough Additions

1/3 cup brown sugar; 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 2 Tbsp dry potato flakes; 3 3/4 cups flour.

Bowl 10 — Roll Finish

Egg wash: 1 egg white or whole egg + 1 Tbsp water/milk. Butter top: 2 Tbsp melted butter + optional 1 tsp honey + tiny pinch salt.

Crunch and Assembly Bowls

Bowl 11 — Pickle-Onion Crunch

1/2 cup chopped sweet-spicy pickles; 1/4 cup minced purple onion; 1 Tbsp pickle brine; 1 tsp mustard; 1 tsp honey/brown sugar; pinch black pepper; optional pinch cayenne.

Bowl 12 — Slider Assembly

Split rolls; warm pulled pork; BBQ jus glaze; pickle-onion crunch; optional cheese; honey butter.

Do Not Forget

Do not skip the overnight seasoning, sear, reduced cooking juices, or broiled bark finish.

Do not stop the pork at 165°F; pulled pork tenderness usually lands around 195°F to 203°F.

Do not drown the pork in sauce; glaze lightly and keep it juicy, not wet.

Do not over-flour the Hawaiian-style roll dough. Use plain instant potato flakes dry; do not make mashed potatoes first.

Do not use fresh pineapple juice unless it is heated to 200°F and cooled first.

Toast the roll cut sides before building sliders.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Season pork the night before. Pat 4 to 5 lb pork butt or shoulder very dry. Trim only large thick pieces of surface fat. Rub evenly with 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.
  2. Apply wet slather. Mix 2 Tbsp yellow mustard, 1 Tbsp hot sauce, 1 Tbsp Worcestershire, 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar or pickle brine, 1 Tbsp neutral oil, 1 Tbsp tomato paste, and 2 Tbsp brown sugar. Coat pork on all sides.
  3. Apply dry rub and refrigerate. Mix 1 Tbsp smoked paprika, 1 Tbsp paprika, 1 Tbsp chili powder, 2 tsp garlic powder, 2 tsp onion powder, 2 tsp mustard powder, 1 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp chipotle/cayenne, optional 1/4 tsp ginger, and optional tiny cinnamon. Coat pork heavily. Refrigerate 8 to 18 hours.
  4. Temper pork. Remove pork from the refrigerator 30 to 45 minutes before cooking. Do not leave it out for hours.
  5. Start Hawaiian-style rolls. In mixer bowl, combine 1/4 cup warm water, 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast, and 1 Tbsp sugar. Rest 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.
  6. Mix dough. Add 1/2 cup warm pineapple juice, 1/2 cup warm milk, 2 eggs, 1 egg yolk, 1/3 cup brown sugar, 4 Tbsp butter, 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 2 Tbsp dry potato flakes, 3 3/4 cups flour, and optional 1 tsp vanilla. Mix low until shaggy, then knead with dough hook 8 to 10 minutes until soft, tacky, and elastic.
  7. First rise. Place dough in greased bowl, cover, and let rise 1 1/2 to 2 hours, until very puffy and nearly doubled.
  8. Sear pork. Heat Dutch oven or heavy skillet over medium-high heat with 1 to 2 Tbsp neutral oil. Sear pork on all sides until dark mahogany, not burned black sugar.
  9. Build braise. Place 1 sliced large onion and 6 smashed garlic cloves in Dutch oven or roasting pan. Add 1/2 cup pineapple juice, 1/2 cup broth/apple juice/water, 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar, 1 Tbsp Worcestershire, 1 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp prepared mustard, and optional tiny liquid smoke. Place seared pork on top and cover tightly.
  10. Oven-braise. Bake at 300°F until fork-shreddable: about 4 1/2 to 6 hours for large hunks or 6 to 7 hours for whole bone-in pork butt. Start checking around 4 1/2 hours.
  11. Shape rolls during braise. Grease or line a 9x13 pan. Divide dough into 12 equal pieces, shape into tight smooth balls, place in pan, cover, and let rise 45 to 75 minutes until puffy and touching or nearly touching.
  12. Bake rolls. Brush risen rolls with egg wash. Bake at 350°F for 20 to 25 minutes, until golden and about 190°F in the center. Brush hot rolls with 2 Tbsp melted butter, optional 1 tsp honey, and a tiny pinch salt. Cool at least 20 minutes before slicing.
  13. Rest pork and save juices. Transfer pork to a tray and rest 20 to 30 minutes. Strain cooking juices, skim excess fat, and reserve juices for the BBQ jus glaze.
  14. Make BBQ jus glaze. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup reduced pork cooking juices, 1/2 cup tomato sauce or ketchup, 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar, 2 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 Tbsp honey or molasses, 1 Tbsp Worcestershire, 1 Tbsp mustard, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp chili powder, 1/4 tsp black pepper, optional cayenne, and optional 1 Tbsp bourbon/whiskey. Simmer 10 to 15 minutes until glossy.
  15. Make pickle-onion crunch. Mix 1/2 cup chopped sweet-spicy pickles, 1/4 cup minced purple onion, 1 Tbsp pickle brine, 1 tsp mustard, 1 tsp honey or brown sugar, a pinch black pepper, and optional cayenne. Chill until serving.
  16. Pull pork. Shred pork into large tender strands. Discard large pieces of fat but keep barky pieces. Add a few spoonfuls of reduced pork juices and a small amount of BBQ jus glaze. Toss gently so pork is juicy and glossy, not swimming.
  17. Broil for barky edges. Spread sauced pulled pork on a sheet pan. Broil 3 to 6 minutes, watching closely, until some edges crisp and caramelize. Toss once and broil 1 to 2 minutes more if needed.
  18. Toast rolls. Slice rolls and lightly toast cut sides with butter.
  19. Build sliders. Add a thin spoonful of BBQ jus glaze to the bottom roll, then warm pulled pork, pickle-onion crunch, optional cheese, and top roll. Brush tops with honey butter.
  20. Warm tray sliders if serving together. Warm covered at 325°F for 8 to 10 minutes. Uncover 2 to 3 minutes to set the tops.

BBQ Jus Adjustment Notes

Too sweet: add more apple cider vinegar. Too sharp: add brown sugar or honey. Flat: add salt or a splash more reduced pork jus. Too gentle: add cayenne or hot sauce. Too thick: add pork jus. Too thin: simmer longer.

Building the Final Slider

1. Toasted Roll

Split Hawaiian-style roll and toast the cut sides lightly with butter.

2. BBQ Jus Base

Add a thin spoonful of pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus glaze to the bottom roll.

3. Pork Anchor

Pile on warm pulled pork with broiled barky edges.

4. Bright Crunch

Add a small spoonful of sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch.

5. Optional Cheese

Add pepper jack for creamy heat, cheddar for classic BBQ, or provolone for mild melt.

6. Honey-Butter Top

Add top roll, brush with honey butter, and warm as tray sliders if desired.

Final Taste Target

The finished slider should hit in layers: soft buttery pineapple roll, smoky savory pork, sticky barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze, sweet-spicy pickle crunch, warm BBQ spice, and a clean acidic finish.

It should be sweet, but not candy-sweet. It should be saucy, but not wet. It should feel like barbecue met homemade sweet-roll nostalgia and became a food-truck slider worth chasing again.
