# Bratkartoffeln-Style German Fried Potatoes

> A shortcut electric-skillet version of German fried potatoes using cubed hashbrown potatoes, bacon, onion, caraway, marjoram, thyme, butter, and mandatory green onion for a crisp, smoky, savory schnitzel side.

## Flavor Target

These potatoes should be crisp and golden on the outside, soft inside, smoky from bacon, savory-sweet from onion, buttery at the finish, lightly herbal from marjoram and thyme, and gently German-feeling from caraway. The caraway should stay in the background.

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

- Keep the frozen cubed potatoes frozen until cooking.
- Chop bacon and onion ahead if needed.
- Lightly crush the caraway seed before cooking if possible.
- Slice the green onions shortly before finishing so they taste fresh.

## Cross-Component Prep Map

- Cook bacon first so the potatoes can cook in the seasoned pan.
- Cook onion before potatoes, then remove it so it does not overbrown while the potatoes crisp.
- Do not stir constantly. Let the cubes sit long enough to develop browned edges.
- Add herbs and spices near the end so garlic powder, paprika, caraway, marjoram, and thyme stay fragrant.
- Add green onions at the very end so they stay fresh and bright.
- Do not add vinegar or mustard to this version. The Rotkohl already carries the sweet-sour role.

## Do Not Forget

- Use the electric skillet surface to your advantage: spread the potatoes out.
- Cook bacon first.
- Remove onion before crisping potatoes.
- Do not stir constantly; browning needs contact time.
- Add seasoning near the end.
- Green onion is mandatory for this version.
- Do not add a mustard-vinegar finish here; Rotkohl already brings tang.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Preheat skillet.** Set electric skillet to **350°F** and let it heat fully. |
| 2 | **Cook bacon.** Add **4 slices chopped bacon** to the dry skillet. Cook until crisp. Remove bacon to a plate. Leave about **2 Tbsp drippings** in the skillet; spoon off and reserve any extra. |
| 3 | **Cook onion.** Add **1 medium diced or thin-sliced onion**. Cook **5 to 7 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until softened and lightly golden. Remove onion and hold with the bacon. |
| 4 | **Raise heat and add potatoes.** Turn skillet to **375°F**. Add **1 Tbsp butter**. Add **4 cups frozen cubed hashbrown potatoes** in as even a layer as possible. |
| 5 | **Build crust.** Let potatoes sit untouched **4 to 5 minutes** before turning. Continue cooking **15 to 20 minutes total**, turning every few minutes, until browned and crisp. |
| 6 | **Season.** Once potatoes are mostly golden, sprinkle over **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp black pepper**, **1/2 tsp garlic powder**, **1/4 tsp plain paprika**, **1/8 tsp lightly crushed caraway seed**, **1/4 tsp dried marjoram**, and a **tiny pinch dried thyme**. Toss well and cook **1 to 2 minutes**. |
| 7 | **Return bacon and onion.** Add the cooked bacon and onion back to the skillet. Toss and cook **2 to 3 minutes**, until hot and deeply savory. |
| 8 | **Finish.** Add **2 Tbsp thin-sliced green onions** and toss briefly. Add a tiny pat of butter only if the potatoes need gloss. Taste and adjust salt or pepper. |
| 9 | **Serve.** Plate as the savory starch beside pork schnitzel and Rotkohl. Keep potatoes away from cabbage juices so they stay crisp. |

## Final Balance Map

| Issue | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Tastes flat | Add a pinch more salt. |
| Needs more bite | Add a little more black pepper. |
| Too dry | Add a tiny pat butter or spoon of reserved drippings. |
| Too herbal | Add a tiny pat butter and more green onion. |
| Not crisp enough | Spread out and cook longer without stirring constantly. |
| Too rich | Serve with lemony schnitzel and Rotkohl; do not add vinegar to the potatoes. |

## Plate Role

These potatoes anchor the Pork Loin Schnitzel with Rotkohl plate. Schnitzel brings crisp fried pork, Rotkohl brings glossy sweet-sour cabbage, these potatoes bring smoky-salty savory crunch, and lemon brings brightness.
