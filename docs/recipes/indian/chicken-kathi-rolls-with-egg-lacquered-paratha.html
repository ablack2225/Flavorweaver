Chicken Kathi Rolls with Egg-Lacquered Paratha

Scratch flaky roll paratha griddled with a thin egg lacquer, wrapped around charred yogurt-spiced chicken thighs, lime-dressed onion crunch, cilantro-mint green chutney, and a final hit of lime and chaat-style tang.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Scratch Flaky Paratha

Thin, layered, flexible griddle bread made from scratch with AP flour, warm water, oil/ghee, rest, fat-brush lamination, and coil rolling.

Egg Lacquer

A thin egg layer is cooked onto each paratha before filling so the wrap eats like a true kathi roll instead of a generic chicken wrap.

Yogurt-Spiced Chicken

Chicken thighs marinated with yogurt, ginger-garlic, lime, garam masala, cumin, coriander, chile, turmeric, salt, and pepper, then skillet-seared.

Green Chutney

Cilantro, mint, green chile, lime, garlic, ginger, salt, and a little sweetness for a bright, punchy sauce.

Onion-Lime Crunch

Thin onion dressed with lime, salt, chile, cilantro, and optional chaat masala for street-food tang.

Service Countdown

Morning or night before: marinate chicken.

2 hours before serving: mix paratha dough and rest.

75 minutes before serving: make chutney and prep onions/herbs/limes.

60 minutes before serving: laminate and coil parathas; rest again.

35 minutes before serving: cook chicken and hold warm loosely covered.

20 minutes before serving: griddle parathas, egg-lacquer each one, fill, roll, and serve hot.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Scratch Flaky Roll Paratha

2 cups AP flour, plus more for rolling; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1 tsp sugar; 2 Tbsp neutral oil or melted ghee for dough; 3/4 cup warm water, plus 1–2 Tbsp if needed; 3–4 Tbsp oil/ghee for layering; 2–3 Tbsp oil/ghee for griddling.

Yogurt-Spiced Chicken

1 1/2 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, thin strips; 1/3 cup yogurt; 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste; 1 Tbsp lime juice; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 1/2 tsp garam masala; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; 1 tsp paprika or Kashmiri chile powder; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4–1/2 tsp cayenne/chile powder; 1 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 Tbsp butter/ghee; 1–2 tsp lime juice for final adjustment.

Green Chutney, Onion Crunch + Egg

Chutney: 1 packed cup cilantro, 1/2 packed cup mint, 1 serrano/green chile, 1 garlic clove, 1/2 tsp ginger, 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 tsp sugar/honey, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 2–4 Tbsp cold water as needed, optional 1 Tbsp yogurt.

Onion crunch: 1 medium thin-sliced onion, 1 Tbsp lime juice, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp sugar, 1/4 tsp paprika/chile powder, 2 Tbsp cilantro, optional 1/2 tsp chaat masala, optional serrano.

Egg lacquer: 4 large eggs, pinch salt, 1–2 tsp oil/ghee as needed.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Paratha Dough Dry

2 cups AP flour, 3/4 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp sugar.

Paratha Dough Wet

2 Tbsp oil/ghee, 3/4 cup warm water, plus 1–2 Tbsp if needed.

Layering + Griddle Fat

3–4 Tbsp oil/ghee for lamination and 2–3 Tbsp for griddling.

Chicken Marinade

1/3 cup yogurt, 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic, 1 Tbsp lime, 1 Tbsp oil, spices, salt, and pepper for 1 1/2 lb chicken thighs.

Chutney

1 cup cilantro, 1/2 cup mint, green chile, garlic, ginger, lime, sugar/honey, salt, and water as needed.

Onion Crunch

Thin onion, lime, salt, sugar, chile/paprika, cilantro, optional chaat masala and serrano.

Egg Lacquer

4 eggs with a pinch of salt, beaten right before griddling.

Do Not Forget

This is a scratch paratha recipe, not naan, tortilla, or shortcut wrap.

Rest dough twice: after mixing and after coiling.

Cook chicken in batches so it browns instead of steaming.

The egg layer should be thin and bonded to the paratha, not scrambled egg filling.

Serve hot; the magic is hot flaky bread + egg + charred chicken + cold bright chutney/onion.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken. Whisk 1/3 cup yogurt, 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp oil, 1 1/2 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp paprika/Kashmiri chile, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/4–1/2 tsp cayenne, 1 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Toss with 1 1/2 lb thin-sliced chicken thighs. Marinate 2 hours to overnight.
  2. Mix paratha dough. Whisk 2 cups AP flour, 3/4 tsp kosher salt, and 1 tsp sugar. Add 2 Tbsp oil/ghee and 3/4 cup warm water. Knead 6–8 minutes until smooth and elastic. Add 1 Tbsp water at a time only if dry.
  3. Rest dough. Cover and rest 45–60 minutes.
  4. Make chutney. Blend cilantro, mint, chile, garlic, ginger, lime, sugar/honey, salt, and enough cold water to blend. Taste for salt/lime.
  5. Make onion crunch. Toss onion with lime, salt, sugar, chile/paprika, cilantro, optional chaat masala, and optional serrano. Rest 15–30 minutes.
  6. Laminate parathas. Divide dough into 4 balls. Roll thin, brush with oil/ghee, pleat or roll into a rope, coil into spirals, cover, and rest 20–30 minutes.
  7. Cook chicken. Heat electric skillet around 300°F. Sear chicken in batches 3–5 minutes per batch until browned, lightly charred, and cooked through. Finish with 1 Tbsp butter/ghee and 1–2 tsp lime juice.
  8. Roll and griddle parathas. Roll each coil into an 8–9 inch paratha. Cook with oil/ghee around 300°F until lightly golden, layered, and flexible, about 1–2 minutes per side.
  9. Egg-lacquer. Beat 4 eggs with a pinch of salt. For each roll, pour about 1 beaten egg in a thin circle, press one cooked paratha onto the wet egg, cook 45–75 seconds until bonded, then flip briefly to re-warm the outside.
  10. Fill and roll. Place egg-side-up paratha on a board. Spread chutney, add hot chicken, onion-lime crunch, cilantro, optional chaat masala, and lime. Roll tight. Return seam-side down to skillet for 30–45 seconds if needed to seal.
  11. Serve immediately. Serve hot with extra chutney, lime wedges, cilantro, and optional chaat masala.

Electric Skillet Notes

Start chicken and paratha around 300°F. If yogurt marinade darkens too fast, drop to 275°F and keep turning. If paratha stays pale and oily, raise slightly toward 325°F. Egg lacquer should set gently, not brown hard; use 275–300°F for the egg step if needed.

Final Taste Target

The finished roll should be hot, flaky, juicy, tangy, and herb-bright: crisp-edged layered paratha, thin savory egg bonded to the bread, deeply spiced charred chicken, limey onion crunch, and vivid cilantro-mint chutney. It should eat like elite street food — bold, handheld, hot, fresh, and impossible to mistake for a generic chicken wrap.
