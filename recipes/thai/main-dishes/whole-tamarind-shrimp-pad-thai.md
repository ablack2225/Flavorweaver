# Whole-Tamarind Shrimp Pad Thai

> Sauce-forward Thai-inspired shrimp Pad Thai built for Amy and Don's bold flavor target: reduced sweet tamarind, dried shrimp, fresh shrimp-shell concentrate, Thai chiles, double shallot, egg ribbons, cooked-then-finished rice stick noodles, seasoned warmed peanuts, bean sprouts, lime, and controlled electric-skillet finishing.

## Flavor Profile

This Pad Thai is built around a glossy sauce-forward finish: fruity tamarind tang first, salty seafood umami second, gentle dark sweetness underneath, and Thai chile heat at the end. Because the available tamarind is sweet tamarind rather than sharp sour tamarind, the sauce is reduced and corrected with rice vinegar, lime, fish sauce, dried shrimp, and shrimp-shell concentrate.

The best texture comes from a quick final toss. Cook the noodles separately per package directions, rinse and oil lightly, then finish them briefly in sauce. If the electric skillet looks crowded, finish the noodles in **two half-batches** so the sauce coats the noodles instead of steaming them.

## Before Cooking Day / Shopping Note

- Buy **fresh bean sprouts the day of cooking**. They should be crisp, pale, not slimy, and not sour-smelling.
- Use **medium rice stick noodles** for the best Pad Thai texture.
- Fresh lime wedges are required.
- Dried shrimp is now part of the elevated version.
- Optional side: **steamed dumplings with bold salty umami dipping sauce**.

## Sauce Balance Check

Taste the saucepan Pad Thai sauce before it goes into the skillet. It should taste intense alone because noodles, egg, shrimp, and sprouts will soften it.

| If sauce tastes... | Adjust with... |
|---|---|
| Too sweet or dark | 1 tsp rice vinegar plus 1 tsp fish sauce. |
| Too sharp | 1 tsp honey or dark brown sugar. |
| Too salty | 1 Tbsp water plus a little more tamarind/sugar balance. |
| Flat | More fish sauce, a tiny squeeze of lime, or a little more chile. |
| Not seafood-savory enough | Add up to 1 Tbsp more fish sauce or a spoon of shell concentrate if available. |
| Not hot enough | More minced Thai chile, chili flakes, or chili-garlic paste. |
| Thin/weak | Simmer 1–2 minutes gently before using. |

## Do Not Forget

- Cook noodles **per package directions**, then rinse, drain, lightly oil, and add only at the end.
- Do not use the soak-only method for this recipe.
- Make the Pad Thai sauce **in a saucepan**, taste-correct it, then add it to the electric skillet only as needed.
- Dried shrimp must be **rinsed, soaked, drained, patted dry, minced finely, and bloomed**; do not add wet soaked shrimp directly to the skillet.
- Shrimp quick-seasoning is **10–15 minutes ideal**, **20 minutes max**.
- Do not put lime or tamarind in the shrimp seasoning bowl.
- Use **60–70% of the sauce first**, then hold back the rest for correction after tasting a noodle.
- Pull shrimp at **80–90% cooked**; they finish in the final toss.
- Warm dry-roasted unsalted peanuts **2–3 minutes**, then season them lightly before crushing or just after crushing.
- If the electric skillet looks crowded, finish in **two half-batches**.
- Fresh lime wedges are required at service.
- For hot-running electric skillets, keep most cooking around **275°F to 300°F**.

## Instructions

### 1. Make Reduced Sweet Tamarind Extract

Remove shells and tough strings from **2 oz / 55 g sweet whole tamarind pods or pulp**. Cover with **1 cup hot water**. Soak **15–20 minutes**, mash thoroughly, then strain through a fine mesh strainer, pressing hard. Simmer the strained liquid gently until reduced to **1/2 cup**. This reduction is required for the sweet tamarind version because it concentrates fruity body while the rice vinegar restores brightness.

### 2. Peel and Quick-Season Shrimp

Peel **1 lb shrimp**, saving shells/tails. Toss shrimp with **1 tsp fish sauce**, **1 tsp neutral oil**, **1/2 tsp brown sugar**, **1 small grated garlic clove**, pepper, and optional tiny chile pinch. Let sit while making shell concentrate, ideally **10–15 minutes**.

### 3. Make Fresh Shrimp-Shell Concentrate

Put the saved shells/tails in a small saucepan with about **1/2 cup water**, just enough to barely cover. Simmer gently **8–10 minutes**, pressing the shells. Strain, then reduce the liquid until you have **2 Tbsp** concentrated shrimp broth. Add this to the saucepan sauce.

### 4. Prep Dried Shrimp

Rinse **2 Tbsp dried shrimp** briefly. Cover with hot water for **8–10 minutes**. Drain very well, pat dry with paper towels, and mince finely. The target is tiny savory bits, not large chewy shrimp pieces.

### 5. Make Saucepan Pad Thai Sauce

In a small saucepan, combine **1/2 cup reduced sweet tamarind extract**, **4 Tbsp fish sauce**, **2 Tbsp dark brown sugar**, **2 Tbsp fresh shrimp-shell concentrate**, **1 Tbsp rice vinegar**, **1 minced Thai chile**, and **1 tsp red pepper flakes or chili-garlic paste**. Warm over low to medium-low heat **3–5 minutes**, just until glossy and dissolved. Do not hard-boil. Taste and correct before cooking the noodles.

### 6. Cook Noodles Separately

Cook **7 to 8 oz medium rice stick noodles** according to package directions, aiming for the low end. Drain immediately, rinse briefly with cool water, drain well, and toss with a few drops neutral oil. Hold for final toss.

### 7. Warm, Crush, and Season Peanuts

Warm **1/3 cup dry-roasted unsalted peanuts** in a dry skillet or air fryer for **2–3 minutes** until fragrant. Crush and season with **1/8 tsp kosher salt**, a tiny pinch brown sugar, and optional tiny pinch chili flakes. Reserve about **2 Tbsp** for the final toss and the rest for topping.

### 8. Prep Fresh Finish

Rinse and dry **1 to 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts**. Slice **2 to 3 green onions**, chop **2 Tbsp cilantro** if using, and cut **1 to 2 limes** into wedges.

### 9. Make Dumpling Sauce, If Serving

Stir together **3 Tbsp soy sauce**, **1 Tbsp rice vinegar**, **1 Tbsp water**, **1 tsp brown sugar or honey**, **1 tsp sesame oil**, **1 tsp chili garlic paste or sriracha**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp grated ginger**, and **1–2 tsp sliced green onion**. Add optional tiny splash fish sauce or sesame seeds if desired.

### 10. Make Golden Shallot Topping

Heat the electric skillet around **275–300°F** with enough neutral oil to coat the surface. Cook **1 large thin-sliced shallot** until golden and lightly crisp. Remove to paper towels and season with a tiny pinch salt. Keep the shallot oil in the skillet if it is not burnt.

### 11. Sear Shrimp and Remove

At about **300°F**, sear quick-seasoned shrimp just until **80–90% cooked**. Remove to a plate. Do not fully tighten the shrimp here; they finish in the final toss.

### 12. Bloom Aromatics and Dried Shrimp

Lower skillet toward **275–300°F**. Add a little oil if needed. Cook **1 large minced shallot** until softened and lightly golden. Add **3 minced garlic cloves**, **1 thin-sliced or minced Thai chile**, and the minced dried shrimp. Cook **30–60 seconds**, just until fragrant. Do not brown the garlic.

### 13. Make Egg Ribbons

Push aromatics aside. Add **2 lightly beaten eggs** with a tiny pinch salt. Let them set into soft curds, then fold gently through the aromatics.

### 14. Decide One-Batch or Two-Batch Finish

If the skillet surface looks crowded for **7–8 oz noodles plus 1 lb shrimp**, finish in two batches. For two batches, divide the noodles, shrimp, sauce, egg-aromatic base, bean sprouts, peanuts, and green onion roughly in half. This gives the best glossy texture.

### 15. Add Sauce in Stages

Add about **60–70% of the saucepan Pad Thai sauce** to the skillet, or half of that amount if cooking in two batches. Let it bubble **30–60 seconds** until glossy, not syrupy. Hold the remaining sauce for correction.

### 16. Finish Noodles

Add cooked noodles and toss quickly **1–2 minutes max**, just until coated and absorbing sauce. Taste one noodle. Add held-back sauce only if needed.

### 17. Final Fold

Fold shrimp back in with **1/2 cup bean sprouts**, **2 Tbsp crushed peanuts**, and most green onion. Toss **10–15 seconds** only, just to warm sprouts and finish shrimp.

### 18. Plate Immediately

Top with fresh bean sprouts, golden shallots, remaining peanuts, green onion, cilantro if using, lime wedges, and extra chili flakes at the table. Serve steamed dumplings with bold umami sauce on the side if using.

## Electric Skillet Notes

| Stage | Suggested Temp | Cue |
|---|---:|---|
| Golden shallot topping | 275–300°F | Golden, fragrant, lightly crisp. |
| Shrimp sear | 300°F | Shrimp mostly opaque, not fully tight. |
| Aromatic base + dried shrimp | 275–300°F | Shallot softened/golden, garlic fragrant, dried shrimp bloomed. |
| Egg ribbons | 275–300°F | Soft set curds, not dry scramble. |
| Sauce + noodles | About 275°F | Glossy coating, no scorching. |
| Final fold | Warm/off if possible | Shrimp finishes, sprouts stay crisp. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished Pad Thai should be glossy but not wet, fruity-tangy from reduced sweet tamarind, salty-savory from fish sauce, dried shrimp, and shrimp-shell concentrate, gently rounded with dark brown sugar, aromatic from double shallot, garlic, and Thai chile, rich with egg ribbons, juicy with shrimp, fresh with bean sprouts and lime, and crunchy from seasoned warmed peanuts and golden shallots. The noodles should be tender but resilient, never mushy or underdone.
