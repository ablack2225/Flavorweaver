# Gochujang Crema

> A creamy, tangy, lightly spicy gochujang finishing sauce developed for Bold Bulgogi Bowls and now kept as a reusable Flavorweaver house condiment.

## Measured Ingredients

### Canon Version

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1/4 cup | Sour cream | Base of the crema |
| 2 tsp | Gochujang | Add more for deeper heat |
| 1-2 tsp | Fresh lime juice | To taste |
| Pinch | Sugar | Rounds out the gochujang |
| 1-3 tsp | Water | Add as needed to thin |

### Optional Adjustments

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1/2 tsp | Rice vinegar | For extra brightness |
| 1/4 tsp | Honey | Use instead of sugar for rounder sweetness |
| Tiny pinch | Garlic powder | For a deeper savory note |
| Few drops | Sesame oil | For a stronger Korean-inspired flavor |
| Extra | Gochujang | For more heat and depth |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Add sour cream, gochujang, lime juice, and sugar to a small bowl. |
| 2 | Stir until completely smooth and evenly colored. |
| 3 | Add water 1 teaspoon at a time until the sauce reaches drizzle consistency. |
| 4 | Taste and adjust. |

<!-- recipe-card:end -->

## Texture Goal

For bowls, the crema should be thin enough to drizzle but thick enough to cling to rice, meat, and vegetables.

If using as a dip or spread, leave it slightly thicker.

## Flavor Balance Guide

| Issue | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Too thick | Add water 1 teaspoon at a time. |
| Too spicy | Add more sour cream. |
| Too flat | Add a little more lime juice or rice vinegar. |
| Too sharp | Add a tiny pinch more sugar or a few drops of honey. |
| Too mild | Add more gochujang in 1/2 teaspoon increments. |

## Storage

Store in a clean airtight container in the refrigerator.

Best used within **3 to 4 days**.

Stir before using.

Do not store at room temperature.

Discard if it develops an off smell, visible mold, bubbling, separation that will not stir back together, or any unusual discoloration.

## Chef's Notes

This crema was developed as the finishing sauce for the **Bold Bulgogi Bowls** and is now a reusable condiment for rice bowls, wraps, grilled meats, roasted vegetables, and other sauce-driven meals.

It adds creamy heat, tang, and sweetness without overpowering the main dish.

The finished crema should be creamy, lightly spicy, tangy, and just sweet enough to round out the gochujang.
