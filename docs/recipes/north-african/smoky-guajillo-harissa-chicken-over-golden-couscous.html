Smoky Guajillo-Harissa Chicken over Spicy Tomato-Olive Couscous

Smoky guajillo-harissa chicken thighs served over spicy tomato-olive couscous with generous Thai chili hot honey, smoky sweet-hot cashews, and crispy pepper jack egg-olive brik-inspired triangles with bold tamarind-date chutney.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Chicken

Chicken thighs marinated in a smoky guajillo-harissa paste with tomato paste, garlic, cumin, coriander, caraway, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, acid, and salt.

Couscous

Spicy tomato-olive couscous built from butter, olive oil, onion, garlic, tomato paste, guajillo-harissa paste, spices, chicken broth, green olives, and herbs.

Signature Glaze

Generous Thai chili hot honey made with reserved paste, honey, butter, Thai chili, acid, and salt. It should drip into the couscous.

Crunch

Smoky sweet-hot cashews replace chickpeas for a faster, richer, more restaurant-style crunch.

Side Event

Crispy egg roll wrapper brik-inspired triangles filled with soft egg, pepper jack, mozzarella, olives, Thai chili, and spices.

Showstopper Sauce

Bold tamarind-date chutney gives the side its own sweet-sour, dark-fruity, spicy lane instead of repeating the harissa profile.

Flavorweaver Standard

This rebuilt version removed the older lemon-herb couscous, yogurt-tahini sauce, cucumber-mint salad, and canned chickpeas. The public version now follows the Amy & Don boldness rule: no polite components; every bite has to punch.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Smoky Guajillo-Harissa Paste

3 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded; 3 garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp olive oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice; optional 1 tsp lemon zest; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; 1/4 tsp caraway; 3/4 tsp smoked paprika; 3/4 tsp red pepper flakes; tiny pinch cinnamon or allspice; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1 to 3 Tbsp chile soaking water only as needed.

Chicken Marinade

4 chicken thighs; remaining guajillo-harissa paste after reserving paste for couscous and hot honey; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp honey; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper.

Spicy Tomato-Olive Couscous

1 cup plain couscous; 1 Tbsp butter; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 1/4 cup finely diced onion; 2 cloves garlic; 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste; 1 1/2 to 2 Tbsp guajillo-harissa paste; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp smoked paprika; pinch caraway; tiny pinch cinnamon or allspice; 1 cup chicken broth; 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped green olives; 2 to 3 Tbsp parsley or cilantro; optional tiny splash olive brine; salt to taste.

Increased Thai Chili Hot Honey

2 1/2 Tbsp reserved guajillo-harissa paste; 3 Tbsp honey; 1 1/2 Tbsp butter; 1 to 2 finely minced red Thai chiles; 2 tsp vinegar or lemon juice; generous pinch salt; optional pinch cayenne or red pepper flakes.

Smoky Sweet-Hot Cashews

1/2 cup cashews; 1 tsp olive oil or melted butter; 1/2 tsp smoked paprika; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/4 tsp coriander; 1/8 tsp garlic powder; pinch cayenne or red pepper flakes; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp honey or brown sugar.

Crispy Pepper Jack Egg-Olive Brik Triangles

6 to 8 egg roll wrappers; 2 large eggs; 1/3 cup pepper jack; 2 Tbsp mozzarella; 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped green olives; 1 finely minced Thai chili; optional 1 to 2 Tbsp parsley or cilantro; 1/4 tsp smoked paprika; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/4 tsp garlic powder; pinch black pepper; tiny pinch salt only if needed; flour-water paste or beaten egg for sealing; oil for frying.

Bold Tamarind-Date Chutney

Small handful whole tamarind plus 1 cup hot water for extract. For chutney: 1/2 cup strong tamarind extract; 4 to 6 Tbsp brown sugar or honey; 2 to 4 Tbsp chopped dates or raisins; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp ginger powder or 1 tsp fresh ginger; cayenne or red pepper flakes to taste; 1/4 tsp salt; optional tiny pinch garlic powder; splash water as needed.

Equipment Map

Electric skillet: cook chicken around 300°F, raising only if browning is too slow.

Oven: roast cashews at 325°F.

Deep fryer or deep pot: fry brik triangles around 350°F.

Small saucepan: simmer tamarind-date chutney and warm Thai chili hot honey.

Small skillet/pan: soft-scramble eggs and cook couscous base.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Paste Bowl

Toasted soaked guajillos, garlic, olive oil, tomato paste, acid, cumin, coriander, caraway, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, cinnamon/allspice, and salt.

Chicken Bowl

Chicken thighs with remaining paste, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper. Reserve paste first for hot honey and couscous.

Couscous Bowl

Dry couscous; tomato-harissa broth base; chopped olives and herbs for finishing.

Brik Filling Bowl

Soft-scrambled eggs, pepper jack, mozzarella, chopped olives, Thai chili, smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, and pepper.

Cashew Bowl

Cashews, fat, smoked paprika, cumin, coriander, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, and honey or brown sugar.

Chutney Bowl

Whole tamarind extract, brown sugar or honey, dates or raisins, cumin, ginger, chile heat, and salt.

Do Not Forget

Reserve enough paste: 2 1/2 Tbsp for hot honey and 1 1/2 to 2 Tbsp for couscous before marinating chicken.

The couscous broth must taste slightly too bold before couscous goes in.

The Thai chili hot honey is the signature sauce. Make it generous.

Do not overfill the brik wrappers or they may burst while frying.

Cashews burn quickly; roast gently and watch closely.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Make tamarind extract. Break up whole tamarind, cover with 1 cup hot water, soak 15 to 20 minutes, mash thoroughly, then strain and press hard. Save 1/2 cup strong extract.
  2. Toast and soak guajillos. Stem and seed 3 chiles. Toast 10 to 20 seconds per side, then soak in hot water 10 to 15 minutes.
  3. Blend paste. Blend softened chiles with garlic, olive oil, tomato paste, vinegar or lemon, optional zest, cumin, coriander, caraway, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, cinnamon/allspice, salt, and only enough soaking water to make a thick paste. Taste for smoky, garlicky, chile-rich, salty, tangy impact.
  4. Reserve paste and marinate chicken. Reserve 2 1/2 Tbsp paste for hot honey and 1 1/2 to 2 Tbsp for couscous. Coat chicken with remaining paste, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper. Marinate at least 1 hour.
  5. Simmer chutney. Combine tamarind extract, brown sugar or honey, dates or raisins, cumin, ginger, chile heat, salt, and optional garlic powder. Simmer 6 to 10 minutes until glossy and spoonable. Adjust until dark sweet-sour, spicy, and salty enough to pop.
  6. Roast cashews. Heat oven to 325°F. Toss cashews with fat, smoked paprika, cumin, coriander, garlic powder, chile, salt, and honey or brown sugar. Roast 8 to 12 minutes, stirring once or twice. Taste while warm and adjust salt or heat.
  7. Make brik filling. Soft-scramble eggs just until barely set. Cool slightly, then mix with pepper jack, mozzarella, olives, Thai chili, herbs if using, smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper. Taste; salt only if needed.
  8. Wrap brik triangles. Use 1 1/2 Tbsp filling per wrapper. Fold into triangles or sealed packets and seal well with flour-water paste or beaten egg.
  9. Make couscous base. Heat butter and olive oil. Cook onion until softened. Add garlic briefly. Add tomato paste, reserved paste, cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, caraway, and cinnamon/allspice. Cook 1 to 2 minutes until dark and fragrant.
  10. Steam couscous. Add broth and simmer. Taste the liquid; it should be slightly too bold. Add salt, paste, spice, or olive brine if needed. Stir in couscous, cover, remove from heat, rest 5 minutes, fluff, then fold in olives and herbs.
  11. Fry brik triangles. Fry around 350°F until deeply golden and crisp, about 2 to 4 minutes depending size. Drain on a rack.
  12. Cook chicken. Heat electric skillet to about 300°F. Cook chicken until browned and cooked through, about 5 to 7 minutes per side for boneless thighs, longer for bone-in. Raise toward 325°F only if needed. Rest 5 minutes.
  13. Make hot honey. Warm reserved paste, honey, butter, Thai chili, vinegar or lemon, and salt until glossy. Taste: sweet, smoky, hot, tangy, buttery, addictive.
  14. Glaze and plate. Spoon hot honey over rested chicken. Plate couscous, chicken, extra glaze, cashews, brik triangles, and generous tamarind-date chutney.

Final Taste Target

The finished meal should taste smoky, spicy, tomato-rich, garlicky, briny, buttery, sweet-hot, crisp, cheesy, and darkly sweet-sour. The main plate delivers chile chicken, bold couscous, hot honey, and cashew crunch. The side feels like a separate street-food event: crisp wrappers, melty pepper jack, soft egg, olives, Thai chili, and bold tamarind-date chutney.
