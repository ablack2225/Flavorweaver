# Don's Food-Truck Memory Falafel Naan Wraps

> Hot herb falafel served in warm homemade naan with tomato, onion, tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce, fiery red sauce, and optional lemon tahini. Don called the finished wraps **spot on**.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Element | Notes |
|---|---|
| Falafel | Dried chickpeas are soaked overnight, drained, dried, ground twice with herbs and aromatics, seasoned, chilled, shaped, and deep-fried until crisp outside and fluffy inside. |
| White Sauce | Thick, creamy, garlicky, herby, lemony, and cool against the hot falafel and fiery red sauce. |
| Red Sauce | Spicy, tangy, vinegary, garlicky, red-chile-forward, and hot enough to use as a stripe sauce. |
| Tahini | Optional lemon tahini adds classic sesame depth. |
| Wrap Build | Warm naan, tomato, onion, hot falafel, white sauce, fiery red sauce, and optional tahini. Lettuce is not required. |

## Before Cooking / Advance Prep

- Soak **1 lb dried chickpeas** in cold water overnight in the refrigerator.
- Drain, rinse, and dry the chickpeas **30 to 60 minutes** before grinding.
- Make all sauces before frying the falafel.
- Chill the falafel mixture **45 to 60 minutes** before shaping.
- Fry one test falafel before frying the full batch.
- Warm naan immediately before building the wraps.

## Measured Ingredients by Component

- 1 lb dried chickpeas
- Cold water, enough to cover by 3 to 4 inches
- 1/2 tsp baking soda, optional

- Soaked, drained, dried chickpeas
- 1 small onion, roughly chopped
- 4 scallions, white and light green parts
- 5 cloves garlic
- 1 small carrot, peeled and roughly chopped
- 1 packed cup parsley leaves and tender stems
- 1 packed cup cilantro leaves and tender stems
- 2 Tbsp fresh mint
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 1 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
- 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 Tbsp baking powder
- 2 to 3 Tbsp chickpea flour or all-purpose flour
- 1 to 3 Tbsp cold water, only if needed
- Neutral oil, for deep frying

## Do Not Forget

- Do not cook the chickpeas.
- Do not use canned chickpeas.
- Dry chickpeas well before grinding.
- Grind the falafel mixture twice.
- Chill the mixture before shaping.
- Fry one test falafel before frying the batch.
- Keep oil at 350°F.
- Serve falafel hot.
- Warm naan immediately before building.
- Use the red sauce as a stripe sauce.
- White sauce and fiery red sauce are required.
- Lemon tahini is optional.
- Lettuce is not required.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Soak the chickpeas.** Place **1 lb dried chickpeas** in a large bowl. Cover with cold water by **3 to 4 inches** and add **1/2 tsp baking soda** if using. Refrigerate **18 to 24 hours**. Do not cook the chickpeas.

2. **Drain and dry.** Drain the soaked chickpeas, rinse well, shake off excess water, spread on towels or a baking sheet, and dry thoroughly. Rest uncovered **30 to 60 minutes**.

3. **Grind the falafel.** Run the dried chickpeas, **1 small onion**, **4 scallions**, **5 garlic cloves**, **1 small carrot**, **1 packed cup parsley**, **1 packed cup cilantro**, and **2 Tbsp mint** through the meat grinder twice.

4. **Season the falafel.** Add **2 tsp cumin**, **1 1/2 tsp coriander**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/4 tsp cardamom**, **1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne**, **1/2 tsp black pepper**, **2 tsp kosher salt**, **1 Tbsp baking powder**, and **2 to 3 Tbsp flour**. Mix by hand. Add **1 Tbsp cold water at a time**, up to **3 Tbsp**, only if the mixture crumbles badly.

5. **Chill the falafel mixture.** Cover and refrigerate **45 to 60 minutes**.

6. **Make the white sauce.** Whisk **1/2 cup mayonnaise** with **1/2 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt**. Stir in **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1 tsp dried dill or 1 Tbsp fresh dill**, **1/2 tsp onion powder**, **1/2 tsp garlic powder**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/4 tsp black pepper**. Add optional cucumber, parsley, or cumin. Thin with **1 tsp milk or water at a time**, using no more than **2 Tbsp**, until thick and spoonable. Chill until service.

7. **Make the fiery red sauce.** Soak **4 guajillo chiles** in very hot water for **10 to 15 minutes**, then drain. Blend with **3 garlic cloves**, **2 Tbsp tomato paste**, **2 Tbsp white vinegar**, **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, **2 Tbsp water**, **1 Tbsp vinegar-style hot sauce**, **1 tsp cumin**, **1/2 tsp coriander**, **1/2 tsp paprika**, **1/2 tsp chipotle powder**, **1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper**, **1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp sugar**, and **1 Tbsp oil**. Add extra water **1 tsp at a time** until pourable. Simmer **3 to 5 minutes** if a smoother, deeper sauce is desired, then cool.

8. **Make the lemon tahini.** Whisk **1/2 cup tahini**, **3 Tbsp lemon juice**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/4 tsp cumin**. Whisk in **1/4 cup ice-cold water**, **1 Tbsp at a time**, until pale, creamy, and pourable. Add **1/4 to 1/2 tsp honey** and **1 Tbsp olive oil** only if needed.

9. **Shape the falafel.** Shape the chilled mixture into **24 to 30 small balls or slightly flattened patties**, about **1 heaping tablespoon each**.

10. **Fry one falafel.** Heat neutral oil to **350°F** and fry one falafel. If it falls apart, mix **1 Tbsp additional flour** into the remaining mixture and chill longer.

11. **Fry the batch.** Fry falafel in small batches at **350°F** for **3 to 4 minutes**, until deeply golden and crisp. Drain on a rack and salt lightly while hot.

12. **Prepare the wrap station.** Warm **4 to 6 naan** until soft and flexible. Set out **1 to 2 sliced tomatoes**, **1 thinly sliced small onion**, **1 cup white sauce**, **3/4 to 1 cup fiery red sauce**, and optional **3/4 to 1 cup lemon tahini**.

13. **Build each wrap.** Spread white sauce over warm naan. Add tomato, onion, and **4 to 6 hot falafel**. Stripe fiery red sauce over the falafel, add another spoonful of white sauce, fold, and serve immediately. Add lemon tahini only as desired.

## Final Naan Wrap Build

### Don's Memory Wrap

1. Warm 1 naan until soft and flexible.
2. Spread 2 to 3 Tbsp white sauce over the naan.
3. Add 2 to 3 tomato slices.
4. Add 2 to 3 Tbsp thinly sliced onion.
5. Add 4 to 6 hot falafel.
6. Stripe 1 to 2 tsp fiery red sauce over the falafel.
7. Add 1 Tbsp additional white sauce.
8. Fold and serve immediately.

### Classic Falafel Wrap

1. Warm 1 naan.
2. Spread 1 to 2 Tbsp lemon tahini over the naan.
3. Add tomato and onion.
4. Add 4 to 6 hot falafel.
5. Add 1 to 2 tsp fiery red sauce.
6. Add 1 Tbsp white sauce.
7. Fold and serve immediately.

## Final Taste Target

The wrap should have hot crispy falafel, soft warm naan, cool creamy white sauce, fiery red sauce, juicy tomato, sharp onion, and optional nutty lemon tahini.

The red sauce should bring aggressive heat and tang. The white sauce should cool the bite. The falafel should remain crisp long enough to contrast with the sauces and vegetables.
