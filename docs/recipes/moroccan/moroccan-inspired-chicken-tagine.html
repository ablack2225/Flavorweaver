Moroccan-Inspired Chicken Tagine

Warm-spiced Moroccan-inspired chicken tagine with jammy onion, preserved lemon, green olives, dried apricots, golden cashew-herb couscous, toasted cumin cashews, and a fresh herb finish.

Meal TypeMain Dish
Total1 hr 50 min
Serves4 portions

Kitchen Execution Summary

Spiced Chicken Thighs

Direct seasoning keeps the chicken flavorful, not just the sauce.

Jammy Onion Base

Slow-cooked onions create sweetness, body, and sauce depth.

Preserved Lemon

Minced preserved lemon rind gives salty citrus depth and Moroccan-style brightness.

Apricot + Olive Balance

Dried apricots bring sweet-tart roundness while green olives add controlled briny contrast.

Golden Couscous + Cashews

Brothy, plush couscous catches sauce; toasted cumin cashews finish with savory crunch.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Spiced Chicken Rub

4 chicken thighs, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, tiny pinch cinnamon, and 1 tsp olive oil.

Tagine Sauce

1 very large or 2 medium onions, 4 garlic cloves, 1 Tbsp fresh grated ginger, 1 Tbsp tomato paste, 1 cup chicken broth, 1/2 cup dried apricots, 1/3 cup green olives, 1 preserved lemon quarter, optional brine, optional honey or orange marmalade, optional 1 tsp butter.

Sauce Spice Bloom

1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp turmeric, 1/8 tsp cinnamon, and a pinch to 1/4 tsp cayenne or red pepper flakes.

Golden Cashew-Herb Couscous

1 cup couscous, 1 cup chicken broth, 1 Tbsp butter or olive oil, 1/4 tsp turmeric, tiny pinch cumin, 2 Tbsp parsley, 1 Tbsp cilantro, and toasted cashews.

Toasted Cumin Cashews and Finish

1/3 cup chopped cashews, tiny pinch salt, tiny pinch cumin, parsley, cilantro, optional tiny mint, and fresh lemon juice only if needed.

Do Not Forget

Use mostly preserved lemon rind, not a large amount of pulp.

Cook onions 10–12 minutes until collapsed, glossy, lightly golden, and sweet-smelling.

Add olives only in the final 10–15 minutes of braising.

Use honey or orange marmalade only as a final correction, not a main sweetener.

Stir in butter off heat only after final sauce balance is close.

Keep cashews out of the braise; toast and add at plating.

For hot-running electric skillets, brown around 300°F, then lower toward 275°F for onions and sauce.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Chicken Rub Bowl

Chicken, salt, pepper, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, and oil. Rest 20–30 minutes if possible.

Jammy Onion Bowl

Thin-sliced onions. This gets the longest cook after browning the chicken.

Aromatics Bowl

Minced garlic and fresh grated ginger.

Spice Bloom Bowl

Cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, and cayenne or red pepper flakes.

Apricot Bowl

Sliced dried apricots with 1/2 cup hot broth. Soften 10 minutes.

Finish Bowl

Minced preserved lemon rind, halved olives, toasted cumin cashews, herbs, optional brine, butter, honey or marmalade.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Rub the chicken. Combine salt, pepper, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, and olive oil. Rub over the chicken thighs. Rest 20–30 minutes if possible.
  2. Soften apricots. Warm 1/2 cup chicken broth and pour it over sliced dried apricots. Let sit 10 minutes. Save both apricots and soaking broth.
  3. Prep preserved lemon. Scrape pulp from 1 preserved lemon quarter. Finely mince the rind. Reserve a small pinch of rind for garnish.
  4. Brown chicken. Heat a large electric skillet around 300°F with a thin layer of oil. Brown chicken on both sides until golden but not cooked through. Remove to a plate.
  5. Build jammy onion. Add sliced onion to the chicken fond with a pinch of salt. Cook 10–12 minutes, lowering toward 275°F if needed, until onions are collapsed, glossy, lightly golden, and sweet-smelling.
  6. Add aromatics. Stir in garlic and fresh ginger. Cook 45–60 seconds until fragrant.
  7. Bloom spices and tomato paste. Add the sauce spice bloom and cook 30 seconds. Add tomato paste and cook 60–90 seconds until darker and fragrant.
  8. Build sauce. Add softened apricots with their soaking broth plus the remaining 1/2 cup broth. Scrape the skillet well. Stir in minced preserved lemon rind.
  9. Braise chicken. Return chicken and juices to the skillet. Cover and simmer gently until chicken is cooked through and tender, about 25–35 minutes depending on thigh size.
  10. Add olives late. Add halved green olives during the final 10–15 minutes of cooking.
  11. Reduce sauce. Uncover and simmer 3–5 minutes if sauce is too thin. It should be spoonable and glossy, not watery.
  12. Make couscous. Bring 1 cup chicken broth with butter or olive oil, turmeric, and tiny pinch cumin to a boil. Pour over 1 cup couscous, cover 5 minutes, then fluff. Fold in herbs and a small amount of toasted cashews if desired.
  13. Toast cumin cashews. Toast chopped cashews in a dry pan 2–4 minutes until fragrant and lightly golden. Season with tiny pinch salt and tiny pinch cumin.
  14. Final sauce correction. Taste sauce. Add preserved lemon brine or olive brine for more briny pop, honey or orange marmalade for roundness, lemon juice for lift, or a splash of broth if too intense. Stir in 1 tsp butter off heat for gloss if desired.
  15. Plate. Spoon golden couscous into bowls or plates. Top with chicken and sauce. Finish with toasted cumin cashews, parsley, cilantro, optional tiny mint, and reserved preserved lemon rind.

Final Taste Target

The finished plate should be savory first, warm-spiced second, then lemon-bright, apricot-rounded, and olive-briny. The chicken should be juicy and seasoned all the way through. The onions should disappear into the sauce body. The couscous should be plush and sauce-catching. The cashews should bring buttery crunch without softening in the braise.
