| 1 (16 oz) can | Refried beans |  |
| 2 (10 oz) cans | Enchilada sauce |  |
| 1 small | Onion | Chopped |
| 12 | Corn tortillas |  |
| 2 cups | Monterey Jack cheese | Divided |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Brown ground beef and drain well. |
| 2 | Add taco seasoning and water; cook according to package directions. |
| 3 | Stir in refried beans and heat until warm. |
| 4 | Warm enchilada sauce in a saucepan. |
| 5 | Dip each tortilla into the sauce. |
| 6 | Fill each tortilla with a small amount of meat mixture, chopped onion, and cheese. |
| 7 | Roll to enclose. |
| 8 | Arrange rolls seam-side down in a greased casserole dish that has a small amount of enchilada sauce covering the bottom. |
| 9 | Pour remaining sauce over the rolls. |
| 10 | Sprinkle with remaining cheese. |
| 11 | Bake at 350°F for 25 minutes, or until heated through. |

## Notes

Family dinners were important to Goga, and enchiladas were one of the meals she often made for those gatherings. This recipe carries memories of sitting around the table together, then lingering afterward with games, movies, and time as a family.

The printed recipe lists 6 servings and 50 minutes prep time. Bake time is from the recipe text.
