Mughlai-Style Chicken Korma

Tender chicken thighs in a generous browned onion-cashew gravy with yogurt marinade, whole warm spices, chicken broth, and a small cream finish.

Meal TypeSauce-Driven Curry
Total1 hr 30 min, plus marinade
Serves2 generous or 4 smaller
StatusFlavorweaver Test Recipe
chicken kormaMughlai-stylecashew gravyyogurt marinadeelectric skilletbasmati rice

Kitchen Execution Summary

Yogurt-Marinated Chicken

Boneless chicken thighs are marinated with yogurt, ginger-garlic, turmeric, coriander, mild chile, garam masala, salt, and oil for tenderness and flavor penetration.

Browned Onion-Cashew Gravy

Slow-browned onions and lightly toasted cashews are blended smooth to create the silky body of the korma.

Whole Spice Bloom

Cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and bay leaf are bloomed in fat for fragrant Mughlai-style depth.

Extra Sauce Build

Chicken broth is added gradually so the gravy is generous enough for rice, naan, and sides without becoming watery.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

Marinate chicken at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

Slice onions before cooking day if desired.

Measure cashews and spices before starting the skillet work.

This recipe is written for an electric skillet because controlled heat helps protect the yogurt and cream from splitting.

Service Countdown

Night before or morning of: marinate chicken.

75 minutes before serving: slice onions, measure cashews, measure spices, and set up blender.

60 minutes before serving: brown onions and build the onion-cashew paste.

35 minutes before serving: sear chicken and build the korma gravy.

15 minutes before serving: simmer gently until chicken is tender and cooked through.

5 to 10 minutes before serving: finish with cream and garam masala, then rest covered off heat.

Full Ingredient Roll-Up

Chicken Marinade

Boneless chicken thighs, plain yogurt, ginger-garlic, turmeric, coriander, paprika or Kashmiri-style chile powder, garam masala, kosher salt, neutral oil.

Browned Onion-Cashew Paste

Onion, cashews, neutral oil or butter/oil mix, chicken broth or hot water.

Whole Spice Bloom

Green cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, cloves, bay leaf, neutral oil or butter/oil mix.

Sauce and Finish

Chicken broth, browned onion-cashew paste, seared marinated chicken, heavy cream, garam masala, optional sugar or honey, optional lemon or lime.

Measured Ingredients by Component

These component sections are reference only. The cooking flow is handled by the stage-based prep bowls and full timeline below.

Chicken Marinade

4 boneless chicken thighs; 1/3 cup plain full-fat yogurt; 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/2 tsp ground coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile powder; 1/4 tsp garam masala; 1 tsp neutral oil.

Browned Onion-Cashew Paste

1 large or 1 1/2 medium onions, thin sliced; 1 1/2 Tbsp neutral oil or butter/oil mix; 1/3 cup cashews; 3 to 5 Tbsp chicken broth or hot water for blending.

Whole Spice Bloom

1 Tbsp neutral oil or butter/oil mix; 2 green cardamom pods, lightly cracked; 1 small cinnamon stick; 2 cloves; 1 bay leaf.

Powdered Spice Layer

1 tsp ground coriander; 1/2 tsp ground cumin; 1/4 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile powder; optional pinch cayenne.

Sauce and Finish

3/4 cup chicken broth, plus more up to 1 cup total; 2 Tbsp heavy cream; 1/4 tsp garam masala; optional 1/4 tsp sugar or honey; kosher salt to taste; optional tiny squeeze lemon or lime.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Onions: used only for the browned onion-cashew paste. Cook deeply enough to give sweetness and color to the gravy.

Cashews: toast briefly with browned onions, then blend smooth. Do not let them burn.

Chicken broth: appears in the paste and final gravy. Keep at least 1 cup nearby.

Garam masala: appears lightly in the marinade and again as a finish. Do not overload it early.

Cream: only added at the end on low heat or off heat.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Marinade Bowls

Bowl 1 — Chicken

4 boneless chicken thighs. Trim excess fat. Leave whole or cut into large pieces.

Bowl 2 — Yogurt Marinade

1/3 cup yogurt; 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile; 1/4 tsp garam masala; 1 tsp neutral oil.

Sauce Bowls

Bowl 1 — Onion-Cashew Paste

1 large or 1 1/2 medium sliced onions; 1/3 cup cashews; 3 to 5 Tbsp broth or hot water for blending.

Bowl 2 — Whole Spices

2 cracked green cardamom pods; 1 small cinnamon stick; 2 cloves; 1 bay leaf.

Bowl 3 — Powdered Spices

1 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/4 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile; optional pinch cayenne.

Bowl 4 — Sauce Liquids

3/4 cup chicken broth, plus extra up to 1 cup total.

Bowl 5 — Finish

2 Tbsp heavy cream; 1/4 tsp garam masala; optional sugar or honey; optional citrus.

Electric Skillet Temperature Map

Brown onions: 300 to 325°F. Toast cashews: 300°F. Sear chicken: 325 to 350°F. Bloom spices: 275 to 300°F. Simmer sauce: 250 to 275°F. Add cream: warm, 200 to 225°F, or off heat. Rest: off heat, covered.

Do Not Forget

This recipe is written with yogurt as the proper korma marinade base.

The sauce is intentionally generous for rice, naan, and sides.

Brown the onions slowly. Pale onions make a flat korma; burnt onions make a bitter korma.

Blend the onion-cashew paste very smooth.

Do not hard-boil the sauce after the marinated chicken goes in.

Add heavy cream at the end on low heat or off heat.

Rest the finished korma 5 to 10 minutes before serving.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken. In a bowl, combine 1/3 cup plain full-fat yogurt, 1 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 3/4 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp ground coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile powder, 1/4 tsp garam masala, and 1 tsp neutral oil. Coat 4 boneless chicken thighs completely. Cover and refrigerate at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  2. Start the browned onion-cashew paste. Set the electric skillet to 300 to 325°F. Add 1 1/2 Tbsp neutral oil or butter/oil mix and 1 large or 1 1/2 medium thin-sliced onions with a small pinch of salt. Cook, stirring often, until deeply golden, about 15 to 25 minutes.
  3. Toast the cashews. Lower heat around 300°F. Add 1/3 cup cashews and cook 1 to 2 minutes, just until lightly toasted and fragrant.
  4. Blend the paste. Transfer onion-cashew mixture to a blender cup. Add 3 Tbsp chicken broth or hot water and blend smooth, adding up to 2 Tbsp more liquid as needed.
  5. Sear the marinated chicken. Set skillet to 325 to 350°F. Add a little oil if the skillet is dry. Add chicken and sear 2 to 3 minutes per side, just until lightly browned. Remove to a plate.
  6. Clean only if needed. If any marinade scorched black, wipe that spot out. Keep golden fond if it smells savory.
  7. Bloom whole spices. Lower skillet to 275 to 300°F. Add 1 Tbsp neutral oil or butter/oil mix. Add 2 cracked green cardamom pods, 1 small cinnamon stick, 2 cloves, and 1 bay leaf. Bloom 30 to 60 seconds.
  8. Bloom powdered spices. Add 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/4 tsp turmeric, 1/4 tsp paprika or Kashmiri-style chile, and optional pinch cayenne. Stir 20 to 30 seconds. If dry, splash in 1 Tbsp broth.
  9. Fry the onion-cashew paste. Add the blended paste and cook 2 to 4 minutes, stirring often, until nutty and rounded.
  10. Build the gravy. Add 3/4 cup chicken broth slowly, stirring until smooth. For extra sauce, add more broth gradually, up to 1 cup total, keeping the gravy thick and spoonable.
  11. Simmer the chicken. Return chicken and juices to the skillet. Lower to 250 to 275°F. Cover and simmer gently 12 to 18 minutes, turning once, until tender and cooked through.
  12. Reduce if needed. If the sauce is thinner than desired, uncover and simmer gently 3 to 5 minutes before adding cream.
  13. Finish the korma. Turn skillet to warm, 200 to 225°F, or turn heat off. Stir in 2 Tbsp heavy cream and 1/4 tsp garam masala. Taste and adjust with salt. Add 1/4 tsp sugar or honey only if sharp or too tangy. Add a tiny squeeze of lemon or lime only if flat.
  14. Rest. Turn off heat, cover, and rest 5 to 10 minutes. Remove the cinnamon stick, bay leaf, cloves, and cardamom pods if you can find them.
  15. Serve. Spoon generously over basmati rice or serve with naan and sides.

Building the Final Plate

1. Rice or Bread Base

Add basmati rice to the plate or bowl, or serve naan alongside for scooping.

2. Korma Anchor

Spoon chicken and generous browned onion-cashew gravy over or beside the rice. Keep visible sauce around the chicken.

3. Green Curry Contrast

If serving with palak paneer, keep it beside the korma rather than mixing the sauces.

4. Crispy Side

Add aloo pakora or another crisp potato side for texture contrast.

5. Chutney Finish

Serve carrot coconut chutney on the side so it can cut the richness.

Final Taste Target

The finished korma should be silky, warmly spiced, savory-sweet from browned onion, rounded with cashew, gently tangy from the yogurt marinade, and lightly enriched with cream.

The chicken should be tender and juicy. The sauce should be generous, smooth, and spoonable, with enough body to cling to rice or naan without becoming heavy.

Chef's Notes

This korma gets its restaurant-level feel from technique, not expensive finishing ingredients. The important moves are slow-browning the onions, lightly toasting the cashews, blending the paste smooth, blooming whole spices in fat, controlling heat in the electric skillet, and finishing with a small amount of cream.

This recipe does not use saffron, rose water, kewra, silver leaf, or other luxury finishes. It leans Mughlai-style through browned onion-cashew gravy, yogurt marinade, whole warm spices, gentle richness, and restrained heat.

Sour Cream Substitution Note

Replace the 1/3 cup plain yogurt in the marinade with 1/3 cup sour cream.

Sear the chicken gently, avoid scorching the marinade, and do not hard-boil the sauce after the chicken is added. Add 1/4 tsp sugar or honey near the end only if the sauce tastes too sharp or tangy.
