Split Pea Alicha

A mild golden split pea stew designed as the practical, repeatable side for the Ethiopian-inspired Doro Wat platter.

Prep15m
Cook40-60m
Total1h 15m
Serves2-4 side servings
Yellow split peasAlichaMild sideCreamy stewDoro Wat platter

Kitchen Execution Summary

Recipe Role

Practical mild side for the Ethiopian-inspired Doro Wat platter.

Flavor Target

Mild, golden, warm, lightly aromatic, and creamy enough to balance spicy berbere-style chicken.

Texture Target

Thick, spoonable, creamy stew with some split pea texture remaining.

Key Technique

Simmer until very tender, then mash part of the peas against the pot to create creamy body.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Split Peas

1 cup yellow split peas, rinsed until the water runs mostly clear and checked for debris.

Bowl 2 — Cooking Liquid

3 to 3 1/2 cups water or broth, plus extra nearby only if the peas need more simmering liquid.

Bowl 3 — Aromatics

1 small finely diced onion, 2 minced garlic cloves, and 1 tsp fresh grated ginger or ginger paste.

Bowl 4 — Spice Bloom

1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/4 tsp black pepper, and optional small pinch cayenne or berbere.

Bowl 5 — Cooking Fat

1 to 2 Tbsp unsalted butter or oil for cooking the onion and blooming the spices.

Bowl 6 — Finish

Salt to taste for final seasoning and optional small squeeze lemon juice only if the stew tastes heavy or flat.

Do Not Forget

Rinse the split peas well before cooking.

Yellow split peas are preferred; green split peas are backup only.

Cook the onion long enough to soften, reduce, and lightly golden before adding garlic, ginger, and spices.

Bloom the spices briefly, but do not burn them.

Start with 3 cups water or broth and add more only as needed while the peas soften.

Mash 1/3 to 1/2 of the peas for a creamier stew body.

Add salt near the end, then use lemon only if the stew tastes heavy or flat.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Rinse 1 cup yellow split peas in a fine mesh strainer until the water runs mostly clear. Pick out debris or damaged pieces.
  2. Melt 1 to 2 Tbsp unsalted butter or oil in a saucepan or small Dutch oven. Add 1 small finely diced onion and cook 8 to 12 minutes until softened, reduced, and lightly golden. Add tiny splashes of water if needed to prevent sticking.
  3. Add 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp fresh grated ginger or ginger paste, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/4 tsp black pepper, and optional small pinch cayenne or berbere. Cook 30 to 60 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant. Do not burn the spices.
  4. Add the rinsed 1 cup yellow split peas and 3 cups water or broth. Stir well and scrape the bottom of the pot. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Simmer uncovered or partially covered for 40 to 60 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the peas are very tender and beginning to break down. Add the remaining 1/2 cup water or broth, or more as needed, only if the peas need more liquid to soften.
  6. Mash some peas against the side of the pot to create a creamy body. For a creamier stew, mash 1/3 to 1/2 of the mixture.
  7. Continue simmering until thick, spoonable, and stew-like.
  8. Season with salt to taste near the end. Add an optional small squeeze of lemon juice only if the stew tastes heavy or flat.

Timing Notes

Optional soaking can reduce cooking time and help the peas soften more evenly. Quick soak 1 cup yellow split peas in water for 2 to 4 hours, then drain and rinse.

No-soak works, but may need more simmering time and additional liquid beyond the listed 3 to 3 1/2 cups water or broth.

Final Texture and Taste Target

The finished alicha should be thick, spoonable, creamy, warm, and mild, with enough body to sit on a plate beside Doro Wat-style chicken without running.

It should provide a gentle golden counterpoint to the spicy berbere-style chicken and the fresh acidic Timatim Salad.

Chef's Notes

Yellow split peas are the preferred version. They cook into a creamy golden stew and provide a mild counterpoint to the spicy Doro Wat-style chicken.

Green split peas are a backup. They work with the same method, but the flavor will be earthier and the color will be darker green-brown.

Avoid forcing brown or green whole lentils if split peas are available. Whole lentils hold their shape more and do not create the same creamy stew texture.
