## Measured Ingredients

### Vegetables

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 2 medium or 3 small | Red potatoes | Unpeeled, scrubbed, cut into 3/4-inch chunks or thick half-moons |
| 2 | Carrots | Peeled if needed, sliced on the bias into 1/4-inch thick pieces |
| 1/4 to 1/3 small | Green cabbage | Chopped |
| 1 | Onion | Thinly sliced into fajita-style half-moons, about 1/8 to 1/4 inch thick |

| 1 Tbsp | Fresh ginger or ginger paste |  |

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

| Bowl | Contents | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bowl 1 — Potatoes | Red potatoes, scrubbed and cut into 3/4-inch chunks or thick half-moons | Keep pieces large enough to stay intact. |
| Bowl 2 — Carrots | Carrots sliced on the bias into 1/4-inch pieces | Bias cut gives better surface area and plate presence. |
| Bowl 3 — Cabbage | Chopped green cabbage | Add after potatoes and carrots begin softening. |
| Bowl 4 — Onion | Thin fajita-style half-moons | Cook first until softened and lightly golden. |
| Bowl 5 — Aromatics | Garlic, ginger, optional jalapeño or serrano | Add after onion softens. |
| Bowl 6 — Spice Bloom | Turmeric, cumin, black pepper, fenugreek, green cardamom or korarima, optional cinnamon, optional clove, kosher salt | Bloom in fat and aromatics before adding vegetables. |
| Bowl 7 — Cooking Liquid | 2 to 4 Tbsp water or broth | Add more only if pan dries before vegetables are tender. |

## Do Not Forget

- This V2 is intentionally more seasoned than the first version.
- Do not remove the measured salt. Unsalted butter is used for salt control, not for salt omission.
- Bloom the spices in the fat and aromatics for 30 to 60 seconds before adding vegetables.
- Add potatoes and carrots before cabbage so the denser vegetables get a head start.
- Add only small splashes of water or broth as needed; the cabbage should be tender, not watery.
- Add lemon only if the finished vegetables taste flat after the measured salt has had time to work.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Heat the oil, unsalted butter, or oil + butter in a large skillet, sauté pan, or Dutch oven over medium heat. |
| 2 | Add the sliced onion and cook until softened and lightly golden, about 6 to 8 minutes. |
| 3 | Add the garlic, ginger, and jalapeño or serrano if using. Cook for 30 to 60 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant. |
| 4 | Add the turmeric, cumin, black pepper, fenugreek, green cardamom or korarima if using, cinnamon if using, clove if using, and kosher salt. Stir the spices into the fat and aromatics for 30 to 60 seconds to bloom them. |
| 5 | Add the potatoes and carrots. Stir to coat them well in the spiced fat. |
| 6 | Add 2 to 4 Tbsp water or broth. Cover and cook until the potatoes and carrots are beginning to soften, about 8 to 12 minutes. |
| 7 | Add the cabbage. Stir gently to coat. |
| 8 | Cover again and cook until the cabbage is tender and the potatoes are fully cooked, about 8 to 12 more minutes. |
| 9 | If the pan gets dry before the vegetables are tender, add another small splash of water or broth. |
| 10 | Taste before serving. Add a tiny squeeze of lemon only if the finished vegetables taste flat after the measured salt has had time to work. |

## Flavor Target

The finished Atakilt Wat should be warm, savory, aromatic, and intentionally seasoned.

The potatoes and carrots should be tender but not falling apart.

The cabbage should be tender, not watery or bland.

Use red potatoes for this V2. Keep the pieces large enough that they become tender without disappearing into the cabbage.
