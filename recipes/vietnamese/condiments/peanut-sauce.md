# Peanut Dipping Sauce

> A creamy peanut dipping sauce built with a generous amount of ginger nuoc cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime.

## Recipe Dashboard

| Status | Total Time | Yield | Difficulty |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Working Recipe | 10 min | About 1 to 1 1/4 cups | Easy |

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Area | Notes |
|---|---|
| Sauce style | A flexible one-bowl peanut dipping sauce that can be kept thick for dipping or thinned for drizzling. |
| Required base | [Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham](ginger-nuoc-cham.md) provides the sweet-salty acidic backbone. Use enough for it to come through against the peanut butter. |
| Best use | Primary dipping sauce for fresh spring rolls and an optional drizzle for bowls or grilled chicken. |
| Texture target | Creamy, smooth, glossy, and dippable. Add warm water slowly only after the flavor balance is right. |

## Required Components

| Component | Role |
|---|---|
| Peanut Base | Creamy peanut butter gives the sauce its body and richness. |
| Seasoning Base | Ginger Nuoc Cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime create the sweet-salty-bright balance. |
| Texture Adjustment | Warm water is added 1 tablespoon at a time only if the sauce needs thinning after the Ginger Nuoc Cham is balanced. |
| Optional Finish | Sesame oil adds deeper richness; minced chile adds heat. |

## Full Sauce Ingredient Roll-Up

| Component | Ingredients |
|---|---|
| One-Bowl Sauce | 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter, 1/4 cup to 1/2 cup Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham, 1 Tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 minced garlic clove, 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste, juice from 1/2 lime, and 1 to 3 Tbsp warm water only as needed. |
| Optional Additions | Optional 1/2 tsp sesame oil for deeper richness and optional minced chile for heat. |

## Cross-Component Bulk Prep Map

- **Garlic:** mince **1 garlic clove**.
- **Ginger:** grate or measure **1 tsp ginger**.
- **Citrus:** juice **1/2 lime**.
- **Nuoc cham:** measure **1/4 cup** first, with up to **1/2 cup total** available for the final brightness.
- **Texture liquid:** warm **1 to 3 Tbsp water** and add it slowly only if the sauce needs thinning.
- **Optional heat:** mince chile only if using.

## Do Not Forget

- This is a one-bowl sauce. Do not over-stage it.
- Use enough Ginger Nuoc Cham for the sweet-salty-bright flavor to come through the peanut butter.
- Add warm water **1 Tbsp at a time** only after the flavor is balanced, so the sauce stays thick enough for dipping.
- Use less water for a thicker dipping sauce or more water for a drizzleable bowl sauce.

## Full Method and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Combine the sauce base.** In one bowl, whisk **1/2 cup creamy peanut butter**, **1/4 cup Ginger Nuoc Cham**, **1 Tbsp hoisin sauce**, **1 Tbsp soy sauce**, **1 minced garlic clove**, **1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste**, and juice from **1/2 lime** until combined. |
| 2 | **Brighten if needed.** Taste the sauce. Add more Ginger Nuoc Cham, up to **1/2 cup total**, until the peanut sauce tastes sweet-salty-bright enough. |
| 3 | **Thin gradually.** Add **1 to 3 Tbsp warm water**, **1 Tbsp at a time**, whisking after each addition until smooth and dippable. |
| 4 | **Add optional finish.** Add optional **1/2 tsp sesame oil** or minced chile if desired. |
| 5 | **Adjust only if needed.** Taste and adjust lime, water, heat, or saltiness as needed. |

## Final Taste Target

The sauce should be creamy, smooth, nutty, sweet-salty, and bright enough that the Ginger Nuoc Cham is clearly present through the peanut butter. It should be thick enough to cling to fresh spring rolls without overpowering them.

## Chef's Notes

This sauce is meant to be flexible. Use less water for a thicker dipping sauce or more water for a drizzleable bowl sauce.

Live test correction: 2 Tbsp Ginger Nuoc Cham was too quiet against 1/2 cup peanut butter. Start with 1/4 cup and increase toward 1/2 cup until the sauce tastes balanced.
