# Western Fries

> A preserved family side dish made with skin-on russet potato wedges coated in chili powder, Cajun spice, sugar, optional salt, and baked until crisp outside and tender inside.

## Measured Ingredients

### Western Fries

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 3 very large | Russet potatoes | Washed, peel left on, sliced lengthwise into eighths |
| 1/4 tsp | Sugar |  |
| Optional | Salt |  |
| 2-3 Tbsp | Chili powder |  |
| 1 tsp | Cajun spice blend |  |
| As needed | Non-stick cooking spray | For foil and coating potato wedges |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Preheat oven to 400°F. |
| 2 | Cover a large baking sheet with foil. |
| 3 | Wash potatoes and leave the peeling on. |
| 4 | Slice potatoes lengthwise into eighths. |
| 5 | Pat potato wedges dry with paper towels. |
| 6 | Place potatoes on the baking sheet. |
| 7 | Coat all sides of the potatoes with non-stick cooking spray. |
| 8 | Blend together sugar, optional salt, chili powder, and Cajun spice. |
| 9 | Place seasoning mixture in a shaker, if available, for easier coating. |
| 10 | Sprinkle seasoning evenly over the potatoes, coating all sides. |
| 11 | Place potatoes in the oven, then lower temperature to 350°F. |
| 12 | Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until potatoes are crisp on the outside and tender when pierced with a sharp knife or fork. |

Original hint: putting the spice mixture into a shaker makes it much easier to coat the potatoes evenly.
