Thai-Inspired Bang Bang Shrimp Bowls

Sweet, spicy, creamy, crispy shrimp bowls layered with coconut-lime rice, purple cabbage-carrot mint slaw, toasted coconut, crispy fried shallot rings, green onion, mint, cashews, and lime.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Complete Bowl

This card covers the full bowl: chili-garlic paste, sweet chili sauce, creamy shrimp sauce, crispy fried shrimp, coconut-lime rice, purple cabbage-carrot mint slaw, and crispy fried shallot rings.

Shrimp

Shrimp are briefly soaked, coated in seasoned cornstarch, fried until light and crisp, then tossed with creamy sweet-spicy sauce right before serving.

Rice

Basmati rice is cooked with coconut milk, water, salt, and sugar, then finished with lime zest, lime juice, toasted coconut flakes, and green onion.

Slaw

Purple cabbage and carrots are softened with lime, rice vinegar, fish sauce, ginger, garlic, chile, mint, green onion, and toasted cashews.

Crunch Finish

Crispy fried shallot rings act like tiny onion rings and give the bowl its restaurant-style finish.

Before Cooking / Advance Prep

Thaw, peel, devein, and pat dry the shrimp. Slice cabbage very thin, julienne or shred carrots, and slice shallots into rings. Set out the rice cooker, frying pot, sheet tray or rack, and mixing bowls. Toast coconut and cashews before final assembly when possible. Sauce the shrimp at service and add crispy shallots last.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Quick Chili-Garlic Paste

2 to 4 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded; 4 garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp rice vinegar or white vinegar; 1 Tbsp water; 1 tsp sugar; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper or 1/4 tsp cayenne.

Homemade Sweet Chili Sauce

1/2 cup water; 1/4 cup rice vinegar or white vinegar; 1/3 cup sugar; 1 to 2 Tbsp chili-garlic paste; 1 grated garlic clove; 1/2 tsp ginger paste; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; slurry of 1 tsp cornstarch and 1 Tbsp cold water.

Quick Dairy Soak

Evaporated milk version: 3/4 cup evaporated milk, 3/4 cup water, 1 1/2 Tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice.

Heavy cream version: 1/2 cup heavy cream, 1 cup water, 1 1/2 Tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice.

Creamy Shrimp Sauce

1/2 cup mayonnaise; 2 to 3 Tbsp homemade sweet chili sauce; 1 to 2 tsp chili-garlic paste; 1/2 tsp lime juice; tiny pinch kosher salt.

Crispy Shrimp

1 lb shrimp, peeled, deveined, thawed, and patted dry; 1 cup quick dairy soak; 1 cup cornstarch; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper; neutral oil; creamy shrimp sauce; sliced green onion.

Coconut-Lime Rice

1 cup basmati rice, rinsed; 3/4 cup coconut milk; 3/4 cup water; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1 tsp sugar; zest of 1 lime; 1 to 2 tsp lime juice; 1/4 to 1/3 cup toasted unsweetened coconut flakes; 1 sliced green onion; optional 1 tsp neutral oil or clean shallot oil.

Thick coconut milk rice cooker ratio: 1 cup basmati rice, 1/2 cup coconut milk, 1 cup water, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp sugar.

Purple Cabbage-Carrot Mint Slaw

3 cups very thin sliced purple cabbage; 1 1/2 cups shredded or julienned carrots; 2 sliced green onions; 1 to 2 Tbsp chopped mint; 1/3 cup toasted chopped cashews; lime zest from 1/2 lime.

Dressing: 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 Tbsp fish sauce, 1 tsp soy sauce, 1 1/2 tsp honey or sugar, 1 tsp ginger paste, 1 grated garlic clove, 1 tsp chili-garlic paste, 1 tsp neutral oil, 1/2 tsp sesame oil, pinch black pepper.

Crispy Fried Shallot Rings

3 to 4 large shallots; 1/2 cup quick dairy soak; 1/2 cup all-purpose flour; 1/4 cup cornstarch; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper; 1/4 tsp garlic powder; 1/4 tsp paprika; 1/4 tsp baking powder; tiny pinch cayenne; neutral oil.

Do Not Forget

Cool the sweet chili sauce before mixing it into mayonnaise. Make enough quick dairy soak for both the shrimp and shallots. Soak shrimp only 10 minutes. Do not add water to the shrimp coating. Fry shrimp in small batches and toss with sauce right before serving. Add toasted coconut after rice cooks. Add mint, cashews, and crispy shallots at the end. Use tongs for slaw if it releases extra dressing. Keep extra sauce on the side instead of drowning the bowl.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Make chili-garlic paste. Soak guajillo chiles 10 to 15 minutes, drain, then blend with garlic, vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and heat into a thick red paste.
  2. Make sweet chili sauce. Simmer water, vinegar, sugar, chili-garlic paste, garlic, ginger, and salt for 3 to 5 minutes.
  3. Gloss sweet chili sauce. Add cornstarch slurry and simmer 30 to 60 seconds. Cool completely.
  4. Make quick dairy soak. Mix one dairy soak version and let sit 5 to 10 minutes.
  5. Cook coconut-lime rice. Rinse rice and cook with coconut milk, water, salt, and sugar. Fold in lime zest, lime juice, toasted coconut, green onion, and a small drizzle of oil after cooking.
  6. Toast coconut and cashews. Toast each separately in a dry skillet until fragrant and lightly golden.
  7. Make slaw dressing. Whisk lime juice, rice vinegar, fish sauce, soy sauce, honey or sugar, ginger, garlic, chili-garlic paste, neutral oil, sesame oil, and pepper.
  8. Build slaw. Toss cabbage and carrots with dressing. Massage 30 to 60 seconds. Rest 15 to 20 minutes. Add green onion, mint, cashews, and lime zest at service.
  9. Make creamy shrimp sauce. Whisk mayonnaise, cooled sweet chili sauce, chili-garlic paste, lime juice, and salt. Chill until needed.
  10. Make crispy shallot rings. Coat shallot rings in quick dairy soak and dry mix, then fry at 350°F for 1 to 2 minutes. Salt lightly and hold uncovered.
  11. Soak shrimp. Pat shrimp dry and soak in quick dairy soak for 10 minutes only.
  12. Coat shrimp. Toss drained shrimp in seasoned cornstarch and shake off excess.
  13. Fry shrimp. Fry at 350°F to 375°F in small batches for 1 to 2 minutes, until crisp and cooked through.
  14. Sauce shrimp. Toss hot fried shrimp with just enough creamy sauce to coat.
  15. Build bowls. Add coconut-lime rice, slaw, sauced shrimp, crispy shallot rings, toasted coconut, cashews, green onion, mint, and lime wedge. Serve immediately.

Building the Final Bowl

1 — Rice Foundation

Add coconut-lime rice to each bowl and spread slightly so it can catch sauce without becoming buried.

2 — Slaw Layer

Add purple cabbage-carrot mint slaw beside the rice or slightly over one edge. Keep the color visible.

3 — Shrimp Anchor

Pile sauced crispy shrimp as the main anchor. Let some sauce touch the rice, but do not cover everything.

4 — Crunch Finish

Top with crispy fried shallot rings, toasted coconut flakes, and chopped toasted cashews.

5 — Fresh Finish

Finish with green onion, mint, and lime wedge. Serve extra sauce on the side.

Final Taste Target

The finished bowl should be creamy, crispy, sweet, spicy, bright, savory, nutty, and fresh. The shrimp should be crisp under a light coating of sauce. The rice should be soft, fragrant, coconut-lime bright, and lightly nutty from toasted coconut. The slaw should be sharp enough to cut the creamy shrimp but not harsh. The shallot rings should give a sweet-savory onion-ring crunch. The whole bowl should feel layered, intentional, and restaurant-worthy.

Chef's Notes

Cool the sweet chili sauce before mixing it into mayonnaise. Warm sauce can loosen the creamy shrimp sauce. Cornstarch gives the shrimp its light crisp coating. The slaw should rest before serving, but mint, cashews, and crispy shallots should go in at the end. Toasted coconut belongs as a finish for the rice, not inside the rice cooker. Crispy shallot rings are a final crunch element.
