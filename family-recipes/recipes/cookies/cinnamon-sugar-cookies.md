# Cinnamon Sugar Cookies

> A preserved family cinnamon sugar cookie recipe made with softened margarine, vegetable oil, vanilla, cream of tartar, and cinnamon.

## Measured Ingredients

### Cinnamon Sugar Cookies

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 stick | Margarine | Softened |
| 1 cup | Vegetable oil |  |
| 1 | Egg |  |
| 1 cup | Sugar |  |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla |  |
| 2 1/2-3 cups | Flour | Use enough to make a good stiff, workable dough |
| 1 tsp | Cream of tartar |  |

| 1/2 tsp | Cinnamon |  |
| As needed | Sugar | For dipping the glass before pressing cookies |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Preheat oven to 350°F. |
| 2 | Mix softened margarine, vegetable oil, egg, sugar, and vanilla together. Beat until thoroughly mixed. |
| 3 | Add all dry ingredients and blend until all the flour is moistened. |
| 4 | Add enough flour to make a good stiff dough that is workable. |
| 5 | Roll dough into small balls. |
| 6 | Press each cookie with the bottom of a glass dipped in sugar. Do not press too hard or the cookies will be too thin and may break. |
| 7 | Bake approximately 10 minutes, or until lightly browned. |

## Notes

Original family note: the recipe originally called for 2 1/2 cups flour, but more flour is often needed to make a good stiff dough that is workable.

Original family note: at Christmas time, use red and green colored sugar for pressing the cookies. It makes pretty cookies and they are simple and quick to prepare.

The original recipe note says a whole recipe can be made in about 1 hour. Servings are estimated from the ingredient amounts and small-ball cookie method.
