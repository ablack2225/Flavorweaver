# Dill Pickles

> A preserved family dill pickle recipe made with small whole cucumbers, white vinegar, water, pickling salt, turmeric, fresh dill, garlic, optional hot pepper, and alum for each quart jar.

## Measured Ingredients

### Brine

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 quart | White vinegar |  |
| 3 quarts | Water |  |
| 1 cup | Pickling salt |  |
| 1 1/2 tsp | Turmeric |  |

### Per Quart Jar

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| Enough to fill jar | Small whole cucumbers | Washed |
| 1/2 tsp | Alum | Per quart jar |
| As desired | Fresh dill |  |
| 1-2 cloves | Garlic | Per quart jar |
| 1 | Hot pepper | Optional; per quart jar |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Wash cucumbers. |
| 2 | Pack cucumbers into clean, hot quart-size jars. |
| 3 | Add 1/2 tsp alum, 1-2 cloves garlic, fresh dill, and hot pepper if desired to each quart jar. |
| 4 | In a saucepan, mix vinegar, water, pickling salt, and turmeric. |
| 5 | Bring brine to a rolling boil. |
| 6 | Pour the hot vinegar solution over the fresh cucumbers. |
| 7 | Seal immediately. |

## Notes

Alum is preserved as written in the family recipe. It appears here as the traditional crisping ingredient for each quart jar.

For modern shelf-stable canning, use current tested canning guidance. This file preserves the family recipe as written, including the original immediate-seal method.

Yield depends on cucumber size and how tightly jars are packed. The brine itself makes about 4 quarts before accounting for cucumber displacement.
