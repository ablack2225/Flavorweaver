# Onion Bhaji-Inspired Fritters with Quick Green Chutney

> Crisp, onion-heavy Indian-inspired fritters made with an emergency all-purpose flour and cornstarch dry mix, served hot with a bright quick green chutney. The batter should be shaggy and sticky, never pourable.

## Do Not Forget

- Salt and rest the onions first so their moisture helps form the batter.
- The batter should be thick, sticky, shaggy, and onion-heavy.
- Do not make a smooth or pourable batter.
- Add water only **1 Tbsp at a time**, and only if needed.
- Drop loose clumps into the oil; do not roll tight balls.
- Fry a test fritter before frying the full batch.
- Optional double-fry gives extra crispness right before serving.
- This is an emergency version made without besan or rice flour.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Slice and salt onions.** Thinly slice **2 cups onion** and separate the layers with your fingers. Add **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, mix, and gently squeeze with your hands. Rest **15 to 20 minutes** so the onions release moisture. |
| 2 | **Add aromatics.** To the salted onions, add **1 Tbsp ginger**, **1 Tbsp garlic**, optional minced serrano or green chili, and **1/4 cup chopped cilantro**. Mix well. |
| 3 | **Add dry mix.** Add **1/2 cup all-purpose flour**, **1/4 cup cornstarch**, **1 tsp coriander**, **3/4 tsp garam masala**, **1/2 tsp cumin**, **1/2 tsp chili powder or flakes**, **1/8 tsp baking soda**, and optional **1/2 tsp crushed fennel seed**. Mix until onions are coated. |
| 4 | **Adjust batter texture.** Squeeze and mix well. If too dry to hold together, add water **1 Tbsp at a time**, only enough to make loose clumps. The final texture should be thick, sticky, shaggy, and onion-heavy. |
| 5 | **Make quick green chutney.** Blend **1 packed cup cilantro**, **1/4 cup mint or parsley**, **1 serrano or jalapeño**, **1 garlic clove**, **1 tsp ginger**, **1 Tbsp lemon or lime juice**, **1/2 tsp cumin**, optional **1/4 tsp coriander**, **1/4 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp sugar or honey**, and **2 Tbsp cold water**. Add more water 1 Tbsp at a time only if needed. Chill until serving if time allows. |
| 6 | **Heat oil.** Heat neutral oil to **350°F**. Use enough oil for fritters to float or mostly float. |
| 7 | **Fry a test fritter.** Drop one small loose spoonful of batter into the hot oil. Do not roll it into a tight ball. Fry **2 to 4 minutes**, turning once, until golden and crisp. Taste and adjust salt or spice if needed. |
| 8 | **Fry the full batch.** Drop small loose clumps into the oil. Fry in batches so the oil is not crowded. Cook each batch **3 to 5 minutes**, turning as needed, until deep golden and crisp. Drain on a rack or paper towels. |
| 9 | **Optional double-fry.** For extra crisp fritters, fry all batches once. Right before serving, return them to hot oil for **1 minute**, then drain again. |
| 10 | **Serve hot.** Serve with quick green chutney, lemon wedges, and any Indian-inspired main dish or bread. |

## Test Fry Troubleshooting

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Batter will not hold | Add water 1 Tbsp at a time, or let onions rest a few more minutes. |
| Batter is too wet | Add a small spoonful of flour or cornstarch and mix again. |
| Fritter tastes flat | Add a pinch more salt, garam masala, cumin, or chili. |
| Fritter browns too fast | Lower oil temperature slightly and keep closer to 350°F. |
| Fritter is greasy | Oil may be too cool or batch may be crowded. |

## Final Taste Target

The fritters should be deeply golden, crisp-edged, onion-forward, warmly spiced, and savory with enough structure to hold together without becoming bready.

The chutney should be bright, green, pourable, spicy-fresh, lightly tangy, balanced with salt, and not watery.

## Chef's Notes

This is not a traditional onion bhaji because it does not use besan or rice flour. It is an emergency pantry version using all-purpose flour for structure and cornstarch for crispness.

For a future true bhaji version, test with besan or gram flour, rice flour, and ajwain.
