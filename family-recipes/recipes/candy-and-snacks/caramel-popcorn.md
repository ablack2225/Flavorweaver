# Caramel Popcorn

> A preserved family caramel popcorn recipe cooked to candy temperature and poured over popped corn for a crisp holiday treat.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | In a large saucepan, combine sugars, syrup, water, and salt. |
| 2 | Bring to a boil. As the mixture begins to thicken, about 30 minutes, stir frequently. |
| 3 | Using a candy thermometer, cook mixture until it reaches 234°F. |
| 4 | Stir in pecan pieces, if using. |
| 5 | Continue cooking until mixture reaches 300°F. Make sure the mixture cooks at 300°F for 30 seconds. |
| 6 | Remove from heat and quickly add margarine, baking soda, and vanilla. |
| 7 | Blend well and pour over the popped corn. Stir to coat. |
| 8 | Turn out onto waxed paper and spread. |
| 9 | Cool, then break into pieces. |
| 10 | Store in an airtight container. |

## Notes

Original printed title appears as “Carmel Popcorn”; this file uses the standard spelling “Caramel Popcorn” for searchability.

Original family note: neighbor Paula shared this recipe in December 1982. It is one of Rudy's favorite treats during the holiday season, and when made, it quickly disappears.

The instructions mention pecan pieces, but the original ingredient list does not provide an amount. Pecan pieces are preserved as an unlisted optional ingredient.
