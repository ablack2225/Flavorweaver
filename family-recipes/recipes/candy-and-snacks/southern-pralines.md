# Southern Pralines

> A preserved family praline recipe made with granulated sugar, brown sugar, half-and-half cream, pecan halves, and a handwritten margarine addition.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Lightly butter a sheet of aluminum foil. |
| 2 | Combine granulated sugar, brown sugar, half-and-half cream, and salt in a large saucepan. |
| 3 | Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until mixture reaches 228°F on a candy thermometer. |
| 4 | Stir in margarine and pecans. |
| 5 | Continue cooking, stirring constantly, until mixture reaches 236°F. |
| 6 | Remove from heat. |
| 7 | Beat or stir until mixture begins to thicken and lose its gloss. |
| 8 | Drop by spoonfuls onto the buttered aluminum foil. |
| 9 | Let cool until set. |
