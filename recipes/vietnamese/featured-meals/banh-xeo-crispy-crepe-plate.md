# Banh Xeo Crispy Crepe Plate

> Crispy Vietnamese-inspired rice crepes filled with shrimp, thin pork, scallion, and bean sprouts, served with lettuce, herbs, cucumber, pickled vegetables, and bright nước chấm.

## Recipe Dashboard

| Status | Total Time | Servings | Difficulty |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Flavorweaver Test Recipe | 2 hr active + batter rest | 2 large servings / 4 medium crepes | Project Meal / Skillet Technique |

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Component | Role |
|---|---|
| Crispy Banh Xeo Batter | Rice flour, cornstarch, turmeric, coconut milk, cold bubbly water, scallion. Thin, crisp, lacy. |
| Shrimp + Pork Filling | Quick-seared shrimp and thin pork for classic savory filling. |
| Bean Sprouts | Added late so they stay crisp and fresh. |
| Lettuce + Herb Wrap Plate | The freshness that makes bánh xèo feel bright instead of heavy. |
| Nước Chấm | Sweet-sour-salty fish sauce dip; required. |
| Optional Peanut-Chile Drizzle | Flavorweaver accent only; use lightly, never as the main sauce. |

## Flavor Direction

The target is shatter-crisp edges, thin golden turmeric crepe, juicy shrimp, savory pork, hot bean sprouts, cold lettuce, herbs, cucumber, and bright nước chấm. This plate should feel sizzling, fresh, crunchy, acidic, and herb-heavy — not dense or pancake-like.

## Advance Prep

| Timing | Task |
|---|---|
| 2–24 hr ahead | Mix batter and refrigerate. Longer rest improves hydration and crispness. |
| Up to 1 day ahead | Make nước chấm and refrigerate. |
| Same day | Prep lettuce, herbs, cucumber, bean sprouts, shrimp, and pork. |
| At service | Cook crepes one at a time and eat hot. |

## Full Plate Roll-Up

For 2 large servings, plan on **4 medium crepes**. Serve each crepe cut or torn into lettuce cups with herbs and nước chấm. The crepes should be cooked fresh and eaten immediately; bánh xèo loses its magic if held too long.

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

| Bowl | Contents | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bowl 1 — Batter Dry | **1 cup rice flour**, **2 Tbsp cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp sugar** | Whisk thoroughly before liquids. |
| Bowl 2 — Batter Liquids | **3/4 cup coconut milk**, **3/4 cup cold sparkling water or cold water** | Whisk into dry bowl until smooth. Rest 30 min to overnight. |
| Bowl 3 — Scallions | **2 thin-sliced scallions** | Stir into batter right before cooking. |

## Do Not Forget

- Batter should be thin like heavy cream, not pancake batter.
- Rest batter at least **30 minutes**; longer is better.
- Stir batter before every crepe because rice flour settles.
- Use enough oil and a hot pan for crisp edges.
- Do not overload the crepe; too much filling makes it steam instead of crisp.
- Serve immediately with lettuce, herbs, and nước chấm.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Make batter.** In Bowl 1, whisk **1 cup rice flour**, **2 Tbsp cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/2 tsp sugar**. Whisk in Bowl 2: **3/4 cup coconut milk** and **3/4 cup cold sparkling water or cold water** until smooth.
2. **Rest batter.** Cover batter and rest at least **30 minutes**, or refrigerate up to **24 hours**. After resting, stir in Bowl 3: **2 thin-sliced scallions**. Stir well before each crepe.
3. **Make nước chấm.** In Bowl 7, stir **3 Tbsp warm water** with **2 Tbsp sugar** until dissolved. Add **2 Tbsp fish sauce**, **2 Tbsp lime juice**, **1 Tbsp rice vinegar**, **1 minced/grated garlic clove**, chile to taste, and optional **1 Tbsp shredded carrot**. Taste: it should be sweet-salty-sour and bright.
4. **Make optional drizzle.** If using, stir Bowl 8: **2 Tbsp peanut butter**, **1 Tbsp nước chấm**, **1–2 tsp warm water**, **1 tsp chili-garlic paste**, and **1/2 tsp lime juice** until spoonable. Keep this light; it is an accent, not the main sauce.
5. **Prep fresh plate.** Arrange Bowl 6: **8–10 lettuce leaves**, **1 cup fresh herbs**, **1/2 cucumber**, optional **1/2 cup pickled veg**, and lime wedges. Keep cold.
6. **Season filling.** Toss Bowl 4: **8 oz shrimp**, **8 oz thin pork**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, and **1 tsp fish sauce**. Rest **10–15 minutes**.
7. **Pre-cook filling lightly.** In a skillet, cook the pork and shrimp over medium-high heat with optional **1 tsp oil** just until pork loses raw color and shrimp is barely opaque, about **2–3 minutes**. Remove to a plate. Do not cook fully dry.
8. **Heat crepe pan.** Wipe skillet clean. Heat over medium-high until hot. Add **1–2 Tbsp neutral oil** and swirl. The batter should sizzle immediately when it hits the pan.
9. **Pour one crepe.** Stir batter well. Add about **1/2 cup batter** to the hot pan and immediately swirl into a thin crepe, about **9–10 inches** wide. It should hiss and bubble.
10. **Fill one side.** Scatter one-quarter of the pork/shrimp filling over one half of the crepe. Add one-quarter of Bowl 5: **bean sprouts and onion** over the same half.
11. **Crisp.** Cook uncovered **4–6 minutes**, adjusting heat as needed, until the edges are crisp, the bottom is golden, and the center is set. Add a small extra drizzle of oil around the edge if needed for crisping.
12. **Fold.** Fold the empty half over the filling. Slide onto a rack or plate. Do not stack directly or the crepes will soften.
13. **Repeat.** Repeat with remaining batter, filling, and sprouts for **4 crepes total**, stirring batter before each one and adding fresh oil as needed.
14. **Serve immediately.** Tear pieces of hot crepe and tuck into lettuce with herbs, cucumber, pickled veg, and nước chấm. Add a tiny drizzle of peanut-chile sauce only if desired.

## Electric Skillet Notes

- Preheat the electric skillet around **325°F**, then adjust between **300–325°F** based on browning.
- If the batter browns before crisping, lower slightly.
- If it steams and stays soft, raise slightly and use a little more oil.
- Cook one crepe at a time unless the skillet has enough room to swirl thinly.

## Final Taste Target

The plate should be hot-cold, crisp-fresh, and sauce-bright: brittle golden crepe edges, tender shrimp and pork, crunchy bean sprouts, cold lettuce, herbs, cucumber, and punchy nước chấm. The best bite is crepe + lettuce + herbs + cucumber + dip.
