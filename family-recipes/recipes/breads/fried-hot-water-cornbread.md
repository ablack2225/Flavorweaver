# Fried Hot Water Cornbread

> A preserved family fried hot water cornbread made from flour, cornmeal, baking powder, sugar, salt, and boiling water, formed into patties and fried until golden brown.

## Measured Ingredients

### Fried Hot Water Cornbread

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 cup | Flour |  |
| 1 cup | Cornmeal |  |
| 1 heaping tsp | Baking powder |  |
| 2 Tbsp | Sugar |  |
| 1 tsp | Salt |  |
| As needed | Boiling water | Add a little at a time to form a sticky dough |
| About 1/2 inch | Cooking oil | For frying in skillet |
| For serving | Butter |  |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Mix all dry ingredients together. |
| 2 | Pour in boiling water a little at a time until a good sticky dough forms. |
| 3 | Shape the dough into patties. |
| 4 | Heat about 1/2 inch of cooking oil in a large skillet over medium heat. |
| 5 | Add the patties and fry until golden brown, flipping about halfway through cooking time. |
| 6 | Cook about 12-14 minutes total, or until cooked completely. |
| 7 | Drain on paper towels. |
| 8 | Serve with lots of butter. |
