# Cornbread Dressing

> A preserved family cornbread dressing recipe made with a large pan of cornbread, toasted bread, onion, green onions, celery, eggs, chicken broth, and simple salt and pepper seasoning.

## Measured Ingredients

### Cornbread Dressing

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 large pan | Cornbread | Bake ahead of time and allow to cool |
| 4 slices | Bread | Lightly toasted |
| 1/2 medium | Onion | Chopped; uncooked |
| 2 bunches | Green onions | Chopped |
| 2 stalks | Celery | Chopped |
| 1/4 cup | Margarine | For sautéing green onions and celery |
| 2 | Eggs | Beaten into moistened mixture |
| 4-5 (16 oz) cans | Chicken broth | Add until mixture is very soupy |
| To taste | Salt and pepper |  |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Make cornbread using the recipe found in the bread section of the book. Bake ahead of time and allow to cool. |
| 2 | Preheat oven to 400°F. |
| 3 | Crumble cooled cornbread into a large bowl along with the toasted bread slices. |
| 4 | Trim and wash green onions and celery, then slice. |
| 5 | Sauté green onions and celery in margarine until tender. |
| 6 | Mix sautéed green onions and celery into the cornbread crumbs along with the chopped uncooked onion. |
| 7 | Pour chicken broth into a large saucepan and heat to a boil. |
| 8 | Gradually add boiling broth into the cornbread mixture. |
| 9 | When the mixture begins to moisten, beat in the eggs and season with salt and pepper. |
| 10 | Continue adding broth until the cornbread mixture is very soupy. If not enough broth is added, the dressing will be too dry. |
| 11 | Pour dressing into a large, lightly greased casserole dish. |
| 12 | Bake at 400°F for about 35-40 minutes, or until it begins to thicken and turns lightly brown on top. |
| 13 | Serve with Giblet Gravy, if desired. |
