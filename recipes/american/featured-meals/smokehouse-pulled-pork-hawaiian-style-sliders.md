# Smokehouse Pulled Pork Hawaiian-Style Sliders

> Oven-seared pork butt, pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus, sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch, and from-scratch Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls built for a food-truck-level pulled pork slider plate.

## Flavor Target

Smoky, savory, pull-apart pork with sweet barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar lift, soft pineapple-butter rolls, and sweet-spicy pickle brightness.

This should not taste like generic slow-cooker pork in bottled sauce. The difference comes from overnight seasoning, a hard sear, oven braising, reduced pork jus, and a broiled bark finish.

## Locked Method

| Element | Locked Direction |
|---|---|
| Primary cook | Sear the pork, then oven-braise low and slow. |
| Primary finish | Reduce the cooking juices, lightly glaze the shredded pork, then broil for barky edges. |
| Rolls | From-scratch Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls using active dry yeast and plain instant potato flakes. |
| Serving | Pulled pork sliders with pickle-onion crunch and optional cheese. |

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Overnight Smokehouse Pork | Pork butt is salted, slathered, rubbed, and chilled 8 to 18 hours so the seasoning penetrates beyond the surface. |
| Oven Braise | Searing creates crust, then pineapple juice, vinegar, broth, onion, garlic, mustard, brown sugar, and Worcestershire build a tangy BBQ jus. |
| BBQ Jus Glaze | Reduced pork cooking juices become the base for a glossy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze. |
| Broiled Bark Finish | Pulled pork is lightly sauced and broiled so it gets sticky, caramelized food-truck edges. |
| Hawaiian-Style Rolls | Pineapple juice, milk, eggs, butter, brown sugar, and dry potato flakes make plush sweet rolls sturdy enough for sliders. |
| Pickle-Onion Crunch | Sweet-spicy pickles, purple onion, mustard, honey, brine, pepper, and cayenne cut the richness. |

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

| Timing | Task |
|---|---|
| 8 to 18 hours before cooking | Salt the pork, apply wet slather, apply dry rub, cover, and refrigerate. |
| Cook day morning | Pull pork from the fridge 30 to 45 minutes before searing. |
| Early cook day | Start Hawaiian-style roll dough so rolls can rise while pork braises. |
| While pork braises | Make pickle-onion crunch and chill. |
| After pork braises | Strain and skim juices, build BBQ jus glaze, shred pork, glaze lightly, and broil for barky edges. |

## Service Countdown

| Time Before Serving | Task |
|---|---|
| Night before | Salt, slather, rub, and refrigerate the pork. |
| 7 to 8 hours before serving | Start roll dough, pull pork from fridge, preheat oven, and prepare braise base. |
| 6 to 7 hours before serving | Sear pork, build braise, cover, and oven-braise at 300°F. |
| 3 to 4 hours before serving | Shape rolls and let rise. |
| 1 hour before serving | Strain pork juices, skim fat, simmer BBQ jus glaze, pull pork, and heat rolls if needed. |
| 30 minutes before serving | Broil pulled pork for barky edges; slice and toast rolls. |
| At service | Build sliders with glaze, pork, pickle-onion crunch, optional cheese, and honey-buttered tops. |

## Do Not Forget

- Do not skip the overnight seasoning.
- Do not skip the sear.
- Do not stop the pork at 165°F; pulled pork tenderness usually lands around 195°F to 203°F.
- Do not skip reducing the cooking juices.
- Do not drown the pork in sauce; glaze lightly and keep it juicy, not wet.
- Do not skip the broiled bark finish.
- Do not over-flour the Hawaiian-style roll dough.
- Use plain instant potato flakes dry; do not make mashed potatoes first.
- Do not use fresh pineapple juice unless it is heated to 200°F and cooled first.
- Toast the roll cut sides before building sliders.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Season pork the night before.** Pat **4 to 5 lb pork butt or shoulder** very dry. Trim only large thick pieces of surface fat. Rub evenly with **2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt**. |
| 2 | **Apply wet slather.** Mix **2 Tbsp yellow mustard**, **1 Tbsp hot sauce**, **1 Tbsp Worcestershire**, **1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar or pickle brine**, **1 Tbsp neutral oil**, **1 Tbsp tomato paste**, and **2 Tbsp brown sugar**. Coat pork on all sides. |
| 3 | **Apply dry rub and refrigerate.** Mix **1 Tbsp smoked paprika**, **1 Tbsp paprika**, **1 Tbsp chili powder**, **2 tsp garlic powder**, **2 tsp onion powder**, **2 tsp mustard powder**, **1 1/2 tsp black pepper**, **1 tsp coriander**, **1 tsp cumin**, **1/2 tsp chipotle/cayenne**, optional **1/4 tsp ginger**, and optional tiny cinnamon. Coat pork heavily. Refrigerate **8 to 18 hours**. |
| 4 | **Temper pork.** Remove pork from the refrigerator **30 to 45 minutes** before cooking. Do not leave it out for hours. |
| 5 | **Start Hawaiian-style rolls.** In mixer bowl, combine **1/4 cup warm water**, **2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast**, and **1 Tbsp sugar**. Rest **5 to 10 minutes** until foamy. |
| 6 | **Mix dough.** Add **1/2 cup warm pineapple juice**, **1/2 cup warm milk**, **2 eggs**, **1 egg yolk**, **1/3 cup brown sugar**, **4 Tbsp softened or cooled melted butter**, **1 1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **2 Tbsp dry potato flakes**, **3 3/4 cups flour**, and optional **1 tsp vanilla**. Mix low until shaggy, then knead with dough hook **8 to 10 minutes** until soft, tacky, and elastic. |
| 7 | **First rise.** Place dough in greased bowl, cover, and let rise **1 1/2 to 2 hours**, until very puffy and nearly doubled. |
| 8 | **Sear pork.** Heat Dutch oven or heavy skillet over medium-high heat with **1 to 2 Tbsp neutral oil**. Sear pork on all sides until dark mahogany, not burned black sugar. |
| 9 | **Build braise.** Place **1 sliced large onion** and **6 smashed garlic cloves** in Dutch oven or roasting pan. Add **1/2 cup pineapple juice**, **1/2 cup broth/apple juice/water**, **2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar**, **1 Tbsp Worcestershire**, **1 Tbsp brown sugar**, **1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp prepared mustard**, and optional tiny liquid smoke. Place seared pork on top and cover tightly. |
| 10 | **Oven-braise.** Bake at **300°F** until fork-shreddable: about **4 1/2 to 6 hours** for large hunks or **6 to 7 hours** for whole bone-in pork butt. Start checking around **4 1/2 hours**. Done cues: probe slides in easily, pork pulls apart, and bone twists loose if bone-in. |
| 11 | **Shape rolls during braise.** Grease or line a **9x13 pan**. Divide dough into **12 equal pieces**, shape into tight smooth balls, place in pan, cover, and let rise **45 to 75 minutes** until puffy and touching or nearly touching. |
| 12 | **Bake rolls.** Brush risen rolls with egg wash made from **1 egg white or 1 whole egg** plus **1 Tbsp water or milk**. Bake at **350°F for 20 to 25 minutes**, until golden and about **190°F** in the center. Brush hot rolls with **2 Tbsp melted butter**, optional **1 tsp honey**, and a tiny pinch salt. Cool at least **20 minutes** before slicing. |
| 13 | **Rest pork and save juices.** Transfer pork to a tray and rest **20 to 30 minutes**. Strain cooking juices, skim excess fat, and reserve juices for the BBQ jus glaze. |
| 14 | **Make BBQ jus glaze.** In a saucepan, combine **1 cup reduced pork cooking juices**, **1/2 cup tomato sauce or ketchup**, **1/4 cup apple cider vinegar**, **2 Tbsp brown sugar**, **1 Tbsp honey or molasses**, **1 Tbsp Worcestershire**, **1 Tbsp mustard**, **1 tsp smoked paprika**, **1/2 tsp chili powder**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, optional cayenne, and optional **1 Tbsp bourbon/whiskey**. Simmer **10 to 15 minutes** until glossy and slightly thickened. |
| 15 | **Make pickle-onion crunch.** Mix **1/2 cup chopped sweet-spicy pickles**, **1/4 cup minced purple onion**, **1 Tbsp pickle brine**, **1 tsp mustard**, **1 tsp honey or brown sugar**, a pinch black pepper, and optional cayenne. Chill until serving. |
| 16 | **Pull pork.** Shred pork into large tender strands. Discard large pieces of fat but keep barky pieces. Add a few spoonfuls of reduced pork juices and a small amount of BBQ jus glaze. Toss gently so pork is juicy and glossy, not swimming. |
| 17 | **Broil for barky edges.** Spread sauced pulled pork on a sheet pan. Broil **3 to 6 minutes**, watching closely, until some edges crisp and caramelize. Toss once and broil **1 to 2 minutes** more if needed. |
| 18 | **Toast rolls.** Slice rolls and lightly toast cut sides with butter. |
| 19 | **Build sliders.** Add a thin spoonful of BBQ jus glaze to the bottom roll, then warm pulled pork, pickle-onion crunch, optional cheese, and top roll. Brush tops with honey butter. |
| 20 | **Warm tray sliders if serving together.** Warm covered at **325°F for 8 to 10 minutes**. Uncover **2 to 3 minutes** to set the tops. Serve while rolls are soft and pork is glossy. |

## BBQ Jus Adjustment Notes

| Issue | Correction |
|---|---|
| Too sweet | Add more apple cider vinegar. |
| Too sharp | Add brown sugar or honey. |
| Flat | Add salt or a splash more reduced pork jus. |
| Too gentle | Add cayenne or hot sauce. |
| Too thick | Add pork jus. |
| Too thin | Simmer longer. |

## Building the Final Slider

| Layer | Build Detail |
|---|---|
| 1. Toasted Roll | Split Hawaiian-style roll and toast the cut sides lightly with butter. |
| 2. BBQ Jus Base | Add a thin spoonful of pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus glaze to the bottom roll. |
| 3. Pork Anchor | Pile on warm pulled pork with broiled barky edges. |
| 4. Bright Crunch | Add a small spoonful of sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch. |
| 5. Optional Cheese | Add pepper jack for creamy heat, cheddar for classic BBQ, or provolone for mild melt. |
| 6. Honey-Butter Top | Add top roll, brush with honey butter, and warm as tray sliders if desired. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished slider should hit in layers: soft buttery pineapple roll, smoky savory pork, sticky barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze, sweet-spicy pickle crunch, warm BBQ spice, and a clean acidic finish.

It should be sweet, but not candy-sweet. It should be saucy, but not wet. It should feel like barbecue met homemade sweet-roll nostalgia and became a food-truck slider worth chasing again.
