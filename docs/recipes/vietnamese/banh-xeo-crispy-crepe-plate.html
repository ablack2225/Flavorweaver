Banh Xeo Crispy Crepe Plate

Crispy Vietnamese-inspired rice crepes filled with shrimp, 1/8-inch pork loin strips, scallion, and bean sprouts, served with mustard greens or lettuce wraps, mint, cilantro, Thai basil, English cucumber, Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and bright nước chấm.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Crispy Batter

Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour, cornstarch, turmeric, coconut milk, cold club soda, and scallion.

Shrimp + Pork Loin

Shrimp and 1/8-inch pork loin strips integrated directly into each crepe.

Bean Sprouts

Added to the hot crepe so they stay crisp and fresh.

Greens + Herbs

Mustard greens preferred, with mint, cilantro, and Thai basil for the wrap plate.

Nước Chấm

Sweet-sour-salty fish sauce dip; required.

Chef-Level Control Points

Batter Thinness

After resting, batter should pour like light cream. If thick, whisk in 1 Tbsp cold club soda at a time, up to 4 Tbsp.

Calibration Crepe

Treat the first crepe as the test crepe. Adjust oil, heat, and batter thinness before continuing.

Hot Oil

Oil should shimmer and the batter should hiss immediately. No sizzle means wait longer.

Edge-Fry

After the crepe sets, drizzle 1 tsp neutral oil around the outer edge to fry the lace crisp.

Steam Control

Move finished crepes to a wire rack, vented tray, or tilted plate. Never stack.

Cold Herbs

Greens, herbs, cucumber, and pickles should be washed, dried, chilled, and ready before the first crepe.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Crispy Banh Xeo Batter

1 cup Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp sugar, 1/2 cup coconut milk, 1 cup cold club soda or cold sparkling water, 2–4 Tbsp extra cold club soda if needed, 2 sliced scallions, and 1–2 Tbsp neutral oil per crepe plus 1 tsp edge oil as needed. Do not use glutinous, sweet, sticky, or mochiko rice flour.

Shrimp + Pork Loin Filling

8 oz shrimp, 8 oz pork loin cut into 1/8-inch thin strips/slices, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp fish sauce, 1/2 small thin-sliced onion, and 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts.

Greens, Herbs + Nước Chấm

Wrap plate: 8–10 Vietnamese mustard green or Asian mustard green leaves, green leaf lettuce backup, romaine hearts backup if needed, 1/2 cup mint, 1/2 cup cilantro, 1/2 cup Thai basil, optional perilla/shiso/tía tô, 1 English cucumber cut into sticks, 1/2–3/4 cup drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, lime wedges. Nước chấm: 3 Tbsp warm water, 2 Tbsp sugar, 2 Tbsp fish sauce, 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 garlic clove, and chile to taste.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Batter Dry

1 cup Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp sugar.

Bowl 2 — Batter Liquids

1/2 cup coconut milk, 1 cup cold club soda or cold sparkling water.

Bowl 3 — Batter Adjustment + Scallions

2–4 Tbsp extra cold club soda if needed, 2 thin-sliced scallions.

Bowl 4 — Pork + Shrimp

8 oz shrimp, 8 oz pork loin cut into 1/8-inch strips, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp fish sauce.

Bowl 5 — Crepe Fill

1 1/2 cups bean sprouts, 1/2 small thin-sliced onion.

Bowl 6 — Greens + Herb Plate

8–10 mustard green or lettuce leaves, 1/2 cup mint, 1/2 cup cilantro, 1/2 cup Thai basil, optional perilla/shiso, 1 English cucumber cut into sticks, 1/2–3/4 cup drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, lime wedges.

Bowl 7 — Nước Chấm

3 Tbsp warm water, 2 Tbsp sugar, 2 Tbsp fish sauce, 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 garlic clove, chile to taste.

Bowl 8 — Service Tools

Wire rack or vented tray, scissors or sharp knife, serving platter.

Do Not Forget

Use regular white rice flour / Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết, not glutinous or sweet rice flour.

Batter should be thin like light cream or thin heavy cream, not pancake batter.

Rest batter at least 30 minutes; longer is better.

Stir batter before every crepe because rice flour settles.

Use enough oil to lightly fry the base and lace the edges.

The first crepe is the calibration crepe; adjust before continuing.

Do not overload the crepe; too much filling makes it steam instead of crisp.

Serve immediately with mustard greens or lettuce, herbs, cucumber, Asian pickles, and nước chấm.

No peanut drizzle in the main build; nước chấm is the sauce identity.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Make batter. Whisk 1 cup Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour, 2 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp sugar. Whisk in 1/2 cup coconut milk and 1 cup cold club soda or cold sparkling water until smooth.
  2. Rest batter. Cover and rest at least 30 minutes, or refrigerate up to 24 hours. Rice flour settles heavily; this is expected.
  3. Make nước chấm. Stir 3 Tbsp warm water with 2 Tbsp sugar until dissolved. Add 2 Tbsp fish sauce, 2 Tbsp lime juice, 1 Tbsp rice vinegar, 1 minced garlic clove, and chile to taste. Taste and calibrate: sweet first, salty second, sour-bright finish, garlic/chile lift.
  4. Prep fresh plate. Arrange 8–10 mustard green or lettuce leaves, 1/2 cup mint, 1/2 cup cilantro, 1/2 cup Thai basil, optional perilla/shiso, 1 English cucumber cut into sticks, 1/2–3/4 cup drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and lime wedges. Keep cold and dry.
  5. Finish batter texture. Stir batter thoroughly from the bottom. It should pour like light cream and swirl quickly. If thick, whisk in 1 Tbsp extra cold club soda at a time, up to 4 Tbsp, until thin and fluid. Stir in 2 thin-sliced scallions after adjusting.
  6. Season filling. Toss 8 oz shrimp, 8 oz pork loin cut into 1/8-inch strips, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp fish sauce. Rest 10–15 minutes.
  7. Heat crepe pan. Heat skillet until hot. Add 1–2 Tbsp neutral oil and swirl. Oil should shimmer and batter should sizzle immediately.
  8. Start the calibration crepe. Add a small portion of 1/2 small thin-sliced onion and a few 1/8-inch pork loin strips to the hot oil. Cook 30–45 seconds, just until the pork starts losing its raw color.
  9. Add shrimp and batter. Add a few shrimp pieces, then immediately stir the batter and pour about 1/2 cup batter into the hot pan. Swirl quickly into a thin 9–10 inch crepe. The batter should hiss, bubble, and set almost immediately at the edges.
  10. Add sprouts. Scatter one-quarter of the 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts over one half of the crepe.
  11. Edge-fry and crisp. Cook uncovered 4–6 minutes, adjusting heat as needed. Once the crepe has set, drizzle 1 tsp neutral oil around the outer edge so the lace fries crisp. If the top refuses to set, cover only 30–60 seconds, then uncover and finish crisping.
  12. Evaluate the first crepe. If soft or steamy, increase oil slightly, thin the batter with another 1 Tbsp club soda, and/or raise heat slightly. If it browns before crisping, lower heat slightly. If it sticks, wait longer before moving and use a little more oil.
  13. Fold and vent. Fold empty half over filling. Slide onto a wire rack, vented tray, or tilted plate. Do not stack directly.
  14. Repeat. Repeat with remaining batter, pork, shrimp, onion, and sprouts for 4 crepes total, stirring batter before each one and adding fresh oil as needed.
  15. Serve immediately. Cut or tear hot crepe into crisp pieces. Tuck into mustard greens or lettuce with mint, cilantro, Thai basil, cucumber sticks, drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and nước chấm.

Electric Skillet Notes

Start around 325°F because bánh xèo needs immediate sizzle. Because this skillet runs hot, be ready to drop to 300°F if the batter browns before crisping. If the crepe steams and stays soft, raise toward 325°F, use a little more oil, and thin the batter if needed. Keep a small spoon of oil nearby for the 1 tsp edge-fry after the crepe sets.

Final Taste Target

The plate should be hot-cold, crisp-fresh, and sauce-bright: brittle golden crepe edges, tender shrimp and 1/8-inch pork loin, crunchy bean sprouts, cold mustard greens or lettuce, mint, cilantro, Thai basil, English cucumber, drained Asian pickles, and punchy nước chấm.
