# Turkish-Inspired Spiced Lamb Pide

> Homemade yogurt pide boats filled with garlic-paprika spiced lamb, mozzarella, feta, garlic-parsley butter, sumac onion salad, and cooling garlic yogurt.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Component | Role |
|---|---|

## Flavor Direction

This is not a fake sucuk pide. It is a Turkish-inspired spiced lamb pide built around homemade dough, lamb, garlic, paprika, cumin, sumac onion, yogurt, and a buttered crust. The target is savory, aromatic, cheesy, crisp-edged, fresh, and balanced.

## Do Not Forget

- Rest the lamb filling at least **1 hour**, ideally overnight if timing allows.
- Partially pre-cook the lamb filling before topping the dough so the pide does not get greasy or soggy.
- Keep feta as an accent; mozzarella is the main melt.
- Sumac onion salad and garlic yogurt are required for the 9.5+ version.
- Brush the crust with garlic-parsley butter immediately after baking.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Bloom yeast.** In Bowl 1, combine **1 cup warm water**, **2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast**, and **1 tsp sugar**. Rest **5–10 minutes** until foamy.
2. **Mix dough.** In Bowl 2, combine **2 cups bread flour**, **1 cup AP flour**, and **1 1/4 tsp kosher salt**. Add the foamy yeast mixture plus Bowl 3: **2 Tbsp olive oil** and **2 Tbsp yogurt**. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
3. **Knead dough.** Knead **8–10 minutes by hand** or **5–6 minutes in a mixer** until soft, smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky. Add only enough extra flour to prevent sticking.
4. **First rise.** Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and rise **60–90 minutes** until doubled.
5. **Season lamb.** Mix **1 lb ground lamb** with Bowl 4: **4 grated garlic cloves**, **1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste**, **2 tsp paprika**, **1 tsp cumin**, **1 tsp kosher salt**, **3/4 tsp black pepper**, **1/2 tsp red pepper flakes**, **1/4 tsp allspice**, **tiny pinch cinnamon**, **1 tsp lemon juice or vinegar**, and optional **1 Tbsp parsley**. Mix until slightly tacky. Rest refrigerated at least **1 hour**, or overnight if possible.
6. **Divide dough.** Punch down dough and divide into **4 equal balls**. Cover and rest **10–15 minutes**.
7. **Partially cook lamb.** Cook seasoned lamb in a skillet over medium heat until mostly cooked but still juicy, about **5–7 minutes**. Break into small crumbles. Drain excess fat if needed and cool slightly before topping.
8. **Make garlic yogurt.** Stir together Bowl 8: **1/2 cup yogurt**, **1 small grated garlic clove**, **1 tsp lemon juice**, **1 tsp olive oil**, and a pinch of salt. Chill until serving.
9. **Make sumac onion salad.** Toss Bowl 7: **1/2 thin-sliced onion**, **1 tsp sumac**, **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, **1 Tbsp parsley**, **1 tsp olive oil**, and a pinch of salt. Rest **15–20 minutes**.
10. **Preheat oven.** Heat oven to **475°F**. Preheat a sheet pan, pizza steel, or baking stone if using one.
11. **Shape pide.** Roll each dough ball into a long oval, about **10–12 inches long**. Add a light layer of mozzarella from the **1 1/2 cups total**, leaving a border.
12. **Fill pide.** Divide the cooked lamb filling across the 4 ovals. Sprinkle remaining mozzarella and **1/3 to 1/2 cup feta** lightly over the filling.
13. **Form boats.** Fold the long edges inward over the filling edge and pinch both ends tightly to create pide boats. Brush exposed crust lightly with olive oil if desired.
14. **Bake.** Bake at **475°F** for **10–14 minutes**, until crust is deeply golden, cheese is melted, and filling is sizzling. Rotate pan if needed.
15. **Butter crust.** Stir Bowl 6: **3 Tbsp melted butter**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1 Tbsp parsley**, and a pinch of salt if needed. Brush over hot crusts immediately after baking.
16. **Plate.** Top each pide with a small amount of sumac onion salad or serve it alongside. Drizzle or dollop with garlic yogurt. Finish with extra parsley and a light pinch of sumac if desired.

## Final Taste Target

The pide should have a soft-chewy interior, crisp golden edges, juicy spiced lamb, melty mozzarella, salty feta pops, buttery garlic crust, sharp sumac onion, and cooling yogurt. It should feel Turkish-inspired and chef-level, not like pizza with lamb.
