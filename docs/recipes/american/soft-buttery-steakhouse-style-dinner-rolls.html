Soft Buttery Steakhouse-Style Dinner Rolls

Soft, fluffy, lightly sweet enriched yeast rolls made with warm milk, butter, egg, honey, an all-purpose and bread flour blend, a double butter finish, and whipped honey butter for serving.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Soft Enriched Dough

Warm milk, yeast, sugar, honey, softened butter, egg, salt, all-purpose flour, and bread flour build a soft, plush dinner-roll dough.

Flour Blend

All-purpose flour keeps the crumb tender while bread flour adds enough structure for a tall, fluffy rise.

Double Butter Finish

The rolls are brushed with melted butter immediately after baking, rested briefly, and brushed again for a soft glossy top.

Whipped Honey Butter

Softened butter, honey, powdered sugar, cinnamon, and a tiny pinch of salt turn the warm rolls into a special bread service.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

Check yeast: active dry yeast should foam in warm milk with sugar and honey before the dough is mixed.

Bring egg and butter toward room temperature: room-temperature egg and softened butter blend into the dough more evenly.

Plan for rise time: the total time depends on room temperature. A cool kitchen may need longer than the written rise windows.

Honey butter: the whipped honey butter can be made ahead and held covered at room temperature for same-day service, or refrigerated and softened before serving.

Service Countdown

2 1/2 to 3 hours before serving: warm milk, proof yeast, mix dough, and begin the first rise.

1 1/2 hours before serving: shape rolls and begin the second rise.

25 minutes before serving: preheat oven to 350°F and make sure the rolls are puffy and nearly doubled.

15 minutes before serving: bake rolls until lightly golden.

At service: brush hot rolls with butter, rest 5 minutes, brush again, and serve warm with whipped honey butter.

Full Bread Service Ingredient Roll-Up

Quick-glance ingredient check for the full roll service. Detailed measurements and division points are handled in the measured ingredients, prep map, prep bowls, and full timeline below.

Roll Dough

Whole milk, active dry yeast, granulated sugar, honey, unsalted butter, egg, kosher salt, all-purpose flour, and bread flour.

Butter Finish

Melted unsalted butter plus an optional tiny pinch of fine salt for brushing the rolls after baking.

Whipped Honey Butter

Softened unsalted butter, honey, powdered sugar, cinnamon, and fine salt.

Texture Target

The dough should be soft, smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky. Do not add extra flour unless the dough truly needs it.

Measured Ingredients by Component

These component sections are reference only. The cooking flow is handled by the stage-based prep bowls and full timeline below.

Roll Dough

1 1/4 cups whole milk, warmed to 105–110°F; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1/4 cup granulated sugar; 2 Tbsp honey; 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened; 1 large egg, room temperature; 1 tsp kosher salt; 2 cups all-purpose flour; 1 1/2 cups bread flour; up to 1/2 cup additional all-purpose flour, only as needed.

Butter Finish

4 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted; optional tiny pinch fine salt.

Whipped Honey Butter

1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened; 3 Tbsp honey; 1 Tbsp powdered sugar; 1/4 tsp cinnamon; tiny pinch fine salt.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Butter: use 1/4 cup softened butter in the dough, 4 Tbsp melted butter for brushing, and 1/2 cup softened butter for the honey butter.

Honey: use 2 Tbsp honey in the dough and 3 Tbsp honey in the whipped serving butter.

Flour: use 2 cups all-purpose flour and 1 1/2 cups bread flour as the base. Add extra all-purpose flour only 1 Tbsp at a time if the dough is too sticky after kneading.

Temperature: milk should be warm but not hot, 105–110°F. Hot milk can damage the yeast.

Rise cues: use dough appearance more than the clock. First rise should double; second rise should look puffy and nearly doubled.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Dough Bowls

Bowl 1 — Yeast Milk

1 1/4 cups whole milk, warmed to 105–110°F; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1/4 cup granulated sugar; 2 Tbsp honey.

Bowl 2 — Enrichment

1/4 cup softened unsalted butter; 1 large room-temperature egg; 1 tsp kosher salt.

Bowl 3 — Base Flour

2 cups all-purpose flour; 1 cup bread flour. Add to start the dough.

Bowl 4 — Final Flour

1/2 cup bread flour; up to 1/2 cup additional all-purpose flour, held back and added only as needed.

Finish and Serving Bowls

Bowl 1 — Butter Brush

4 Tbsp melted unsalted butter; optional tiny pinch fine salt.

Bowl 2 — Honey Butter

1/2 cup softened unsalted butter; 3 Tbsp honey; 1 Tbsp powdered sugar; 1/4 tsp cinnamon; tiny pinch fine salt.

Do Not Forget

Keep the milk between 105°F and 110°F.

Do not over-flour the dough. Soft, tacky dough makes softer rolls.

Use rise cues, not just the clock.

Fold the dough once before cutting for a soft folded-roll feel.

Brush with butter twice: once immediately after baking and once after a short rest.

Serve warm with whipped honey butter.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Warm the milk. Warm 1 1/4 cups whole milk to 105–110°F.
  2. Proof the yeast. Stir 1/4 cup granulated sugar and 2 Tbsp honey into the warm milk. Sprinkle 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast over the top and let stand 5 to 10 minutes, until foamy.
  3. Start the dough. Add the foamy yeast mixture to a mixing bowl with 1/4 cup softened butter, 1 room-temperature egg, 1 tsp kosher salt, 2 cups all-purpose flour, and 1 cup bread flour. Mix until combined.
  4. Add final flour gradually. Add the remaining 1/2 cup bread flour. Knead with a dough hook 5 to 7 minutes, or by hand 8 to 10 minutes. Add extra all-purpose flour only 1 Tbsp at a time if the dough is still smearing badly after kneading.
  5. Check dough texture. The dough should be soft, smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky. It should not feel dry or stiff.
  6. First rise. Transfer dough to a lightly greased bowl. Turn once to coat. Cover and rise in a warm place 45 to 75 minutes, until doubled.
  7. Prepare the pan. Line a baking sheet with parchment or lightly grease it.
  8. Shape the rolls. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface. Roll to about 1/2 inch thick. Fold the dough over once like a book, gently roll again, then cut into 24 pieces.
  9. Second rise. Transfer rolls to the prepared baking sheet. Cover and rise 45 to 60 minutes, until puffy and nearly doubled.
  10. Preheat oven. About 20 to 25 minutes before the second rise is done, preheat oven to 350°F.
  11. Bake. Bake rolls at 350°F for 12 to 15 minutes, until lightly golden on top.
  12. Butter finish. Brush hot rolls immediately with melted butter. Rest 5 minutes, then brush again.
  13. Make honey butter. Beat 1/2 cup softened butter, 3 Tbsp honey, 1 Tbsp powdered sugar, 1/4 tsp cinnamon, and a tiny pinch of fine salt until fluffy.
  14. Serve. Serve rolls warm with whipped honey butter.

Flavor Target

The finished rolls should be plush, soft, lightly sweet, buttery on top, and tender enough to pull apart easily. The honey butter should be fluffy, spreadable, sweet-salty, and lightly cinnamon-warm without tasting like frosting.
