# Fresh Vegetable Spring Rolls

> Fresh rice paper spring rolls filled with crisp vegetables, cilantro, and cooled angel hair pasta, served with peanut dipping sauce and Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham.

## Measured Ingredients

### Spring Roll Components

These amounts make about 8-10 vegetable spring rolls. Use them as a starting point and adjust based on wrapper size and how full you like each roll.

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 8-10 | Rice paper wrappers | Keep extras nearby in case any tear |
| 2 cups | Shredded napa cabbage | Lightly packed |
| 1 cup | Julienned cucumber | Pat dry if very wet |
| 1 cup | Julienned carrot | Thin matchsticks roll easiest |
| 1/2 cup | Fresh cilantro leaves | Stems removed if preferred |
| 4 oz dry | Angel hair pasta | Cook, drain, rinse or cool, then portion into rolls |
| For serving | Peanut Dipping Sauce | Required dipping sauce |
| For serving | Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham | Bright dipping sauce |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Cook the angel hair pasta until tender. Drain, rinse or cool, and set aside. |
| 2 | Prep the vegetables: shred napa cabbage, julienne cucumber and carrot, and gather cilantro leaves. |
| 3 | Set up a rolling station with rice paper wrappers, a shallow dish of warm water, a damp towel or board, the vegetables, pasta, and serving sauces. |
| 4 | Soften one rice paper wrapper at a time according to package directions, just until pliable. |
| 5 | Layer a small amount of cabbage, cucumber, carrot, cilantro, and cooled angel hair pasta in the lower third of the wrapper. Do not overfill. |
| 6 | Fold the sides inward, then roll tightly from the bottom up to form a compact spring roll. |
| 7 | Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling. Keep finished rolls lightly covered so they do not dry out. |
| 8 | Serve with peanut dipping sauce and Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham. |

## Chef's Notes

Fresh vegetable spring rolls are flexible by nature. The amounts are written as a practical starting point, but the best roll is the one that feels balanced in your hand: crisp vegetables, a little noodle softness, fresh herbs, and enough sauce to make each bite bright.

Do not overfill the wrappers. Smaller, tighter rolls are easier to eat and less likely to split.
