# Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables

> Quick refrigerator pickles made with thinly sliced garden vegetables, a simple rice vinegar brine, and optional sesame oil and sesame seeds for a more restaurant-style finish.

## Measured Ingredients

### Vegetables

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Zucchini | Thinly sliced |
| 1 | Yellow squash | Thinly sliced |
| 1 | Carrot | Peeled into ribbons or julienned |
| 2 | Green onions | Sliced |

### Pickling Brine

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1/4 cup | Rice vinegar |  |
| 2 Tbsp | Sugar |  |
| 1 tsp | Kosher salt |  |
| 1 Tbsp | Water |  |
| 1/4 tsp | Red pepper flakes | Optional |

### Optional Finish

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 tsp | Sesame oil | Add before serving |
| 1 tsp | Sesame seeds | Add before serving |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Place vegetables into a jar or container. |
| 2 | Whisk together rice vinegar, sugar, kosher salt, water, and optional red pepper flakes. |
| 3 | Pour the brine over the vegetables. |
| 4 | Stir or shake to combine. |
| 5 | Refrigerate until ready to serve. |
| 6 | Before serving, drain lightly. Add sesame oil and sesame seeds if using. |

## Chef's Notes

Cucumbers can be used in place of the zucchini and yellow squash when you want a more classic cucumber-style quick pickle or when garden squash is not available.

The sesame oil and sesame seeds are optional, but those two little additions make the pickles feel much more restaurant-style.

Drain lightly before serving so the vegetables stay bright and crisp without flooding the plate or bowl.
