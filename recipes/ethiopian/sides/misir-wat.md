# Misir Wat with Berbere Butter

> Ethiopian-inspired red lentils cooked into a thick, glossy, deeply spiced stew with slow-reduced onion, garlic, ginger, tomato paste, House Berbere-Style Spice Blend, and a final berbere-butter gloss.

| Garlic and Ginger | Add sharp warmth and aromatic lift against the earthy lentils. |

## Flavor Target

Misir Wat should be thick, glossy, deeply spiced, gently hot, savory-sweet from slow onion, warm with garlic and ginger, and rich enough to stand beside Doro Wat without tasting like a quick lentil side.

The lentils should mostly break down into a cohesive stew, but the finished texture should not be watery or baby-food thin. It should mound softly on a spoon and hold its shape briefly before settling.

## Before Cooking / Advance Prep

- Rinse the red lentils until the water runs mostly clear.
- Dice the onion very finely so it melts into the stew.
- Mix or measure the House Berbere-Style Spice Blend before cooking.
- Garlic and ginger can be minced together shortly before cooking.
- Keep extra water or broth nearby; red lentils thicken quickly once they start breaking down.

## Cross-Component Bulk Prep Map

- **Lentils:** rinse **1 cup red lentils** very well and set aside.
- **Onion:** very finely dice **1 large yellow onion**.
- **Garlic and ginger:** mince **3 garlic cloves** and measure **1 Tbsp ginger**.
- **Primary spice bowl:** measure **2 Tbsp House Berbere-Style Spice Blend**.
- **Finish spice bowl:** measure **1 tsp House Berbere-Style Spice Blend** separately for the final bloom.
- **Liquid:** measure **2 3/4 cups water or broth** first, with **1/4 to 1/2 cup more** available if the lentils thicken before softening.

## Do Not Forget

- Use red lentils for this version.
- Dice the onion very finely so it melts into the stew.
- Cook the onion longer than a quick sauté; the sweetness and body matter.
- Bloom berbere and tomato paste in fat before adding the lentils and liquid.
- Stir more often near the end as the lentils thicken.
- Add salt after the lentils begin softening.
- Finish with a small berbere-butter or berbere-oil bloom for gloss and aroma.
- Use lemon only if the finished lentils taste heavy or flat.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Rinse the lentils.** Rinse **1 cup red lentils** very well until the water runs mostly clear. Drain and set aside. |
| 2 | **Cook the onion slowly.** Add **1 large very finely diced onion** to a pot over medium heat with a pinch of salt and a splash of water. Cook **6 to 8 minutes**, stirring often, until softened, reduced, and starting to smell sweet. |
| 3 | **Add fat.** Add **2 Tbsp butter, oil, or a mix**. Continue cooking **4 to 6 minutes**, until the onion looks glossy and lightly golden at the edges. |
| 4 | **Add garlic and ginger.** Stir in **3 minced garlic cloves** and **1 Tbsp ginger**. Cook **1 minute**, until fragrant. |
| 5 | **Bloom berbere and tomato.** Add **2 Tbsp House Berbere-Style Spice Blend** and **2 Tbsp tomato paste**. Cook **1 to 2 minutes**, stirring constantly, until the paste darkens slightly and the spice smells rounded. Do not let it burn. |
| 6 | **Add lentils and liquid.** Add the rinsed lentils and **2 3/4 cups water or broth**. Stir well, scraping the bottom of the pot. |
| 7 | **Simmer.** Bring to a gentle simmer. Cook uncovered **20 to 30 minutes**, stirring occasionally at first and more often as the lentils thicken. Add the remaining liquid as needed until the lentils are fully soft. |
| 8 | **Season.** Once the lentils begin softening, add **1 tsp kosher salt** and **1/4 tsp black pepper**. Continue simmering until thick, soft, and spoonable. |
| 9 | **Make the berbere butter finish.** In a small pan or cleared spot in the pot, warm **1 Tbsp butter or oil** with **1 tsp berbere-style spice blend** for **20 to 30 seconds**, just until fragrant. Stir through the lentils. |
| 10 | **Balance.** Taste. Add salt if flat, a splash of water or broth if too thick, or a small squeeze of lemon only if the stew tastes heavy. |
| 11 | **Serve.** Serve warm as the red-lentil component beside Doro Wat, Atakilt Wat, Timatim Salad, and injera or warm scoop bread. |

## Final Taste Target

Misir Wat should land thick, soft, glossy, deeply seasoned, savory, gently spicy, and aromatic. The onion should disappear into the body of the stew. The berbere should taste bloomed and rounded, not raw or dusty.

## Chef's Notes

This is the red-lentil direction for the Ethiopian-inspired platter. It is designed to sit beside Doro Wat as a rich, spoonable, berbere-forward legume component.

If the stew thickens while holding, loosen with a splash of hot water or broth and stir until glossy again.
