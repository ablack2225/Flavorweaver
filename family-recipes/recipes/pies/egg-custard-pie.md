# Egg Custard Pie

> A preserved family egg custard pie made with eggs, sugar, evaporated milk, whole milk, margarine, vanilla, and a 10-inch unbaked pie crust.

## Measured Ingredients

### Egg Custard Pie

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 cup | Sugar |  |
| 4 | Eggs | Beat well |
| 1 large can | Evaporated milk | Combine with whole milk to make 2 cups total |
| Enough to make 2 cups total | Whole milk | Combined with evaporated milk |
| 1/4 cup | Margarine | Melted, then heated with milk mixture |
| 1 1/2 tsp | Vanilla |  |
| 1 | 10-inch unbaked pie crust |  |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Preheat oven to 475°F. |
| 2 | Melt margarine in a small saucepan. |
| 3 | Mix evaporated milk and whole milk together to make 2 cups total. |
| 4 | Add milk mixture to the melted margarine. |
| 5 | Heat milk mixture to scalding, but do not boil. |
| 6 | Beat eggs well. |
| 7 | Gradually beat in sugar and continue beating until mixture is very creamy. |
| 8 | Stir in scalded milk mixture and vanilla. |
| 9 | Pour custard mixture into a 10-inch unbaked pie crust. |
| 10 | Bake at 475°F for 10 minutes. |
| 11 | Reduce oven temperature to 375°F and bake an additional 10 minutes, or until pie is set. |
| 12 | Slice and serve while still warm, if desired. |

## Notes

This pie carries the memory of Sundays after church at Mama Hatton's house — a cup of coffee, a slice of pie, and the kind of conversations that stayed with you long after the visit was over.

Original family note: believe it or not, this pie can actually be sliced and served while it is still warm.

Jimmie and Sue were both known in the family for their pies. This egg custard pie is preserved as part of that family pie tradition.
