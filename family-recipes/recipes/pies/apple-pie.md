# Apple Pie

> A preserved family apple pie recipe made with tart apples, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a homemade double-crust Perfect Pastry.

## Measured Ingredients

### Apple Filling

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 5 cups | Tart apples | Sliced |
| 1 Tbsp | Margarine | Added to warm apple filling |
| 1/2 cup | Sugar |  |
| 1/4 tsp | Nutmeg |  |
| 1/2 tsp | Cinnamon |  |
| 3 Tbsp | Flour |  |
| Very small amount | Water | To keep apples from sticking while warming |

### Perfect Pastry

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 2 cups | Flour |  |
| 3/4 cup | Crisco shortening |  |
| 1 tsp | Salt |  |
| 4-5 Tbsp | Ice water | Add gradually, 1 spoonful at a time |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Preheat oven to 425°F. |
| 2 | To prepare pastry, mix salt and flour together. |
| 3 | Cut in shortening thoroughly. |
| 4 | Gradually add ice water, 1 spoonful at a time, until dough is moistened and almost cleans the side of the bowl. |
| 5 | Divide dough in half. |
| 6 | Roll half of the dough to fit an 8-inch pie plate. |
| 7 | Place dough in the pie plate and set aside; do not trim the edges. |
| 8 | Roll remaining dough to make the top crust. |
| 9 | To make apple filling, peel, core, and thinly slice apples. |
| 10 | Place apples in a saucepan with a very little bit of water to keep them from sticking to the bottom of the pan. |
| 11 | Heat slowly on the stove, allowing apples to tenderize slightly and natural juices to begin forming. |
| 12 | Mix sugar, flour, nutmeg, and cinnamon together. |
| 13 | Gradually stir sugar mixture into apples and add margarine. |
| 14 | Stir until margarine melts. |
| 15 | Pour apple filling into prepared pie crust. |
| 16 | Cover with top crust that has slits cut in it. |
| 17 | Seal and flute the edges. |
| 18 | Bake in a preheated 425°F oven for about 40 minutes, or until crust is brown and juice begins to bubble through the slits in the crust. |

## Notes

Jimmie and Sue were both known in the family for their pies. This apple pie is preserved as part of that family pie tradition.

The pastry is included with the recipe as the original page lists it as Perfect Pastry alongside the apple filling.
