Onion Bhaji-Inspired Fritters with Quick Green Chutney

Crisp, onion-heavy Indian-inspired fritters made with an emergency all-purpose flour and cornstarch dry mix, served hot with a bright quick green chutney. The batter should be shaggy and sticky, never pourable.

Measured Ingredients

Onion Mixture

2 cups thinly sliced onion; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1 Tbsp ginger paste or fresh grated ginger; 1 Tbsp grated or minced garlic; optional 1 finely minced serrano or green chili; 1/4 cup chopped cilantro.

Dry Mix

1/2 cup all-purpose flour; 1/4 cup cornstarch; 1 tsp ground coriander; 3/4 tsp garam masala; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp chili powder or red pepper flakes; 1/8 tsp baking soda; optional 1/2 tsp crushed fennel seed.

Batter Adjustment and Frying

1 to 3 Tbsp water, only if needed, added 1 Tbsp at a time; neutral oil for frying at 350°F.

Quick Green Chutney

1 packed cup cilantro; optional 1/4 cup mint or parsley; 1 serrano or jalapeño; 1 small garlic clove; 1 tsp ginger; 1 Tbsp lemon or lime juice; 1/2 tsp cumin; optional 1/4 tsp coriander; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp sugar or honey; 2 to 4 Tbsp cold water.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Fritter Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Sliced Onions

2 cups thinly sliced onion; 1/2 tsp kosher salt. Mix and gently squeeze. Rest 15 to 20 minutes.

Bowl 2 — Aromatics

1 Tbsp ginger; 1 Tbsp garlic; optional minced serrano or green chili; 1/4 cup cilantro.

Bowl 3 — Dry Mix

1/2 cup AP flour; 1/4 cup cornstarch; coriander; garam masala; cumin; chili powder or flakes; baking soda; optional fennel.

Bowl 4 — Batter Adjustment

1 to 3 Tbsp water. Use only if mixture will not hold together in loose clumps.

Bowl 5 — Test Fritter

One small loose spoonful of batter. Always test before frying the whole batch.

Bowl 6 — Fry Station

Neutral oil at 350°F; rack or paper towels.

Quick Green Chutney Bowls

Bowl 1 — Green Base

1 packed cup cilantro; optional 1/4 cup mint or parsley.

Bowl 2 — Heat and Aromatics

1 serrano or jalapeño; 1 small garlic clove; 1 tsp ginger.

Bowl 3 — Seasoning

1 Tbsp lemon or lime juice; 1/2 tsp cumin; optional coriander; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp sugar or honey.

Bowl 4 — Blend Water

2 to 4 Tbsp cold water. Add 1 Tbsp at a time. Chutney should be pourable, not watery.

Do Not Forget

Salt and rest the onions first so their moisture helps form the batter.

The batter should be thick, sticky, shaggy, and onion-heavy.

Do not make a smooth or pourable batter.

Add water only 1 Tbsp at a time, and only if needed.

Drop loose clumps into the oil; do not roll tight balls.

Fry a test fritter before frying the full batch.

Optional double-fry gives extra crispness right before serving.

This is an emergency version made without besan or rice flour.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Slice and salt onions. Thinly slice 2 cups onion and separate the layers. Add 1/2 tsp kosher salt, mix, and gently squeeze. Rest 15 to 20 minutes.
  2. Add aromatics. Add 1 Tbsp ginger, 1 Tbsp garlic, optional minced serrano or green chili, and 1/4 cup chopped cilantro. Mix well.
  3. Add dry mix. Add 1/2 cup AP flour, 1/4 cup cornstarch, coriander, garam masala, cumin, chili powder or flakes, baking soda, and optional fennel. Mix until onions are coated.
  4. Adjust batter texture. Squeeze and mix well. If too dry to hold, add water 1 Tbsp at a time. Texture should be thick, sticky, shaggy, and onion-heavy.
  5. Make quick green chutney. Blend cilantro, mint or parsley, serrano or jalapeño, garlic, ginger, lemon or lime, cumin, optional coriander, salt, sugar or honey, and cold water. Add more water 1 Tbsp at a time only if needed.
  6. Heat oil. Heat neutral oil to 350°F. Use enough oil for fritters to float or mostly float.
  7. Fry a test fritter. Drop one small loose spoonful into hot oil. Do not roll tight. Fry 2 to 4 minutes, turning once, until golden and crisp. Taste and adjust salt or spice if needed.
  8. Fry the full batch. Drop small loose clumps into oil. Fry in batches for 3 to 5 minutes, turning as needed, until deep golden and crisp. Drain on a rack or paper towels.
  9. Optional double-fry. For extra crisp fritters, return them to hot oil for 1 minute right before serving.
  10. Serve hot. Serve with quick green chutney, lemon wedges, and any Indian-inspired main dish or bread.

Test Fry Troubleshooting

Batter Will Not Hold

Add water 1 Tbsp at a time, or let onions rest a few more minutes.

Batter Too Wet

Add a small spoonful of flour or cornstarch and mix again.

Fritter Tastes Flat

Add a pinch more salt, garam masala, cumin, or chili.

Browns Too Fast

Lower oil temperature slightly and keep closer to 350°F.

Greasy

Oil may be too cool or batch may be crowded.

Serving Board

Hot Fritters

Serve immediately while crisp.

Quick Green Chutney

Bright, green, spicy-fresh dipping sauce.

Lemon Wedges

Indian Mains

Serve with Malai Kofta, Palak Paneer, Saag Paneer, or curry plates.

Bread

Serve with naan if making a larger plate.

Final Taste Target

The fritters should be deeply golden, crisp-edged, onion-forward, warmly spiced, and savory with enough structure to hold together without becoming bready.

The chutney should be bright, green, pourable, spicy-fresh, lightly tangy, balanced with salt, and not watery.
