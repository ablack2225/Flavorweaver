# Yeast & Leavening

## Always Kept

- Active dry yeast
- Baking powder
- Baking soda
- Cream of tartar

## Yeast Standard

Active dry yeast is Amy’s default yeast.

Bread recipes should be written for active dry yeast unless Amy says otherwise.

Preferred method:

1. Bloom active dry yeast in warm water with sugar.
2. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.
3. Continue with the dough recipe.

## Leavening Planning Note

Do not default to instant yeast.

If a recipe uses instant yeast, include an active dry yeast conversion or activation step.

# Nuts & Seeds

## Always Kept

- Sesame seeds
- Poppy seeds
- Cashews

- Almonds
- Pecans
- Walnuts

## Peanut Note

Peanut butter is kept and used.

Whole peanuts are not preferred because Amy and Don do not like the flavor or texture of whole peanuts.

Do not default to whole peanuts as a garnish or ingredient.

Use peanut butter instead when peanut flavor is desired.

## Nut & Seed Planning Note

Useful applications:

- Sesame seeds for Asian bowls, Korean dishes, rice bowls, sauces, and garnishes.
- Poppy seeds for baking and breads.
- Cashews for Asian-inspired bowls, stir-fries, curries, and texture.
- Almonds for baking, sauces, crusts, and lower-carb cooking.
- Pecans and walnuts for baking, salads, breakfast dishes, and sweet-savory applications.
