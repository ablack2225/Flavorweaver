Ethiopian-Inspired Doro Wat Platter

A practical complete meal plate with spiced chicken and eggs, yellow split pea alicha, atakilt wat, timatim salad, and naan as a scoop-bread substitute until real injera is ready.

Prep1h + overnight marinade
Cook1h 30m
TotalOvernight + 2h 30m
Serves2 generous plates

Instructions

  1. Mix the house spice blend and store airtight.
  2. Coat the chicken with spice blend, oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper. Cover and marinate overnight.
  3. Prepare and peel the hard-boiled eggs. Lightly score each egg so sauce can cling.
  4. Cook the diced onion until softened, reduced, and beginning to turn golden.
  5. Add butter or oil, bloom the spice blend briefly, then add garlic, ginger, and tomato paste.
  6. Add marinated chicken and sear lightly on both sides.
  7. Add broth and simmer covered on low until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  8. Add eggs during the last part of cooking and spoon sauce over them.
  9. Simmer uncovered until the sauce thickens and clings. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  10. Prepare the linked alicha, atakilt wat, timatim salad, and naan or injera.
  11. Assemble each plate with scoop bread, chicken, 2 eggs per person, split pea alicha, atakilt wat, timatim salad, and extra sauce.

Chef's Notes

This recipe is intentionally Ethiopian-inspired. The first working version uses practical substitutions for ingredients that can be expensive, delayed, or hard to source in the U.S.

Yellow split pea alicha is the repeatable working side because it is accessible, creamy, mild, and a good counterpoint to the spicy chicken. Red lentil Misir Wat remains the more classic direction and should be linked once that component is created.

Naan is the practical scoop-bread substitute for this first version. Use real injera when building the traditional-style Ethiopian version.
