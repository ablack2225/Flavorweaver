# Peanut Dipping Sauce

> A creamy peanut dipping sauce built with ginger nuoc cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime.

## Recipe Dashboard

| Status | Total Time | Yield | Difficulty |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Working Recipe | 10 min | About 1 cup | Easy |

## Ingredient List

| Component | Ingredients |
|---|---|
| Sauce Base | Peanut butter, [Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham](ginger-nuoc-cham.md), hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, lime, warm water |
| Optional Additions | Sesame oil or fresh chile |

## Measured Ingredients

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1/2 cup | Creamy peanut butter |  |
| 2 Tbsp | [Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham](ginger-nuoc-cham.md) | Required sauce component |
| 1 Tbsp | Hoisin sauce |  |
| 1 Tbsp | Soy sauce |  |
| 1 clove | Garlic | Minced |
| 1 tsp | Grated ginger or ginger paste |  |
| 1/2 | Lime | Juiced |
| 2-4 Tbsp | Warm water | Use to thin |
| Optional 1/2 tsp | Sesame oil | For deeper richness |
| Optional | Fresh chile | Minced, for heat |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Whisk peanut butter, ginger nuoc cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime juice until combined. |
| 2 | Add warm water 1 tablespoon at a time until smooth and dippable. |
| 3 | Add optional sesame oil or fresh chile if desired. |
| 4 | Taste and adjust lime, water, heat, or saltiness as needed. |

<!-- recipe-card:end -->

## Chef's Notes

This sauce is meant to be flexible. Use less water for a thicker dipping sauce or more water for a drizzleable bowl sauce.
