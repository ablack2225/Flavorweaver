# Creole Chicken Gumbo

> A preserved family Creole chicken gumbo made with chicken broth, chopped chicken, shrimp, okra, tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, celery, and Creole-style seasonings, served over hot cooked rice.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Cut chicken into pieces and place in a large pot. |
| 2 | Cover chicken with water and season with salt. |
| 3 | Bring to a boil, then simmer about 1 1/2-2 hours, until tender. |
| 4 | Remove chicken from broth. Remove skin, debone chicken, and chop into small pieces. |
| 5 | Sauté onion, bell pepper, and celery in cooking oil for about 5 minutes, until tender. |
| 6 | Add flour to the sautéed vegetables and mix. |
| 7 | Add vegetable mixture to the chicken broth used to cook the chicken, using about 3 cups broth. |
| 8 | Mix in seasonings and okra. |
| 9 | Cover and simmer about 30 minutes. |
| 10 | Add chopped chicken and rinsed, drained shrimp. |
| 11 | For a richer juice, add a small can of tomato sauce before cooking the gumbo mixture. |
| 12 | Serve over hot cooked rice. |

## Notes

Original family note: this serves several people very generously.

Original leftover note: any leftover rice can be mixed into the gumbo and refrigerated. It is really good after it all marinates together.

The recipe may be made a little richer by adding a small can of tomato sauce before cooking the gumbo mixture.

Servings are preserved from the recipe language. Prep time and total time are estimated. Chicken simmer time and gumbo simmer time are from the recipe text.
