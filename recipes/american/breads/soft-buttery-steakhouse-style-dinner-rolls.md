# Soft Buttery Steakhouse-Style Dinner Rolls

Status: Flavorweaver Test Recipe  
Culture: American Inspired  
Category: Bread  
Yield: 24 rolls  
Total Time: 2 hr 30 min, depending on room temperature  

---

## Recipe Intent

This is the Amy & Don house version of soft, fluffy, slightly sweet steakhouse-style yeast rolls served warm with whipped honey butter.

The source inspiration was a printed copycat-style roll formula Amy liked, but the Flavorweaver version removes outside naming, improves the flour handling, adds honey for warmth and softness, uses an all-purpose / bread flour blend, and includes a dedicated whipped honey butter for serving.

## Flavor Target

Soft, plush, lightly sweet dinner rolls with a tender enriched crumb, golden butter-brushed tops, and a warm yeast-roll aroma. They should pull apart softly, not crumble, and should taste good plain but excellent with honey butter.

## Key House Decisions

- No dry milk powder required.
- Use mostly all-purpose flour for tenderness with some bread flour for lift and structure.
- Do not force in all the flour.
- Dough should be soft, smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
- Brush with butter immediately after baking and again after a short rest.
- Serve warm with whipped honey butter.

---

## Ingredients

### Roll Dough

- 1 1/4 cups whole milk, warmed to 105–110°F
- 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 2 Tbsp honey
- 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
- 1 large egg, room temperature
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 1/2 cups bread flour
- Up to 1/2 cup additional all-purpose flour, only as needed

### Butter Finish

- 4 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted
- Tiny pinch fine salt, optional

### Whipped Honey Butter

- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
- 3 Tbsp honey
- 1 Tbsp powdered sugar
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon
- Tiny pinch fine salt

---

## Process Notes

### Dough Texture

The dough should be tacky but not wet. It should pull from the bowl in a soft mass and feel elastic. If it is still smearing badly after kneading, add flour 1 Tbsp at a time.

### Flour Handling

Start with the base 3 1/2 cups total flour. Add more only if the dough truly needs it. Too much flour will make the rolls bready instead of soft.

### Shaping

For the classic folded-roll feel, roll the dough to about 1/2 inch thick, fold it over once like a book, gently roll again, then cut into 24 pieces.

---

## Instructions

1. Warm milk to 105–110°F.
2. Stir in sugar and honey. Sprinkle yeast over the milk and let stand 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.
3. Add yeast mixture to a mixing bowl with softened butter, egg, salt, 2 cups all-purpose flour, and 1 cup bread flour. Mix until combined.
4. Add remaining 1/2 cup bread flour gradually. Knead 5 to 7 minutes with a dough hook, or 8 to 10 minutes by hand, adding only enough extra all-purpose flour to make a soft, slightly tacky dough.
5. Transfer dough to a lightly greased bowl. Cover and rise 45 to 75 minutes, until doubled.
6. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface. Roll to about 1/2 inch thick.
7. Fold dough over once like a book, gently roll again, and cut into 24 pieces.
8. Transfer rolls to a greased or parchment-lined baking sheet. Cover and rise 45 to 60 minutes, until puffy and nearly doubled.
9. Bake at 350°F for 12 to 15 minutes, until lightly golden.
10. Brush immediately with melted butter. Rest 5 minutes, then brush again.
11. Beat softened butter, honey, powdered sugar, cinnamon, and salt until fluffy. Serve rolls warm with honey butter.

---

## Do Not Forget

- Keep milk under 110°F so the yeast is not damaged.
- Room-temperature egg and softened butter blend more evenly.
- Do not over-flour the dough.
- Let the second rise go until the rolls look puffy, not just until the clock says they are ready.
- Brush with butter twice.
- Serve warm with whipped honey butter.
