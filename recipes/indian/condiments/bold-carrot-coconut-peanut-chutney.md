# Bold Carrot Coconut Peanut Chutney

> A bold, spicy-sweet, nutty carrot-coconut chutney built to give aloo pakora a real purpose on a creamy Indian-inspired plate. Carrot and coconut are the identity; peanut, cashew, sesame, lime, cumin, chili, and ginger-garlic give it the punch.

## Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

- This is a live-test pantry version built for the aloo pakora plate.
- The chutney should be bold, spicy, tangy, and purposeful because the korma is intentionally creamy, nutty, and less spicy.
- Sweetened coconut is being used because that is what is currently available; balance it with salt, chili, and extra lime/lemon.
- Future traditional versions can be explored after stocking tamarind, amchur, kala namak, curry leaves, mustard seed, chana dal, urad dal, and/or fresh coconut.

## Do Not Forget

- Toast the coconut, cashews, and sesame gently; sweetened coconut burns fast.
- Start with only **2 Tbsp water** so the chutney does not get thin.
- This chutney should be bold, not polite.
- If it tastes sweet, add lime/lemon and salt.
- If it tastes flat, add cumin, chili, salt, or the optional tiny soy splash.
- If it tastes too heavy, add lime/lemon and a spoon of water.
- Texture should be thick enough to cling to pakora but loose enough to dip.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Toast the base.** In a dry skillet over medium-low heat, add **2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes**, **1 Tbsp cashews**, and **1 tsp sesame seeds**. Stir constantly until fragrant and lightly golden. Do not walk away. |
| 2 | **Build the blender bowl.** Add the toasted base to a blender or Ninja cup with **1/2 cup shredded carrot**, **1 Tbsp peanut butter**, **1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste**, **1/4 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed**, **1/8 tsp garam masala**, **1/8 tsp cayenne or chili powder**, **1/4 tsp kosher salt**, **2 1/2 to 3 tsp lime/lemon juice**, and **2 Tbsp water**. |
| 3 | **Blend.** Blend until mostly smooth. Stop and scrape as needed. Add water **1 tsp to 1 Tbsp at a time** until thick and dippable. |
| 4 | **Taste hard.** It should be sweet-savory, nutty, tangy, and spicy. Adjust with lime/lemon, salt, chili, cumin, or water. |
| 5 | **Optional boldness boost.** If it tastes too polite or not savory enough, blend in **1/4 tsp soy sauce**. |
| 6 | **Serve.** Serve in a small bowl beside hot aloo pakora. |

## Taste Adjustment Map

| If it tastes... | Fix |
|---|---|
| Too sweet | Add more lime/lemon and a tiny pinch salt. |
| Too flat | Add more cumin, chili, acid, or optional soy sauce. |
| Too thick | Add water 1 tsp at a time. |
| Too thin | Add a few cashews or 1/2 tsp peanut butter. |
| Too peanut-heavy | Add more carrot and lime/lemon. |
| Not spicy enough | Add cayenne or chili powder. |
| Too sharp | Add a little more coconut, cashew, or peanut butter. |

## Final Taste Target

The finished chutney should hit sweet first, nutty second, then tangy-spicy-savory right behind it. It should stand out against creamy korma and palak paneer and make crispy aloo pakora feel intentional.

## Chef's Notes

This chutney is intentionally not a mild side. The korma is less spicy and more creamy/nutty, so this sauce needs to create contrast: heat, acid, salt, sweetness, and bold nutty body.

Peanut butter is included because Don loves bold peanut-style sauces, especially the Vietnamese peanut sauce already used in Flavorweaver. The goal is not to turn this into Vietnamese peanut sauce, but to borrow that bold body while keeping carrot and coconut as the identity.

Sweetened coconut requires more acid and salt than unsweetened coconut. Taste after blending before deciding whether to add soy sauce.
