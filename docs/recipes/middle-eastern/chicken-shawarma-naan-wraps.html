Chicken Shawarma Naan Wraps with Garlic Sauce, Lemon Tahini & Crispy Potatoes

Deeply spiced chicken thighs cooked hot, sliced thin, and re-crisped into shawarma-style edges, wrapped in warm homemade naan with bold garlic sauce, lemon tahini, shawarma-dusted crispy potatoes, pickled acid, tomato, cucumber, onion, flat-leaf parsley, and optional fresh mint.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Shawarma-Spiced Chicken Thighs

Chicken thighs are marinated with garlic, lemon, yogurt or sour cream, oil, warm spices, plain paprika, and an optional tiny liquid-smoke note, then cooked hot, sliced thin, and re-crisped for browned shawarma-style edges.

Warm Naan Wrap Base

Fluffy Skillet Naan is the preferred house wrap base. It should be warm, flexible, and lightly griddled before filling.

Bold Garlic Sauce

A toum-style garlic sauce anchors the wrap with creamy, bright, pungent garlic flavor.

Lemon Tahini Stripe

Lemon tahini adds nutty depth as a controlled finishing stripe or side dip.

Shawarma-Dusted Crispy Potatoes

Crisp potatoes add the street-wrap crunch and starch satisfaction, finished with paprika, garlic, cumin, pepper, and a little lemon.

Pickled and Fresh Finish

Pickles or pickled onions, tomato, cucumber, onion, flat-leaf parsley, optional fresh mint, and lemon bring acid, crunch, and freshness.

Flavor Target

The finished wrap should taste juicy, deeply spiced, garlicky, lemon-bright, lightly smoky, tangy from pickles, creamy from garlic sauce, nutty from a thin tahini stripe, and fresh from tomato, cucumber, onion, flat-leaf parsley, and optional mint.

The chicken should have browned edges and a sliced shawarma texture. The naan should stay warm and flexible. The potatoes should stay crisp enough to give a clear bite inside the wrap. The sauces should be bold without making the wrap soggy.

Measured Ingredients

Shawarma Chicken Marinade

1 1/2 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs; 3 Tbsp oil; 1 Tbsp lemon juice; 1 tsp lemon zest; 5 grated garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp Greek yogurt or sour cream; optional 1/8 tsp liquid smoke; 1 1/4 tsp kosher salt; 2 1/2 tsp cumin; 1 1/2 tsp coriander; 1 1/2 tsp plain paprika; 3/4 tsp turmeric; 3/4 tsp allspice; 1/2 tsp cardamom; 1/4 tsp cinnamon; 1/2 tsp ginger; tiny pinch nutmeg; 3/4 tsp black pepper; 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne; 1 1/2 tsp honey or brown sugar.

Toum-Style Garlic Sauce

1/2 cup mayonnaise; 1/4 cup Greek yogurt or sour cream; 3 grated garlic cloves; 1 Tbsp lemon juice; 2 tsp olive oil; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; tiny pinch sugar or honey; 1 to 2 tsp cold water only if needed.

Lemon Tahini Sauce

1/3 cup tahini; 2 Tbsp lemon juice; 1 small grated garlic clove; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp cumin; 4 to 6 Tbsp ice-cold water; tiny drizzle honey only if bitter.

Shawarma-Dusted Crispy Potatoes

2 to 3 medium potatoes, cut into fries or small wedges, or frozen fries; 1 to 2 Tbsp neutral oil; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp plain paprika; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/4 tsp black pepper; optional tiny pinch cayenne; tiny pinch lemon zest or tiny splash lemon juice.

Fresh Wrap Fill and Assembly

4 warm naan, preferably Fluffy Skillet Naan; 1 large tomato; 1/2 to 1 cucumber; 1/4 to 1/2 small onion; 1/2 to 3/4 cup pickles or pickled onions; small handful flat-leaf parsley, chopped; optional 1 to 2 tsp fresh mint, finely chopped; optional lemon wedges.

Do Not Forget

Marinate the chicken 12 to 18 hours before cooking.

Use liquid smoke sparingly if using it; the target is a quiet smoky note, not barbecue flavor.

Use chicken thighs for juiciness and browned shawarma-style edges.

Cook chicken pieces whole, then rest, slice thin, and re-crisp.

Garlic sauce is the primary sauce. Lemon tahini is a stripe or side dip.

Drain pickles or pickled onions very well.

Potatoes should be crisp before they go into the wrap.

Warm naan before filling. Cold naan will make the wrap feel flat.

Use flat-leaf parsley as the main fresh herb. Use fresh mint only as a small optional cooling note.

Griddle seam-side down after wrapping if the naan needs help setting.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Mix the dry shawarma spice blend. Combine the measured salt, cumin, coriander, plain paprika, turmeric, allspice, cardamom, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, black pepper, and cayenne.
  2. Make the chicken marinade. Whisk oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, yogurt or sour cream, optional liquid smoke, honey or brown sugar, and the full spice blend.
  3. Marinate the chicken. Add chicken thighs, turn until coated, keep pieces whole, cover, and refrigerate 12 to 18 hours.
  4. Make the garlic sauce. Stir mayonnaise, yogurt or sour cream, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and tiny sweetener. Thin only if needed.
  5. Make the lemon tahini. Whisk tahini, lemon, garlic, salt, and cumin. Add ice-cold water gradually until pourable.
  6. Prepare the naan. Make or warm 4 naan. Keep covered and warm.
  7. Prepare the potatoes. Cook potatoes or fries until crisp. While hot, season with salt, plain paprika, garlic powder, cumin, black pepper, optional cayenne, and a little lemon.
  8. Prep the fresh and pickled fill. Slice tomato, cucumber, and onion. Chop flat-leaf parsley and optional fresh mint. Drain pickles or pickled onions very well.
  9. Cook the chicken hot. Remove chicken from the refrigerator 20 to 30 minutes before cooking. Cook in a hot skillet, griddle, broiler, grill pan, or electric skillet until browned and cooked through.
  10. Rest and slice the chicken. Rest 5 to 10 minutes. Slice very thin across the grain into shawarma-style strips. Save the juices.
  11. Re-crisp the sliced chicken. Return sliced chicken and saved juices to a hot skillet for 1 to 2 minutes, just until edges pick up color.
  12. Build each wrap. Spread garlic sauce on warm naan, add hot sliced chicken, crispy potatoes, drained pickles or pickled onions, fresh vegetables, flat-leaf parsley, optional mint, a little more garlic sauce, and a thin lemon tahini stripe.
  13. Set the wrap and serve. Fold or roll the naan. Optional: griddle seam-side down for 30 to 60 seconds. Serve immediately with extra garlic sauce, lemon tahini, pickles, and lemon wedges.

Chef's Notes

Plain paprika works well here. If using liquid smoke for a smoky note, add it to the chicken marinade in a tiny amount only. Do not add liquid smoke to the potatoes or sauces.

Garlic sauce is the main chicken-shawarma sauce. Lemon tahini adds nutty depth as a thin finishing stripe or side dip.

This wrap is built from required layers: hot sliced shawarma chicken, warm naan, garlic sauce, pickled acid, crisp potato texture, fresh vegetables, flat-leaf parsley, and controlled tahini. Fresh mint may be added lightly for cool brightness.
