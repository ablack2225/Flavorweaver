Kitchen Execution Summary

Falafel

The falafel recipe itself needs no changes from the successful test. Dried chickpeas are soaked overnight, drained, dried, ground with herbs and aromatics, seasoned, chilled, shaped, and deep-fried until crisp outside and fluffy inside.

White Sauce

The winning white sauce is a thick tzatziki-ish ranch sauce: creamy, garlicky, herby, lemony, and cool against the hot falafel and fiery red sauce.

Red Sauce

The red sauce is spicy, tangy, vinegary, garlicky, red-chile-forward, and hot enough to feel aggressive by itself. Vinegar-style hot sauce and chipotle powder carry the tested heat direction.

Tahini Sauce

Lemon tahini remains optional. It is classic with falafel, but it is not the memory-critical sauce for Don's wrap.

Wrap Build

Warm naan replaces pita. The tested version used tomato and onion, with no required lettuce.

Before Cooking / Advance Prep

Soak chickpeas overnight in the refrigerator. Drain chickpeas 30 to 60 minutes before grinding, then rinse and dry very well. Make sauces before frying falafel. Chill falafel mixture before shaping. Fry one test falafel before frying the whole batch. Warm naan right before serving and serve falafel hot from the fryer.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Herb Falafel

Chickpea soak: 1 lb dried chickpeas, cold water to cover by 3 to 4 inches, optional 1/2 tsp baking soda.

Mixture: soaked drained dried chickpeas, 1 small onion, 4 scallions, 5 garlic cloves, 1 small carrot, 1 packed cup parsley, 1 packed cup cilantro, optional 2 Tbsp mint, 2 tsp cumin, 1 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/4 tsp cardamom, 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 2 tsp kosher salt, 1 Tbsp baking powder, 2 to 3 Tbsp flour, and 1 to 3 Tbsp cold water only if needed.

Tzatziki-ish Ranch White Sauce

1/2 cup mayonnaise, 1/2 cup sour cream or plain Greek yogurt, 1 Tbsp lemon juice, 1 grated garlic clove, 1 tsp dried dill or 1 Tbsp fresh dill, 1/2 tsp onion powder, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp black pepper, milk or water only if needed, optional squeezed-dry minced cucumber, parsley, or cumin.

Fiery Red Sauce

4 dried guajillo chiles, 3 garlic cloves, 2 Tbsp tomato paste, 2 Tbsp white vinegar, 1 Tbsp lemon juice, 2 Tbsp water, 1 Tbsp vinegar-style hot sauce, 1 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/2 tsp chipotle powder, 1 to 2 tsp crushed red pepper, 1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp sugar, and 1 Tbsp olive oil or neutral oil.

Lemon Tahini Sauce

1/2 cup tahini, 3 Tbsp lemon juice, 1 grated garlic clove, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/4 tsp cumin, 1/4 cup ice-cold water plus more as needed, honey and olive oil only if needed to round bitterness.

Wrap Build

Do Not Forget

Do not cook the chickpeas. Do not use canned chickpeas. Dry chickpeas well before grinding. Grind twice for the tested texture. Chill falafel mixture before shaping. Test-fry one falafel before frying all. Serve falafel hot. Warm naan right before building. Red sauce should be spicy enough to use carefully. Ranch white sauce and fiery red sauce are the memory-critical sauces. Tahini is optional. Lettuce is optional only, not required.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Soak chickpeas. Place 1 lb dried chickpeas in a large bowl. Cover with cold water by 3 to 4 inches. Add baking soda if using. Refrigerate 18 to 24 hours. Do not cook the chickpeas.
  2. Drain and dry. Drain, rinse, shake off excess water, spread on a towel or baking sheet, pat dry, and rest uncovered 30 to 60 minutes.
  3. Grind falafel mixture. Run chickpeas, onion, scallions, garlic, carrot, parsley, cilantro, and mint through the meat grinder. Grind twice for the tested texture.
  4. Season falafel. Mix in cumin, coriander, turmeric, cardamom, cayenne, black pepper, kosher salt, baking powder, and flour. Add cold water only if the mixture crumbles badly.
  5. Chill mixture. Cover and refrigerate 45 to 60 minutes.
  6. Make white sauce. Mix mayonnaise and sour cream or Greek yogurt. Add lemon, garlic, dill, powders, salt, pepper, and optional cucumber or herbs. Chill if possible.
  7. Make fiery red sauce. Soak guajillo chiles 10 to 15 minutes. Blend with garlic, tomato paste, vinegar, lemon, water, hot sauce, spices, chipotle, crushed red pepper, cayenne, salt, sugar, and oil. Adjust carefully. Simmer briefly for a smoother deeper sauce if desired.
  8. Make lemon tahini. Whisk tahini, lemon, garlic, salt, and cumin. Slowly whisk in ice-cold water until creamy and pourable. Round only if needed.
  9. Shape falafel. Shape small balls or slightly flattened patties, about 1 heaping tablespoon each. Keep chilled until frying.
  10. Test fry. Fry one falafel at 350°F. If it holds and tastes good, continue. Adjust only if needed.
  11. Fry full batch. Fry in small batches at 350°F for 3 to 4 minutes, until deeply golden and crisp. Drain and salt lightly while hot.
  12. Build wraps. Warm naan until soft and flexible. Build immediately while falafel are hot.

Final Naan Wrap Build

Don's Spot-On Memory Wrap

Warm naan, spread ranch white sauce, add tomato, onion, hot falafel, stripe fiery red sauce, add extra ranch white sauce, fold, and serve immediately.

Classic Falafel Wrap

Warm naan, 2 to 3 falafel, optional tahini, light ranch white sauce, light red sauce, small amount of tomato/onion if tolerated, and pickles if they sound good.

Tested Assembly Notes

The successful test used warm homemade naan, hot deep-fried falafel, tomato, onion, tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce, fiery red sauce, and optional tahini.

Lettuce was available but was not used. Do not list lettuce as required for the tested version.

Final Taste Target

The final wrap should have hot crispy falafel, soft warm naan, cool creamy white sauce, fiery red sauce, juicy tomato, sharp onion, and optional nutty lemon tahini.

The red sauce should bring the heat. The ranch white sauce should cool the bite. The tahini should add optional classic falafel depth. The falafel should stay crisp long enough to contrast with the sauces and fresh vegetables.

Chef's Notes

Preserve the falafel recipe as written. The tested falafel texture worked, the meat grinder method worked, and warm naan worked well as the wrap base.

The winning sauce direction is tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce, fiery red sauce with vinegar-style hot sauce and chipotle powder, and optional lemon tahini.

The tested wrap build uses tomato and onion without required lettuce. The winning version is the version Don called spot on.
