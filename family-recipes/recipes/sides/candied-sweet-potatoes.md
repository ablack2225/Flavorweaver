# Candied Sweet Potatoes

> A preserved family side dish from Sue Wallesch, made with sliced sweet potatoes, brown sugar, margarine, and a light cinnamon finish.

## Measured Ingredients

### Candied Sweet Potatoes

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 6 medium | Sweet potatoes | Peeled, washed, and sliced 1/2 inch thick |
| 3/4 cup | Brown sugar |  |
| 1 tsp | Salt |  |
| 1/4 cup | Margarine | Cut into slices for dotting the top |
| To taste | Cinnamon | Amount not specified on recipe page |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Peel and wash sweet potatoes. |
| 2 | Cut sweet potatoes into 1/2-inch slices. |
| 3 | Place sliced sweet potatoes in a large pan and cover with water. |
| 4 | Bring to a boil and cook about 10 minutes to tenderize the potatoes. |
| 5 | Drain sweet potatoes. |
| 6 | Place sweet potatoes into a large greased casserole dish. |
| 7 | Sprinkle with salt and brown sugar. |
| 8 | Dot the top with sliced margarine. |
| 9 | Bake at 375°F for about 1 hour, or until juice begins to thicken. |
| 10 | Sprinkle the top lightly with cinnamon. |

## Notes

This recipe was preserved from Sue Wallesch's original family cookbook.

The printed category was Vegetables. It has been filed under Sides in the digital archive.

Sue noted that she lightly sprinkled the top with cinnamon because it gave the sweet potatoes a nice touch that the family really liked.

Family note from Sue: this was the way Rudy's grandmother cooked them.

Parboil time and bake time are from the recipe. Prep time, total time, and servings are estimated. Cinnamon amount is flexible because the original recipe did not specify an amount.
