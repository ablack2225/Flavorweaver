# Don's Birthday Top-Shelf Falafel

> Crisp, deeply golden falafel with a fluffy herb-green interior, strong chickpea flavor, warm cumin-coriander depth, gentle garlic, subtle cardamom, bright herbs, lemon tahini sauce, and quick green zhug-style sauce. This should taste like serious falafel-shop falafel, not a dry baked chickpea patty.

## Golden Rule

**Do not use canned chickpeas.**

Falafel needs dried chickpeas that have been soaked overnight but not cooked. Cooked or canned chickpeas make the mixture too wet and soft, which can cause the falafel to fall apart or turn dense.

## Do Not Forget

- Do **not** use canned chickpeas.
- Chickpeas should be soaked, drained, rinsed, and dried, but not cooked.
- Meat grinder is preferred for the best texture.
- For meat grinder texture, pass mixture through the grinder twice on a fine plate.
- Food processor backup should be pulsed, not pureed.
- Finished mixture should look like damp couscous or very finely chopped nuts, not hummus paste.
- Chill mixture at least **1 hour** before shaping and frying.
- Shape small, firm falafel balls or slightly flattened patties, about **1 heaping tablespoon each**.
- Test-fry one falafel before frying the whole batch.
- Fry fresh right before dinner.
- Keep oil between **350°F and 365°F**.
- Salt lightly as soon as falafel come out of the fryer.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Soak the chickpeas.** Place **1 lb dried chickpeas** in a large bowl. Cover with cold water by at least **3 inches**. Add optional **1/2 tsp baking soda**. Soak **18 to 24 hours**. |
| 2 | **Drain and dry.** Drain chickpeas very well, rinse, then spread on towels or a baking sheet and dry thoroughly. The chickpeas should be hydrated but not cooked. |
| 3 | **Grind with the preferred meat-grinder method.** Run the soaked chickpeas, **1 small onion**, **4 scallions**, **5 garlic cloves**, **1 small carrot**, **1 packed cup parsley**, **1 packed cup cilantro**, and optional **2 Tbsp mint** through the meat grinder on a fine plate. For best texture, pass the mixture through the grinder twice. |
| 4 | **Use food processor backup only if needed.** Work in batches. Pulse chickpeas, onion, scallions, garlic, carrot, parsley, cilantro, and mint until finely minced. Do not puree. The mixture should look like damp couscous or very fine chopped nuts. |
| 5 | **Season the mixture.** Transfer ground mixture to a large bowl. Add **2 tsp cumin**, **1 1/2 tsp coriander**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/4 tsp cardamom**, **1/4 to 1/2 tsp cayenne**, **1/2 tsp black pepper**, **2 tsp kosher salt**, **1 Tbsp baking powder**, and **2 to 3 Tbsp chickpea flour or all-purpose flour**. Mix by hand until evenly combined. |
| 6 | **Check texture.** Squeeze a small handful. It should hold together when pressed but should not feel like hummus paste. If it crumbles badly, mix in **1 Tbsp cold water at a time**, only as needed. |
| 7 | **Chill mixture.** Cover and refrigerate at least **1 hour**. Keep chilled until shaping. |
| 8 | **Make lemon tahini sauce.** Whisk **1/2 cup tahini**, **1/4 cup lemon juice**, **1 grated garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/4 tsp cumin**. Slowly whisk in **1/4 to 1/2 cup ice-cold water** until smooth, pale, creamy, and pourable. Taste and adjust with lemon, salt, or water. |
| 9 | **Make quick green zhug-style sauce.** Blend or finely chop **1 cup cilantro**, **1/2 cup parsley**, **1 serrano or jalapeño**, **1 garlic clove**, **1/2 tsp cumin**, **1/4 tsp coriander**, a **pinch of cardamom**, a **pinch of salt**, **1 Tbsp lemon juice**, and **2 to 4 Tbsp oil** into a loose green sauce. Taste for salt and heat. |
| 10 | **Shape falafel.** Use wet hands or a small scoop. Shape into small balls or slightly flattened patties, about **1 heaping tablespoon each**. Press firmly enough that they hold together. Do not roll loosely like cookie dough. Place shaped falafel on a tray and keep chilled until frying. |
| 11 | **Test-fry one falafel.** Heat oil to **350°F**. Fry one test falafel. If it falls apart, press the next ones more firmly, chill mixture longer, add **1 Tbsp more flour**, or grind/pulse a small portion finer and mix it back in. |
| 12 | **Adjust after test fry if needed.** If flavor tastes flat, add a little more salt, cumin, or coriander. If texture is too dense, shape smaller, avoid packing too hard, and make sure oil is hot enough. |
| 13 | **Deep-fry in small batches.** Fry falafel at **350°F** for **3 to 4 minutes**, until deeply golden brown and crisp. Do not overcrowd. Keep oil between **350°F and 365°F**. |
| 14 | **Drain and salt.** Lift falafel with a spider strainer or slotted spoon. Drain on a rack or paper towels. Salt lightly immediately. |
| 15 | **Serve hot.** Serve falafel hot from the fryer with lemon tahini sauce, zhug-style green sauce, warm pita or soft flatbread, pickles, salad or soft cooked vegetables, herbs, and lemon wedges. |

## Test Fry Troubleshooting

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Falls apart | Press shaped falafel more firmly, chill longer, add 1 Tbsp more flour, or grind/pulse a small portion finer and mix it back in. |
| Tastes flat | Add a little more salt, cumin, or coriander. |
| Too dense | Shape smaller, avoid packing too hard, and make sure oil is hot enough. |
| Too wet | Dry chickpeas better next time, add a little more flour, and chill longer. |

## Make-Ahead and Leftover Notes

The mixture can be ground and seasoned earlier in the day, then chilled until dinner.

Do not fry too early for the birthday dinner. Falafel is best immediately after frying.

Leftover cooked falafel can be reheated in the air fryer at **350°F** until hot and re-crisped.

## Final Taste Target

The falafel should be crisp and deeply golden outside, fluffy and herb-green inside, strongly chickpea-forward, warmly spiced with cumin and coriander, gently garlicky, subtly cardamom-warm, bright from herbs, and finished with clean salt.

The lemon tahini should be creamy, pale, pourable, nutty, lemony, and balanced. The zhug-style sauce should be fresh, green, spicy, and bright.

## Chef's Notes

This recipe is built as a birthday dinner project meal. The success point is texture: dried soaked chickpeas, dry herbs, meat-grinder texture, proper chill time, firm shaping, test fry, and hot fresh service.

The meat grinder is the preferred method because it creates a fluffy, grainy falafel texture without turning the mixture into paste. The food processor works as backup, but it must be pulsed carefully.
