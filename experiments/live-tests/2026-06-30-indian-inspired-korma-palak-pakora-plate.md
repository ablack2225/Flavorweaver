# 2026-06-30 Live Test — Indian-Inspired Korma, Palak Paneer, Aloo Pakora Plate

## Meal Components

- Mughlai-Style Chicken Korma
- Palak Paneer
- Basmati Rice
- Aloo Pakora-Inspired Potato Fritters
- Bold Carrot Coconut Peanut Chutney
- Fluffy Skillet Naan

## Overall Verdict

Don and Amy both scored this meal very high and want it added to the rotation.

The standout components were:

1. Aloo Pakora-Inspired Potato Fritters
2. Bold Carrot Coconut Peanut Chutney

The chutney was excellent with both the pakora and the naan.

## Korma Notes

- Korma was successful overall.
- Add a touch more salt at the final seasoning stage.
- The korma remained a milder, creamy, nutty anchor for the plate, which made the chutney and pakora contrast especially important.

## Aloo Pakora Notes

- Pakora was a major success and should stay in rotation.
- The AP flour/cornstarch version worked well enough to preserve even after a future besan/rice flour version is tested.
- Coating read more smooth/bajji-like than craggy pakora, but the eating result was excellent.
- Best with the Bold Carrot Coconut Peanut Chutney.

## Chutney Notes

- Initial blend was too chunky/thick; extra water was added and it was blended smoother.
- Initial flavor read carrot + nutty, not bold enough.
- Live corrections included extra lemon/lime, extra water, salt/seasoning, and curry powder.
- Don noted that warm and smooth, it read more like a sauce than a chutney.
- Despite the texture note, both Don and Amy loved it.
- Chutney was one of the highlights of the plate and was excellent on naan.

## Canon Impact

- Update chutney working recipe to include curry powder, extra water range, extra lemon/lime adjustment, and optional texture stir-in.
- Update pakora as tested / rotation-worthy.
- Keep korma final seasoning note: taste at the end and add a touch of salt if needed.
- This meal supports the Flavorweaver direction of adapting world-cuisine-inspired recipes with locally available ingredients while keeping bold, memorable flavor as the standard.
