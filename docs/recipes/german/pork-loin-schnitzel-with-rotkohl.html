Pork Loin Schnitzel with Blackberry Rotkohl, Egg Noodles & Jäger-Style Gravy

Thin pork-loin schnitzel fried feather-crisp and served with buttered spätzle-style egg noodles, mushroom-onion brown cream gravy, bold blackberry Rotkohl, and generous fresh lemon. Optional bread may be served at the table for extra gravy.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Pork Loin Cutlets

Thin pork loin fries quickly and stays tender.

Feather-Crisp Breading

Half plain breadcrumb and half crushed panko keep the crust crisp without heaviness.

Jäger-Style Gravy

Browned mushrooms and onions create the savory sauce for the noodles.

Buttered Egg Noodles

Spätzle-style sauce catcher for the gravy.

Blackberry Rotkohl

Red cabbage braised with separately staged onion and apple, then finished with blackberry, Dijon, and butter.

Fresh Lemon

Brightens the schnitzel, gravy, noodles, and Rotkohl at service.

Flavor Target

The plate should be crisp, rich, savory, dark-fruited, buttery, and lifted by generous fresh lemon so the richness stays alive. Keep gravy mostly on the noodles and leave the schnitzel crust exposed.

Advance Prep

Rotkohl onion and apple must be held in separate prep bowls because they enter the pot at different times.

If the apple will sit before cooking, toss it with 1 tsp lemon juice to slow browning.

Rotkohl may be made ahead and gently rewarmed.

Gravy can hold warm while the schnitzel is fried close to service.

Measured Ingredients

Pork Loin Schnitzel

1 1/4 to 1 1/2 lb boneless pork loin, sliced into 4 cutlets and pounded to 1/8 to 3/16 inch; 1 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1/2 tsp garlic powder; 1/2 tsp plain paprika; 1/4 tsp onion powder; 3/4 cup flour; 2 eggs, plus 1 more if cutlets are very wide; 1 Tbsp milk or water; 3/4 cup plain breadcrumbs; 3/4 cup crushed panko; neutral oil or brisket tallow for frying; 2 large or 3 small lemons, cut into 12 wedges.

Jäger-Style Mushroom-Onion Brown Cream Gravy

8 oz mushrooms, sliced; 1 large yellow onion, thin sliced; 2 Tbsp butter; 1 Tbsp oil; optional garlic or 1/4 tsp garlic powder; 2 Tbsp flour; 1 1/2 cups beef broth or chicken broth; 1 tsp Worcestershire or soy; 1 tsp Dijon; 1/2 tsp black pepper; salt; 1/4 cup cream or tempered sour cream; 1 tsp butter.

Buttered Spätzle-Style Egg Noodles

12 oz wide egg noodles; 2 Tbsp butter; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp black pepper; optional tiny pinch nutmeg; optional parsley or green onion.

Blackberry Rotkohl

1 medium head purple/red cabbage, cored and thin sliced; 1 medium yellow onion, thin half-moons and held separately; 1 Granny Smith apple, peeled and grated or finely diced and held separately; optional 1 tsp lemon juice for prepped apple; 3 Tbsp butter, bacon fat, or a mix; 1/3 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 to 3 Tbsp brown sugar; 1/2 cup apple juice, apple cider, broth, or water; 1 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper; 1 bay leaf; optional clove and cinnamon; 1 to 1 1/2 Tbsp blackberry jelly; 1 tsp Dijon or stone-ground mustard; 1 tsp butter.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Rotkohl Bowls

Bowl 1 — Cabbage

1 medium head purple/red cabbage, cored and thin sliced. Add after onion softens.

Bowl 2 — Onion

1 medium yellow onion, thin half-moons. Keep separate; this cooks first.

Bowl 3 — Apple

1 Granny Smith apple, peeled and grated or finely diced. Keep separate; add with cabbage. Toss with 1 tsp lemon juice only if it will sit before cooking.

Bowl 4 — Braising Fat

3 Tbsp butter, bacon fat, or a mix. Start onion in this.

Bowl 5 — Sweet-Sour Liquid

1/3 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 to 3 Tbsp brown sugar; 1/2 cup apple juice, cider, broth, or water.

Bowl 6 — Seasoning

1 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper; 1 bay leaf; optional clove and cinnamon.

Bowl 7 — Finish

1 to 1 1/2 Tbsp blackberry jelly; 1 tsp Dijon; 1 tsp butter; tiny splash vinegar if needed.

Schnitzel, Gravy, and Noodle Bowls

Pork Cutlets

Pork loin cutlets pounded to 1/8 to 3/16 inch.

Breading Station

3/4 cup flour, egg wash, and breadcrumb-panko mix. Increase egg or crumbs only if cutlets are extra wide.

Gravy Base

Mushrooms and sliced onion for deep browning.

Egg Noodles

Wide egg noodles, butter, salt, pepper, optional nutmeg.

Lemon Service

12 lemon wedges. Serve 3 wedges per plate for all components.

Do Not Forget

Keep Rotkohl onion and apple in separate prep bowls. Onion cooks first; apple joins with cabbage.

If apple is prepped early, toss it with 1 tsp lemon juice to slow browning.

Pound pork to 1/8 to 3/16 inch.

Use half plain breadcrumbs and half crushed panko.

Let breaded schnitzel rest 10 minutes before frying.

Brown mushrooms and onions deeply for the gravy.

Keep gravy on the noodles, not over the whole schnitzel.

Fry thin schnitzel fast: 350°F, 60 seconds, flip, 60 seconds more, then check temperature.

Serve 3 lemon wedges per plate and squeeze fresh lemon over the schnitzel, noodles, gravy, and Rotkohl.

Finish Rotkohl only after the cabbage is tender.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Prep Rotkohl. Thin slice cabbage. Thin slice onion into half-moons and keep it separate. Peel and grate or finely dice apple and keep it separate. If the apple will sit, toss it with 1 tsp lemon juice.
  2. Sauté Rotkohl onion. Melt 3 Tbsp butter, bacon fat, or a mix over medium heat. Add only the onion and cook 4 to 5 minutes, until softened.
  3. Add cabbage and apple. Add cabbage, apple, salt, pepper, bay, cinnamon, and clove if using. Stir well.
  4. Add liquid. Add cider vinegar, 2 Tbsp brown sugar, and apple juice/cider/broth/water. Stir. Reserve extra brown sugar for adjustment.
  5. Braise. Cover and cook low to medium-low for 45 to 75 minutes, stirring occasionally, until tender and glossy. Uncover near the end if too liquidy.
  6. Build gravy. Brown mushrooms, then onion, then garlic or garlic powder. Add flour, cook briefly, then add broth, Worcestershire or soy, Dijon, and black pepper. Simmer until glossy. Finish with cream or tempered sour cream and butter.
  7. Prepare pork. Slice pork loin into 4 cutlets. Pound to 1/8 to 3/16 inch and season both sides.
  8. Bread pork. Dredge in flour, dip in egg wash, coat lightly in breadcrumb-panko mix, and rest on a rack for 10 minutes.
  9. Finish Rotkohl. Remove bay leaf. Stir in blackberry jelly, Dijon, and butter. Balance with salt, sugar, vinegar, or extra blackberry jelly as needed.
  10. Cook noodles. Boil noodles until just shy of tender. Drain and toss with butter, salt, pepper, optional nutmeg, and herbs if using.
  11. Fry schnitzel. Fry in 350°F oil or brisket tallow for about 2 minutes total, flipping at 60 seconds. Pull around 145 to 150°F internal and salt while hot.
  12. Plate. Spoon gravy over noodles. Keep schnitzel partly exposed and crisp. Add Rotkohl as a glossy mound and serve with 3 lemon wedges per plate. Squeeze fresh lemon over all components to finish.

Rotkohl Balance Map

IssueAdjustment
Too sharpAdd brown sugar, 1 tsp at a time, or a little more blackberry jelly.
Too sweetAdd apple cider vinegar, 1 tsp at a time.
FlatAdd salt first, then reassess.
Too waterySimmer uncovered longer.
Too heavyAdd a tiny splash vinegar and black pepper.
Missing depthAdd Dijon or stone-ground mustard, 1/2 tsp at a time.
Missing dark fruitAdd up to 1/2 Tbsp more blackberry jelly.

Final Taste Target

The final plate should land as crisp, rich, savory, dark-fruited, buttery, bright, and deeply comforting without becoming heavy. The schnitzel should stay crisp. The gravy should live mostly on the noodles. The Rotkohl should cut through the richness. Fresh lemon over all components is part of the final balance.

Live-Test Lock Notes

Brisket tallow at 350°F worked beautifully for the schnitzel.

For thin pounded pork loin cutlets, 2 minutes total fry time was perfect: 60 seconds, flip, 60 seconds more.

Internal temperature landed around 150°F and the pork was perfectly cooked.

Fresh lemon juice over the schnitzel, noodles, gravy, and Rotkohl made the plate top-tier.
