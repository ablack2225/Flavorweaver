Bold Carrot Coconut Peanut Chutney

A smooth, spicy-sweet carrot-coconut peanut chutney for aloo pakora, naan, and creamy Indian-inspired plates.

Meal TypeCondiment
Total15 min
YieldAbout 3/4 cup
StatusTested / Rotation
Indian-Inspiredcarrot chutneycoconut chutneypeanut chutneypakora sauce

Kitchen Execution Summary

Toasted Base

Sweetened coconut flakes, cashews, and sesame seeds are gently toasted for aroma and body.

Carrot Identity

Shredded carrot keeps the chutney bright, sweet, and distinct from a standard peanut sauce.

Bold Body

Peanut butter gives the chutney a clingy dipping texture for fried pakora and naan.

Curry-Spice Lift

Curry powder, cumin, garam masala, chili, salt, and lemon/lime push the chutney savory and bright.

Ingredient Roll-Up

Toasted Base

2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes, 1 Tbsp cashews, 1 tsp sesame seeds.

Blender Bowl

1/2 cup shredded carrot, toasted base, 1 Tbsp peanut butter, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/4 tsp crushed cumin seed, 1/4 tsp curry powder, 1/8 tsp garam masala, 1/8 tsp cayenne/chili powder, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 1 Tbsp lemon/lime juice, and water.

Adjustments

Extra water, extra lemon/lime, extra salt, extra chili, tiny extra curry powder, optional soy sauce.

Optional Texture

Finely shredded carrot, toasted sesame, finely chopped toasted coconut, or chopped cashew.

Measured Ingredients

Toasted Coconut-Nut Base

2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes; 1 Tbsp cashews; 1 tsp sesame seeds.

Blender Bowl

1/2 cup shredded carrot; all toasted coconut-nut base; 1 Tbsp peanut butter; 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste; 1/4 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed; 1/4 tsp curry powder; 1/8 tsp garam masala; 1/8 tsp cayenne or chili powder; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; 1 Tbsp lemon or lime juice; 2 Tbsp water to start; 1 to 3 Tbsp additional water as needed.

Live-Test Adjustments

1 to 2 tsp extra lemon/lime juice, as needed; extra kosher salt to taste; extra cayenne/chili powder to taste; tiny extra curry powder if needed; optional 1/4 tsp soy sauce.

Optional Texture Stir-In

1 Tbsp finely shredded carrot; 1 tsp toasted sesame; 1 tsp finely chopped toasted coconut; or 1 tsp chopped cashew.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Toasted Coconut-Nut Base

2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes, 1 Tbsp cashews, 1 tsp sesame seeds.

Bowl 2 — Blender Bowl

1/2 cup shredded carrot, all toasted base, 1 Tbsp peanut butter, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/4 tsp crushed cumin seed, 1/4 tsp curry powder, 1/8 tsp garam masala, 1/8 tsp cayenne/chili powder, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 1 Tbsp lemon/lime juice, 2 Tbsp water.

Bowl 3 — Adjustment Bowl

1 to 3 Tbsp additional water, 1 to 2 tsp extra lemon/lime, extra salt, extra chili, tiny extra curry powder, optional 1/4 tsp soy sauce.

Bowl 4 — Optional Texture

Finely shredded carrot, toasted sesame, chopped toasted coconut, or chopped cashew.

Do Not Forget

Toast gently; sweetened coconut burns fast.

Start with only 2 Tbsp water, then add more gradually until smooth and dippable.

This chutney needs enough lemon/lime and salt to stay savory.

Curry powder belongs in the tested version.

If it tastes carrot-nutty but not bold, add lemon/lime, salt, chili, and a tiny bit more curry powder.

Serve room temperature for the best chutney feel. Warm chutney reads creamier and more sauce-like.

Texture should be thick enough to cling to pakora and naan, but loose enough to spread or drag through.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Toast the base. In a dry skillet over medium-low heat, add 2 Tbsp sweetened coconut flakes, 1 Tbsp cashews, and 1 tsp sesame seeds. Stir constantly until fragrant and lightly golden. Do not walk away.
  2. Build the blender bowl. Add the toasted base to a blender or Ninja cup with 1/2 cup shredded carrot, 1 Tbsp peanut butter, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/4 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed, 1/4 tsp curry powder, 1/8 tsp garam masala, 1/8 tsp cayenne or chili powder, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, 1 Tbsp lemon/lime juice, and 2 Tbsp water.
  3. Blend and loosen. Blend until mostly smooth. Stop and scrape as needed. Add additional water 1 tsp to 1 Tbsp at a time, up to about 1 to 3 Tbsp more, until smooth, thick, and dippable.
  4. Taste hard. If the chutney tastes mostly carrot-nutty, add 1 tsp extra lemon/lime juice, a tiny pinch of salt, a pinch of chili, and a tiny pinch more curry powder. Taste again.
  5. Optional savory boost. If it still tastes too polite or not savory enough, blend in 1/4 tsp soy sauce.
  6. Optional texture correction. If a more chutney-like texture is wanted, stir in finely shredded carrot, toasted sesame, chopped toasted coconut, or chopped cashew after blending.
  7. Serve. Serve in a small bowl beside hot aloo pakora and naan. Room temperature gives the best balance.

Taste Adjustment Map

Carrot-Nutty, Not Bold

Add lemon/lime, salt, chili, and a tiny pinch more curry powder.

Too Sweet

Add more lemon/lime and a tiny pinch salt.

Too Thick or Chunky

Add water 1 tsp at a time and blend again.

Too Thin

Add a few cashews or 1/2 tsp peanut butter.

Too Peanut-Heavy

Add more carrot and lemon/lime.

Too Sauce-Like

Serve room temperature or stir in carrot, sesame, coconut, or cashew texture.

Final Taste Target

The finished chutney should be smooth, thick, clingy, carrot-bright, coconut-sweet, nutty, lightly spicy, and lifted by lemon/lime. It should make crispy aloo pakora feel intentional and should also be good enough to scoop with naan.
