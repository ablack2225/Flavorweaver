Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Day before — make the spice blend. Measure the House Berbere-Style Spice Blend. Reserve 1 Tbsp for the chicken marinade and 1 Tbsp for the Doro sauce base.
  2. Day before — marinate the chicken. Coat 2 whole chicken thighs with 1 Tbsp berbere blend, 1 Tbsp oil, 1 Tbsp lemon juice, 2 minced garlic cloves, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp black pepper. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  3. Bread planning. Prepare Fluffy Skillet Naan from its own recipe so it is ready before final plating.
  4. Start Misir Wat. Prepare Misir Wat from its standalone recipe. Hold warm.
  5. Hard-boil eggs. Cook 4 large eggs, chill, peel, and lightly score or prick so sauce can cling.
  6. Start Doro sauce. Cook 1 large finely diced onion until softened, reduced, and beginning to turn golden, about 10 to 15 minutes.
  7. Bloom Doro sauce spices. Add 1 to 2 Tbsp unsalted butter or oil and 1 Tbsp reserved berbere blend. Bloom 30 to 60 seconds. Add 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp ginger, and 1 tsp tomato paste; cook 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. Sear and simmer chicken. Add the 2 whole marinated chicken thighs and sear lightly 2 to 3 minutes per side. Add 1 cup chicken broth and optional bay leaf. Simmer covered on low 25 to 35 minutes.
  9. Sauce the eggs. During the last 10 to 15 minutes, nestle the 4 peeled eggs into the sauce and turn gently so they stain evenly.
  10. Thicken Doro sauce. Uncover and simmer 5 to 10 minutes until the sauce clings to the chicken and eggs.
  11. Rewarm naan and plate. Serve Doro Wat chicken, eggs, sauce, and Misir Wat with warm naan.

Building the Final Plate

Naan: place prepared naan beside the warm sauced components.

Chicken: place 1 whole Doro Wat-style chicken thigh on each plate and spoon sauce over the top.

Eggs: add 2 sauced eggs per plate.

Misir Wat: add a generous spoon of warm red-lentil Misir Wat beside the chicken and eggs.

Final sauce: spoon extra Doro sauce over the chicken and eggs. Keep the plate rich, saucy, and scoopable.

Final Taste Target

The plate should move between spicy rich Doro sauce, tender whole-thigh chicken, sauced eggs, earthy red-lentil Misir Wat, and warm naan.

The winning version is the Doro Wat + Misir Wat pairing. Misir Wat brings enough depth and earthiness to stand beside the Doro sauce without muting it.
