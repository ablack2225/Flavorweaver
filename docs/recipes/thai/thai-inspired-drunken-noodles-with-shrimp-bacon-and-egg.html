Thai-Inspired Drunken Noodles with Shrimp, Bacon & Egg

A complete Thai-inspired noodle dish with wide rice noodles, shrimp, bacon, soft egg curds, chunky garlic, Thai chiles, shallot, red bell pepper, vine tomato, Thai basil, and glossy oyster-fish-soy sauce.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Complete Noodle Dish

This card covers the full stir-fry: soaked wide rice noodles, shrimp, bacon, soft egg curds, chunky aromatics, red bell pepper, tomato, Thai basil, and sauce.

Chunky Aromatic Backbone

Use 8 large garlic cloves with Thai chiles and shallot. Keep them chunky and visible, not pasted.

Dry Noodle Path

Soak dry wide rice noodles until bendy but slightly firm so they finish in the sauce without turning mushy.

Final Fresh Finish

Vine tomato and Thai basil go in off heat so the tomato carries the basil aroma without flooding the noodles.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

Confirm noodles — Use dry wide rice noodles for this test. Fresh wide rice noodles remain the future upgrade path.

Confirm basil — Thai basil is the available house version. Holy basil is the future ideal.

Sauce can be mixed ahead — Mix up to 1 day ahead and refrigerate. Stir before cooking.

Do not marinate shrimp early — Toss shrimp with its marinade only about 10 minutes before cooking.

No zucchini in this test — It was removed to protect noodle texture.

Service Countdown

45-50 minutes before serving: start soaking the dry wide rice noodles.

35 minutes before serving: cut garlic, Thai chiles, shallot, onion, red bell pepper, tomato, bacon, and basil.

25 minutes before serving: mix sauce and beat eggs.

15 minutes before serving: toss shrimp with marinade.

At service: render bacon, stir-fry aromatics, eggs, shrimp, vegetables, noodles, and sauce. Finish with tomato, Thai basil, and bacon.

Full Noodle Dish Ingredient Roll-Up

Quick-glance ingredient check for the full noodle dish. Detailed measurements and division points are handled below.

Noodle Base

8 oz dry wide rice noodles; tiny drizzle neutral oil only if needed after draining.

Protein Set

8 oz to 1 lb shrimp; 2 strips bacon; 2 large eggs; fish sauce, garlic, sweetener, and oil for shrimp marinade.

Aromatics + Vegetables

8 large garlic cloves; 2-5 Thai chiles; 1 large shallot; 1/2 onion; 1 red bell pepper.

Sauce + Finish

Oyster sauce, fish sauce, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, honey or brown sugar, water, vine tomato, and 1 1/2 to 2 packed cups Thai basil.

Measured Ingredients by Component

These component sections are reference only. The cooking flow is handled by the prep bowls and timeline below.

Noodles

8 oz dry wide rice noodles, soaked in hot tap water or very warm water until bendy but still slightly firm; tiny drizzle neutral oil only if needed.

Shrimp Marinade

8 oz to 1 lb shrimp, peeled, deveined, and patted dry; 1 tsp fish sauce; 1 small garlic clove, finely chopped or grated; tiny pinch honey or sugar; 1 tsp neutral oil. Marinate 10 minutes only.

Bacon, Eggs, Aromatics, Vegetables, and Finish

2 strips bacon, cut into 1/4-inch pieces; 2 large eggs, lightly beaten; 8 large garlic cloves, smashed and rough chopped; 2-5 Thai chiles; 1 large shallot, thin half-moons; 1/2 onion, 1/4-inch strips; 1 red bell pepper, 1/4-inch by 2-inch strips; 1 medium or 2 small vine tomatoes; 1 1/2 to 2 packed cups Thai basil.

Glossy Sauce

2 Tbsp oyster sauce; 1 Tbsp fish sauce; 1 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 tsp dark soy sauce; 1 1/2 Tbsp honey or brown sugar; 1 Tbsp water; optional tiny pinch black pepper.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Garlic: use 1 small clove for the shrimp marinade and 8 large cloves for the chunky aromatic bowl. Do not combine them.

Fish sauce: use 1 tsp for shrimp and 1 Tbsp for sauce.

Bacon: render first, reserve crisp pieces for the finish, and leave about 1 Tbsp fat in the pan.

Tomato and Thai basil: keep separate until the final off-heat finish.

Proper Defining Cuts

Garlic: smash and rough chop into visible pieces. Thai chiles: slice or rough chop with garlic. Shallot: 1/8-inch half-moons.

Onion: 1/4-inch strips. Red bell pepper: 1/4-inch wide by 2-inch strips. Tomato: wedges or chunky bite-size pieces. Bacon: 1/4-inch pieces.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Noodle Bowl

Bowl 1 — Soaked Noodles

8 oz dry wide rice noodles, soaked, drained, and rested 10-15 minutes; tiny drizzle oil only if needed.

Sauce and Protein Bowls

Bowl 2 — Glossy Sauce

2 Tbsp oyster sauce; 1 Tbsp fish sauce; 1 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 tsp dark soy sauce; 1 1/2 Tbsp honey or brown sugar; 1 Tbsp water.

Bowl 3 — Shrimp

8 oz to 1 lb shrimp; 1 tsp fish sauce; 1 small garlic clove; tiny pinch sweetener; 1 tsp oil.

Bowl 4 — Bacon

2 strips bacon, cut into 1/4-inch pieces.

Bowl 5 — Eggs

2 large eggs, lightly beaten; optional 1 tsp water.

Aromatic, Vegetable, and Finish Bowls

Bowl 6 — Chunky Aromatics

8 large garlic cloves, rough chopped; 2-5 Thai chiles; 1 large shallot.

Bowl 7 — Stir-Fry Vegetables

1/2 onion; 1 red bell pepper.

Bowl 8 — Fresh Finish

Vine tomato; 1 1/2 to 2 packed cups Thai basil; reserved cooked bacon.

Do Not Forget

Use the full 8 large cloves garlic. Keep garlic and Thai chiles chunky, not pasted. Do not boil dry noodles fully soft. Drain noodles very well and rest before stir-frying. Leave only about 1 Tbsp bacon fat in the pan. Keep egg curds large and soft. Add water only 1 Tbsp at a time if noodles need help. Tomato and Thai basil go in at the end.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Soak the noodles. Place 8 oz dry wide rice noodles in hot tap water or very warm water. Start checking at 20 minutes. Stop when flexible and bendy but still slightly firm. Drain very well and rest 10-15 minutes.
  2. Mix the sauce. Stir together 2 Tbsp oyster sauce, 1 Tbsp fish sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp dark soy sauce, 1 1/2 Tbsp honey or brown sugar, and 1 Tbsp water.
  3. Marinate the shrimp. Toss 8 oz to 1 lb shrimp with 1 tsp fish sauce, 1 small garlic clove, tiny pinch sweetener, and 1 tsp neutral oil. Rest 10 minutes only.
  4. Render bacon. Cook 2 strips bacon, cut into 1/4-inch pieces, until fat renders and edges are lightly crisp. Remove pieces to Bowl 8. Leave about 1 Tbsp fat in the pan.
  5. Heat the pan. Raise heat to high. Add 1 Tbsp neutral oil if the pan looks dry.
  6. Bloom aromatics. Add 8 rough-chopped garlic cloves, 2-5 Thai chiles, and 1 thin-sliced shallot. Stir-fry 30-60 seconds. Do not burn the garlic.
  7. Add eggs. Push aromatics aside. Add 2 lightly beaten eggs, let set briefly, then fold into large soft curds.
  8. Add shrimp. Add marinated shrimp and toss until just starting to turn pink.
  9. Add onion and red bell pepper. Add 1/2 sliced onion and 1 sliced red bell pepper. Stir-fry 45-75 seconds.
  10. Add noodles and sauce. Add soaked noodles and sauce. Fold gently for 2-4 minutes, until glossy, darker, and tender.
  11. Adjust only if needed. If noodles are still too firm, add water 1 Tbsp at a time and keep folding.
  12. Finish off heat. Turn heat low or off. Add reserved bacon, vine tomato, and 1 1/2 to 2 packed cups Thai basil. Fold 30-60 seconds.
  13. Serve immediately.

Building the Final Noodle Bowl

Divide hot glossy noodles between bowls. Keep shrimp, bacon pieces, egg curds, red bell pepper, tomato, and Thai basil visible. Serve immediately so the noodles stay tender-chewy and basil-fragrant.

Final Taste Target

The finished noodles should be glossy, spicy, garlicky, savory-sweet, and basil-heavy. Shrimp should be tender, bacon should support without taking over, egg should stay in soft curds, and red bell pepper should keep a little crispness.

Chef's Notes

This version keeps the garlic and Thai chiles chunky instead of turning them into a smooth paste. Red bell pepper is the preferred pantry vegetable for this test because it brings color and sweetness without the water risk of zucchini. Tomato belongs at the end with Thai basil.
