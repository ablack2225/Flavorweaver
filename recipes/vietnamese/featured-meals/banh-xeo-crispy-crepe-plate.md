# Banh Xeo Crispy Crepe Plate

> Crispy Vietnamese-inspired rice crepes filled with shrimp, **1/8-inch pork loin strips**, scallion, and bean sprouts, served with mustard greens or lettuce wraps, mint, cilantro, Thai basil, English cucumber, Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and bright nước chấm.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Component | Role |
|---|---|
| Crispy Banh Xeo Batter | Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour, cornstarch, turmeric, coconut milk, cold club soda, scallion. Thin, crisp, lacy. |
| Shrimp + 1/8-Inch Pork Loin | Integrated directly into each crepe for savory, classic-style filling. |
| Bean Sprouts | Added to the hot crepe so they stay crisp and fresh. |
| Mustard Greens + Herb Wrap Plate | The freshness that makes bánh xèo bright instead of heavy. |
| Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables | House pickle accent; drained and used lightly in the lettuce wraps. |
| Nước Chấm | Sweet-sour-salty fish sauce dip; required. |

## Flavor Direction

The target is shatter-crisp edges, a thin golden turmeric crepe, tender shrimp, savory 1/8-inch pork loin strips, crisp bean sprouts, cold mustard greens or lettuce, fresh herbs, English cucumber, light Asian pickles, and bright nước chấm. This plate should feel sizzling, fresh, crunchy, acidic, and herb-heavy — not dense or pancake-like.

## Chef-Level Control Points

| Control Point | Standard |
|---|---|
| Batter thinness | After resting, batter should look like light cream or thin heavy cream. If it looks like pancake batter, whisk in **1 Tbsp cold club soda at a time**, up to **4 Tbsp**, until it pours and swirls quickly. |
| First crepe | Treat the first crepe as the calibration crepe. Adjust oil, heat, and batter thinness before cooking the remaining three. |
| Hot oil | Oil should shimmer and the batter should hiss immediately. If there is no sizzle, wait longer before pouring. |
| Edge-fry | After the crepe sets, drizzle **1 tsp neutral oil** around the outer edge to fry the lace and prevent a soft rim. |
| Steam control | Do not stack crepes. Move each finished crepe to a wire rack, vented tray, or tilted plate so steam escapes. |
| Herb quality | Greens, herbs, cucumber, and pickles must be cold, washed, spun/dried well, and ready before the first crepe hits the skillet. |
| Sauce calibration | Nước chấm should taste sweet first, salty second, sour-bright at the finish, with garlic/chile lift. It should taste slightly intense alone because it seasons the whole lettuce-wrapped bite. |
| Service | Serve as hot crispy shards tucked into cold greens with herbs, cucumber, pickles, and nước chấm. Scissors are acceptable for cutting the crepe cleanly at the table. |

## Advance Prep

| Timing | Task |
|---|---|
| 2–24 hr ahead | Mix batter and refrigerate. Longer rest improves hydration and crispness. |
| Up to 1 day ahead | Make nước chấm and refrigerate. Re-taste before serving. |
| Same day | Prep mustard greens/lettuce, herbs, English cucumber, bean sprouts, shrimp, pork loin, and Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables. Dry greens and herbs thoroughly. |
| At service | Cook crepes one at a time and eat hot. |

## Full Plate Roll-Up

For 2 large servings, plan on **4 medium crepes**. Serve each crepe cut or torn into mustard greens or lettuce cups with herbs, cucumber, Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and nước chấm. The crepes should be cooked fresh and eaten immediately; bánh xèo loses its magic if held too long.

## Do Not Forget

- Use **regular white rice flour / Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết**, not glutinous or sweet rice flour.
- Batter should be thin like light cream or thin heavy cream, not pancake batter.
- Rest batter at least **30 minutes**; longer is better.
- Stir batter before every crepe because rice flour settles.
- Use enough oil to lightly fry the base and lace the edges.
- The first crepe is the calibration crepe; adjust before continuing.
- Do not overload the crepe; too much filling makes it steam instead of crisp.
- Serve immediately with mustard greens/lettuce, herbs, cucumber, Asian pickles, and nước chấm.
- No peanut drizzle in the main build; nước chấm is the sauce identity.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Make batter.** In Bowl 1, whisk **1 cup Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour**, **2 Tbsp cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/2 tsp sugar**. Whisk in Bowl 2: **1/2 cup coconut milk** and **1 cup cold club soda or cold sparkling water** until smooth.
2. **Rest batter.** Cover batter and rest at least **30 minutes**, or refrigerate up to **24 hours**. Rice flour settles heavily; this is expected.
3. **Make nước chấm.** In Bowl 7, stir **3 Tbsp warm water** with **2 Tbsp sugar** until dissolved. Add **2 Tbsp fish sauce**, **2 Tbsp lime juice**, **1 Tbsp rice vinegar**, **1 minced/grated garlic clove**, and chile to taste. Taste and calibrate: sweet first, salty second, sour-bright finish, garlic/chile lift.
4. **Prep fresh plate.** Arrange Bowl 6: **8–10 mustard green or lettuce leaves**, **1/2 cup mint**, **1/2 cup cilantro**, **1/2 cup Thai basil**, optional perilla/shiso, **1 English cucumber cut into sticks**, **1/2–3/4 cup drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables**, and lime wedges. Keep cold and dry.
5. **Finish batter texture.** Stir batter thoroughly from the bottom. It should pour like light cream and swirl quickly. If thick, whisk in Bowl 3: **1 Tbsp extra cold club soda at a time**, up to **4 Tbsp**, until thin and fluid. Stir in **2 thin-sliced scallions** after adjusting.
6. **Season filling.** Toss Bowl 4: **8 oz shrimp**, **8 oz pork loin cut into 1/8-inch strips**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, and **1 tsp fish sauce**. Rest **10–15 minutes**.
7. **Heat crepe pan.** Heat skillet until hot. Add **1–2 Tbsp neutral oil** and swirl. The oil should shimmer and the batter should sizzle immediately when it hits the pan.
8. **Start the calibration crepe.** Add a small portion of **1/2 small thin-sliced onion** and a few **1/8-inch pork loin strips** to the hot oil. Cook **30–45 seconds**, just until the pork starts losing its raw color.
9. **Add shrimp and batter.** Add a few shrimp pieces, then immediately stir the batter and pour about **1/2 cup batter** into the hot pan. Swirl quickly into a thin **9–10 inch** crepe. The batter should hiss, bubble, and set almost immediately at the edges.
10. **Add sprouts.** Scatter one-quarter of the **1 1/2 cups bean sprouts** over one half of the crepe.
11. **Edge-fry and crisp.** Cook uncovered **4–6 minutes**, adjusting heat as needed. Once the crepe has set, drizzle **1 tsp neutral oil** around the outer edge so the lace fries crisp. If the top refuses to set, cover only **30–60 seconds**, then uncover and finish crisping.
12. **Evaluate the first crepe.** If it is soft or steamy, increase oil slightly, thin the batter with another **1 Tbsp club soda**, and/or raise heat slightly. If it browns before crisping, lower heat slightly. If it sticks, wait longer before moving and use a little more oil.
13. **Fold and vent.** Fold the empty half over the filling. Slide onto a wire rack, vented tray, or tilted plate. Do not stack directly or the crepes will soften.
14. **Repeat.** Repeat with remaining batter, pork, shrimp, onion, and sprouts for **4 crepes total**, stirring batter before each one and adding fresh oil as needed.
15. **Serve immediately.** Cut or tear hot crepe into crisp pieces. Tuck into mustard greens or lettuce with mint, cilantro, Thai basil, cucumber sticks, drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and nước chấm.

## Electric Skillet Notes

- Start the electric skillet around **325°F** because bánh xèo needs immediate sizzle.
- Because this skillet runs hot, be ready to drop to **300°F** if the batter browns before crisping.
- If the crepe steams and stays soft, raise toward **325°F**, use a little more oil, and thin the batter if needed.
- Cook one crepe at a time unless the skillet has enough room to swirl thinly.
- Keep a small spoon of oil nearby for the **1 tsp edge-fry** after the crepe sets.

## Final Taste Target

The plate should be hot-cold, crisp-fresh, and sauce-bright: brittle golden crepe edges, tender shrimp and 1/8-inch pork loin, crunchy bean sprouts, cold mustard greens or lettuce, mint, cilantro, Thai basil, English cucumber, drained Asian pickles, and punchy nước chấm. The best bite is crepe + greens + herbs + cucumber + nước chấm.

## Source / Research Notes

- Bánh xèo should remain a sizzling rice-flour crepe experience, not a filled pancake.
- The elite texture depends on hot oil, thin batter, quick swirling, immediate bubbling/set, and steam control after cooking.
- The eating architecture is hot crisp crepe wrapped in cold greens/herbs with cucumber, pickles, and nước chấm.
- This version uses 1/8-inch pork loin as the Amy & Don pantry-optimized cut. Future luxury tests could compare very thin pork belly or shoulder for additional fat and richness, but pork loin remains the locked version for this cook.
