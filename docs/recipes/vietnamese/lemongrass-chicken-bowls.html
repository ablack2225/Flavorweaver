Lemongrass Chicken Bowls

Fresh Vietnamese-inspired chicken bowls with lemongrass-marinated chicken, angel hair pasta, crisp vegetables, pickled vegetables, crushed cashews, and generous ginger nuoc cham.

Prep45-60 min
Cook20 min
Marinate2 to 4 hr
Serves2 bowls
top 10 mealchicken bowllemongrass chickenvietnamese-inspiredcomplete meal platesauce-driven

Ingredient List

Lemongrass Chicken

Chicken thighs, ginger nuoc cham, lemongrass paste, garlic, neutral oil.

Bowl Base

Cooked angel hair pasta.

Fresh Vegetables

Napa cabbage, cucumber, zucchini ribbons or matchsticks.

Acid + Crunch

Asian pickled vegetables and crushed cashews.

Finish

Extra ginger nuoc cham for drizzling and serving.

Measured Ingredients

Lemongrass Chicken Marinade

3 smallBoneless chicken thighsAbout 12-16 oz total.
2 TbspRestaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc ChamRequired for the marinade.
1 TbspLemongrass paste
1 cloveGarlicMinced.
1 tspNeutral oil

Bowl Components

These amounts make 2 bowls. Layer the bowls in the order below, then adjust the vegetable, noodle, sauce, and crunch levels to your liking.

Layer 1 · 4 oz dryAngel hair pastaCook, drain, and cool slightly. Divide between 2 bowls as the base.
Layer 2 · AllSliced lemongrass chickenSlice the cooked chicken thinly and divide over the pasta.
Layer 3 · 2 cupsShredded napa cabbageAbout 1 cup per bowl.
Layer 4 · 1 cupJulienned cucumberAbout 1/2 cup per bowl.
Layer 5 · 1 cupGarden zucchini ribbons or matchsticksAbout 1/2 cup per bowl.
Layer 6 · 1/2-3/4 cupAsian pickled vegetablesScatter over the top for acid, crunch, and brightness.
Layer 7 · 2-4 TbspCrushed cashewsSprinkle over finished bowls.
Layer 8 · 1/4-1/2 cupRestaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc ChamDrizzle generously and serve extra on the side.

Required Components

1 batchRestaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc ChamBright, sweet-salty sauce that marinates the chicken and finishes the bowl.
1 batchAsian Pickled Garden VegetablesAcidic garden crunch that keeps the bowl fresh, lively, and balanced.

Plating & Serving Architecture

Per bowl: serve half the pasta base, half the sliced lemongrass chicken, about 1 cup napa cabbage, 1/2 cup cucumber, 1/2 cup zucchini, a scatter of pickled vegetables, crushed cashews, and generous ginger nuoc cham.

Visual build: start with pasta as the soft base. Fan or pile the chicken across the center, arrange fresh vegetables around it, scatter pickles where the color is visible, then finish with cashews and glossy sauce.

Eating experience: the bowl should feel cool, fresh, bright, crunchy, juicy, and aromatic. It should eat like a restaurant noodle bowl: refreshing but still satisfying.

Instructions

  1. Combine chicken thighs, ginger nuoc cham, lemongrass paste, minced garlic, and neutral oil. Toss until evenly coated.
  2. Cover and marinate 2-4 hours.
  3. Cook the chicken until browned and cooked through. Rest 5-10 minutes, then slice thinly.
  4. Cook the angel hair pasta until tender. Drain, rinse or cool slightly, and set aside.
  5. Prep napa cabbage, cucumber, and zucchini.
  6. Build 2 bowls by dividing pasta first, then layering sliced chicken over the base.
  7. Add napa cabbage, cucumber, and zucchini around or over the chicken.
  8. Scatter Asian pickled vegetables over the top, then finish with crushed cashews.
  9. Drizzle generously with ginger nuoc cham and serve extra sauce on the side.

Chef's Notes

This bowl is built around reusable Flavorweaver components: lemongrass chicken, ginger nuoc cham, pickled vegetables, and optional peanut sauce.

The final assembly should stay flexible: more vegetables for freshness, more pasta for heartiness, more pickles for brightness, or extra sauce if you want it more juicy and punchy.
