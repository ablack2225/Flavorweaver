# Loaded Moroccan Harissa Falafel Naan Plate

> Crispy deep-fried harissa falafel balls served over golden cilantro couscous with jammy apricot-orange preserved lemon sauce, green olives, preserved lemon cilantro sauce, sweet-spicy cumin cashews, and warm naan for scooping and folding.

## Flavor Profile

This plate is built around contrast: hot crispy falafel, jammy apricot-orange preserved lemon sauce, briny green olives, golden couscous, fresh cilantro sauce, sweet-spicy cashews, and warm naan. The goal is bold, spicy, sweet, tangy, salty, savory, fresh, and scoopable.

Target bite:

> warm naan + golden couscous + crispy harissa falafel + apricot-orange preserved lemon sauce + cilantro sauce + sweet-spicy cashew crunch.

## Measured Ingredients by Component

### Moroccan Harissa Falafel Balls

#### Chickpea Soak

- 1 lb dried chickpeas
- Cold water, enough to cover by 3 to 4 inches
- 1/2 tsp baking soda, optional

#### Falafel Mixture

- Soaked, drained, well-dried chickpeas
- 1 small onion, roughly chopped
- 4 scallions, white and light green parts
- 5 garlic cloves
- 1 small carrot, peeled and roughly chopped
- 1/2 packed cup parsley leaves and tender stems
- 1 1/2 packed cups cilantro leaves and tender stems
- 2 Tbsp fresh mint
- 2 tsp preserved lemon rind, finely minced
- 2 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 1 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
- Tiny pinch cinnamon or allspice
- 1/2 tsp cayenne, plus more to taste
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 Tbsp baking powder
- 2 Tbsp chickpea flour to start; up to 4 Tbsp total as needed
- All-purpose flour only as backup if chickpea flour is unavailable
- 1 to 3 Tbsp cold water, only if mixture is dry/crumbly and will not hold
- Neutral oil, for deep frying

#### Falafel Binder Rule

- Use **chickpea flour first**. It matches the falafel flavor and keeps the texture cleaner.
- Start with **2 Tbsp chickpea flour** after grinding and seasoning.
- After chilling, test shape one ball. If the mixture feels loose, wet, or breaks in the fryer, add **1 Tbsp more chickpea flour at a time**, up to **4 Tbsp total**.
- Use **all-purpose flour only as backup** if chickpea flour is unavailable.
- Add water only for the opposite problem: if the mixture is too dry or crumbly to hold together.

### Jammy Apricot-Orange Preserved Lemon Sauce

- 1 very large or 2 medium yellow onions, thinly sliced
- 1 Tbsp olive oil
- 1 Tbsp butter
- Pinch kosher salt
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 Tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste
- 1 cup chicken broth or vegetable broth, divided
- 1/2 cup dried apricots, chopped small
- 1 preserved lemon quarter, pulp scraped away and rind minced
- 1/3 cup green olives, halved
- 1 Tbsp orange marmalade
- 1 tsp honey
- 1/2 tsp soy sauce or 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp coriander
- 1/2 tsp paprika
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- 1/8 tsp cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp cayenne or red pepper flakes
- 1 tsp preserved lemon brine or lemon juice, plus more to taste

### Preserved Lemon Cilantro Sauce

- 1 packed cup cilantro
- 2 Tbsp fresh mint
- 1 small garlic clove
- 1 tsp preserved lemon rind, minced
- 1 Tbsp lemon juice or preserved lemon brine
- 3 Tbsp olive oil
- 1 Tbsp water, plus more as needed
- 1/4 tsp cumin
- 1/8 tsp coriander
- Pinch cayenne
- 1/4 tsp kosher salt, plus more to taste
- 1/2 tsp honey or orange marmalade, only if too sharp

### Golden Cilantro Couscous

- 1 cup couscous
- 1 cup chicken broth or vegetable broth
- 1 Tbsp butter
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- Tiny pinch cumin
- 1 to 2 tsp preserved lemon rind, minced
- 2 to 3 Tbsp cilantro, chopped
- Salt to taste

### Sweet-Spicy Cumin Cashews

- 1/3 cup cashews, roughly chopped
- 1 tsp butter or olive oil
- 1/4 tsp cumin
- 1/8 tsp coriander
- Pinch cayenne
- 1/2 tsp brown sugar
- 1/4 tsp kosher salt

### Service

- 4 to 6 warm naan, cut into wedges or left whole for folding
- Extra cilantro and mint
- Lemon wedges
- Extra preserved lemon rind
- Optional pickled onions

## Do Not Forget

- Do not cook the chickpeas.
- Do not use canned chickpeas for this version.
- Dry chickpeas well before grinding.
- Grind the falafel mixture twice.
- Use **chickpea flour first** for binder; all-purpose flour is backup only.
- Start with **2 Tbsp chickpea flour**, then add **1 Tbsp more at a time** only if the chilled mixture is loose or breaks during the test fry.
- Add cold water only if the mixture is too dry/crumbly to hold.
- Chill the falafel mixture 45 to 60 minutes before shaping.
- Fry one test falafel before frying the full batch.
- Keep oil at 350°F.
- Falafel should be balls, not patties: about 1 heaping tablespoon each.
- The apricot-orange sauce should be glossy and spoonable, not watery.
- The cilantro sauce should be punchy and fresh, not mild.
- Warm naan right before service.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Soak chickpeas.** Place **1 lb dried chickpeas** in a large bowl. Cover with cold water by **3 to 4 inches** and add **1/2 tsp baking soda** if using. Refrigerate **18 to 24 hours**. Do not cook.
2. **Drain and dry.** Drain, rinse, and spread chickpeas on towels. Dry **30 to 60 minutes**.
3. **Grind falafel.** Grind chickpeas with onion, scallions, garlic, carrot, parsley, cilantro, mint, and preserved lemon rind. Grind twice for a cohesive, coarse mixture.
4. **Season falafel.** Mix in cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cardamom, cinnamon/allspice, cayenne, black pepper, salt, baking powder, and **2 Tbsp chickpea flour**. Use all-purpose flour only if chickpea flour is unavailable. Add more chickpea flour only if the mixture is wet or loose. Add cold water only if too crumbly to hold.
5. **Chill.** Cover falafel mixture and chill **45 to 60 minutes**.
6. **Start sauce.** Pour **1/2 cup hot broth** over chopped apricots. Set aside. Cook onions with olive oil, butter, and salt over medium heat until collapsed, golden, and jammy, **15 to 20 minutes**.
7. **Build sauce base.** Add garlic and ginger; cook **45 to 60 seconds**. Add sauce spices; bloom **30 seconds**. Add tomato paste; cook **60 to 90 seconds** until darker.
8. **Simmer sauce.** Add softened apricots with broth, remaining **1/2 cup broth**, preserved lemon rind, marmalade, honey, and soy or Worcestershire. Simmer **10 to 15 minutes** until glossy. Add olives near the end. Balance with preserved lemon brine or lemon juice.
9. **Blend cilantro sauce.** Blend cilantro, mint, garlic, preserved lemon, lemon juice/brine, olive oil, water, cumin, coriander, cayenne, and salt. Taste. It should be bright, salty, tangy, and fresh. Add honey or marmalade only if too sharp.
10. **Toast cashews.** Toast cashews with butter/oil, cumin, coriander, cayenne, brown sugar, and salt for **2 to 4 minutes**. Remove before scorching.
11. **Shape falafel balls.** Roll into balls about **1 heaping tablespoon each**. Keep them compact but not crushed.
12. **Heat oil.** Heat deep-frying oil to **350°F**.
13. **Test fry.** Fry one falafel. If it falls apart, add **1 Tbsp chickpea flour** and chill briefly. Repeat only if needed, up to **4 Tbsp total chickpea flour**. If it tastes flat, add salt/cayenne. If it tastes heavy, make the balls slightly smaller.
14. **Fry batch.** Fry falafel in small batches at **350°F** until deep golden and crisp, about **3 to 5 minutes** depending on size. Drain and salt lightly while hot.
15. **Make couscous.** Bring broth with butter, turmeric, cumin, and salt to a boil. Pour over couscous, cover **5 minutes**, fluff, then fold in preserved lemon and cilantro.
16. **Warm naan.** Warm naan until soft and flexible.
17. **Plate.** Add a smaller mound of golden couscous. Spoon jammy apricot-orange sauce around and over it. Add hot falafel balls. Drizzle with preserved lemon cilantro sauce. Scatter sweet-spicy cashews, herbs, and preserved lemon. Serve warm naan on the side for scooping and folding.

## Sauce Balance Check

| Need | Correct With |
|---|---|
| More sour/tangy | Preserved lemon brine, lemon juice, tiny vinegar splash. |
| More sweet | Orange marmalade or honey, 1/2 tsp at a time. |
| More salty | Kosher salt, olive brine, or a few drops soy. |
| More savory | Reduce sauce longer, add tiny Worcestershire, or add a small knob butter. |
| More spicy | Cayenne, red pepper flakes, or hot sauce. |
| More fresh | More cilantro sauce, mint, lemon, or fresh herbs. |

## Pairs Well With

- Warm naan or flatbread for scooping.
- Pickled onions for extra tang.
- Extra preserved lemon cilantro sauce at the table.
- Lemon wedges.
