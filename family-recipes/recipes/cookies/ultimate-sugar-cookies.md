# Ultimate Sugar Cookies

> A preserved family cutout sugar cookie recipe from Sue Wallesch (Mom / Goga), made with butter flavor Crisco, light corn syrup, vanilla, and a chilled dough rolled between waxed paper.

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Preheat oven to 375°F. |
| 2 | Combine sugar and Crisco in a large bowl. |
| 3 | Beat at medium speed until well blended. |
| 4 | Add eggs, corn syrup, and vanilla; beat until well blended and fluffy. |
| 5 | Combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. |
| 6 | Add dry ingredients gradually to the creamed mixture until all dry ingredients are blended in. |
| 7 | Divide dough into 4 quarters. |
| 8 | Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. |
| 9 | Spread a small amount of flour on waxed paper. |
| 10 | Place one fourth of the dough on the floured waxed paper and flatten slightly with hands. |
| 11 | Turn dough over and cover with another large sheet of waxed paper. |
| 12 | Roll dough to 1/4-inch thickness. |
| 13 | Cut out cookies with floured cookie cutters. |
| 14 | Transfer cookies to ungreased cookie sheets with a large pancake turner. |
| 15 | Place cookies 2 inches apart. |
| 16 | Sprinkle with granulated sugar or colored sugar crystals, or leave plain to frost after cooling. |
| 17 | Bake for 5-9 minutes, depending on the size of the cookies. Smaller, thinner cookies bake faster than larger, thicker cookies. Do not overbake. |
| 18 | Cool on cookie sheets for 2 minutes. |
| 19 | Remove to a sheet of aluminum foil to cool completely before frosting. |

## Notes

This recipe is especially important to the family because there are many memories of sitting at the bar making these cookies with Sue Wallesch — Mom / Goga.

The recipe is preserved with Sue's family labels because those names matter on this one.

The original recipe gives a 5-9 minute bake range depending on cookie size and thickness. Smaller, thinner cookies bake faster than larger, thicker cookies. Do not overbake.
