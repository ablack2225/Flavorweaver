> A preserved non-edible craft recipe Sue used for making Christmas ornaments. Also known as bread dough or clay dough.

## Materials

| Amount | Material | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 4 cups | Flour |  |
| 1 cup | Salt |  |
| 1 1/2 cups | Water | Add slowly; additional water may be needed depending on humidity |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | In a large, flat-bottomed bowl, mix flour and salt with a rubber spatula or wooden spoon. |
| 2 | Add water slowly, mixing as you pour. |
| 3 | Add additional water only if needed, depending on humidity. Use caution because the dough should be very stiff for best results. |
| 4 | Knead for 10 minutes. The dough is ready to use when the texture appears soft and smooth. |
| 5 | Shape dough into ornaments or other cookie-style creations. |
| 6 | Bake finished creations in a 325-350°F oven, never hotter. |
| 7 | Baking time depends on the thickness of the dough. Usually allow about 30 minutes baking time per 1/4-inch thickness. Average baking time is about 1 hour. |
