Aloo Pakora-Inspired Potato Fritters

Golden potato slices in a spiced AP-cornstarch batter with ginger-garlic, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, thyme, and crushed cumin seed.

Meal TypeSide Dish
Total40 min
Serves2 to 4

Kitchen Execution Summary

Potato Slices

Slice potatoes about 1/8 inch thick so they cook through before the coating gets too dark.

Spiced Batter

AP flour and cornstarch make the coating. Ginger-garlic, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, thyme, and cumin season the batter.

Hot Oil Finish

Stir 1 tsp hot frying oil into the batter once the oil is heated to help the coating fry crisp.

Serve Hot

Finish with salt, a tiny pinch of garam masala and cumin, and optional citrus right before serving.

Ingredient Roll-Up

Potatoes

2 medium potatoes and cold water for soaking.

Batter

1/2 cup AP flour, 3 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/4 tsp garam masala, 1/4 tsp paprika/chili powder, 1/8 tsp turmeric, 1/8 tsp dried thyme, 1/8 tsp crushed cumin seed, optional cayenne, about 1/3 cup water, and 1 tsp hot frying oil.

Frying

Neutral oil, enough for shallow or deep frying.

Finish

Salt, tiny pinch garam masala, tiny pinch cumin, optional tiny squeeze lemon or lime.

Measured Ingredients

Potatoes

2 medium potatoes, sliced into 1/8-inch rounds; cold water for soaking.

Pantry Batter

1/2 cup AP flour; 3 Tbsp cornstarch; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste; 1/2 tsp ground coriander; 1/4 tsp garam masala; 1/4 tsp paprika or chili powder; 1/8 tsp turmeric; 1/8 tsp dried thyme; 1/8 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed; optional pinch cayenne; about 1/3 cup water; 1 tsp hot frying oil.

Frying and Finish

Neutral oil for frying; salt; tiny pinch garam masala; tiny pinch cumin; optional tiny squeeze lemon or lime.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Potato Bowls

Bowl 1 — Sliced Potatoes

2 medium potatoes, sliced about 1/8 inch thick.

Bowl 2 — Soak Water

Cold water, as needed. Soak potato slices 10 to 15 minutes, then drain.

Bowl 3 — Dry Potatoes

Drained potato slices plus clean towel or paper towels. Dry slices very well so batter clings.

Batter and Fry Bowls

Bowl 1 — Dry Batter

1/2 cup AP flour, 3 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp ground coriander, 1/4 tsp garam masala, 1/4 tsp paprika or chili powder, 1/8 tsp turmeric, 1/8 tsp dried thyme, 1/8 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed, optional pinch cayenne.

Bowl 2 — Wet Additions

1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste and 1/3 cup water. Keep extra water nearby to adjust by teaspoons.

Bowl 3 — Hot Oil Addition

1 tsp hot frying oil, stirred into batter once oil is hot.

Bowl 4 — Finish

Salt to taste, tiny pinch garam masala, tiny pinch cumin, optional tiny squeeze lemon or lime.

Do Not Forget

Slice potatoes about 1/8 inch thick.

Soak potatoes 10 to 15 minutes, then dry very well.

Batter should be medium-thick and cling in a thin layer.

If the coating tastes bready, thin the batter slightly.

If the coating slides off, add 1 tsp AP flour plus 1 tsp cornstarch.

Add 1 tsp hot frying oil to the batter after the oil heats.

Fry in batches and do not overcrowd.

Serve hot.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Slice and soak potatoes. Slice 2 medium potatoes into 1/8-inch rounds. Soak in cold water 10 to 15 minutes. Drain and dry very well.
  2. Mix dry batter. In a bowl, whisk together 1/2 cup AP flour, 3 Tbsp cornstarch, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp ground coriander, 1/4 tsp garam masala, 1/4 tsp paprika or chili powder, 1/8 tsp turmeric, 1/8 tsp dried thyme, 1/8 tsp lightly crushed cumin seed, and optional pinch cayenne.
  3. Add wet ingredients. Add 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste and 1/3 cup water. Whisk smooth. Add more water 1 tsp at a time until the batter coats potato slices in a thin layer.
  4. Heat oil. Heat neutral oil to about 350°F. If not using a thermometer, drop in a tiny bit of batter; it should rise steadily and bubble without turning dark immediately.
  5. Add hot oil to batter. Stir 1 tsp hot frying oil into the batter.
  6. Fry potatoes. Dip potato slices into batter one at a time. Let excess drip off briefly. Fry in batches until golden and crisp, about 3 to 5 minutes per batch, flipping as needed.
  7. Drain and finish. Drain on a rack or paper towels. While hot, sprinkle lightly with salt, a tiny pinch garam masala, and a tiny pinch cumin. Add a tiny squeeze lemon or lime right before serving if desired.

Final Taste Target

The finished fritters should be golden and crisp outside, tender inside, savory and gently spiced, with enough crunch to contrast creamy curries.
