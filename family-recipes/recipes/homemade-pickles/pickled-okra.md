# Pickled Okra

> A preserved family pickled okra recipe made with fresh okra, garlic, hot pepper, dill seed, white vinegar, water, and pickling salt.

## Ingredient List

| Component | Ingredients |
|---|---|

| Pickling Solution | White vinegar, water, pickling salt |

## Measured Ingredients

### Pickled Okra

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 clove per jar | Fresh garlic | Place in bottom of each jar |
| 1 per jar | Hot pepper | Place in bottom of each jar |
| Enough to fill jars | Fresh okra | Use small, tender pods |
| 1 tsp per jar | Dill seed |  |
| 1 quart | White vinegar |  |
| 1 cup | Water |  |
| 1/2 cup | Pickling salt |  |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Place garlic and hot pepper in the bottom of clean, hot pint-size jars. |
| 2 | Pack jars firmly with clean, young okra pods from which only part of the stem has been removed. Stem end must be open. |
| 3 | Add dill seed to each jar. |
| 4 | After packing the jars, mix vinegar, water, and pickling salt together in a saucepan. |
| 5 | Bring mixture to a boil and allow to simmer about 5 minutes. |
| 6 | Pour the vinegar mixture over the okra. |
| 7 | Seal jars immediately. |
