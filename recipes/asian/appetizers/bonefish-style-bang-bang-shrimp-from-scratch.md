# Bonefish-Style Bang Bang Shrimp — From Scratch Version

## Do Not Forget

- Cool the homemade sweet chili sauce before mixing it into mayonnaise.
- Soak shrimp only **10 minutes**.
- Use cornstarch for the lightest crispy coating.
- Shake excess cornstarch off before frying.
- Fry shrimp in small batches.
- Shrimp cook fast: usually **1 to 2 minutes**.
- Toss shrimp with Bang Bang sauce right before serving.
- Do not drown the shrimp in sauce.
- Serve immediately for best texture.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Soak guajillo chiles.** Place **2 to 4 stemmed and seeded dried guajillo chiles** in a bowl. Cover with very hot water and soak **10 to 15 minutes**, until softened. Drain. |
| 2 | **Blend chili-garlic paste.** Add softened chiles, **4 garlic cloves**, **2 Tbsp vinegar**, **1 Tbsp water**, **1 tsp sugar**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and optional crushed red pepper or cayenne to a Ninja Bullet, blender, or food processor. Blend into a thick paste. Add more water **1 tsp at a time** only if needed. |
| 3 | **Make sweet chili sauce.** In a small saucepan, combine **1/2 cup water**, **1/4 cup vinegar**, **1/3 cup sugar**, **1 to 2 Tbsp chili-garlic paste**, optional garlic, optional ginger, and **1/4 tsp salt**. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook **3 to 5 minutes**, stirring until sugar dissolves. |
| 4 | **Thicken sweet chili sauce.** Mix **1 tsp cornstarch** with **1 Tbsp cold water**. Stir slurry into simmering sauce and cook **30 to 60 seconds**, until glossy and lightly thickened. Remove from heat and cool before using in Bang Bang sauce. |
| 5 | **Make buttermilk substitute.** Preferred: mix **1/2 cup evaporated milk**, **1/2 cup water**, and **1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice**. Let sit **5 to 10 minutes**. Backup: use **1/3 cup heavy cream**, **2/3 cup water**, and **1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice**. |
| 6 | **Make Bang Bang sauce.** Whisk **1/2 cup mayonnaise**, **2 to 3 Tbsp cooled sweet chili sauce**, **1 to 2 tsp chili-garlic paste**, a tiny pinch of salt, and optional tiny squeeze of lime. Taste and adjust. Keep chilled. |
| 7 | **Make optional slaw.** Toss cabbage, carrots, optional green onion, and optional cilantro. Whisk dressing ingredients, pour over vegetables, massage lightly **30 seconds**, and rest **15 to 20 minutes**. |
| 8 | **Soak shrimp.** Pat **1 lb shrimp** dry. Place shrimp in **1 cup buttermilk substitute** and soak **10 minutes only**. Do not soak too long. |
| 9 | **Prepare coating.** In a bowl, mix **1 cup cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/4 tsp black pepper**. |
| 10 | **Coat shrimp.** Remove shrimp from buttermilk and let excess drip off. Toss shrimp in cornstarch mixture until fully coated. Shake off excess and place coated shrimp on a plate or rack while oil heats. |
| 11 | **Fry shrimp.** Heat oil to **350°F to 375°F**. Fry shrimp in small batches for **1 to 2 minutes**, just until crisp, lightly golden, and cooked through. Do not overcrowd. Drain on a rack or paper towels. |
| 12 | **Sauce shrimp.** Place hot fried shrimp in a bowl. Add enough Bang Bang sauce to coat and toss gently. Do not drown the shrimp. |
| 13 | **Serve immediately.** Garnish with sliced green onions and serve over rice, beside slaw, tucked into lettuce or cabbage cups, or as an appetizer with extra sauce on the side. |

## Sauce Adjustments

| If the sauce is... | Adjust with... |
|---|---|
| Too sweet | Add a tiny squeeze of lime or a touch more chili-garlic paste. |
| Too spicy | Add more mayonnaise or a little more sweet chili sauce. |
| Too thick | Add a few drops of water or lime juice. |
| Too mild | Add more chili-garlic paste. |
| Too flat | Add a tiny pinch of salt or lime. |

## Frying Troubleshooting

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Coating is gummy | Let excess buttermilk drip off longer and shake off excess cornstarch. |
| Shrimp are greasy | Oil is too cool or the batch is crowded. |
| Shrimp are tough | They cooked too long; fry just 1 to 2 minutes. |
| Sauce makes shrimp soggy | Toss with less sauce and serve immediately. |

## Final Taste Target

The shrimp should be light, crisp, tender, and just-coated in creamy, sweet, spicy, garlicky Bang Bang sauce.

The homemade chili-garlic paste should be thick, red, garlicky, and gently earthy from guajillo chiles. The sweet chili sauce should be glossy, sweet, tangy, garlicky, and lightly spicy. The slaw should be bright, crisp, lightly softened, and acidic enough to balance the creamy shrimp sauce.
