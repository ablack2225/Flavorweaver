# Meatball Stroganoff

> A preserved family meatball stroganoff recipe made with seasoned ground beef meatballs simmered in a cream of mushroom soup, water, and sour cream sauce, then served over hot rice or noodles.

## Measured Ingredients

### Meatball Stroganoff

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 lb | Ground beef | Makes about 16 meatballs per pound |
| 1/2 medium | Onion | Chopped |
| 1 slice | Bread | Crumbled |
| 1 | Egg |  |
| To taste | Salt and pepper | 1/2-1 tsp suggested |
| To taste | Garlic powder | 1/2-1 tsp suggested |
| Small amount | Cooking oil | For browning meatballs |
| 1 can | Cream of mushroom soup |  |
| 1/3 cup | Water |  |
| 1/2 cup | Sour cream |  |
| As desired | Hot rice or noodles | For serving |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Mix ground beef, onion, salt, pepper, garlic powder, egg, and bread crumbs together. |
| 2 | Form into meatballs, about 16 per pound of meat. |
| 3 | Brown meatballs in a small amount of cooking oil in a large skillet. |
| 4 | Drain off excess fat. |
| 5 | Mix cream of mushroom soup, water, and sour cream together. |
| 6 | Pour sauce mixture over the meatballs. |
| 7 | Reduce to a simmer and cook uncovered about 20 minutes. |
| 8 | Serve over hot rice or noodles. |

## Notes

This recipe is preserved as a separate family stroganoff from Hamburger Stroganoff and Chicken Stroganoff.

The original recipe notes about 16 meatballs per pound of meat and gives the option to serve over hot rice or noodles.
