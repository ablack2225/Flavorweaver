## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Mix the berbere-style spice blend and store airtight. Go light with fenugreek and clove because they can dominate quickly. |
| 2 | Mix the chicken marinade ingredients into a paste, coat the chicken thighs thoroughly, cover, and refrigerate overnight. |
| 3 | Hard-boil the eggs, cool in an ice bath, peel, and lightly score or prick each egg so the sauce can cling and stain them. |
| 4 | For the doro wat-style sauce, cook the diced onion over medium heat for 10-15 minutes until softened, reduced, and starting to turn golden. Add tiny splashes of water if needed to prevent sticking. |
| 5 | Add butter or oil, then bloom the berbere-style spice blend for 30-60 seconds, stirring constantly. Do not burn the spices. |
| 6 | Add garlic, ginger, and tomato paste. Cook 1-2 minutes until fragrant and deepened. |
| 7 | Add the marinated chicken thighs and sear lightly for 2-3 minutes per side so the spice paste clings. |
| 8 | Add 1 cup chicken broth and bay leaf if using. Simmer covered on low for 25-35 minutes, adding broth as needed, until the chicken is tender and cooked through. |
| 9 | During the last 10-15 minutes, nestle the scored eggs into the sauce. Spoon sauce over them and turn gently so they stain evenly. |
| 10 | Uncover and simmer 5-10 minutes until the sauce thickens and clings. Adjust with salt, black pepper, lemon, or a tiny pinch of sugar if needed. |
| 11 | Prepare the split pea alicha, atakilt wat, timatim salad, and naan or injera according to their linked recipes. |
| 12 | Assemble each plate with naan or injera, doro wat-style chicken, 2 eggs per person, split pea alicha, atakilt wat, and timatim salad. Spoon extra sauce over the chicken and eggs. |

## Chef's Notes

This is intentionally labeled Ethiopian-inspired because the first working version uses practical substitutions for ingredients that can be expensive, delayed, or hard to source in the U.S.

The working platter uses [Split Pea Alicha](../sides/split-pea-alicha.md) as the repeatable side because yellow split peas are accessible, creamy, mild, and a good counterpoint to the spicy doro wat-style chicken. Red lentil Misir Wat remains the classic/traditional direction and should be linked later as the red-lentil version when that component recipe is created.

Naan is a practical scoop-bread substitute for this first version. Replace with Amy's adapted Indian naan recipe once tested and written cleanly. For the traditional-style Ethiopian version, use [Teff Starter & Real Injera](../breads/teff-starter-and-real-injera.md).

Because the chicken thighs are small, this version includes 4 eggs total, or 2 eggs per person, so the plate still feels generous.
