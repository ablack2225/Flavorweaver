## Measured Ingredients

### Banana Date Bars

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 3 medium | Ripe bananas | Peeled; mash 1 banana and dice remaining 2 bananas |
| 1/2 cup | Margarine |  |
| 1 cup | Brown sugar | Packed |
| 2 | Eggs |  |
| 2 cups | Flour |  |
| 1 tsp | Baking soda |  |
| 1 tsp | Ground cinnamon |  |
| 1/2 tsp | Baking powder |  |
| 1/2 tsp | Ground nutmeg |  |
| 1/4 tsp | Salt |  |
| 1 cup | Chopped dates |  |
| 1 cup | Chopped nuts |  |
| As desired | Confectioners' sugar | Optional finish |

## Instructions

| Step | Action |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Preheat oven to 350°F. |
| 2 | Mash 1 banana to make about 1/2 cup mashed banana. |
| 3 | Dice the remaining 2 bananas. |
| 4 | Cream margarine with brown sugar until light and fluffy. |
| 5 | Beat in eggs. |
| 6 | Beat in mashed banana. |
| 7 | Combine dry ingredients and beat into creamed mixture until blended. |
| 8 | Fold in chopped dates, chopped nuts, and diced bananas. |
| 9 | Pour batter into a greased 13x9x2-inch baking pan. |
| 10 | Bake for 25 minutes. |
| 11 | Cool in pan. |
| 12 | Sprinkle with confectioners' sugar, if desired. |
| 13 | Cut into bars. |

## Notes

Original family note: dates and nuts are perfect time-honored complements to the mellow flavor of ripe bananas in banana date bars. These dense, chewy morsels are best enjoyed in bite-sized treats. This is a good way to use bananas that sometimes get too ripe to eat. Delicious and easy to prepare.

The printed category is Cookies. Because this recipe is prepared as bars in a 13x9 pan, it is also tagged and categorized as Bars for browsing.
