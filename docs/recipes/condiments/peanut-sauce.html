Peanut Dipping Sauce

A creamy peanut dipping sauce built with a generous amount of ginger nuoc cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime.

Prep10 min
Cook0 min
Total10 min
YieldAbout 1 to 1 1/4 cups

Kitchen Execution Summary

Sauce Style

A flexible one-bowl peanut dipping sauce that can be kept thick for dipping or thinned for drizzling.

Required Base

Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham provides the sweet-salty acidic backbone. Use enough for it to come through against the peanut butter.

Best Use

Primary dipping sauce for fresh spring rolls and an optional drizzle for bowls or grilled chicken.

Texture Target

Creamy, smooth, glossy, and dippable. Add warm water slowly only after the flavor balance is right.

Required Components

Peanut Base

Creamy peanut butter gives the sauce its body and richness.

Seasoning Base

Ginger Nuoc Cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime create the sweet-salty-bright balance.

Texture Adjustment

Warm water is added 1 tablespoon at a time only if the sauce needs thinning after the Ginger Nuoc Cham is balanced.

Optional Finish

Sesame oil adds deeper richness; fresh chile adds heat.

Full Sauce Ingredient Roll-Up

One-Bowl Sauce

1/2 cup creamy peanut butter, 1/4 cup to 1/2 cup Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham, 1 Tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 minced garlic clove, 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste, juice from 1/2 lime, and 1 to 3 Tbsp warm water only as needed.

Optional Additions

Optional 1/2 tsp sesame oil for deeper richness and optional minced fresh chile for heat.

Cross-Component Bulk Prep Map

Ginger: grate or measure 1 tsp ginger.

Citrus: juice 1/2 lime.

Stage-Based Prep Bowl

One Bowl — Peanut Sauce

1/2 cup creamy peanut butter, 1/4 cup to 1/2 cup Ginger Nuoc Cham, 1 Tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 minced garlic clove, 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste, juice from 1/2 lime, and 1 to 3 Tbsp warm water only as needed.

Optional Finish

Add optional 1/2 tsp sesame oil or minced fresh chile only after the base sauce is smooth.

Do Not Forget

This is a one-bowl sauce. Do not over-stage it.

Use enough Ginger Nuoc Cham for the sweet-salty-bright flavor to come through the peanut butter.

Add warm water 1 Tbsp at a time only after the flavor is balanced, so the sauce stays thick enough for dipping.

Use less water for a thicker dipping sauce or more water for a drizzleable bowl sauce.

Full Method and Instructions

  1. Combine the sauce base. In one bowl, whisk 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter, 1/4 cup Ginger Nuoc Cham, 1 Tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 minced garlic clove, 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste, and juice from 1/2 lime until combined.
  2. Brighten if needed. Taste the sauce. Add more Ginger Nuoc Cham, up to 1/2 cup total, until the peanut sauce tastes sweet-salty-bright enough.
  3. Thin gradually. Add 1 to 3 Tbsp warm water, 1 Tbsp at a time, whisking after each addition until smooth and dippable.
  4. Add optional finish. Add optional 1/2 tsp sesame oil or minced fresh chile if desired.
  5. Adjust only if needed. Taste and adjust lime, water, heat, or saltiness as needed.

Final Taste Target

The sauce should be creamy, smooth, nutty, sweet-salty, and bright enough that the Ginger Nuoc Cham is clearly present through the peanut butter. It should be thick enough to cling to fresh spring rolls without overpowering them.

Chef's Notes

This sauce is meant to be flexible. Use less water for a thicker dipping sauce or more water for a drizzleable bowl sauce.

Live test correction: 2 Tbsp Ginger Nuoc Cham was too quiet against 1/2 cup peanut butter. Start with 1/4 cup and increase toward 1/2 cup until the sauce tastes balanced.
