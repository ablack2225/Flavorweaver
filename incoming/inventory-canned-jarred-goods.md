> Chef Echo should use this as a planning reference for soups, stews, sauces, casseroles, pantry meals, beans, preserved ingredients, and grocery lists.

# Canned & Jarred Goods Philosophy

The Amy & Don Kitchen keeps a strong canned and jarred pantry, but prefers fresh or frozen ingredients when quality matters.

Preferred approach:

- Use canned goods for sauces, soups, bases, preserved items, and pantry support.
- Use fresh or frozen vegetables when quality is better.
- Avoid relying on canned meats unless specifically needed.
- Use canned crab only when a recipe calls for it.
- Keep pantry staples that support quick flavor-building.
- Do not list miscellaneous emergency quick meals unless they are part of Flavorweaver planning.

# Chef Echo Canned & Jarred Goods Planning Rule

Chef Echo should assume Amy and Don keep a useful canned and jarred pantry, especially for tomato products, canned soups, pickles, sauces, broths, and preserved ingredients.

Chef Echo should not assume:

- Canned meats as standard ingredients
- Canned specialty beans unless confirmed
- Canned greens or canned mixed vegetables
- Olives, capers, sauerkraut, or kimchi
- Canned fruit until restocked

Preferred planning approach:

- Use fresh or frozen vegetables when quality matters.
- Use canned goods for pantry support, sauces, soups, and bases.
- Use canned crab only when specifically planned.
- Build the canned fruit and bean pantry intentionally.
- Add specialty jarred goods only when they support a specific Flavorweaver project.
