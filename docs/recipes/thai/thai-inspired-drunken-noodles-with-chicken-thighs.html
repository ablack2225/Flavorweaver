Thai-Inspired Drunken Noodles with Chicken Thighs

Wide rice noodles, juicy velveted chicken thighs, garlic, bell pepper, green onion, Thai basil, and a dark glossy stir-fry sauce.

StatusFlavorweaver Working Recipe
Total45-55 min
Serves3-4
HeatMedium-hot to bold
Thai-Inspireddrunken noodleswide rice noodleschicken thighsThai basilfirst live test

Kitchen Execution Summary

Wide Rice Noodles

Soft, chewy, glossy noodles with lightly charred edges.

Chicken Thighs

Quick velveted chicken that stays juicy under high heat.

Dark Glossy Sauce

Oyster sauce, soy, fish sauce, sweetness, and pepper cling to the noodles.

Thai Basil + Green Onion

Fresh, fragrant finish added at the end.

Measured Ingredients

Chicken + Quick Velvet Marinade

1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, sliced thin; 1 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 tsp fish sauce; 1 tsp cornstarch; 1 tsp neutral oil; 1/2 tsp sugar; 1/4 tsp black or white pepper.

Noodles + Vegetables

8 oz dry wide rice noodles; 2-3 Tbsp neutral oil; 4-6 cloves garlic, minced; 2-4 Thai chiles, chopped; 1/2 large onion, sliced; 1 bell pepper, sliced; 3-4 green onions; 1 packed cup Thai basil leaves; 1 lime.

Dark Glossy Sauce

2 Tbsp oyster sauce; 1 Tbsp soy sauce; 1 Tbsp dark soy sauce; 1 Tbsp fish sauce; 1 Tbsp brown sugar or honey; 1 Tbsp water or chicken broth; optional 1 tsp rice vinegar or lime juice; 1/2 tsp white or black pepper.

Cooking Timeline

  1. Prepare noodles according to package direction, stopping just shy of fully soft.
  2. Mix sauce completely before turning on the heat.
  3. Toss sliced chicken with marinade ingredients and rest 20-30 minutes.
  4. Sear chicken in hot oil in a single layer; remove chicken.
  5. Stir-fry garlic and Thai chiles briefly until fragrant.
  6. Add onion and bell pepper; stir-fry 1-2 minutes.
  7. Add noodles and sauce. Toss to coat, then let noodles sit briefly against the pan for char.
  8. Return chicken and toss until hot and glossy.
  9. Turn off heat. Fold in green onions and Thai basil until just wilted.
  10. Serve immediately with lime wedges.

Do Not Forget

Do not marinate the chicken overnight. Twenty to thirty minutes is the sweet spot.

Have every bowl ready before cooking. This dish is fast and high-heat.

Do not make the noodles soupy. Sauce should cling and gloss.

Thai basil and green onion go in at the end, not early.

First Live-Test Notes to Capture

Did the noodles char without breaking?

Was the sauce dark and glossy enough?

Did the chicken stay juicy after the quick velvet marinade?

Was the Thai basil amount enough, or should it be heavier?
