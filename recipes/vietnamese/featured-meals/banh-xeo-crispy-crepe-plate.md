# Banh Xeo Crispy Crepe Plate

> Crispy Vietnamese-inspired rice crepes filled with shrimp, **1/8-inch pork loin strips**, scallion, and bean sprouts, served with mustard greens or lettuce wraps, mint, cilantro, Thai basil, English cucumber, Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and bright nước chấm.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Component | Role |
|---|---|
| Crispy Banh Xeo Batter | Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour, cornstarch, turmeric, coconut milk, cold club soda, scallion. Thin, crisp, lacy. |
| Shrimp + 1/8-Inch Pork Loin | Integrated directly into each crepe for savory, classic-style filling. |
| Bean Sprouts | Added to the hot crepe so they stay crisp and fresh. |
| Mustard Greens + Herb Wrap Plate | The freshness that makes bánh xèo bright instead of heavy. |
| Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables | House pickle accent; drained and used lightly in the lettuce wraps. |
| Nước Chấm | Sweet-sour-salty fish sauce dip; required. |

## Flavor Direction

The target is shatter-crisp edges, a thin golden turmeric crepe, tender shrimp, savory 1/8-inch pork loin strips, crisp bean sprouts, cold mustard greens or lettuce, fresh herbs, English cucumber, light Asian pickles, and bright nước chấm. This plate should feel sizzling, fresh, crunchy, acidic, and herb-heavy — not dense or pancake-like.

## Advance Prep

| Timing | Task |
|---|---|
| 2–24 hr ahead | Mix batter and refrigerate. Longer rest improves hydration and crispness. |
| Up to 1 day ahead | Make nước chấm and refrigerate. |
| Same day | Prep mustard greens/lettuce, herbs, English cucumber, bean sprouts, shrimp, pork loin, and Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables. |
| At service | Cook crepes one at a time and eat hot. |

## Full Plate Roll-Up

For 2 large servings, plan on **4 medium crepes**. Serve each crepe cut or torn into mustard greens or lettuce cups with herbs, cucumber, Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and nước chấm. The crepes should be cooked fresh and eaten immediately; bánh xèo loses its magic if held too long.

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

| Bowl | Contents | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bowl 1 — Batter Dry | **1 cup Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour**, **2 Tbsp cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/2 tsp sugar** | Whisk thoroughly before liquids. |
| Bowl 2 — Batter Liquids | **1/2 cup coconut milk**, **1 cup cold club soda or cold sparkling water** | Whisk into dry bowl until smooth. Rest 30 min to overnight. |
| Bowl 3 — Scallions | **2 thin-sliced scallions** | Stir into batter right before cooking. |

## Do Not Forget

- Use **regular white rice flour / Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết**, not glutinous or sweet rice flour.
- Batter should be thin like heavy cream, not pancake batter.
- Rest batter at least **30 minutes**; longer is better.
- Stir batter before every crepe because rice flour settles.
- Use enough oil to lightly fry the edges.
- Do not overload the crepe; too much filling makes it steam instead of crisp.
- Serve immediately with mustard greens/lettuce, herbs, cucumber, Asian pickles, and nước chấm.
- No peanut drizzle in the main build; nước chấm is the sauce identity.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Make batter.** In Bowl 1, whisk **1 cup Bột Tẻ Tinh Khiết / fine white rice flour**, **2 Tbsp cornstarch**, **1/2 tsp turmeric**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, and **1/2 tsp sugar**. Whisk in Bowl 2: **1/2 cup coconut milk** and **1 cup cold club soda or cold sparkling water** until smooth.
2. **Rest batter.** Cover batter and rest at least **30 minutes**, or refrigerate up to **24 hours**. After resting, stir in Bowl 3: **2 thin-sliced scallions**. Stir well before each crepe.
3. **Make nước chấm.** In Bowl 7, stir **3 Tbsp warm water** with **2 Tbsp sugar** until dissolved. Add **2 Tbsp fish sauce**, **2 Tbsp lime juice**, **1 Tbsp rice vinegar**, **1 minced/grated garlic clove**, and chile to taste. Taste: it should be sweet-salty-sour and bright.
4. **Prep fresh plate.** Arrange Bowl 6: **8–10 mustard green or lettuce leaves**, **1/2 cup mint**, **1/2 cup cilantro**, **1/2 cup Thai basil**, optional perilla/shiso, **1 English cucumber cut into sticks**, **1/2–3/4 cup drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables**, and lime wedges. Keep cold.
5. **Season filling.** Toss Bowl 4: **8 oz shrimp**, **8 oz pork loin cut into 1/8-inch strips**, **1/2 tsp kosher salt**, **1/4 tsp black pepper**, and **1 tsp fish sauce**. Rest **10–15 minutes**.
6. **Heat crepe pan.** Heat skillet until hot. Add **1–2 Tbsp neutral oil** and swirl. The batter should sizzle immediately when it hits the pan.
7. **Start one crepe filling.** Add a small portion of **1/2 small thin-sliced onion** and a few **1/8-inch pork loin strips** to the hot oil. Cook **30–45 seconds**, just until the pork starts losing its raw color.
8. **Add shrimp and batter.** Add a few shrimp pieces, then immediately stir the batter and pour about **1/2 cup batter** into the hot pan. Swirl quickly into a thin **9–10 inch** crepe.
9. **Add sprouts.** Scatter one-quarter of the **1 1/2 cups bean sprouts** over one half of the crepe.
10. **Crisp.** Cook uncovered **4–6 minutes**, adjusting heat as needed, until the edges are crisp, the bottom is golden, and the center is set. If the top refuses to set, cover only **30–60 seconds**, then uncover and finish crisping.
11. **Fold.** Fold the empty half over the filling. Slide onto a rack or plate. Do not stack directly or the crepes will soften.
12. **Repeat.** Repeat with remaining batter, pork, shrimp, onion, and sprouts for **4 crepes total**, stirring batter before each one and adding fresh oil as needed.
13. **Serve immediately.** Tear pieces of hot crepe and tuck into mustard greens or lettuce with mint, cilantro, Thai basil, cucumber sticks, drained Asian Pickled Garden Vegetables, and nước chấm.

## Electric Skillet Notes

- Preheat the electric skillet around **325°F**, then adjust between **300–325°F** based on browning.
- If the batter browns before crisping, lower slightly.
- If it steams and stays soft, raise slightly and use a little more oil.
- Cook one crepe at a time unless the skillet has enough room to swirl thinly.

## Final Taste Target

The plate should be hot-cold, crisp-fresh, and sauce-bright: brittle golden crepe edges, tender shrimp and 1/8-inch pork loin, crunchy bean sprouts, cold mustard greens or lettuce, mint, cilantro, Thai basil, English cucumber, drained Asian pickles, and punchy nước chấm. The best bite is crepe + greens + herbs + cucumber + nước chấm.
