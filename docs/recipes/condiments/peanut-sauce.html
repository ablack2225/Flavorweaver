Peanut Dipping Sauce

A creamy peanut dipping sauce built with ginger nuoc cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime.

Prep10 min
Cook0 min
Total10 min
YieldAbout 1 cup
dipping saucepeanut saucevietnamese-inspiredhoisingingergarliccreamy

Kitchen Execution Summary

Sauce Style

A flexible one-bowl peanut dipping sauce that can be kept thick for dipping or thinned for drizzling.

Required Base

Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham provides the sweet-salty acidic backbone.

Best Use

Primary dipping sauce for fresh spring rolls and an optional drizzle for bowls or grilled chicken.

Texture Target

Creamy, smooth, glossy, and dippable. Add warm water slowly so it does not become too thin.

Required Components

Peanut Base

Creamy peanut butter gives the sauce its body and richness.

Seasoning Base

Ginger Nuoc Cham, hoisin, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and lime create the sweet-salty-bright balance.

Thinning Liquid

Warm water is added 1 tablespoon at a time until the sauce is smooth and dippable.

Optional Finish

Sesame oil adds deeper richness; fresh chile adds heat.

Full Sauce Ingredient Roll-Up

One-Bowl Sauce

1/2 cup creamy peanut butter, 2 Tbsp Restaurant-Style Ginger Nuoc Cham, 1 Tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 minced garlic clove, 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste, juice from 1/2 lime, and 2 to 4 Tbsp warm water.

Optional Additions

Optional 1/2 tsp sesame oil for deeper richness and optional minced fresh chile for heat.

Cross-Component Bulk Prep Map

Garlic: mince 1 garlic clove.

Ginger: grate or measure 1 tsp ginger.

Citrus: juice 1/2 lime.

Thinning liquid: warm 2 to 4 Tbsp water and add it slowly while whisking.

Optional heat: mince fresh chile only if using.

Stage-Based Prep Bowl

One Bowl — Peanut Sauce

1/2 cup creamy peanut butter, 2 Tbsp Ginger Nuoc Cham, 1 Tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 minced garlic clove, 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste, juice from 1/2 lime, and 2 to 4 Tbsp warm water added gradually.

Optional Finish

Add optional 1/2 tsp sesame oil or minced fresh chile only after the base sauce is smooth.

Do Not Forget

This is a one-bowl sauce. Do not over-stage it.

Add warm water 1 Tbsp at a time so the sauce stays thick enough for dipping.

Use less water for a thicker dipping sauce or more water for a drizzleable bowl sauce.

Full Method and Instructions

  1. Combine the sauce base. In one bowl, whisk 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter, 2 Tbsp Ginger Nuoc Cham, 1 Tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 minced garlic clove, 1 tsp grated ginger or ginger paste, and juice from 1/2 lime until combined.
  2. Thin gradually. Add 2 to 4 Tbsp warm water, 1 Tbsp at a time, whisking after each addition until smooth and dippable.
  3. Add optional finish. Add optional 1/2 tsp sesame oil or minced fresh chile if desired.
  4. Adjust only if needed. Taste and adjust lime, water, heat, or saltiness as needed.

Final Taste Target

The sauce should be creamy, smooth, nutty, sweet-salty, lightly bright from lime, and balanced enough to dip fresh spring rolls without overpowering them.

Chef's Notes

This sauce is meant to be flexible. Use less water for a thicker dipping sauce or more water for a drizzleable bowl sauce.

Because Ginger Nuoc Cham is already salty, sweet, acidic, and aromatic, taste before adding anything extra.
