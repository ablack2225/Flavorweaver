Palak Paneer

Fresh spinach curry with homemade whole-milk lemon paneer, pan-fried paneer cubes, soft onion-tomato spice base, curry powder to taste, cream, lemon, and a partial puree method that keeps the curry lush without turning it into a flat green sauce.

Measured Ingredients

Homemade Whole-Milk Paneer

1/2 gallon whole milk; 2 to 3 Tbsp lemon juice, added gradually; optional 2 Tbsp water to dilute lemon juice; cheesecloth or thin clean towel.

Pan-Fried Paneer

1 batch homemade paneer, cut into 1/2-inch slabs and bite-size pieces; 2 Tbsp ghee or unsalted butter.

Curry Spice Base

2 Tbsp ghee or unsalted butter; 1 1/2 finely chopped onions; 1 tsp fenugreek seeds; 1 tsp ground cumin; 1 tsp ground coriander; 1/2 to 1 tsp curry powder, to taste; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper.

Aromatics and Tomato

2 finely chopped garlic cloves; 2 tsp finely grated ginger; 1 sliced green chili; 2 peeled, seeded, diced tomatoes, or 3/4 cup canned crushed tomato in a hurry.

Spinach and Finish

1.4 lb fresh spinach leaves, about 9 cups tightly packed, washed well and roughly chopped; 1/4 cup water; 1/3 cup + 1 Tbsp cream; 1 Tbsp lemon juice.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Homemade Paneer Bowls

Bowl 1 — Milk

1/2 gallon whole milk. Use whole milk for richer paneer.

Bowl 2 — Lemon Juice

2 to 3 Tbsp lemon juice, optionally diluted with 2 Tbsp water. Add gradually and stop once curds separate.

Bowl 3 — Drain Setup

Cheesecloth or thin towel set over a strainer. Have ready before adding lemon juice.

Bowl 4 — Press Setup

Wrapped curds with light weight. Press until sliceable but not dry.

Paneer Fry Bowls

Bowl 1 — Cut Paneer

Homemade paneer cut into 1/2-inch slabs, then bite-size rectangles.

Bowl 2 — Fry Fat

2 Tbsp ghee or unsalted butter. Fry in batches until lightly golden.

Bowl 3 — Fried Paneer Hold

Paper towel-lined plate or rack. Hold until final assembly.

Curry Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Onion

1 1/2 finely chopped onions. Cook until softened, not deeply browned.

Bowl 2 — Dry Spices

1 tsp fenugreek seeds; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; 1/2 to 1 tsp curry powder; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper. Bloom with onion and butter.

Bowl 3 — Garlic Ginger

2 chopped garlic cloves; 2 tsp grated ginger. Add after onion softens.

Bowl 4 — Tomato Chili

2 peeled seeded diced tomatoes; 1 sliced green chili. Cook briefly before spinach.

Bowl 5 — Spinach

1.4 lb fresh spinach leaves, washed and roughly chopped. Add in batches.

Bowl 6 — Liquid Finish

1/4 cup water; 1/3 cup + 1 Tbsp cream; 1 Tbsp lemon juice.

Do Not Forget

Homemade paneer should be made with whole milk and lemon juice.

Pan-fry paneer before adding it to the curry.

Add curry powder to taste with the dry spices so it blooms in the butter or ghee.

Cook onion until softened, not deeply browned.

Add spinach in batches and stir until wilted.

Cook the spinach curry for 10 minutes before adding cream and lemon.

Puree only about half the spinach mixture, then return it to the pot for texture.

Add pan-fried paneer gently at the end so it stays intact.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Make homemade paneer if needed. Bring 1/2 gallon whole milk just to a boil, stirring so it does not scorch. Lower heat. Add 2 to 3 Tbsp lemon juice, optionally diluted with 2 Tbsp water, a little at a time until the milk separates into curds and pale whey. Turn off heat.
  2. Drain and press paneer. Pour curds into cheesecloth set over a strainer. Gather the cloth and drain. Press with a light weight until firm enough to slice. Use once cooled.
  3. Cut paneer. Cut paneer into about 1/2-inch thick slabs, then into small bite-size rectangles.
  4. Pan-fry paneer. Melt half of the 2 Tbsp ghee or butter in a nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Fry paneer in batches until lightly golden on both sides, about 1 to 2 minutes per side. Remove and hold.
  5. Start curry spice base. Melt 2 Tbsp ghee or butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add 1 1/2 finely chopped onions, 1 tsp fenugreek seeds, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 to 1 tsp curry powder, 3/4 tsp kosher salt, and 1/4 tsp black pepper. Cook about 3 minutes, until onion is softened but not golden.
  6. Add garlic and ginger. Add 2 finely chopped garlic cloves and 2 tsp grated ginger. Cook 2 minutes.
  7. Add tomato and chili. Add 2 peeled seeded diced tomatoes and 1 sliced green chili. Cook about 3 minutes over medium heat.
  8. Wilt spinach in batches. Add about one-third of the 1.4 lb fresh spinach leaves and stir until wilted. Repeat with remaining spinach. Add 1/4 cup water if needed.
  9. Cook spinach curry. Cook, stirring now and then, for 10 minutes over medium heat.
  10. Add cream and lemon. Add 1/3 cup + 1 Tbsp cream and 1 Tbsp lemon juice. Cook gently for 3 minutes. Taste and add a small pinch more curry powder only if the curry needs more warmth.
  11. Puree half. Remove about half of the spinach curry to a tall container and puree with a stick blender. Return the puree to the pot and stir to combine.
  12. Add paneer. Gently fold in the pan-fried paneer. Stir carefully so the paneer does not crumble.
  13. Serve. Serve hot over basmati rice with Fluffy Skillet Naan on the side.

Building the Final Bowl or Plate

1. Rice or Bread Base

Serve over basmati rice, or with naan for scooping.

2. Spinach Curry

Spoon the creamy spinach curry over the rice or into a shallow bowl.

3. Paneer Finish

Keep pan-fried paneer visible on top and folded through the curry.

4. Final Brightness

Add a small squeeze of lemon only if the curry needs extra lift.

Final Taste Target

The finished palak paneer should be creamy, spinach-forward, gently spiced, savory, lightly tangy, and fresh rather than heavy.

The paneer should be soft inside, lightly golden outside, and sturdy enough to stay intact when folded through the curry.
