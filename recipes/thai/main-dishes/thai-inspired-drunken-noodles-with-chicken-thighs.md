# Thai-Inspired Drunken Noodles with Chicken Thighs

> Wide rice noodles, juicy velveted chicken thighs, Thai chiles, garlic, bell pepper, green onion, Thai basil, and a dark glossy stir-fry sauce.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Wide Rice Noodles | The signature texture: soft, chewy, glossy, and lightly charred at the edges. |
| Chicken Thighs | Stay juicy under high heat and take the quick velvet marinade well. |
| Garlic + Thai Chiles | The bold aromatic backbone of the dish. |
| Dark Glossy Sauce | Oyster sauce, soy, fish sauce, sweetness, and pepper cling to the noodles. |
| Thai Basil + Green Onion | The fresh, fragrant finish that makes the dish feel alive. |

## Flavor Target

- Savory first from oyster sauce, soy sauce, and fish sauce.
- Slightly sweet from brown sugar or honey.
- Peppery-hot from Thai chiles and black or white pepper.
- Fragrant and fresh from Thai basil and green onion.
- Glossy and high-heat, not soupy.
- Noodles should have cling, shine, and a few browned edges.

## Advance Prep

| Task | Best Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Slice chicken thighs | Night before or day-of | Slice thin across the grain; store covered in fridge. |
| Marinate chicken | 20-30 minutes before cooking | Do **not** marinate overnight; the soy/fish sauce and cornstarch are meant for a quick velvet coating. |
| Mix sauce | Up to 1 day ahead | Keep covered in fridge; stir before using. |
| Prep aromatics/vegetables | Up to 1 day ahead | Keep garlic/chiles separate from onion and bell pepper if possible. |
| Noodles | Day-of only | Soak/cook just shy of done so they finish in the pan. |

## Measured Ingredients

### Chicken + Quick Velvet Marinade

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 lb | Boneless skinless chicken thighs | Slice thin. |
| 1 Tbsp | Soy sauce | Regular soy sauce. |
| 1 tsp | Fish sauce | Savory depth. |
| 1 tsp | Cornstarch | Velvet texture and sauce cling. |
| 1 tsp | Neutral oil | Helps coating and searing. |
| 1/2 tsp | Sugar | Balances salt and browning. |
| 1/4 tsp | Black or white pepper | White pepper preferred if available. |

### Noodles + Vegetables

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 8 oz | Dry wide rice noodles | Fresh wide noodles are best if available; dry wide noodles work. |
| 2-3 Tbsp | Neutral oil | Use as needed for high-heat stir-fry. |
| 4-6 cloves | Garlic, minced | Do not skimp. |
| 2-4 | Thai chiles, chopped | Use 2 for medium-hot, 3-4 for Don-hot. |
| 1/2 large | Onion, sliced | Yellow or white onion. |
| 1 | Bell pepper, sliced | Any color. |
| 3-4 | Green onions, cut in 1-inch pieces | Mandatory finish and stir-in. |
| 1 packed cup | Thai basil leaves | Add at the end. Use heavy if you have it. |
| 1 | Lime, cut into wedges | For serving. |

### Dark Glossy Sauce

| Amount | Ingredient | Notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 2 Tbsp | Oyster sauce | Main savory body. |
| 1 Tbsp | Soy sauce | Regular soy sauce. |
| 1 Tbsp | Dark soy sauce | Strongly preferred for color and gloss. If missing, use regular soy and expect lighter noodles. |
| 1 Tbsp | Fish sauce | Deep savory Thai-style backbone. |
| 1 Tbsp | Brown sugar or honey | Balances salt and heat. |
| 1 Tbsp | Water or chicken broth | Keeps sauce fluid enough to coat noodles. |
| 1 tsp | Rice vinegar or lime juice | Optional tiny lift; do not make it sour. |
| 1/2 tsp | White or black pepper | Peppery finish. |

## Stage-Based Prep Bowls

| Bowl | Contents |
|---|---|
| Bowl 1 — Chicken | Sliced chicken tossed with soy sauce, fish sauce, cornstarch, oil, sugar, and pepper. Marinate 20-30 minutes. |
| Bowl 2 — Sauce | Oyster sauce, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, fish sauce, brown sugar or honey, water/broth, optional vinegar/lime, pepper. |
| Bowl 3 — Aromatics | Minced garlic and chopped Thai chiles. |
| Bowl 4 — Vegetables | Sliced onion and bell pepper. |
| Bowl 5 — Finish | Green onions, Thai basil, lime wedges, optional extra sliced Thai chile. |
| Noodles | Soaked or cooked wide rice noodles, drained and ready before stir-frying. |

## Cooking Timeline

1. **Prepare noodles.** Prepare noodles according to package direction, stopping just shy of fully soft. Drain and keep ready. If using fresh wide noodles, gently separate them before cooking.
2. **Mix sauce.** Mix the sauce completely before turning on the heat. Drunken noodles move fast once the pan is hot.
3. **Marinate chicken.** Toss sliced chicken with marinade ingredients and rest **20-30 minutes**.
4. **Sear chicken.** Heat wok, large skillet, or electric skillet as hot as practical. Add oil and sear chicken in a single layer. Cook in batches if needed so it browns instead of steaming. Remove chicken.
5. **Bloom aromatics.** Add a little more oil if needed. Add garlic and Thai chiles and stir-fry **15-30 seconds** until fragrant; do not burn the garlic.
6. **Stir-fry vegetables.** Add onion and bell pepper. Stir-fry **1-2 minutes** so they soften slightly but keep some structure.
7. **Add noodles and sauce.** Add noodles and sauce. Toss to coat, then let the noodles sit briefly against the pan to pick up charred edges. Toss again.
8. **Return chicken.** Return chicken to the pan and toss until hot and glossy, **1-2 minutes**. Add a splash of water only if the noodles are too tight or sticking badly.
9. **Finish off heat.** Turn off heat. Fold in green onions and Thai basil until the basil just wilts.
10. **Serve.** Serve immediately with lime wedges and extra Thai chiles if desired.

## Do Not Forget

- Do **not** marinate the chicken overnight. Twenty to thirty minutes is the sweet spot.
- Have every bowl ready before cooking. This dish is fast and high-heat.
- Use wide noodles if at all possible; they are central to the dish.
- Do not make the noodles soupy. Sauce should cling and gloss.
- Let noodles sit briefly in the hot pan for char. Do not stir constantly.
- Thai basil and green onion go in at the end, not early.

## Final Balance Map

| If It Tastes... | Fix |
|---|---|
| Flat | Add a small splash fish sauce or pinch salt. |
| Too salty | Add a pinch more sugar and a squeeze of lime at the table. |
| Not glossy | Add a tiny splash water or broth and toss hard over heat. |
| Too sweet | Add a few drops fish sauce or lime. |
| Not hot enough | Add sliced Thai chile, chili oil, or chili-garlic paste at the table. |
| Too wet | Keep heat high and toss until sauce tightens onto noodles. |
| Noodles breaking | Use gentler tossing and make sure noodles are not over-soaked. |

## Plate Vision

- Serve in a shallow bowl or wide plate.
- Pile noodles high with chicken visible on top.
- Scatter extra Thai basil and green onion over the top.
- Add lime wedges and optional sliced Thai chile on the side.
- Finished dish should look dark, glossy, hot, and aromatic.

## First Live-Test Notes to Capture

- Did 2, 3, or 4 Thai chiles hit the right heat level?
- Did the noodles char without breaking?
- Was the sauce dark and glossy enough with the available soy sauce?
- Did the chicken stay juicy after the quick velvet marinade?
- Was 1 packed cup Thai basil enough, or should it be heavier?
- Did it need more sweetness, fish sauce, lime, or pepper at the table?
