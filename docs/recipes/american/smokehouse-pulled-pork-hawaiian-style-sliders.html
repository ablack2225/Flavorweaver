Smokehouse Pulled Pork Hawaiian-Style Sliders

Hall of Fame pulled pork sliders with oven-seared pork butt, pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus, sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch, dill pickles, and from-scratch Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls.

Flavor Target

Smoky, savory, pull-apart pork with sweet barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar lift, soft pineapple-butter rolls, sweet-spicy pickle brightness, and dill-pickle snap.

This should not taste like generic slow-cooker pork in bottled sauce. The difference comes from overnight seasoning, a hard sear, a real braise, reduced pork jus, a barky finish, butter-toasted roll interiors, and a required acidic pickle element.

Canon Lock Notes

Final tested result: Don called these the best bread he has ever tasted and the best sliders hands down.

Pull rolls at 190°F internal, brush hot with butter or honey butter, slice as slider buns, and butter-toast only the cut interior faces.

Fill pork generously edge-to-edge, add sauce or reduced jus directly to the meat, include dill pickles for the acid snap, then gently press and rest 1 to 2 minutes before serving.

Ultimate 10+ upgrade path: sear → smoke → braise/finish for deeper real smoke flavor.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Overnight Smokehouse Pork

Pork butt is salted, slathered, rubbed, and chilled 8 to 18 hours so the seasoning penetrates beyond the surface.

Oven Braise

A hard sear builds crust, then pineapple juice, vinegar, broth, onion, garlic, mustard, brown sugar, and Worcestershire build a tangy BBQ jus.

Optional Smoker Phase

After searing, smoke at 225°F to 250°F for 1 1/2 to 3 hours before the braise for true smoke depth.

BBQ Jus Glaze

Reduced pork cooking juices become the base for a glossy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze.

Broiled Bark Finish

Lightly sauced pulled pork is broiled so the edges turn sticky, smoky, and caramelized.

Hawaiian-Style Rolls

Pineapple juice, milk, room-temperature eggs, cooled melted butter, brown sugar, and dry potato flakes make plush sweet rolls sturdy enough for sliders.

Pickle Finish

Sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch and dill pickles cut the richness and complete the bite.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

8 to 18 hours before cooking: salt the pork, apply wet slather, apply dry rub, cover, and refrigerate.

60 to 90 minutes before roll dough: set out 2 large eggs and 1 egg yolk. Melt 4 Tbsp butter and cool until fluid but not hot.

Cook day morning: pull pork from the fridge 30 to 45 minutes before searing.

Early cook day: start Hawaiian-style roll dough so rolls can rise while pork braises.

While pork braises: make pickle-onion crunch and chill.

After pork braises: strain and skim juices, build BBQ jus glaze, shred pork, glaze lightly, and broil for barky edges.

At service: slice rolls, butter-toast cut interiors, fill edge-to-edge, add dill pickles, gently press, and rest 1 to 2 minutes.

Measured Ingredients by Component

These component sections are reference only. The cooking flow is handled by the stage-based prep bowls and full timeline below.

Overnight Pork Seasoning

4 to 5 lb pork butt or pork shoulder, bone-in or boneless; 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.

Wet Slather

2 Tbsp yellow mustard; 1 Tbsp hot sauce; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar or sweet-spicy pickle brine; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp brown sugar.

Dry Rub

1 Tbsp smoked paprika; 1 Tbsp paprika; 1 Tbsp chili powder; 2 tsp garlic powder; 2 tsp onion powder; 2 tsp mustard powder; 1 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 tsp ground coriander; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp chipotle powder or cayenne; optional 1/4 tsp ground ginger; optional tiny pinch cinnamon.

Oven Braising Base

1 large sliced onion; 6 smashed garlic cloves; 1/2 cup pineapple juice; 1/2 cup chicken broth, apple juice, or water; 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp yellow/stone-ground mustard; optional tiny dash liquid smoke only if no smoke/grill element is involved.

Pineapple-Vinegar BBQ Jus Glaze

1 cup reduced pork cooking juices; 1/2 cup tomato sauce or ketchup; 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 Tbsp honey or molasses; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp yellow mustard or stone-ground mustard; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1/2 tsp chili powder; 1/4 tsp black pepper; optional pinch cayenne; optional 1 Tbsp bourbon or whiskey.

Hawaiian-Style Sweet Slider Rolls

1/4 cup warm water at 100°F to 110°F; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1 Tbsp sugar; 1/2 cup pineapple juice, warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 1/2 cup milk, warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 2 room-temperature large eggs; 1 room-temperature egg yolk; 1/3 cup brown sugar; 4 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted and cooled until fluid but not hot; 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 2 Tbsp plain instant potato flakes; 3 3/4 cups bread flour or AP flour, starting with 3 cups and adding the rest gradually; optional 1 tsp vanilla; egg wash from 1 egg white or 1 whole egg plus 1 Tbsp water or milk; butter top from 2 Tbsp melted butter, optional 1 tsp honey, and tiny pinch salt.

Use plain instant potato flakes dry. If using fresh pineapple juice, heat it to 200°F, then cool to lukewarm before adding it to the dough. Bake to about 190°F internal.

Sweet-Spicy Pickle-Onion Crunch

1/2 cup finely chopped sweet-spicy pickles; 1/4 cup finely minced purple onion; 1 Tbsp pickle brine; 1 tsp yellow mustard or stone-ground mustard; 1 tsp honey or brown sugar; pinch black pepper; optional pinch cayenne.

Slider Assembly

12 Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls; warm pulled pork with broiled barky edges; pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus glaze; sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch; dill pickles; optional thin purple onion; optional pepper jack, cheddar, or provolone; honey butter for brushing tops.

Optional Smoker Upgrade

Complete the overnight seasoning as written, then sear the pork hard on all sides. Move pork to a smoker at 225°F to 250°F for 1 1/2 to 3 hours, until the exterior takes on real smoke aroma and color.

Transfer to the braise base and finish covered until shreddable. Continue with reduced BBQ jus glaze, pulling, barky broil finish, and slider assembly. Use apple or cherry for sweet smoke, hickory for stronger BBQ depth, or apple-hickory for the balanced house version.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Night-Before Pork Bowls

Bowl 1 — Pork Salt

4 to 5 lb pork butt or shoulder; 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.

Bowl 2 — Wet Slather

2 Tbsp yellow mustard; 1 Tbsp hot sauce; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp vinegar or pickle brine; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp brown sugar.

Bowl 3 — Dry Rub

1 Tbsp smoked paprika; 1 Tbsp paprika; 1 Tbsp chili powder; 2 tsp garlic powder; 2 tsp onion powder; 2 tsp mustard powder; 1 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 tsp coriander; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp chipotle/cayenne; optional ginger and cinnamon.

Cook-Day Pork Bowls

Bowl 4 — Braise Bed

1 large sliced onion; 6 smashed garlic cloves.

Bowl 5 — Braise Liquid

1/2 cup pineapple juice; 1/2 cup broth/apple juice/water; 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp prepared mustard; optional tiny liquid smoke only if not smoking.

Bowl 6 — BBQ Jus Glaze

1 cup reduced pork juices; 1/2 cup tomato sauce/ketchup; 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 Tbsp honey/molasses; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp mustard; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1/2 tsp chili powder; 1/4 tsp black pepper; optional cayenne and bourbon.

Roll Bowls

Bowl 7 — Yeast Bloom

1/4 cup warm water at 100°F to 110°F; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1 Tbsp sugar. Rest 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.

Bowl 8 — Warm Roll Liquids

1/2 cup pineapple juice warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 1/2 cup milk warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 1/3 cup brown sugar; 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 2 Tbsp dry potato flakes; optional 1 tsp vanilla.

Bowl 9 — Enrichment

2 room-temperature eggs; 1 room-temperature egg yolk; 4 Tbsp melted butter, cooled until fluid but not hot.

Bowl 10 — Flour

3 cups flour to start; remaining 3/4 cup held back. Add gradually only until dough is soft, tacky, and elastic.

Bowl 11 — Roll Finish

Egg wash: 1 egg white or whole egg + 1 Tbsp water/milk. Butter top: 2 Tbsp melted butter + optional 1 tsp honey + tiny pinch salt. Pull at 190°F internal.

Crunch and Assembly Bowls

Bowl 12 — Pickle-Onion Crunch

1/2 cup chopped sweet-spicy pickles; 1/4 cup minced purple onion; 1 Tbsp pickle brine; 1 tsp mustard; 1 tsp honey/brown sugar; pinch black pepper; optional cayenne.

Bowl 13 — Slider Assembly

Split rolls; warm pulled pork; BBQ jus glaze; pickle-onion crunch; dill pickles; optional cheese; honey butter.

Do Not Forget

For the roll dough, use room-temperature eggs and yolk. Cold eggs can slow the yeast and make butter seize into little pieces.

Melt the roll butter first, then cool it until it is fluid but not hot. It should not steam, feel hot to the touch, or cook the eggs.

Bloom active dry yeast in 100°F to 110°F water with sugar before adding the enriched ingredients.

Warm pineapple juice and milk to about 95°F to 105°F. Warm is good; hot is not.

Start with 3 cups flour, then add the remaining 3/4 cup gradually. Do not over-flour the dough.

Do not skip overnight pork seasoning, searing, reduced cooking juices, bark finish, butter-toasted cut sides, or dill pickles at service.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Season pork the night before. Pat 4 to 5 lb pork butt or shoulder very dry. Rub evenly with 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.
  2. Apply wet slather. Mix 2 Tbsp yellow mustard, 1 Tbsp hot sauce, 1 Tbsp Worcestershire, 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar or pickle brine, 1 Tbsp neutral oil, 1 Tbsp tomato paste, and 2 Tbsp brown sugar. Coat pork on all sides.
  3. Apply dry rub and refrigerate. Mix dry rub ingredients and coat pork heavily. Refrigerate 8 to 18 hours.
  4. Temper pork. Remove pork from the refrigerator 30 to 45 minutes before cooking.
  5. Prep roll enrichment before mixing. Set 2 large eggs and 1 egg yolk out until room temperature. Melt 4 Tbsp butter, then cool until fluid but not hot. Warm 1/2 cup pineapple juice and 1/2 cup milk to 95°F to 105°F.
  6. Bloom yeast. In the mixer bowl, combine 1/4 cup warm water at 100°F to 110°F, 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast, and 1 Tbsp sugar. Rest 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.
  7. Build the wet dough base in order. Add warm pineapple juice and milk. Whisk in 1/3 cup brown sugar, 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 2 Tbsp dry potato flakes, and optional vanilla.
  8. Add eggs and cooled butter. Whisk in eggs and yolk, then cooled melted butter.
  9. Add flour gradually and knead. Add 3 cups flour first. Knead 8 to 10 minutes, adding remaining flour only as needed.
  10. First rise. Cover and rise 1 1/2 to 2 hours, until very puffy and nearly doubled.
  11. Sear pork. Sear pork on all sides until dark mahogany, not burned black sugar.
  12. Optional smoker upgrade. Smoke seared pork at 225°F to 250°F for 1 1/2 to 3 hours, then continue with braise.
  13. Build braise. Place onion and garlic in Dutch oven or roasting pan. Add braise liquid ingredients. Place pork on top and cover tightly.
  14. Oven-braise. Bake at 300°F until fork-shreddable: about 4 1/2 to 6 hours for large hunks or 6 to 7 hours for whole bone-in pork butt.
  15. Shape rolls during braise. Divide dough into 12 equal pieces, shape tight smooth balls, cover, and rise 45 to 75 minutes.
  16. Bake rolls. Brush with egg wash. Bake at 350°F for 20 to 25 minutes, until golden and about 190°F in the center. Brush hot rolls with butter-honey top and cool at least 20 minutes.
  17. Rest pork and save juices. Rest pork 20 to 30 minutes. Strain cooking juices, skim excess fat, and reserve for glaze.
  18. Make BBQ jus glaze. Combine glaze ingredients and simmer 10 to 15 minutes until glossy and slightly thickened.
  19. Make pickle-onion crunch. Mix crunch ingredients and chill until serving.
  20. Pull pork. Shred into large tender strands. Add reduced pork juices and a small amount of BBQ jus glaze so pork is juicy and glossy.
  21. Broil for barky edges. Spread pork on a sheet pan. Broil 3 to 6 minutes, watching closely, until some edges crisp and caramelize.
  22. Toast rolls. Slice rolls like slider buns and lightly toast the cut interior faces with butter.
  23. Build sliders. Add glaze to the bottom roll, generous edge-to-edge pulled pork, pickle-onion crunch, dill pickles, optional cheese, and top roll. Brush tops with honey butter.
  24. Set the bite. Gently press each slider and rest 1 to 2 minutes before serving.

Building the Final Slider

1. Toasted Roll

Split Hawaiian-style roll and toast the cut interior faces lightly with butter.

2. BBQ Jus Base

Add a thin spoonful of pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus glaze to the bottom roll.

3. Pork Anchor

Pile on warm pulled pork with broiled barky edges, spread edge-to-edge.

4. Bright Crunch

Add sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch.

5. Dill Pickle Snap

Add dill pickle slices inside or serve dill pickle spears on the plate. This is required for final balance.

6. Honey-Butter Top

Add top roll, brush with honey butter, gently press, and rest 1 to 2 minutes.

Final Taste Target

The finished slider should hit in layers: soft buttery pineapple roll, smoky savory pork, sticky barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze, sweet-spicy pickle crunch, dill-pickle acid, warm BBQ spice, and a clean bright finish.

It should be sweet, but not candy-sweet. It should be saucy, but not wet. It should feel like barbecue met homemade sweet-roll nostalgia and became a Hall of Fame slider worth chasing again.
