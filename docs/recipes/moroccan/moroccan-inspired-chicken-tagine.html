Moroccan-Inspired Chicken Tagine with Apricot-Orange Glaze

Deeply marinated chicken thighs braised with jammy onions, preserved lemon, green olives, apricots, orange marmalade, warm spices, golden cilantro couscous, and sweet-spicy cumin cashews.

Flavor Profile

This tagine layers preserved-lemon tang, apricot-orange sweetness, green olive salt, jammy onion savoriness, cayenne warmth, tomato-spice depth, butter gloss, cilantro brightness, and sweet-spicy cashew crunch.

Target bite: marinated spiced chicken + glossy jammy onion-apricot sauce + preserved lemon tang + green olive salt + orange marmalade chile glaze + golden cilantro couscous + sweet-spicy cumin cashews.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Marinated Chicken

Chicken thighs are marinated with preserved lemon, garlic, ginger, soy, Worcestershire, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cayenne, and olive oil.

Jammy Onion Sauce

Onions cook 15–20 minutes until golden and sweet, creating a rich sauce base with body and depth.

Apricot-Orange Glaze

Orange marmalade, honey, tomato paste, preserved lemon brine, soy, cumin, cayenne, and butter create the glossy finish.

Preserved Lemon + Olives

Preserved lemon rind gives citrus depth; olives are added late for briny contrast.

Golden Couscous

Brothy turmeric couscous with butter, preserved lemon, and cilantro catches the sauce.

Spiced Cashews

Sweet-spicy cumin cashews finish the plate with crunch, salt, warmth, and contrast.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Preserved-Lemon Spiced Chicken Marinade

4 chicken thighs; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 2 grated garlic cloves; 1 Tbsp grated fresh ginger; 1 tsp minced preserved lemon rind; 1 tsp lemon juice or preserved lemon brine; 1 tsp soy sauce; 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 tsp kosher salt; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; 3/4 tsp paprika; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp black pepper; 1/4 tsp cayenne; tiny pinch cinnamon.

Jammy Onion-Apricot-Olive Tagine Sauce

1 very large or 2 medium yellow onions, thinly sliced; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 1 Tbsp butter; pinch kosher salt; 3 minced garlic cloves; 1 Tbsp grated fresh ginger; 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste; 1 cup chicken broth; 1/2 cup chopped dried apricots; 1 preserved lemon quarter, rind minced and pulp scraped away; 1/3 cup halved green olives; 1 tsp orange marmalade; optional 1 tsp soy sauce or 1/2 tsp Worcestershire for final depth.

Sauce Spice Bloom

1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika; 1/4 tsp turmeric; 1/8 tsp cinnamon; 1/4 tsp cayenne or red pepper flakes; pinch black pepper.

Apricot-Orange Marmalade Finishing Glaze

1 Tbsp orange marmalade; 1 tsp honey; 1 tsp tomato paste; 1 tsp preserved lemon brine or lemon juice; 1/2 tsp soy sauce; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/8 to 1/4 tsp cayenne; 1 Tbsp butter.

Golden Cilantro Couscous

1 cup couscous; 1 cup chicken broth; 1 Tbsp butter; 1/4 tsp turmeric; tiny pinch cumin; optional 1–2 tsp minced preserved lemon rind; 2–3 Tbsp chopped cilantro; salt to taste.

Sweet-Spicy Cumin Cashews

1/3 cup chopped cashews; 1 tsp butter or olive oil; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/8 tsp coriander; pinch cayenne; 1/2 tsp brown sugar; 1/4 tsp kosher salt.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Bowl 1 — Chicken Marinade

4 chicken thighs; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 2 grated garlic cloves; 1 Tbsp grated ginger; 1 tsp preserved lemon rind; 1 tsp lemon juice/brine; 1 tsp soy sauce; 1/2 tsp Worcestershire; 1 tsp salt; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; 3/4 tsp paprika; 1/2 tsp turmeric; 1/4 tsp pepper; 1/4 tsp cayenne; tiny pinch cinnamon.

Bowl 2 — Jammy Onion

1 very large or 2 medium thin-sliced onions with a pinch of salt.

Bowl 3 — Sauce Aromatics

3 minced garlic cloves and 1 Tbsp grated ginger.

Bowl 4 — Spice Bloom

1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp paprika; 1/4 tsp turmeric; 1/8 tsp cinnamon; 1/4 tsp cayenne; pinch black pepper.

Bowl 5 — Tomato Paste

1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste.

Bowl 6 — Apricot Broth

1/2 cup chopped dried apricots plus 1/2 cup hot chicken broth.

Bowl 7 — Sauce Build

Remaining 1/2 cup chicken broth, minced rind from 1 preserved lemon quarter, and 1 tsp orange marmalade.

Bowl 8 — Late Olives

1/3 cup halved green olives.

Bowl 9 — Finishing Glaze

1 Tbsp orange marmalade; 1 tsp honey; 1 tsp tomato paste; 1 tsp preserved lemon brine or lemon juice; 1/2 tsp soy sauce; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/8–1/4 tsp cayenne; 1 Tbsp butter.

Bowl 10 — Couscous

1 cup couscous; 1 cup broth; 1 Tbsp butter; 1/4 tsp turmeric; tiny pinch cumin; 1–2 tsp preserved lemon rind; 2–3 Tbsp cilantro.

Bowl 11 — Cashews

1/3 cup cashews; 1 tsp butter/oil; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/8 tsp coriander; pinch cayenne; 1/2 tsp brown sugar; 1/4 tsp salt.

Do Not Forget

Marinate the chicken for the deepest flavor; 2 hours is the minimum, overnight is ideal.

Cook onions 15–20 minutes until collapsed, glossy, golden, and sweet.

Use mostly preserved lemon rind, not a heavy amount of pulp.

Use soy or Worcestershire in tiny amounts for background umami. The sauce should remain warm-spiced and citrus-forward.

Add olives late so they stay bright.

Reduce sauce uncovered until glossy before plating.

Use cilantro-forward finishing.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken. Combine 4 chicken thighs with all marinade ingredients. Cover and marinate 2 hours to overnight.
  2. Temper chicken. Pull from fridge 20–30 minutes before cooking.
  3. Soften apricots. Pour 1/2 cup hot chicken broth over 1/2 cup chopped dried apricots. Let sit while onions cook.
  4. Brown chicken. Heat electric skillet around 300°F with oil. Brown chicken on both sides until golden but not cooked through. Remove.
  5. Build jammy onions. Add 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1 Tbsp butter, onions, and a pinch salt. Cook 15–20 minutes, lowering toward 275°F if needed.
  6. Add aromatics. Stir in 3 minced garlic cloves and 1 Tbsp grated ginger. Cook 45–60 seconds.
  7. Bloom spices and tomato paste. Add spice bloom and cook 30 seconds. Add 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste and cook 60–90 seconds.
  8. Build sauce. Add apricots with soaking broth, remaining 1/2 cup broth, minced preserved lemon rind, and 1 tsp orange marmalade. Scrape fond thoroughly.
  9. Braise. Return chicken and juices. Cover and simmer gently 25–35 minutes until tender.
  10. Add olives late. Add 1/3 cup halved green olives during the final 10–15 minutes.
  11. Make couscous. Pour boiling broth mixture over couscous, cover 5 minutes, fluff, then fold in preserved lemon and cilantro.
  12. Toast cashews. Toast cashews with butter/oil, cumin, coriander, cayenne, brown sugar, and salt for 2–4 minutes.
  13. Make glaze. Warm orange marmalade, honey, tomato paste, preserved lemon brine/lemon juice, soy sauce, cumin, cayenne, and butter until glossy.
  14. Reduce and glaze. Uncover tagine and reduce until spoonable and glossy. Stir in finishing glaze and taste.
  15. Correct sauce. If flat, add a few drops soy or a pinch salt. If too sweet, add preserved lemon brine or lemon juice. If too sharp, add 1/2 tsp honey or marmalade. If it needs more warmth, add cayenne and a tiny splash Worcestershire.
  16. Plate. Spoon golden cilantro couscous onto plates. Add chicken, generous glossy sauce, olives and apricots. Finish with spiced cashews, cilantro, and reserved preserved lemon rind.

Sauce Balance Check

NeedCorrect With
More sour/tangyPreserved lemon brine, lemon juice, tiny vinegar splash.
More sweetOrange marmalade or honey, 1/2 tsp at a time.
More saltyKosher salt, olive brine, or a few drops soy.
More savoryReduce longer, add tiny Worcestershire, or add a small knob butter.
More spicyCayenne or red pepper flakes.
More umamiTiny soy, tiny Worcestershire, or a darker tomato paste bloom next time.

Chef's Note

The finished sauce should be glossy, aromatic, tangy, sweet-savory, lightly spicy, and rich enough to coat the chicken and couscous. If the sauce tastes thin, continue reducing before plating.
