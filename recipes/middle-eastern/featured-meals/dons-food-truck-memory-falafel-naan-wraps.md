# Don's Food-Truck Memory Falafel Naan Wraps

> Hot herb falafel served in warm homemade naan with tomato, onion, tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce, fiery red sauce, and optional lemon tahini. Don called the winning test **spot on**, making this a Flavorweaver Top 10 Candidate and Canon-Lock Candidate.

## Final Don Verdict

> spot on

This version successfully matched Don's remembered food-truck falafel wrap.

## Kitchen Execution Summary

| Element | Notes |
|---|---|
| Meal Role | Complete wrap / sauce bar. |
| Falafel | The falafel recipe itself needs no changes from the successful test. Dried chickpeas are soaked overnight, drained, dried, ground with herbs and aromatics, seasoned, chilled, shaped, and deep-fried until crisp outside and fluffy inside. |
| White Sauce | The winning white sauce is a thick tzatziki-ish ranch sauce: creamy, garlicky, herby, lemony, and cool against the hot falafel and fiery red sauce. |
| Red Sauce | The red sauce is spicy, tangy, vinegary, garlicky, red-chile-forward, and hot enough to feel aggressive by itself. Vinegar-style hot sauce and chipotle powder carry the tested heat direction. |
| Tahini Sauce | Lemon tahini remains optional. It is classic with falafel, but it is not the memory-critical sauce for Don's wrap. |
| Wrap Build | Warm naan replaces pita. The tested version used tomato and onion, with no required lettuce. |

## Before Cooking / Advance Prep

- **Chickpeas** — Soak overnight in the refrigerator.
- **Drain and dry** — Drain chickpeas 30 to 60 minutes before grinding, then rinse and dry very well.
- **Sauces** — Make sauces before frying falafel.
- **Falafel mixture** — Chill before shaping.
- **Test fry** — Fry one test falafel before frying the whole batch.
- **Naan** — Warm right before serving.
- **Service** — Serve falafel hot from the fryer.

## Do Not Forget

- Do not cook the chickpeas.
- Do not use canned chickpeas.
- Dry chickpeas well before grinding.
- Grind twice for the tested texture.
- Chill falafel mixture before shaping.
- Test-fry one falafel before frying all.
- Serve falafel hot.
- Warm naan right before building.
- Red sauce should be spicy enough to use carefully.
- Ranch white sauce and fiery red sauce are the memory-critical sauces.
- Tahini is optional.
- Lettuce is optional only, not required.

## Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

1. **Soak the chickpeas.** Place **1 lb dried chickpeas** in a large bowl. Cover with cold water by **3 to 4 inches**. Add **1/2 tsp baking soda** if using. Cover and refrigerate **18 to 24 hours**. Do not cook the chickpeas and do not use canned chickpeas.

2. **Drain and dry.** Drain chickpeas in a colander, rinse well with cold water, and shake off excess water. Spread chickpeas on a towel or baking sheet, pat dry, and let sit uncovered **30 to 60 minutes**. The chickpeas should be hydrated but surface-dry.

3. **Grind the falafel mixture.** Run chickpeas, onion, scallions, garlic, carrot, parsley, cilantro, and mint through the meat grinder. For the tested version, grind the mixture twice. The mixture should look finely ground and crumbly, not smooth like hummus.

4. **Season the falafel.** Transfer mixture to a large bowl. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, cardamom, cayenne, black pepper, kosher salt, baking powder, and flour. Mix by hand until evenly combined. Squeeze a small handful. It should hold together when pressed. If it crumbles badly, add cold water **1 Tbsp at a time**. Do not make the mixture wet.

5. **Chill the falafel mixture.** Cover and refrigerate at least **45 to 60 minutes** so the falafel hold together when fried.

6. **Make the white sauce.** Mix mayonnaise and sour cream or Greek yogurt. Add lemon juice, grated garlic, dill, onion powder, garlic powder, salt, pepper, and optional parsley or cumin. Stir until smooth. Add cucumber if using. Thin only if needed, **1 tsp at a time**. Chill **20 minutes** if possible.

7. **Make the fiery red sauce.** Soak guajillo chiles in very hot water for **10 to 15 minutes**, then drain. Blend soaked chiles, garlic, tomato paste, vinegar, lemon juice, water, vinegar-style hot sauce, cumin, coriander, paprika, chipotle powder, salt, sugar, oil, crushed red pepper, and cayenne until smooth. Add water **1 tsp at a time** only if needed. Taste a tiny amount and adjust heat, vinegar, lemon, sugar, salt, or texture carefully. Simmer **3 to 5 minutes** for a smoother deeper sauce if desired, then cool.

8. **Make the lemon tahini.** Whisk tahini, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and cumin. Slowly whisk in ice-cold water **1 Tbsp at a time** until smooth, pale, creamy, and pourable. Add honey and olive oil only if the tahini tastes very bitter. Adjust with lemon, salt, cumin, or water.

9. **Shape the falafel.** Use wet hands or a small scoop. Shape into small balls or slightly flattened patties, about **1 heaping tablespoon each**. Press firmly enough that they hold together. Place shaped falafel on a tray and keep chilled until frying.

10. **Test fry.** Heat oil to **350°F**. Fry one test falafel. If it holds together and tastes good, continue. If it falls apart, add **1 Tbsp more flour** and chill longer. If it tastes flat, add a small pinch more salt or cumin.

11. **Fry the full batch.** Fry falafel in small batches at **350°F** for **3 to 4 minutes**, until deeply golden and crisp. Do not overcrowd. Drain on a rack or paper towels. Salt lightly while hot.

12. **Warm naan and build.** Warm homemade naan until soft and flexible. Build wraps immediately while falafel are hot.

## Final Naan Wrap Build

### Don's Spot-On Memory Wrap

1. Warm naan until soft and flexible.
2. Spread tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce over the naan.
3. Add tomato.
4. Add thin onion.
5. Add hot falafel.
6. Spoon or stripe fiery red sauce over falafel.
7. Add extra ranch white sauce.
8. Fold like a soft wrap or taco.
9. Serve immediately.

### Classic Falafel Wrap

1. Warm naan.
2. Spread lemon tahini sauce over the naan.
3. Add tomato and onion.
4. Add hot falafel.
5. Add fiery red sauce.
6. Add a small ranch drizzle if desired.
7. Fold and serve immediately.

### Amy Plate Option

- 2 to 3 falafel
- Lemon tahini sauce, if desired

- Light red sauce
- Small amount tomato/onion if tolerated
- Pickles if they sound good

## Tested Assembly Notes

The successful test used warm homemade naan, hot deep-fried falafel, tomato, onion, tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce, fiery red sauce, and optional tahini.

Lettuce was available but was not used. Do not list lettuce as required for the tested version.

## Final Taste Target

The final wrap should have hot crispy falafel, soft warm naan, cool creamy white sauce, fiery red sauce, juicy tomato, sharp onion, and optional nutty lemon tahini.

The red sauce should bring the heat. The ranch white sauce should cool the bite. The tahini should add optional classic falafel depth. The falafel should stay crisp long enough to contrast with the sauces and fresh vegetables.

## Chef's Notes

Preserve the falafel recipe as written. The tested falafel texture worked, the meat grinder method worked, and warm naan worked well as the wrap base.

The winning sauce direction is tzatziki-ish ranch white sauce, fiery red sauce with vinegar-style hot sauce and chipotle powder, and optional lemon tahini.

The tested wrap build uses tomato and onion without required lettuce. The winning version is the version Don called **spot on**.
