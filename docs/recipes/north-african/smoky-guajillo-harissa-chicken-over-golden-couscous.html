Smoky Guajillo-Harissa Chicken over Spicy Tomato-Olive Couscous

Hall of Fame smoky guajillo-harissa chicken thighs served over spicy tomato-olive couscous with generous Thai chili hot honey, smoky sweet-hot cashews, and crispy pepper jack-Parmesan egg-olive brik-inspired triangles with bold tamarind-date chutney.

Flavor Profile

This complete plate is built around two bold but distinct lanes. The main plate is smoky, tomato-rich, chile-forward, briny, sweet-hot, buttery, and generously sauced. The side event is crispy, cheesy, eggy, spicy, olive-briny, and paired with a dark sweet-sour tamarind-date chutney.

Target bite: smoky chile chicken + spicy tomato-olive couscous + glossy Thai chili hot honey + sweet-hot cashew crunch + crispy pepper jack-Parmesan egg-olive triangles + tangy tamarind-date chutney.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Chicken

Chicken thighs marinated in smoky guajillo-harissa paste with tomato paste, garlic, cumin, coriander, caraway, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, acid, and salt.

Couscous

Spicy tomato-olive couscous built with 2 1/2 Tbsp reserved paste, tomato paste, spices, chicken broth, green olives, cilantro, and optional olive brine.

Signature Glaze

Generous Thai chili hot honey made with reserved paste, honey, butter, Thai chili, acid, and salt. It should drip into the couscous.

Crunch

Smoky sweet-hot cashews add nutty crunch with smoke, salt, brown sugar, and chile warmth.

Side Event

Crispy egg roll wrapper brik-inspired triangles filled with soft egg, pepper jack, Parmesan, olives, Thai chili, cilantro, and spices.

Showstopper Sauce

Bold tamarind-date chutney gives the side its own tangy, dark-fruity, spicy lane.

Measured Ingredients by Component

Smoky Guajillo-Harissa Paste

3 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded; 3 garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp olive oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice; optional 1 tsp lemon zest; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; 1/4 tsp caraway; 3/4 tsp smoked paprika; 3/4 tsp red pepper flakes; tiny pinch cinnamon or allspice; 3/4 tsp kosher salt; 1 to 3 Tbsp chile soaking water only as needed.

Chicken Marinade

4 chicken thighs; remaining guajillo-harissa paste after reserving paste for couscous and hot honey; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 1 tsp honey; 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 1/4 tsp black pepper.

Spicy Tomato-Olive Couscous

1 cup plain couscous; 1 Tbsp butter; 1 Tbsp olive oil; 1/4 cup finely diced onion; 2 cloves garlic; 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 1/2 Tbsp guajillo-harissa paste; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp coriander; 1/2 tsp smoked paprika; pinch caraway; tiny pinch cinnamon or allspice; 1 cup chicken broth; 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped green olives; 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped cilantro; optional 1 to 2 tsp olive brine; salt to taste.

Thai Chili Hot Honey Glaze

2 1/2 Tbsp reserved guajillo-harissa paste; 3 Tbsp honey; 1 1/2 Tbsp butter; 1 to 2 finely minced red Thai chiles; 2 tsp vinegar or lemon juice; generous pinch salt; optional pinch cayenne or red pepper flakes.

Smoky Sweet-Hot Cashews

1/2 cup cashews; 1 tsp olive oil or melted butter; 1/2 tsp smoked paprika; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/4 tsp coriander; 1/8 tsp garlic powder; pinch cayenne or red pepper flakes; 1/4 tsp kosher salt; 1/2 tsp brown sugar.

Crispy Pepper Jack-Parmesan Egg-Olive Brik Triangles

6 to 8 egg roll wrappers; 2 large eggs; 1/3 cup pepper jack; 1 Tbsp Parmesan; 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped green olives; 1 finely minced Thai chili; 1 Tbsp chopped cilantro; 1/4 tsp smoked paprika; 1/4 tsp cumin; 1/4 tsp garlic powder; pinch black pepper; tiny pinch salt only if needed; 1 Tbsp flour + 1 Tbsp water sealing paste or beaten egg; oil for frying.

Bold Tamarind-Date Chutney

Tamarind extract: 2 oz / 55 g whole tamarind pods or pulp, about 1/2 cup loosely packed broken tamarind pieces after shells are removed; 1 cup hot water for extraction. If the strained extract tastes mild, reduce it to 1/2 cup strong extract before making chutney. Chutney: 1/2 cup strong tamarind extract; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 2 Tbsp honey; 2 Tbsp chopped dates or raisins; 1/2 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp ginger powder or 1 tsp fresh ginger; cayenne and red pepper flakes to taste; 1/4 tsp salt; optional tiny pinch garlic powder; rice vinegar only if needed to correct sweetness.

Equipment Map

Electric skillet: cook chicken around 300°F, raising only if browning is too slow.

Oven: roast cashews at 325°F.

Deep fryer or deep pot: fry brik triangles around 350°F.

Small saucepan: reduce tamarind extract if needed, simmer tamarind-date chutney, and warm Thai chili hot honey.

Small skillet/pan: soft-scramble eggs and cook couscous base.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Paste Bowl

3 soaked guajillos; 3 garlic cloves; 2 Tbsp olive oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice; optional 1 tsp zest; 1 tsp cumin; 1 tsp coriander; 1/4 tsp caraway; 3/4 tsp smoked paprika; 3/4 tsp red pepper flakes; tiny pinch cinnamon/allspice; 3/4 tsp salt.

Reserved Paste + Chicken Bowl

Reserve 2 1/2 Tbsp paste for hot honey and 2 1/2 Tbsp paste for couscous. Marinate 4 chicken thighs with remaining paste, 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp honey, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 tsp pepper.

Couscous Bowl

1 cup couscous; base of 1 Tbsp butter, 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1/4 cup onion, 2 garlic cloves, 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste, 2 1/2 Tbsp paste, spices, 1 cup broth; finish with 2 to 3 Tbsp olives and 2 to 3 Tbsp cilantro.

Brik Filling Bowl

2 soft-scrambled eggs, 1/3 cup pepper jack, 1 Tbsp Parmesan, 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped olives, 1 minced Thai chili, 1 Tbsp cilantro, 1/4 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp cumin, 1/4 tsp garlic powder, and black pepper.

Cashew Bowl

1/2 cup cashews, 1 tsp oil or melted butter, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp cumin, 1/4 tsp coriander, 1/8 tsp garlic powder, pinch cayenne, 1/4 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp brown sugar.

Chutney Bowl

2 oz / 55 g whole tamarind with 1 cup hot water for extract. Reduce to 1/2 cup strong extract if mild. Simmer with 2 Tbsp brown sugar, 2 Tbsp honey, 2 Tbsp dates/raisins, 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp ginger, chile heat, and 1/4 tsp salt.

Do Not Forget

Reserve enough paste: 2 1/2 Tbsp for hot honey and 2 1/2 Tbsp for couscous before marinating chicken.

The couscous broth must taste slightly too bold before couscous goes in.

Use cilantro, not parsley, for the final herb lift.

The Thai chili hot honey is the signature sauce. Make it generous.

Use about 1 1/2 Tbsp filling per brik wrapper so the triangles seal cleanly.

Cashews burn quickly; roast gently and use brown sugar instead of honey.

If tamarind extract only tastes slightly sour, reduce it before building chutney.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Make tamarind extract. Remove shells and tough strings from 2 oz / 55 g whole tamarind pods or pulp. Soak with 1 cup hot water for 15 to 20 minutes, mash thoroughly, then strain and press hard. If the liquid tastes only lightly sour, simmer uncovered until reduced to 1/2 cup strong extract.
  2. Toast and soak guajillos. Stem and seed 3 dried guajillo chiles. Toast 10 to 20 seconds per side, then soak in hot water 10 to 15 minutes. Reserve 1 to 3 Tbsp soaking water.
  3. Blend paste. Blend softened chiles with 3 garlic cloves, 2 Tbsp olive oil, 1 Tbsp tomato paste, 1 Tbsp vinegar or lemon juice, optional 1 tsp lemon zest, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1/4 tsp caraway, 3/4 tsp smoked paprika, 3/4 tsp red pepper flakes, tiny pinch cinnamon/allspice, 3/4 tsp salt, and just enough soaking water to make a thick paste.
  4. Reserve paste and marinate chicken. Reserve 2 1/2 Tbsp paste for hot honey and 2 1/2 Tbsp paste for couscous. Coat 4 chicken thighs with remaining paste, 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp honey, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/4 tsp black pepper. Marinate at least 1 hour.
  5. Simmer chutney. Combine 1/2 cup strong tamarind extract, 2 Tbsp brown sugar, 2 Tbsp honey, 2 Tbsp chopped dates or raisins, 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp ginger powder or 1 tsp fresh ginger, cayenne and red pepper flakes to taste, 1/4 tsp salt, and optional tiny pinch garlic powder. Simmer 6 to 10 minutes until glossy and spoonable. Correct sweetness with rice vinegar, tamarind, salt, and heat if needed.
  6. Roast cashews. Heat oven to 325°F. Toss 1/2 cup cashews with 1 tsp olive oil or melted butter, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp cumin, 1/4 tsp coriander, 1/8 tsp garlic powder, a pinch cayenne or red pepper flakes, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp brown sugar. Roast 8 to 12 minutes.
  7. Make brik filling. Soft-scramble 2 eggs until barely set. Cool slightly, then mix with 1/3 cup pepper jack, 1 Tbsp Parmesan, 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped green olives, 1 minced Thai chili, 1 Tbsp cilantro, 1/4 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp cumin, 1/4 tsp garlic powder, and black pepper. Taste before adding any salt.
  8. Wrap brik triangles. Mix 1 Tbsp flour with 1 Tbsp water for sealing paste, or use beaten egg. Place about 1 1/2 Tbsp filling on each wrapper. Fold into triangles or sealed packets and seal well.
  9. Make couscous base. Heat 1 Tbsp butter and 1 Tbsp olive oil. Add 1/4 cup onion and cook until softened. Add 2 minced garlic cloves, then 1 1/2 Tbsp tomato paste, 2 1/2 Tbsp reserved paste, 1/2 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, pinch caraway, and tiny pinch cinnamon/allspice. Cook 1 to 2 minutes.
  10. Steam couscous. Add 1 cup chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Taste for boldness. Add salt or 1 to 2 tsp olive brine if needed. Stir in 1 cup couscous, cover, remove from heat, and rest 5 minutes. Fluff, then fold in 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped olives and 2 to 3 Tbsp chopped cilantro.
  11. Fry brik triangles. Fry around 350°F until deeply golden and crisp, about 2 to 4 minutes. Drain on a rack.
  12. Cook chicken. Heat electric skillet to about 300°F. Cook chicken until browned and cooked through, about 5 to 7 minutes per side for boneless thighs, longer for bone-in. Rest 5 minutes.
  13. Make hot honey. While chicken rests, warm 2 1/2 Tbsp reserved paste, 3 Tbsp honey, 1 1/2 Tbsp butter, 1 to 2 minced Thai chiles, 2 tsp vinegar or lemon juice, and a generous pinch salt until glossy.
  14. Glaze and plate. Spoon Thai chili hot honey over the rested chicken. Plate spicy tomato-olive couscous, glazed chicken, extra hot honey, smoky sweet-hot cashews, crispy brik triangles, and generous tamarind-date chutney.

Final Taste Target

The finished meal should taste smoky, spicy, tomato-rich, garlicky, briny, buttery, sweet-hot, crisp, cheesy, cilantro-bright, and darkly sweet-sour. The main plate delivers chile chicken, bold couscous, hot honey, and cashew crunch. The side feels like a separate street-food event: crisp wrappers, melty pepper jack, savory Parmesan, soft egg, olives, Thai chili, cilantro, and bold tamarind-date chutney.
