Smokehouse Pulled Pork Hawaiian-Style Sliders

Oven-seared pork butt, pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus, sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch, and from-scratch Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls built for a food-truck-level pulled pork slider plate.

Flavor Target

Smoky, savory, pull-apart pork with sweet barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar lift, soft pineapple-butter rolls, and sweet-spicy pickle brightness.

This should not taste like generic slow-cooker pork in bottled sauce. The difference comes from overnight seasoning, a hard sear, oven braising, reduced pork jus, and a broiled bark finish.

Kitchen Execution Summary

Overnight Smokehouse Pork

Pork butt is salted, slathered, rubbed, and chilled 8 to 18 hours so the seasoning penetrates beyond the surface.

Oven Braise

A hard sear builds crust, then pineapple juice, vinegar, broth, onion, garlic, mustard, brown sugar, and Worcestershire build a tangy BBQ jus.

BBQ Jus Glaze

Reduced pork cooking juices become the base for a glossy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze.

Broiled Bark Finish

Lightly sauced pulled pork is broiled so the edges turn sticky, smoky, and caramelized.

Hawaiian-Style Rolls

Pineapple juice, milk, room-temperature eggs, cooled melted butter, brown sugar, and dry potato flakes make plush sweet rolls sturdy enough for sliders.

Pickle-Onion Crunch

Sweet-spicy pickles, purple onion, mustard, honey, brine, pepper, and cayenne cut the richness.

Before Cooking Day / Advance Prep

8 to 18 hours before cooking: salt the pork, apply wet slather, apply dry rub, cover, and refrigerate.

60 to 90 minutes before roll dough: set out 2 large eggs and 1 egg yolk. Melt 4 Tbsp butter and cool until fluid but not hot.

Cook day morning: pull pork from the fridge 30 to 45 minutes before searing.

Early cook day: start Hawaiian-style roll dough so rolls can rise while pork braises.

While pork braises: make pickle-onion crunch and chill.

After pork braises: strain and skim juices, build BBQ jus glaze, shred pork, glaze lightly, and broil for barky edges.

Service Countdown

Night before: salt, slather, rub, and refrigerate pork.

7 to 8 hours before serving: set eggs out for rolls, melt and cool butter, warm roll liquids, start roll dough, pull pork from fridge, preheat oven, and prepare braise base.

6 to 7 hours before serving: sear pork, build braise, cover, and oven-braise at 300°F.

3 to 4 hours before serving: shape rolls and let rise.

1 hour before serving: strain pork juices, skim fat, simmer BBQ jus glaze, pull pork, and heat rolls if needed.

30 minutes before serving: broil pulled pork for barky edges; slice and toast rolls.

At service: build sliders with glaze, pork, pickle-onion crunch, optional cheese, and honey-buttered tops.

Measured Ingredients by Component

These component sections are reference only. The cooking flow is handled by the stage-based prep bowls and full timeline below.

Overnight Pork Seasoning

4 to 5 lb pork butt or pork shoulder, bone-in or boneless; 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.

Wet Slather

2 Tbsp yellow mustard; 1 Tbsp hot sauce; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar or sweet-spicy pickle brine; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp brown sugar.

Dry Rub

1 Tbsp smoked paprika; 1 Tbsp paprika; 1 Tbsp chili powder; 2 tsp garlic powder; 2 tsp onion powder; 2 tsp mustard powder; 1 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 tsp ground coriander; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp chipotle powder or cayenne; optional 1/4 tsp ground ginger; optional tiny pinch cinnamon. Do not add extra salt to the dry rub if pork was salted overnight.

Oven Braising Base

1 large sliced onion; 6 smashed garlic cloves; 1/2 cup pineapple juice; 1/2 cup chicken broth, apple juice, or water; 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp yellow/stone-ground mustard; optional tiny dash liquid smoke.

Pineapple-Vinegar BBQ Jus Glaze

1 cup reduced pork cooking juices; 1/2 cup tomato sauce or ketchup; 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 Tbsp honey or molasses; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce; 1 Tbsp yellow mustard or stone-ground mustard; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1/2 tsp chili powder; 1/4 tsp black pepper; optional pinch cayenne; optional 1 Tbsp bourbon or whiskey.

Hawaiian-Style Sweet Slider Rolls

1/4 cup warm water at 100°F to 110°F; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1 Tbsp sugar; 1/2 cup pineapple juice, warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 1/2 cup milk, warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 2 room-temperature large eggs; 1 room-temperature egg yolk; 1/3 cup brown sugar; 4 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted and cooled until fluid but not hot; 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 2 Tbsp plain instant potato flakes; 3 3/4 cups bread flour or AP flour, starting with 3 cups and adding the rest gradually; optional 1 tsp vanilla; egg wash from 1 egg white or 1 whole egg plus 1 Tbsp water or milk; butter top from 2 Tbsp melted butter, optional 1 tsp honey, and tiny pinch salt.

Use plain instant potato flakes dry. If using fresh pineapple juice, heat it to 200°F, then cool to lukewarm before adding it to the dough.

Sweet-Spicy Pickle-Onion Crunch

1/2 cup finely chopped sweet-spicy pickles; 1/4 cup finely minced purple onion; 1 Tbsp pickle brine; 1 tsp yellow mustard or stone-ground mustard; 1 tsp honey or brown sugar; pinch black pepper; optional pinch cayenne.

Slider Assembly

12 Hawaiian-style sweet slider rolls; warm pulled pork with broiled barky edges; pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus glaze; sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch; optional extra pickles; optional thin purple onion; optional pepper jack, cheddar, or provolone; honey butter for brushing tops.

Cross-Component Prep Map

Pineapple juice: appears in the braise and roll dough. Warm the dough portion to 95°F to 105°F; keep the braise portion measured separately.

Eggs: dough eggs and yolk should be room temperature so they do not chill the yeast mixture or tighten the butter.

Butter: melt roll butter first, then cool until fluid but not hot. It should not steam or feel hot to the touch.

Brown sugar: appears in the slather, braise, glaze, rolls, and optional crunch. Stage by component.

Pork cooking juices: strain, skim, and reduce before building the BBQ jus glaze.

Stage-Based Prep Bowls

Night-Before Pork Bowls

Bowl 1 — Pork Salt

4 to 5 lb pork butt or shoulder; 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.

Bowl 2 — Wet Slather

2 Tbsp yellow mustard; 1 Tbsp hot sauce; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp vinegar or pickle brine; 1 Tbsp neutral oil; 1 Tbsp tomato paste; 2 Tbsp brown sugar.

Bowl 3 — Dry Rub

1 Tbsp smoked paprika; 1 Tbsp paprika; 1 Tbsp chili powder; 2 tsp garlic powder; 2 tsp onion powder; 2 tsp mustard powder; 1 1/2 tsp black pepper; 1 tsp coriander; 1 tsp cumin; 1/2 tsp chipotle/cayenne; optional ginger and cinnamon.

Cook-Day Pork Bowls

Bowl 4 — Braise Bed

1 large sliced onion; 6 smashed garlic cloves.

Bowl 5 — Braise Liquid

1/2 cup pineapple juice; 1/2 cup broth/apple juice/water; 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 tsp mustard powder or 1 Tbsp prepared mustard; optional tiny liquid smoke.

Bowl 6 — BBQ Jus Glaze

1 cup reduced pork juices; 1/2 cup tomato sauce/ketchup; 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar; 2 Tbsp brown sugar; 1 Tbsp honey/molasses; 1 Tbsp Worcestershire; 1 Tbsp mustard; 1 tsp smoked paprika; 1/2 tsp chili powder; 1/4 tsp black pepper; optional cayenne and bourbon.

Roll Bowls

Bowl 7 — Yeast Bloom

1/4 cup warm water at 100°F to 110°F; 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast; 1 Tbsp sugar. Rest 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.

Bowl 8 — Warm Roll Liquids

1/2 cup pineapple juice warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 1/2 cup milk warmed to 95°F to 105°F; 1/3 cup brown sugar; 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt; 2 Tbsp dry potato flakes; optional 1 tsp vanilla. Whisk before flour.

Bowl 9 — Enrichment

2 room-temperature eggs; 1 room-temperature egg yolk; 4 Tbsp melted butter, cooled until fluid but not hot. Whisk eggs into warm liquids first, then cooled butter.

Bowl 10 — Flour

3 cups flour to start; remaining 3/4 cup held back. Add gradually only until dough is soft, tacky, and elastic.

Bowl 11 — Roll Finish

Egg wash: 1 egg white or whole egg + 1 Tbsp water/milk. Butter top: 2 Tbsp melted butter + optional 1 tsp honey + tiny pinch salt.

Crunch and Assembly Bowls

Bowl 12 — Pickle-Onion Crunch

1/2 cup chopped sweet-spicy pickles; 1/4 cup minced purple onion; 1 Tbsp pickle brine; 1 tsp mustard; 1 tsp honey/brown sugar; pinch black pepper; optional cayenne.

Bowl 13 — Slider Assembly

Split rolls; warm pulled pork; BBQ jus glaze; pickle-onion crunch; optional cheese; honey butter.

Do Not Forget

For the roll dough, use room-temperature eggs and yolk. Cold eggs can slow the yeast and make butter seize into little pieces.

Melt the roll butter first, then cool it until it is fluid but not hot. It should not steam, feel hot to the touch, or cook the eggs.

Bloom active dry yeast in 100°F to 110°F water with sugar before adding the enriched ingredients.

Warm pineapple juice and milk to about 95°F to 105°F. Warm is good; hot is not.

Whisk sugar, salt, and potato flakes into the warm liquids before adding flour so they distribute evenly.

Start with 3 cups flour, then add the remaining 3/4 cup gradually. Do not over-flour the dough.

Do not skip overnight pork seasoning, searing, reduced cooking juices, or the broiled bark finish.

Full Cooking Timeline and Instructions

  1. Season pork the night before. Pat 4 to 5 lb pork butt or shoulder very dry. Rub evenly with 2 to 2 1/2 tsp kosher salt.
  2. Apply wet slather. Mix 2 Tbsp yellow mustard, 1 Tbsp hot sauce, 1 Tbsp Worcestershire, 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar or pickle brine, 1 Tbsp neutral oil, 1 Tbsp tomato paste, and 2 Tbsp brown sugar. Coat pork on all sides.
  3. Apply dry rub and refrigerate. Mix dry rub ingredients and coat pork heavily. Refrigerate 8 to 18 hours.
  4. Temper pork. Remove pork from the refrigerator 30 to 45 minutes before cooking. Do not leave it out for hours.
  5. Prep roll enrichment before mixing. Set 2 large eggs and 1 egg yolk out until room temperature. Melt 4 Tbsp butter, then cool until fluid but not hot. Warm 1/2 cup pineapple juice and 1/2 cup milk to 95°F to 105°F. If using fresh pineapple juice, heat it to 200°F first, then cool to lukewarm.
  6. Bloom yeast. In the mixer bowl, combine 1/4 cup warm water at 100°F to 110°F, 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast, and 1 Tbsp sugar. Rest 5 to 10 minutes until foamy. If it does not foam, stop and restart.
  7. Build the wet dough base in order. To the foamy yeast, add warm pineapple juice and warm milk. Whisk in 1/3 cup brown sugar, 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 2 Tbsp dry potato flakes, and optional 1 tsp vanilla.
  8. Add eggs and cooled butter. Whisk in the room-temperature eggs and yolk. Then whisk in cooled melted butter. The butter should disappear into the mixture; it should not be hot enough to cook the eggs or hurt the yeast.
  9. Add flour gradually and knead. Add 3 cups flour first and mix on low until shaggy. Knead with dough hook 8 to 10 minutes, adding the remaining 3/4 cup flour only as needed. Dough should be soft, tacky, stretchy, and elastic.
  10. First rise. Place dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover, and let rise 1 1/2 to 2 hours, until very puffy and nearly doubled.
  11. Sear pork. Heat Dutch oven or heavy skillet with 1 to 2 Tbsp neutral oil. Sear pork on all sides until dark mahogany, not burned black sugar.
  12. Build braise. Place onion and garlic in Dutch oven or roasting pan. Add braise liquid ingredients. Place seared pork on top and cover tightly.
  13. Oven-braise. Bake at 300°F until fork-shreddable: about 4 1/2 to 6 hours for large hunks or 6 to 7 hours for whole bone-in pork butt.
  14. Shape rolls during braise. Grease or line a 9x13 pan. Divide dough into 12 equal pieces, shape tight smooth balls, cover, and rise 45 to 75 minutes until puffy and touching or nearly touching.
  15. Bake rolls. Brush with egg wash. Bake at 350°F for 20 to 25 minutes, until golden and about 190°F in the center. Brush hot rolls with butter-honey top and cool at least 20 minutes before slicing.
  16. Rest pork and save juices. Transfer pork to tray and rest 20 to 30 minutes. Strain cooking juices, skim excess fat, and reserve juices for glaze.
  17. Make BBQ jus glaze. Combine glaze ingredients and simmer 10 to 15 minutes until glossy and slightly thickened.
  18. Make pickle-onion crunch. Mix crunch ingredients and chill until serving.
  19. Pull pork. Shred into large tender strands. Add reduced pork juices and a small amount of BBQ jus glaze so pork is juicy and glossy, not swimming.
  20. Broil for barky edges. Spread pork on a sheet pan. Broil 3 to 6 minutes, watching closely, until some edges crisp and caramelize. Toss once and broil 1 to 2 minutes more if needed.
  21. Toast rolls. Slice rolls and lightly toast cut sides with butter.
  22. Build sliders. Add BBQ jus glaze to the bottom roll, then warm pulled pork, pickle-onion crunch, optional cheese, and top roll. Brush tops with honey butter.
  23. Warm tray sliders if serving together. Warm covered at 325°F for 8 to 10 minutes. Uncover 2 to 3 minutes to set the tops.

Hawaiian Roll Troubleshooting

Yeast does not foam: stop and restart with fresh yeast, warm water, and sugar.

Butter forms little bits: butter was too cool or eggs/liquids were too cold. Next time use room-temperature eggs and fluid-but-not-hot butter.

Dough is very sticky: knead longer before adding more flour. Add flour 1 Tbsp at a time only if it will not gather.

Dough is stiff or dry: stop adding flour and knead in 1 tsp milk at a time until soft and tacky.

Rolls are dense: usually under-proofed, over-floured, cold eggs, or inactive yeast. Let final rise go until puffy and touching.

BBQ Jus Adjustment Notes

Too sweet: add more apple cider vinegar. Too sharp: add brown sugar or honey. Flat: add salt or a splash more reduced pork jus. Too gentle: add cayenne or hot sauce. Too thick: add pork jus. Too thin: simmer longer.

Building the Final Slider

1. Toasted Roll

Split Hawaiian-style roll and toast the cut sides lightly with butter.

2. BBQ Jus Base

Add a thin spoonful of pineapple-vinegar BBQ jus glaze to the bottom roll.

3. Pork Anchor

Pile on warm pulled pork with broiled barky edges.

4. Bright Crunch

Add a small spoonful of sweet-spicy pickle-onion crunch.

5. Optional Cheese

Add pepper jack for creamy heat, cheddar for classic BBQ, or provolone for mild melt.

6. Honey-Butter Top

Add top roll, brush with honey butter, and warm as tray sliders if desired.

Final Taste Target

The finished slider should hit in layers: soft buttery pineapple roll, smoky savory pork, sticky barky edges, tangy pineapple-vinegar BBQ glaze, sweet-spicy pickle crunch, warm BBQ spice, and a clean acidic finish.

It should be sweet, but not candy-sweet. It should be saucy, but not wet. It should feel like barbecue met homemade sweet-roll nostalgia and became a food-truck slider worth chasing again.
